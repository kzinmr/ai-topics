# OpenAI May 2026 Product Reorganization

## Overview

In May 2026, OpenAI announced its **most sweeping product reorganization** since founding, consolidating all product development under **Greg Brockman** (President) and dissolving the boundary between ChatGPT, Codex, and the API platform.

## Key Leadership Changes

| Executive | New Role | Background |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Greg Brockman** | President, Product | All product lines report to him |
| **Fidji Simo** | CEO, AGI Deployment | Medical leave; ex-AppLovin CEO, Meta VP |
| **Thibault Sottiaux** | Head of Core Product + Platform | Former Codex lead → now ChatGPT + Codex + API |

## Product Consolidation

**ChatGPT and Codex merger**:
- Merged into a single product team under Sottiaux
- Goal: "super app" combining ChatGPT consumer + Codex developer + API platform
- ~20 engineers from separate Codex team integrated into main product org

**API as a first-class product**:
- API platform elevated to equal status with ChatGPT/Codex
- Previously treated as secondary distribution channel

## Strategic Context

**Why the reorganization matters**:

1. **Acknowledges ChatGPT's limitations** - Even with 800M weekly active users, ChatGPT alone cannot sustain OpenAI's $80B+ revenue target
2. **Developer platform plays catch-up** - Anthropic's Claude had been winning developer mindshare; OpenAI needed to unify its developer offering
3. **"Super app" ambition** - OpenAI wants to be the single destination for all AI interactions (consumer, developer, enterprise)
4. **Internal culture clash** - Reports describe the reorg as "abrupt and chaotic" with employees struggling to understand new reporting lines

## Market Reaction

- **Wired** characterized OpenAI as "a very stable well-run company" (sarcastic)
- The reorganization came as OpenAI's valuation climbed past $300B
- Questions raised about whether consolidating products can actually accelerate delivery or will slow it down
