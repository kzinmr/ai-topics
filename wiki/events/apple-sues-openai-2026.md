# Apple Sues OpenAI for Trade Secret Theft (July 2026)

On **July 10, 2026**, Apple filed a lawsuit against [[entities/openai|OpenAI]] in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, accusing the company of trade secret theft related to consumer hardware development. The suit names OpenAI, **io Products**, and two former Apple employees — **Chang Liu** and **Tang Tan** — as defendants.

## Key Allegations

### Interview-Based Intelligence Gathering
- **Tang Tan** (former VP of Product Design at Apple, leading iPhone and Apple Watch design) used insider knowledge of Apple's confidential projects to grill job candidates during interviews
- Tan directed candidates still working at Apple to bring **actual Apple hardware components and samples** for "show and tell" sessions at OpenAI
- One candidate commented that he "didn't even know we could take those from the office"
- Tan used an Apple internal project codename to ask "What's the plan?" for an unannounced Apple product

### Document and File Theft
- Tan allegedly possessed and distributed an internal Apple **"Need to Know" document** to new OpenAI hires before they gave notice to Apple
- **Chang Liu** (former senior system electrical engineer, 8 years at Apple) exploited a security bug to download confidential engineering files after leaving, joking about it in messages ("LOL," "so funny")
- Liu downloaded "a compilation of technical files with over a thousand pages" including detailed manufacturing documents for complex circuit boards
- Liu failed to return an Apple-issued laptop after departure

### Supplier Manipulation
- OpenAI allegedly had a trusted Apple partner carry out Apple's proprietary **metal-finishing technique**, misleading the partner into believing it had Apple's permission
- OpenAI approached a second longtime Apple supplier working on power and battery manufacturing, using insider terminology to ask "targeted questions" about specific Apple components

## Scale of the Problem
- Apple's filing states there are **over 400 former Apple employees** now working at OpenAI
- Apple first raised concerns with OpenAI in **February 2026**, asking the company to investigate — OpenAI never responded
- Apple describes the conduct as "the tip of the iceberg"

## Context: OpenAI's Hardware Push
- OpenAI acquired Jony Ive's startup **io** as part of a **$6.5 billion deal** in 2025, including 50+ engineers
- OpenAI is developing its own **smartphone** (rumored 2028 launch) and a **HomePod-style smart speaker**
- The lawsuit comes as OpenAI works to bring its first consumer hardware device to market
- Bloomberg reported OpenAI was separately preparing legal action against Apple over the ChatGPT-Siri integration deal

## Significance
This lawsuit marks a major escalation in the Apple-OpenAI relationship, which had already been strained. Apple is now opening Siri to **Google Gemini** (WWDC 2026) and iOS 27 will allow **Anthropic Claude** and other AI models. The case highlights the intense talent war between frontier AI labs and traditional hardware companies, and the legal risks of aggressive recruiting from competitors with active hardware programs.
