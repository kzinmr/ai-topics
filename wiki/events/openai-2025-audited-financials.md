# OpenAI 2025 Audited Financials — $38.5B Net Loss

On June 15, 2026, Ed Zitron (Where's Your Ed At) exclusively reported **audited financial documents** for OpenAI's 2024 and 2025 calendar years, independently verified by the Financial Times. The numbers reveal losses far exceeding most public estimates.

## 2024 Financials

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| **Revenue** | $3.7B |
| Cost of Revenue | $2.65B |
| R&D | $7.81B |
| Sales & Marketing | $1.11B |
| G&A | $907M |
| **Total Costs & Expenses** | $12.48B |
| **Loss from Operations** | $8.78B |
| **Net Loss (attributable to company)** | $5.09B |

> $3.74B in losses were attributed to "noncontrolling members capital," reducing the reported net loss from $8.84B to $5.09B. The mechanism for this removal is unclear.

## 2025 Financials

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| **Revenue** | $13.07B |
| Cost of Revenue | $7.5B |
| R&D | $19.18B |
| Sales & Marketing | $5.73B |
| G&A | $1.57B |
| **Total Costs & Expenses** | $34B |
| **Loss from Operations** | $20.92B |
| **Net Loss (attributable to company)** | $38.53B |

### Key 2025 Details

- **Non-profit → for-profit conversion** triggered a **$41.55B loss** from changes in fair value of convertible interests and warrant liability
- Gross net loss before adjustments: **$60.35B**, reduced to $38.53B via:
  - $17.87B attributed to "noncontrolling members capital"
  - $3.95B attributed to "redeemable noncontrolling interests"
- **Year-end assets**: ~$50B, nearly half in cash

### Revenue from Key Partners (2025)

| Partner | Payment to OpenAI |
|---------|-------------------|
| SoftBank | $867M |
| Microsoft | $303M |

### OpenAI's Payments to Microsoft (2025)

| Category | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| R&D (likely model training) | $10.59B |
| Cost of Revenue | $6.047B |
| Sales & Marketing | $527M |
| G&A | $42M |
| **Total** | **$17.2B** |

- Liabilities to Microsoft at year-end: $3.64B + $21M accrued + $58M non-current

## Significance

- **Losses increased ~8x** year-over-year (from $5.09B to $38.53B attributable net loss)
- Revenue grew ~3.5x ($3.7B → $13.07B) while costs grew ~2.7x ($12.48B → $34B), but absolute losses widened dramatically
- The $41.55B fair value adjustment from the corporate restructuring dominates the 2025 picture
- OpenAI paid Microsoft **$17.2B** while receiving only **$303M** back — a massive net outflow to its primary infrastructure partner
- At end of 2025, OpenAI had ~$50B in assets (half cash), suggesting runway of roughly 1-2 years at the 2025 burn rate without new funding

## Context

This disclosure sits against the backdrop of [[entities/openai|OpenAI]]'s broader financial trajectory:
