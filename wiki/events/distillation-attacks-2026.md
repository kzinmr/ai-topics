# Anthropic Accuses Chinese Labs of Industrial-Scale Distillation Attacks

## Overview

In April 2026, **Anthropic** made widely viewed and criticized accusations that three leading Chinese AI labs — **DeepSeek**, **Moonshot**, and **MiniMax** — conducted "industrial-scale distillation attacks" exceeding **16 million instances**.

## Key Details

### The Accusation
Anthropic claimed these labs engaged in systematic distillation of their proprietary models, using the outputs of Claude and other frontier models to train competing systems at unprecedented scale.

### Scale Variation
- **MiniMax** allegedly an order of magnitude smaller than the other two
- The accusation was met with significant criticism from the AI community

### Community Response
The accusations were described as:
- "VERY widely viewed and criticized"
- Seen as potentially protectionist given competitive pressure from Chinese labs
- Questioned on technical and legal merits

## Background: Model Distillation

**Distillation** refers to using outputs from a larger/smaller teacher model to train a student model. This is a standard and legitimate technique in ML, but:
- Using frontier model outputs at industrial scale without authorization raises IP concerns
- The debate intersects with broader tensions around open-source AI

## Implications

### For Chinese Labs
- DeepSeek, Moonshot, and MiniMax are three of the leading open model providers
- Accusations could affect perception of their model quality origins
- Moonshot just released Kimi K2.6 as a major refresh

### For Industry
- Highlights ongoing tensions between open and closed model approaches
- Raises questions about what constitutes fair use of API outputs for training
- May accelerate calls for model rights frameworks
