# OpenAI 'Built to Benefit Everyone' Plan

> Published 2026-06-08: [Built to benefit everyone: our plan](https://openai.com/index/built-to-benefit-everyone-our-plan/)

## Summary

[[entities/openai|OpenAI]] published a strategic vision document framing AI as the next general-purpose technology analogous to electricity. The post articulates three main goals and announces the **third phase** of the company.

## Three Main Goals

1. **Build an automated AI researcher** — AI systems that accelerate and automate research while remaining steerable and accountable. Target: by March 2028, a significant fraction of OpenAI research done by AI systems alongside human researchers.
2. **Accelerate the economy** — Drive scientific progress, productivity, and economic growth with widely shared gains.
3. **Give everyone on Earth a personal AGI** — Empower every person to benefit from AGI in whatever way they choose.

## Three Phases of OpenAI

| Phase | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| Phase 1 | Research toward AGI |
| Phase 2 | Product company — deploy systems, learn from users |
| Phase 3 | **Current** — Make advanced AI abundant, affordable, safe, useful, and accessible to every person and organization |

## Key Themes

### AI as General-Purpose Technology
The post draws a direct analogy between AI and electrification (1920s→), noting that the average lifespan increased by 20+ years and median real income tripled by end of 20th century, driven in part by electrification.

### Broad Power Distribution
- Core thesis: concentrated power creates fragility; widely shared power creates resilience
- "A good AI future cannot be one where a small number of institutions control most of the capability and most of the upside"
- Emphasis on safety, privacy, affordability, open ecosystems, and public oversight

### Human Role in an AI Future
- Explicitly rejects full automation: "Entirely automating everything is not the future we want"
- "A key long-term role for people will be deciding what is worth doing"
- Human judgment becomes more important as systems become more capable: setting direction, making tradeoffs, applying judgment

### AI Safety & Alignment
- AI doing AI research expected to become the pace-of-progress factor within the next few years
- Alignment acknowledged as "a hard research problem" requiring AI-assisted iteration
- Calls for international coordination organization to reduce catastrophic risk
- Supports the ability to "slow frontier development when needed" for safety

### AI Resilience
- Defines "AI resilience" as collective organizations, systems, and individuals that anticipate, withstand, adapt to, and recover from AI-driven disruptions
- Analogy: automobiles became broadly beneficial through seatbelts, traffic laws, crash testing, road infrastructure

## Significance

This is one of OpenAI's most explicit public statements on AI alignment and AI governance. The March 2028 target for automated AI researcher is a concrete, time-bound claim. The post positions OpenAI's third phase as infrastructure-and-access focused rather than pure capability research.
