# Event: SpaceXAI Launches Grok 4.5

**Date**: July 9, 2026
**Type**: Model Release
**Significance**: SpaceXAI publicly launched Grok 4.5 as a new coding-and-agents-focused frontier model — the first model co-trained with [[concepts/spacex-cursor-acquisition|Cursor]] following the $60B acquisition. Positioned on capability-per-dollar rather than absolute benchmark supremacy.

## Overview

Grok 4.5 is [[entities/xai|xAI/xAI]]'s first model built specifically for coding and [[concepts/coding-agents/coding-agents|AI coding agents]], developed through a co-training partnership between xAI and Cursor following SpaceX's $60B acquisition of Anysphere. The model represents a strategic pivot from xAI's previous general-purpose frontier models (Grok 4.1 Fast, 4.20, 4.3) toward a specialized coding-and-agents focus.

## Key Details

- **First model trained specifically for coding and agents**, co-trained with [[concepts/spacex-cursor-acquisition|Cursor]]
- **Positioned on capability-per-dollar** rather than absolute benchmark supremacy — a deliberate pricing strategy
- **Elon Musk**: "Opus-class, but faster, more token-efficient, and lower cost"
- **Pricing position**: ~6x cheaper than Claude Opus 4.8, ~3x cheaper than GPT-5.5, reinforcing the capability-per-dollar strategy (per Ben's Bites, Jul 2026)
- **Available in Cursor** with double usage for first week
- **Grok 4.5 and Cursor Composer 2.5** are different model weight classes — Grok 4.5 is a new model, not a rebranding of Composer
- **Supported immediately** by [[entities/hermes-agent|Hermes Agent]] and [[entities/openrouter|OpenRouter]]

## Strategic Context

The launch follows SpaceX's $60B acquisition of Anysphere (Cursor) on June 16, 2026. The co-training arrangement marks the first major product-level integration between xAI and Cursor post-acquisition, combining xAI's model-building expertise with Cursor's coding agent data and feedback.

Grok 4.5 is positioned to compete directly with [[entities/claude-code|Claude Code]] (Anthropic), [[entities/codex|OpenAI Codex]], and other coding-focused models — but with an explicit value-for-money angle rather than raw benchmark performance.
