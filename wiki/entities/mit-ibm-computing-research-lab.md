# MIT-IBM Computing Research Lab

## Overview

**Launched:** April 29, 2026 | **Location:** Cambridge, MA | **Successor to:** MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab (est. 2017)

A joint research initiative between IBM and MIT exploring the convergence of AI, advanced algorithms, and quantum computing. The lab expands beyond the AI-only scope of its predecessor toward "quantum-centric supercomputing" — hybrid systems combining classical, AI, and quantum hardware.

## Research Pillars & Leadership

| Focus Area | MIT Lead | IBM Lead |
|------------|----------|----------|
| **AI** | Jacob Andreas (Assoc. Prof, EECS) | Kenney Ng (Principal Research Scientist) |
| **Algorithms** | Vinod Vaikuntanathan (Prof, EECS) | Vasileios Kalantzis (Senior Research Scientist) |
| **Quantum** | Aram Harrow (Prof, Physics) | Hanhee Paik (Director, Quantum Algorithm Centers) |

**Co-Directors:** Aude Oliva (MIT CSAIL), David Cox (VP, AI Foundations at IBM Research)

## Strategic Objectives

### AI Innovation
- Small, efficient, modular language model architectures
- Enterprise-focused AI with reliability, transparency, and trust
- Novel AI computing paradigms beyond current transformer architectures

### Quantum Integration
- Leveraging IBM's roadmap to **fault-tolerant quantum computer by 2029**
- Integrating quantum hardware with HPC and AI accelerators
- Hamiltonian simulations, PDE solving, ML optimization

### Applications
- **Materials Science**: Predicting protein structures, discovering new materials
- **Finance**: Market performance forecasting, risk optimization
- **Climate**: Weather prediction, air turbulence modeling
- **Logistics**: Global supply chain optimization

## Legacy & Scale
Building on the MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab's track record since 2017:
- **210+** joint research projects
- **1,500+** peer-reviewed publications
- **500+** students and postdoctoral scholars funded
- **150+** MIT faculty and **200+** IBM researchers involved

## Key Insights

> "Together, the brightest minds at MIT and IBM will rethink how models, algorithms, and systems are designed for an era defined by the sum of what's possible when AI and quantum computing come together." — Jay Gambetta, Director of IBM Research

The lab works alongside the MIT Schwarzman College of Computing, MIT Generative AI Impact Consortium, and MIT Quantum Initiative.
