# Reflection AI

## Overview

**Founded:** ~2024 | **Location:** United States | **Valuation:** Seeking $25B | **Stage:** No public model released

Reflection AI is a startup building open-weight AI models, positioned as a Western counterweight to Chinese AI labs like [[entities/deepseek|DeepSeek]]. Founded by former Google DeepMind researchers and backed by Nvidia and 1789 Capital (venture fund where Donald Trump Jr. is a partner).

## Pentagon Partnership (May 2026)

On May 1, 2026, Reflection AI was one of seven companies to sign agreements with the Pentagon for classified military AI deployment on IL6/IL7 networks, agreeing to the "any lawful use" clause. This came as [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]] was excluded over the same clause.

Reflection's statement: "This shared understanding with the Pentagon is a first step in supporting U.S. national security, and sets a precedent for how AI labs could work across the U.S. government — from supporting our servicemembers to our scientists."

## Funding & Backers
- **Nvidia**: Strategic investment (also a Pentagon partner for Nemotron models)
- **1789 Capital**: Venture fund with Donald Trump Jr. as partner
- **$2B raised** in October 2025 round
- **$25B valuation target**

## Strategic Position
- Open-weight model strategy aligned with Pentagon's desire to avoid "vendor lock" to closed-source models
- Nvidia's Pentagon agreement covers Nemotron open-source models for autonomous agent tasks
- Part of government-supported initiative for models customized for South Korean market
