# John Jumper

John Jumper is an AI researcher and **Nobel laureate in Chemistry (2024)** for his pioneering work on **AlphaFold**, the deep learning system that solved the protein structure prediction problem. He led the AlphaFold team at DeepMind and, as of June 2026, announced his move to Anthropic.

## Overview

John Jumper was the lead researcher behind **AlphaFold 2**, which achieved median GDT scores above 92 at CASP14 (2020), effectively solving the decades-old protein folding challenge. For this work, he shared the **2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry** with Demis Hassabis (DeepMind CEO) and David Baker (University of Washington).

His career places him at the center of AI's impact on scientific discovery — and in June 2026, at the center of the **AI talent war** reshaping the industry.

### AlphaFold at DeepMind

As the lead of the AlphaFold team, Jumper oversaw the development of **AlphaFold 2** (2020) and **AlphaFold 3** (2024). AlphaFold 2's performance at CASP14 was widely regarded as a breakthrough — producing predictions competitive with experimental methods and opening up structural biology to computational approaches at scale.

- **AlphaFold Database**: Released 200M+ predicted protein structures publicly (2021)
- **AlphaFold 3**: Extended to DNA, RNA, small molecules, and post-translational modifications (2024)

### Nobel Prize in Chemistry (2024)

Jumper shared the 2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with:
- **Demis Hassabis** (DeepMind co-founder and CEO) — see [[entities/demis-hassabis]]
- **David Baker** (University of Washington, for computational protein design)

The prize recognized AlphaFold as one of the most significant applications of deep learning to fundamental science.

### Move to Anthropic (June 2026)

On **June 18, 2026**, Business Insider and Reuters reported that Jumper is leaving Google DeepMind to join [[entities/anthropic]]. This move represents one of the most significant AI talent migrations to date — a Nobel-caliber researcher moving from one frontier AI lab to another.

## Significance

Jumper's move to Anthropic is emblematic of the broader **AI talent war**:
- Frontier labs are competing intensely for top researchers
- Nobel laureates and senior research leads are increasingly mobile
- The flow of talent between organizations shapes the strategic balance of AI capabilities

His departure from DeepMind, where he co-led one of AI's most celebrated scientific achievements, signals the fluidity of the current AI research landscape.
