# Sankalp Sinha (dejavucoder)

## Overview

**Sankalp Sinha**, known online as **dejavucoder** (@dejavucoder), is an **AI engineer** and applied LLM researcher based in India. He focuses on AI-assisted coding, agentic workflows, LLM evaluation, and developer productivity. He is a contributor to **agentOS** by Rivet — an open-source operating system for AI agents powered by WebAssembly and V8 isolates — and writes extensively about the evolution of coding tools.

He describes himself as a "0.5x engineer made 5x with help of LLMs" and maintains a pseudonymous online persona while being selective about what personal details he shares publicly ("selectively doxxed").

## Core Ideas

### The Gears Analogy for AI Coding
> "The lower the gear in a car, the more control you have over the engine but you can go with less speed. If you feel in control, go to a higher gear. If you are overwhelmed or stuck, go to a lower gear."

He views AI coding assistance as a spectrum from manual control (autocomplete) to full autonomy (agentic), with developers needing to shift gears based on their comfort level and task complexity.

### 0.5x Engineer Made 5x
> "I am a 0.5x engineer made 5x with help of LLMs"

He openly acknowledges that LLMs dramatically amplify his capabilities and believes this is the reality for most developers. Rather than fighting this, he embraces it and focuses on how to work effectively with AI tools.

### Selective Transparency
He maintains a pseudonymous online identity ("dejavucoder") while being selectively transparent about his real name and background. He believes personas help other people open up more easily and allows for more authentic technical discussions.

### Stay Current Through Community
He curates lists of AI researchers and developers on X/Twitter and believes staying updated with the latest progress is essential for being effective in AI engineering. He actively participates in AI discussions and shares findings.

### Play and Experiment
> "Instead of thinking in terms of 'keeping up', a better framing is how can I improve myself with help of these tools i.e augment."

He advocates for hands-on experimentation with new models and tools rather than just reading about them. Building intuition through direct usage is more valuable than theoretical knowledge.

## AI Engineering Philosophy

Sankalp's approach to AI engineering can be summarized as:

1. **Use SoTA models** — Don't be stingy with the best available tools
2. **Build feedback loops** — Create systems where AI output is evaluated and improved
3. **Domain knowledge matters** — The more you know about your field, the better you can prompt and evaluate
4. **Shift gears appropriately** — Use the right level of automation for the task
5. **Play more** — Experimentation builds intuition faster than reading
