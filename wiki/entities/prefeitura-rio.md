## Overview

**IplanRIO** is the municipal IT company of Rio de Janeiro's city government (Prefeitura do Rio de Janeiro). It serves as the technology arm of the city, responsible for digital infrastructure, information systems, and technological innovation for public services.

In June 2026, IplanRIO gained international recognition in the AI community by releasing **[[concepts/rio-3.5-open-397b|Rio 3.5 Open 397B]]**, the first frontier-class open-source AI model developed and released by a municipal government entity.

## Rio 3.5 Open 397B Release

Released on 2026-06-11, Rio 3.5 Open 397B is:

- A **397B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts** model (17B active) post-trained from [[concepts/qwen|Qwen 3.5 397B]]
- Licensed under **MIT** — fully permissive for commercial and research use
- Features **SwiReasoning**, a dynamic latent/explicit reasoning framework
- Competitive with frontier proprietary models (GPT 5.5, DeepSeek V4 Pro, Kimi-K2.6) across coding, math, STEM, and multilingual benchmarks
- Hosted on Hugging Face: `prefeitura-rio/Rio-3.5-Open-397B`
- Achieved 112K+ downloads and 184 likes within 2 days of release

## Significance

IplanRIO's release of a frontier-class model challenges the assumption that cutting-edge AI development is exclusively the domain of major private labs and national governments. Key implications:

- **Public-sector AI capacity**: Demonstrates that well-resourced municipal IT agencies can produce competitive open models
- **Open-source governance**: MIT licensing aligns with public-sector values of transparency and public benefit
- **Global South representation**: A significant AI contribution from Latin America, built for multilingual use including strong Portuguese and English performance
- **Cost democratization**: Frontier-class capabilities available for free under permissive license
