# Lee Robinson

Lee Robinson (@leerob) is an engineer and educator focused on **model behavior** at [[entities/cursor-ai|Cursor AI]]. Previously VP of Product at [[entities/vercel|Vercel]], where he led developer experience and Next.js ecosystem efforts.

## Career

| Period | Role | Company |
|--------|------|---------|
| ~2026–present | Model behavior | Cursor AI |
| ~2020–2026 | VP of Product | Vercel |

At Cursor, Robinson works on training and aligning coding agent models — helping them behave like ideal engineering colleagues rather than just code generators.

## Writing & Communication

Robinson is known for clear, accessible technical explanations aimed at broad audiences. His writing bridges the gap between ML research and product/engineering practitioners:

- **"How we teach AI models"** (2026-07-24) — General-audience explainer on the pretraining → SFT → RL → alignment pipeline, using basketball analogies and the "helpful colleague" framing. Received 1,100+ bookmarks on X.

## Key Ideas

- Models should be understood as **helpful colleagues** with traits like communication, judgment, and problem-solving ability
- The training pipeline (pretraining → SFT → RL) mirrors human learning: reading → studying film → coaching
- **Model specifications** (constitutions/principles) define the "correct behavior" for alignment
- Intelligence alone is insufficient — alignment to human values and communication style is critical
- Current models cannot learn in real-time; continual learning requires external memory via files/rules
