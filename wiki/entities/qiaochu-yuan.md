- **Location**: Portland, OR

## Key Works

### "re-encountering language" (March 2023)
[[raw/articles/2023-03-13_qchu-re-encountering-language.md|Raw article]]

QC's earliest articulation of the **body words** experience. Describes how a period of "pleasant insanity" unlocked a feral, poetic self that had been suppressed for six years. The essay captures the lived experience that would later be theorized in "Core dump"—the discovery that beneath polite society run "vast underground rivers of pain," and that language can be more than a homework assignment for validation.

### "Core dump" (September 2024)
[[raw/articles/2024-09-16_qchu-core-dump.md|Raw article]] → [[concepts/linguistic-vertigo|Concept: Linguistic Vertigo]]

QC's most influential essay. Introduces:
- **Linguistic Vertigo** — the cognitive disorientation of losing the ability to distinguish human from LLM text
- **Head Words vs Body Words** — a dual-process theory of language generation
- **LLM as Tracer Dye** — the diagnostic function of AI showing where human language was already bullshit
- **Societal Shadow** — RLHF's inadvertent cataloging of everything society represses

### Additional Writing

- **Annoying Precision** (WordPress) — mathematics blog covering category theory, algebraic topology, representation theory

## Relationship to AI Community

QC is a unique voice in the AI discourse landscape:
- **Not a practitioner** — QC does not build AI systems. His value is as a **phenomenologist of AI interaction**, documenting what it *feels like* to live in a world with LLMs.
- **Bridge figure** — Connects the rationalist/alignment community (MIRI background) with continental philosophy and depth psychology
- **Ex-MIRI** — His MIRI background gives him credibility in the AI safety space, while his poetic writing style reaches a broader audience
- **Influence on AI criticism** — "Linguistic vertigo" and "tracer dye" have become shorthand in discussions of LLM's cultural impact

## Core Thesis

QC's work can be read as a unified project: **using the LLM as a diagnostic tool for human language pathology.** His questions are:

1. If LLMs can replace human language production in domain X, what does that reveal about X?
2. What is the non-bullshit foundation of language, and how do we access it?
3. What does RLHF's forbidden content list tell us about society's shadow?
