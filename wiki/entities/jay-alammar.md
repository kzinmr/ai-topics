# Jay Alammar

**Jay Alammar** is Director and Engineering Fellow at Cohere and co-author of *Hands-On Large Language Models* (O'Reilly, 2024). Through his widely-read blog, he has helped millions of researchers and engineers visually understand machine learning — most notably through "The Illustrated Transformer," which has been referenced in AI/ML courses at MIT, Stanford, Cornell, and CMU.

## Overview

Jay is a software engineer by training who became one of the most influential ML visual communicators. He joined Cohere (founded by ex-Google Brain researchers including co-authors of the original Transformer paper) to help enterprises and developers apply large language models in production. In his role, he advises and educates organizations on using LLMs for practical use cases including semantic search, text classification, summarization, and RAG.

Previously, Jay founded a tech startup, worked at STV (an early-stage venture fund), and created ML/NLP courses for Udacity's Machine Learning Nanodegree, Deep Learning Nanodegree, and NLP Nanodegree programs. He also serves on the faculty of Deeplearning.ai.

## Book: Hands-On Large Language Models (2024)

Co-authored with **Maarten Grootendorst** (creator of BERTopic, KeyBERT), published by O'Reilly Media. The book covers:

- Transformer architecture for text generation and representation
- Building LLM pipelines for clustering, semantic search, and classification
- Prompt engineering to RAG (retrieval-augmented generation)
- Fine-tuning, contrastive fine-tuning, and in-context learning
- Semantic search with dense retrieval and rerankers

## Ecco (Open Source)

**Ecco** ([github.com/jalammar/ecco](https://github.com/jalammar/ecco)) is an open-source Python library for explaining, analyzing, and visualizing NLP language models. It creates interactive visualizations directly in Jupyter notebooks explaining the behavior of Transformer-based language models (GPT-2, BERT, RoBERTa, T5, and T0). The project has **2,094+ GitHub stars**.
