# Figma AI Challenge

With the arrival of Claude Design, Figma has become a **case study in the vulnerabilities of the SaaS AI era**.

## Figma's Position

| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Employees | ~2,000 |
| Claude Design Dev Team | <10 |

## User Breakdown (Q1 2025)

| User Type | Percentage |
|---------------|------|
| Designers | 33% |
| Developers | 30% |
| Non-designer Roles | 37% |

**Key Insight**: Figma's growth strategy relied on non-designer users — a segment that is replaceable by AI.

## Structural Weaknesses Against Competition

### 1. Competing with Their Own AI Provider
Figma Make uses Sonnet 4.5 — the same company is now competing directly with them.

- Figma keeps paying inference costs to its competitor
- Sonnet 4.5 is **"miles behind"** Claude Design's Opus 4.7
- Inference costs are **high variable costs** — problematic with a shrinking user base

### 2. Employee Efficiency Gap

| Company | Employees | Claude Design Build Team |
|------|----------|------------------------|
| Figma | ~2,000 | — |
| Anthropic (Total) | ~2,500 | **<10** |

## The Collapse of Traditional SaaS Moats

| Traditional Moat | Agent-First Reality |
|------------|----------------|
| Multi-player Coordination | Replaced by prompt iteration |
| Plugin Ecosystem | Replaced by direct functional requests |
| Design System Tools | Now a **core feature** of Claude Design |
| Enterprise SSO | Already supported by Claude |

## Key Points

> "Most of the moats that protect a mature SaaS company are moats against other SaaS companies, not against the thing providing their inference."
