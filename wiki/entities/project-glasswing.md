# Project Glasswing

**Project Glasswing** is [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]]'s cybersecurity initiative, announced in 2026, focused on using AI for vulnerability identification and remediation.

## Key Results

- **1,752 high/critical vulnerabilities** assessed by 6 independent security research firms
- **90.6% true-positive rate** (1,587 confirmed valid)
- **62.4% confirmed as high or critical severity** (1,094 vulnerabilities)
- On track to surface nearly **3,900 high/critical vulnerabilities** in open-source code
- **Claude Security** in public beta for Claude Enterprise customers
- **2,100+ vulnerabilities patched** by Claude Opus 4.7 in 3 weeks

## Mythos Preview

The Mythos-class model (Anthropic's most powerful, previously restricted to cybersecurity) is expected to become generally available "in the coming weeks" pending completion of safety safeguards.

- 5 of 18 Anthropic engineers believe Mythos could substitute for an L4 engineer
- Key weaknesses vs L4: self-managing week-long tasks, understanding org priorities, taste, verification, instruction-following, epistemics
- Alignment team found Mythos Preview reaches "new highs on measures of prosocial traits"

## Significance

The bottleneck has shifted from vulnerability identification to remediation — AI can find problems faster than human maintainers can fix them. This mirrors the broader agentic AI pattern where capability outpaces human coordination.
