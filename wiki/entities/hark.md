# Hark

**Hark** is a GPU infrastructure company building compute, model development, and personal intelligence platforms. Founded by serial entrepreneur Brett Adcock, Hark supports autonomous AI agent runs lasting hundreds of hours without interruption.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | AI Infrastructure / GPU Cloud |
| **Key Products** | GPU infrastructure, model development, multimodal/personal intelligence |
| **Leadership** | Brett Adcock (CEO) |
| **Valuation** | $6B (May 2026) |

## Key Facts

- Raised **$700M+ at $6B valuation** (May 2026). Oversubscribed round, the largest Series A in AI hardware history for a company roughly 2 months out of stealth
- Reported a **200-hour uninterrupted autonomous run** for their model F.03
- Building across GPU infrastructure, model development, hardware, and multimodal/personal intelligence verticals

### Investor Details

The $700M+ round (the largest Series A in AI hardware history for a company roughly two months out of stealth) drew participation from major semiconductor companies — a rare signal of industry-level consensus:

- **Nvidia** — competing AI hardware giant invested, signaling confidence in Hark's differentiated approach
- **AMD** — GPU competitor
- **Intel** — traditional CPU maker expanding into AI hardware
- **Qualcomm** — mobile and edge AI chip leader
- **Salesforce Ventures** — enterprise AI investor

Having four major chipmakers (Nvidia, AMD, Intel, Qualcomm) on the same cap table is virtually unprecedented in AI hardware.

### Roadmap

| Phase | Timing | Details |
|-------|--------|---------|
| AI Models | Summer 2026 | First AI model release expected |
| Devices | Post-model | Family of devices for individuals and the home |

Hark's strategy is to first demonstrate AI model capability, then ship physical devices that bring that intelligence to consumers.
