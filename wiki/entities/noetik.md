# Noetik

## Overview

Noetik is a biotech AI company applying transformer models to oncology, specifically addressing the 95% clinical trial failure rate in cancer drug development. The company's core thesis: many "failed" treatments actually work, but patient-tumor-treatment matching is wrong. Founded by **Ron Alfa** and **Daniel Bear**.

## Core Thesis

> *"95% of cancer treatments fail clinical trials — but it may be a matching problem."*

Noetik's approach:
1. **Collect thousands of actual human tumors** and build large multimodal datasets
2. **Train transformer models** with deep understanding of cancer biology
3. **Simulate patient-treatment response** to find which patients will respond to which treatments
4. **Rescue "failed" drugs** that were safe but not effective in initial trials

## Key Technology

Noetik's models can:
- Distinguish different tumor types (including previously unidentified subtypes)
- Simulate how patients will respond to experimental treatments
- Guide drug discovery toward candidates more likely to pass clinical trials

## Business Model

- Partner with pharmaceutical companies to test "failed" drugs on better-matched patients
- If simulations work, bring treatments to patients without developing new drugs
- Revenue via licensing deals and partnership agreements

## GSK Deal

**April 2026**: GSK signed a **$50M deal** with Noetik that includes:
- Upfront payment for Noetik's technology
- Undisclosed long-term licensing deals for Noetik's models
- Partnership to test promising drugs that were demonstrated safe but not effective

## Scaling Laws

Noetik has figured out how to scale up their model and data, and they claim to see **no limit in their scaling laws** — suggesting the approach may continue improving with more data and compute.
