# Sarah Guo

## Overview

Sarah Guo is the founder and general partner of [Conviction](https://www.conviction.com/), a venture capital firm purpose-built for the AI age. She is a prominent voice on AI investment strategy, enterprise AI, and the evolving landscape of AI-native companies.

## Background

- **Conviction.com**: Founded in 2022 with a $100M first fund to back "Software 3.0" companies
- **Greylock Partners**: Spent almost a decade incubating and investing in startups before founding Conviction
- **Focus**: AI-native companies across the intelligence stack — chips, data infrastructure, developer tools, research labs, and AI-native applications
- **Philosophy**: Hands-on, deep domain expertise, long-term oriented, community-building

## Key Themes in Writing

### The Untrainable (2026-06-10)
Sarah's latest essay argues against the "AI despair" that nothing is investable except compute and frontier weights. Key insights:

- **Legible vs Illegible Work**: Anything measurable becomes commodity; valuable work is illegible by construction
- **Private Ground Truth**: The most defensible AI companies operate where correctness exists only in private data
- **Absorption Frontier**: Labs are pulling model scaffolding into the weights, but this doesn't eliminate the value layer
- **Model Labs vs Agent Labs**: AINews framed Guo's essay as drawing a distinction between labs that build general intelligence (training compute) and labs that build agentic scaffolding (applied systems) — the latter being where investor conviction is hardest to build
- **Permission & Accountability**: Intelligence isn't the bottleneck — being let in the door and putting your name on outcomes is

### Not A Normal Market (2024-10-23)
Argued we're living through a once-a-decade technological shift, comparing AI to the microcomputer revolution, the web, and cloud computing.

### Launching With Conviction (2022-10-4)
Introduced Conviction as a firm purpose-built for "Software 3.0" — companies manipulating foundation models.
