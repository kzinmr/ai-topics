# Decision Lens

**Decision Lens** is an agentic dashboard for stakeholders built by [[entities/thomas-wiecki|PyMC Labs]]. It allows non-technical stakeholders to interact with agentic data science outputs — asking what-if questions, exploring scenarios, and interrogating results through a natural language interface driven by AI agents.

## Overview

Decision Lens bridges the gap between sophisticated causal/Bayesian analysis (performed via [[entities/decision-lab|Decision Lab]] and AI agents) and the decision-makers who need to act on those insights. Rather than receiving static reports, stakeholders can explore the analytical landscape interactively.

## Key Capabilities

- **Natural language querying** — Stakeholders ask questions in plain language, agents translate them into analytical queries
- **Scenario exploration** — What-if analysis across different assumptions and model specifications
- **Result interrogation** — Drill down into specific findings, ask for sensitivity analyses, explore forking paths
- **Decision dashboards** — Visual summaries of robust findings vs. sensitive results
- **Agent-mediated** — AI agents orchestrate the interaction between stakeholder intent and analytical backend

## Role in Agentic Data Science

Decision Lens operates at the orchestration and observability layers of the [[concepts/agentic-data-science|agentic data science]] stack. It provides the human-facing interface that completes the loop from data → analysis → decision. Non-technical stakeholders gain agency over the analytical process without needing to understand the underlying Bayesian machinery.
