# IBM watsonx Orchestrate

IBM's multi-agent orchestration platform, evolving into an "agentic control plane" for the enterprise multi-agent era. Announced as next-generation at IBM Think 2026 (May 5, 2026).

## Overview

watsonx Orchestrate is IBM's answer to the enterprise challenge of scaling from a handful of AI agents to thousands — built by different teams on different platforms. The platform provides a unified control plane where organizations can deploy agents from any source with consistent policy enforcement and accountability.

## Key Capabilities

- **Multi-source agent deployment**: Deploy agents from any framework or platform under unified governance
- **Policy enforcement and accountability**: Consistent controls across all agents regardless of origin
- **Auditability in near real-time**: As agent fleets scale into thousands, maintaining audit trails becomes the core operational challenge
- **Available in private preview** as of May 2026

## Companion Product: IBM Bob

**IBM Bob** is an agentic development partner designed for enterprise use, generally available. It partners with developers to build agents with built-in security and cost controls. It functions as the agentic counterpart to GitHub Copilot or Claude Code, but with enterprise governance built in from the start.

## Context: The IBM AI Stack

watsonx Orchestrate sits within IBM's broader AI operating model alongside:
- **IBM Confluent**: Real-time data streaming (Kafka/Flink) for AI
- **IBM Concert**: Intelligent operations platform
- **IBM Sovereign Core**: Operational independence for regulated environments
- **watsonx.data**: Federated context layer with OpenRAG, OpenSearch, GPU-accelerated Presto

## Strategic Positioning

IBM is positioning itself as the governance and operations layer for enterprise AI — not competing on model quality with OpenAI/Anthropic, but providing the control plane to run their models (and others) at scale with enterprise-grade controls. This is the "AI operating system" play.
