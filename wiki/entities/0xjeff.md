## Hermes Agent as Personal Analyst

0xJeff's most influential contribution is his series of articles documenting how he trained Hermes Agent to become a multi-role personal analyst. His workflow has become a reference implementation for agent-driven research pipelines.

### Three-Week Training Regimen
In his viral X article "3 Things I learnt after 3 weeks of using Hermes as a personal analyst" (Mar 2026, 78K views), he outlined:

1. **Morning Briefing Pipeline**: Hermes tracks macro, geopolitical situations (US-Iran, oil), and reports top insights with portfolio implications every morning
2. **Bookmark Processing**: Takes his X bookmarks, summarizes them, and translates into actionable next steps
3. **Portfolio Tracking**: Reports all holdings across wallets and DeFi positions with latest price movements
4. **Worldview Formation**: Learns from smart people (via ingested content) and forms its own analytical worldview

### Cost Efficiency
He emphasizes that Hermes costs "$5-$10 per month to run" — a fraction of what traditional analyst tools or human analysts cost.

### One-Month Retrospective
In "1 Month with Hermes — I've been using Hermes wrong all along" (Apr 27, 2026, on Substack), he documented advanced patterns:

- **PolyBond Integration**: Hermes acts as a prediction market analyst, scanning PolyBond dashboards every few hours and delivering focused reports
- **Learning from Mistakes**: Debugging, fixing errors, and adjusting configs to prevent repeated mistakes became the primary time investment
- **Hermes + Claude as Learning Augments**: Using both tools to "shorten the time to consume information & make decisions"

### Role Expansion
He envisions Hermes expanding into:
- Investment analyst (identifying undervalued opportunities)
- Trader analyst (executing based on risk appetite and strategy)
- Sales, customer support, back-office operations, accounting, finance ops
