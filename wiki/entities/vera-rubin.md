# NVIDIA Vera Rubin

**Vera Rubin** is NVIDIA's next-generation GPU architecture succeeding Blackwell, named after the American astronomer Vera Rubin who pioneered work on galaxy rotation rates and dark matter. Vera Rubin platforms are scheduled to begin shipping in **Q3 2026**.

## Specifications

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Architecture | Vera Rubin (post-Blackwell) |
| GPU Memory | 288 GB **HBM4** |
| Performance/Watt | **10× improvement** over previous generation |
| CPU | Vera CPU (custom ARM-based) |
| Ship Date | Q3 2026 |

## Vera CPU

The **Vera CPU** is a custom ARM-based processor designed to pair with Vera Rubin GPUs. As of May 2026, Vera CPUs are **already shipping to early partners** including [[entities/openai|OpenAI]], Oracle, [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]], and [[entities/xai|xAI]] for pre-production evaluation and integration.

## Market Context

NVIDIA reported **$81.6 billion in Q1 FY2027 revenue**, with Blackwell described as "the fastest product ramp in the company's history." The Vera Rubin generation represents NVIDIA's bet that the AI infrastructure buildout — particularly for inference-heavy workloads — will continue accelerating through 2027.

## Competitive Landscape

Vera Rubin enters a market where:
- **Custom AI ASICs** are growing at 44.6% YoY vs GPUs at 16.1% ([[concepts/custom-ai-silicon|TrendForce 2026]])
- **Cerebras WSE-3** demonstrates 6.7× faster inference for trillion-parameter models on wafer-scale architecture ([[entities/cerebras-systems|Cerebras]])
- **AMD MI355X** achieves competitive MLPerf results with memory capacity advantages ([[entities/amd|AMD]])
