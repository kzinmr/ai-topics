# Mathematical Methods and Human Thought in the Age of AI

**"Mathematical methods and human thought in the age of AI"** is a philosophical paper by Tanya Klowden (The Courtauld Institute of Art) and **Terence Tao** (UCLA), published as arXiv preprint 2603.26524 in March 2026. The paper was solicited for the Blackwell Companion to the Philosophy of Mathematics.

## Authors

### Terence Tao
[[entities/terence-tao|Terence Tao]] is one of the most influential mathematicians in the world, a Fields Medalist (2006), and a professor at UCLA. This paper represents his first major philosophical essay since his 2007 paper "What is Good Mathematics?"

### Tanya Klowden
Tanya Klowden is a scholar at The Courtauld Institute of Art, bringing an arts and humanities perspective to the dialogue about AI's impact on intellectual work.

## Core Content

The paper considers the rapidly evolving impact of AI on the traditional questions of philosophy, with an emphasis on its application in mathematics and broader real-world outcomes of its general use.

### Key Themes

1. **Purpose of the Paper** — The authors note that while there has been extensive discussion about what AI tools can or cannot do, comparatively less attention has been paid to *why* these tools are being developed and deployed, and *how* they impact the billions of lives that interact with them.

2. **Interdisciplinary Common Ground** — Despite coming from "polar opposite" academic domains (mathematics and art history), the authors found surprising common ground regarding the philosophical questions that real-world AI use poses.

3. **Mathematics as a Model** — The paper uses mathematics as a model system for understanding AI's impact on intellectual workflows, then expands these observations to broader real-world use.

4. **The Smell Test** — Drawing on Thurston's concept and Tao's earlier work (2007), the paper discusses how mathematicians evaluate arguments not just on logical correctness but on providing *understanding* and *insight* — a causal narrative for how conclusions were reached, what parts performed the "heavy lifting," and what was novel or surprising.

### Structure (27 pages)

- Introduction: Purpose and framing
- AI and the nature of mathematical truth
- The benefits, risks, ethics, and outcomes of incorporating AI into routine workflows
- Broader real-world implications beyond mathematics

## Significance

This paper is notable because Terence Tao rarely writes philosophical essays — his last was the influential "What is Good Mathematics?" (2007). The timing (March 2026) places it at a moment when AI tools (Claude Code, GPT-5, Gemini) were becoming deeply integrated into mathematical research workflows, raising fundamental questions about what the nature, purpose, and practice of mathematics actually is or ought to be.
