# Scott Alexander

**Scott Alexander Siskind** is an American psychiatrist and blogger, best known for his blogs **Slate Star Codex** (2013–2020) and **Astral Codex Ten** (2021–present). He is one of the most influential writers in the **rationalist community**, and his essays span psychiatry, philosophy, AI safety, genetics, politics, economics, and meta-science.

## Biographical Sketch

- **Profession**: Practicing psychiatrist in the San Francisco Bay Area
- **Blog**: Slate Star Codex (2013-2020) → Astral Codex Ten on Substack (2021-present)
- **Community Role**: Central figure in the LessWrong/rationalist community; organizer of regular ACX meetups worldwide
- **Anonymity Context**: Blogged pseudonymously as "Scott Alexander" until a 2021 New York Times article prompted him to disclose his full name. Now writes under his full name but retains "Scott Alexander" as his primary byline.

## Key Intellectual Contributions

### 1. **Moloch and Multipolar Traps** (2014, [[concepts/moloch-multipolar-trap]])
His most famous essay, *Meditations On Moloch*, coined the modern usage of "Moloch" as a metaphor for competitive dynamics that lead to destructive equilibria. The essay catalogs 14 examples of multipolar traps — from arms races to cancer — and argues that advancing technology systematically erodes the mechanisms that currently protect humanity from these traps. The essay concludes with a transhumanist call to build a Friendly superintelligence as the only way to permanently escape Moloch dynamics.

> *"The opposite of a trap is a garden. The only way to avoid having all human values gradually ground down by optimization-competition is to install a Gardener over the entire universe who optimizes for human values."*

### 2. **Psychiatric Practice and Culture**
Alexander's psychiatric writing combines clinical experience with cultural analysis, often examining how institutional incentives (another form of Moloch) distort mental health treatment. His essays on psychiatry are widely read by practitioners and patients alike.

### 3. **Meta-Science and Research Criticism**
Systematic critiques of scientific practices — publication bias, p-hacking, replication crises — framed through the lens of incentive structures that trap individual researchers despite collective awareness of the problems.

### 4. **Political and Cultural Commentary**
Known for nuanced, data-driven analysis that resists ideological categorization. His "Non-Libertarian FAQ" and discussions of the political compass directly informed the Moloch essay's analysis of the libertarian-authoritarian tradeoff between discoordination and tyranny.

## Connection to AI Safety

Alexander is a **transhumanist and AI alignment advocate**. His connection to the AI safety community includes:

- **LessWrong / MIRI adjacency** — Part of the same intellectual ecosystem as Eliezer Yudkowsky, Robin Hanson, and Nick Bostrom
- **AI risk communication** — His essays (especially *Meditations On Moloch*) are among the most widely-read popularizations of AI existential risk arguments
- **Epistemic culture** — His emphasis on intellectual honesty, calibration, and Bayesian reasoning aligns with the rationalist foundations of modern AI safety research
