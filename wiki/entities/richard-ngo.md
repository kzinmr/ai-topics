# Richard Ngo

Independent AI researcher and philosopher. Formerly Research Engineer on the AGI Safety team at **DeepMind** (2018-2020) and Research Scientist on the Governance team at **OpenAI** (2021-2024).

## Bio

Richard Ngo is one of the most prominent voices in AI alignment research, known for his first-principles approach to AGI safety, his debate with Eliezer Yudkowsky on alignment difficulty, and his recent work on developing a "scale-free theory of intelligent agency." He left OpenAI in late 2024 to pursue independent research focused on the long-term sociological and philosophical implications of AI.

## Key Works & Contributions

### AGI Safety from First Principles (2020)
His landmark report analyzing AGI safety from foundational principles, providing a framework for thinking about alignment before scaling to superhuman systems. The report became the basis for the **Alignment Fundamentals curriculum** (later adopted by BlueDot Impact as [Technical AI Safety](https://bluedot.org/courses/technical-ai-safety)).

### Ngo-Yudkowsky Debate on Alignment Difficulty (2021)
A high-profile transcribed Discord debate with Eliezer Yudkowsky, moderated by Nate Soares, on the fundamental difficulty of AI alignment. Later expanded to include discussions with Ajeya Cotra, Paul Christiano, Holden Karnofsky, and others. [Read on Alignment Forum](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/7im8at9PmhbT4JHsW/ngo-and-yudkowsky-on-alignment-difficulty).

### Towards a Scale-Free Theory of Intelligent Agency (2025)
His current major research direction, introducing the concept of **coalitional agency** — a unified mathematical framework describing both understanding and influencing the world. Critiques expected utility maximization (EUM) and active inference as insufficient, and proposes a synthesis that accounts for deep learning, RL, and multi-agent dynamics. [Read on Mind the Future](https://www.mindthefuture.info/p/towards-a-scale-free-theory-of-intelligent).

### Why I'm Not a Bayesian (2024)
Philosophical objections to Bayesianism as a complete epistemology, arguing that Bayesian formalisms cannot fully capture how intelligent agents reason and learn. [Read on Mind the Future](https://www.mindthefuture.info/p/why-im-not-a-bayesian).

## Key Ideas

- **Coalitional Agency**: A proposed scale-free theory of intelligent agency that treats agents as coalitions of sub-agents at multiple scales — bridging individual cognition and social dynamics.
- **Alignment as a scaling problem**: Argues that alignment solutions must be scale-free — they must work for systems far more capable than current AI, not just current systems.
- **Critique of EUM**: Expected utility maximization assumes fixed concepts and separates beliefs from goals — real agents learn concepts and heuristics through deep learning and RL, making EUM models insufficient.
- **Pessimization**: Getting the opposite of what you want — a concept about how optimization processes can backfire (introduced in his "On Pessimization" post).
