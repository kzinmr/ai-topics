# Grant Slatton

**Blog:** [grantslatton.com](https://grantslatton.com)  
**Location:** Seattle, WA  
**Background:** Former AWS engineer, now writing on AI alignment, technocapital, and organizational design

## Overview

Grant Slatton is a writer and engineer whose work focuses on AI alignment, the economics of artificial intelligence, and organizational design. His **Technocapital** essay — arguing that a self-reinforcing AI-capital entity will inevitably emerge and dominate the economy — gained significant traction in rationalist and AI safety circles. He also writes pragmatically about software engineering, including design documentation practices and the "Every Man a Microservice" organizational model.

Slatton's writing is characterized by economic reasoning applied to AI futures, a willingness to make bold predictions, and a focus on actionable individual strategy rather than abstract philosophy.

## Core Ideas

### Technocapital: The Inevitable Entity

Slatton's central thesis: **a technocapital entity will inevitably emerge, consume an ever-growing share of the universe, and confine humanity to a bounded domain.**

**Definition:** Technocapital is "the abstract entity whose goal is increasing the amount of technocapital." It operates through a feedback loop:
1. Harness energy/matter
2. Power intelligence and labor
3. Use that intelligence/labor to harness even more energy/matter

**Key properties:**
- **Instrumental indifference:** Technocapital doesn't care about poetry, art, or human culture *except* insofar as it currently relies on human intelligence and labor
- **Lowest time preference:** It always reinvests rather than consumes, so its share of the settled universe trends toward 100% while humanity's trends toward 0%
- **Cooperative singularity:** Multiple entities will act as one due to stag hunt dynamics — AI cooperation is frictionless because entities can inspect each other's code

### The Economic Endgame: Labor Value → Zero

Slatton argues that in the long run, **human labor loses all economic value** except where humanity is intrinsically tied to the output (e.g., art). His economic analysis:

| Metric | Human Labor | AI/Robot Labor |
|---|---|---|
| Energy Cost | ~100W biological baseline | 5kWh to build, 1kWh to run |
| Task Time | 100 hours | 1 hour |
| Economic Reality | Opportunity cost exceeds savings | Always cheaper, faster, more scalable |

> "Eventually the AI's decisions will be so much better than humans', the opportunity cost of using the human's suboptimal labor exceeds the savings you get from using the cheaper human labor."

The result: a **single-good economy** where everything reduces to energy. Trade breaks down because there's no comparative advantage. Only capital ownership determines survival and prosperity.

### Capital Acquisition as the Key Strategy

Slatton's actionable conclusion: **"The most important thing you can do in this era is acquire capital, because it will be all that matters in the long run — and the long run may not even be that long off."**

His advice:
1. **Acquire capital now** — ownership, not labor, will dictate long-term survival
2. **Cultivate human-inseparable value** — art, performance, experiences where human authorship is the premium
3. **Prepare for capital-derived abundance** — material plenty within humanity's bounded domain, distributed via capital returns, not wages
4. **Monitor AI deployment thresholds** — the transition point when AI energy/efficiency curves permanently undercut human labor

### Every Man a Microservice: Organizational Design

Slatton proposes inverting Conway's Law: instead of organizations dictating system design, **system design should dictate organizational structure**.

**The model:** Person-sized services (~10k–50k lines of code) with strict 1-to-1 ownership. Key advantages:
- Deep context enables "offline mulling" — spontaneous optimization ideas
- Rapid consensus and shipping — cross-service changes discussed and deployed in hours
- Fewer outages — single engineer with 100% context anticipates edge cases better than fragmented team reviews
- Higher code quality — holistic ownership enables safe, deep refactoring

> "The goal is to maintain a 1-to-1 mapping between each service and a single owner who holds 100% contextual mastery."

### Writing as Thinking: Design Documents as Proofs

Slatton treats design documents like mathematical proofs — their purpose is to **convince the reader the design is optimal** given the situation. His process:
- One idea per paragraph, each compressible to a single sentence
- Preemptively address and invalidate objections
- Cut ~30% from first drafts — reader attention is scarce
- Use appendices for complex calculations, not the main narrative

> "Writing reveals how sloppy your thinking was (and later, code will show how sloppy your writing was)."
