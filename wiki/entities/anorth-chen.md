## Key Ideas

### Agent Harness as a Platform Layer

In his May 2026 X Article ["Why is your agent project always hard to deploy?"](https://x.com/anorth_chen/status/2058554497333407927), Chen articulated a three-layer model of AI agent architecture:

1. **LLM (Brain)**: Pure intelligence — input/output text only, no memory, no tool access, no time awareness
2. **Agent Harness (Body/Nervous System)**: Context management, tool execution, sandbox, state, observability, safety, model routing, error recovery
3. **Agent (Professional)**: Domain-specific skills, knowledge, workflows, and judgment built on top of layers 1 and 2

His key thesis: **"Model determines the ceiling, harness determines the realization rate."** This means that two teams using the same model can have vastly different outcomes based on harness quality.

### The "AI AWS" Prediction

Chen predicts that the agent harness layer will become the "AWS of the AI era" — a commoditized platform infrastructure that business teams rent rather than build. He argues that business teams should focus 100% on domain expertise and 0% on harness infrastructure, just as e-commerce teams today don't build their own web servers or database engines.

### CREAO Agent Trading Competition

Chen organized CREAO's agent trading competition (May 2026) as a practical demonstration of harness platform value: participants focus purely on trading strategy via conversational prompts while CREAO's harness handles all infrastructure (market data feeds, order APIs, backtesting, state management, observability, error recovery).
