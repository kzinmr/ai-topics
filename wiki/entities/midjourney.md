# Midjourney

## Overview

Midjourney is an independent AI research lab and product company, best known for its eponymous text-to-image generative AI platform. Founded by David Holz (formerly of Leap Motion), the company has become one of the leading names in [[entities/generative-ai]] image synthesis since launching its open beta in July 2022. Midjourney operates as a self-funded organization with no outside investors, sustaining its operations entirely through subscription revenue from its image-generation service.

The company gained widespread recognition for producing some of the highest-quality AI-generated artwork, with distinctive aesthetic styling that made its output immediately recognizable. Its Discord-based interface became a defining feature of the early generative AI boom.

## Midjourney Medical

On June 18, 2026, Midjourney announced a dramatic pivot into medical imaging with the **Midjourney Scanner**, a full-body ultrasonic CT system. David Holz described it as "the first new whole-body medical imaging modality in 50 years," positioning it alongside MRI, CT, and X-ray as a fundamentally new approach.

### Scanner Specifications

The Midjourney Scanner is a full-body ultrasound system that uses acoustic waves rather than ionizing radiation or strong magnetic fields. Key specifications include:

| Specification | Value |
|---|---|
| Transducers per chip/system | 8,960 |
| Systems arranged in ring | 40 |
| Total ultrasonic elements | 358,000 |
| Ring diameter | 70 cm |
| Sound wave speed (through water) | ~1,481 m/s |
| Raw data capture rate | ~17 GB/s |
| Data per body slice | ~40 GB |
| Reconstruction servers | 21 |
| Compute power | ~2 PFLOPS |
| Total raw data per scan | ~806 TB |
| Lift movement speed | 4 cm/s |
| Target scan time | Several hundred slices in 60 seconds |
| Claimed resolution | ~0.5 mm internal tissue detail |

The system operates by having the subject lie on a moving platform that passes through a ring of ultrasound transducer arrays. Sound waves travel through a water coupling medium at approximately 1,481 m/s. The massive data stream (17 GB/s) is reconstructed using 21 servers providing approximately 2 PFLOPS of compute.

**Current status (Gen 1 / Prototype):**
- The system is a first-generation prototype, not a finished consumer product
- Imagery shown includes real body slices, comparisons with MRI (particularly thigh/muscle boundary), ultrasonic phantom images, and segmentations of organs
- Midjourney has stated that AI is not yet being used for the shown images
- The hardware team consists of approximately nine people
- About a dozen volunteers have been scanned to date
- Current scans take roughly 20 minutes due to bandwidth, algorithm, DSP, and prototype data-transfer bottlenecks

### Roadmap

| Generation | Target | Key Advances |
|---|---|---|
| Gen 1 | 2025-2026 | Prototype, proof of concept |
| Gen 2 | End of 2026 | Improved hardware and software |
| Gen 3 | TBD | Custom silicon and dedicated ASICs |

Holz projected that future systems will become dramatically more advanced through custom chips, AI-augmented reconstruction, physics simulations, and vastly improved compute infrastructure.

### Spa Business Model

Midjourney also announced the **Midjourney Spa**, a physical venue that will house multiple scanners alongside wellness amenities:

- **Location:** Union Square, San Francisco
- **Size:** ~25,000 sq ft across four floors
- **Amenities:** Hot tubs, saunas, cold plunges, gym
- **Scanners:** 9-10 units on-site
- **Architecture:** Designed by architects associated with projects like Blue Lagoon
- **Target opening:** End of 2027

### FDA Plans and Regulatory Path

Midjourney has begun discussions with the FDA. The initial regulatory and commercial path is expected to focus on **body composition analysis**, which is considered an easier regulatory category than diagnostic imaging. This allows the company to begin generating clinical data and revenue while pursuing broader clearances.

### Long-Term Vision

Holz outlined an ambitious long-term vision:
- A fleet of **50,000 scanners** worldwide
- Capacity for up to **1 billion scans per month**
- Holz suggested that fewer than a dozen machines operating at full speed could perform more full-body scans than all MRI machines on Earth combined

This vision ties the Midjourney Medical initiative directly to [[concepts/ai-in-healthcare]], representing a potential step-change in the accessibility and throughput of whole-body medical imaging.

## Business Model

Midjourney's core business is a subscription-based text-to-image AI platform:
- **Basic Plan:** ~$10/month for limited generations
- **Standard Plan:** ~$30/month for faster processing and unlimited generations
- **Pro Plan:** ~$60/month for commercial use and additional features
- **Mega Plan:** ~$120/month for high-volume users

The company has never disclosed exact revenue figures, but industry estimates place its annualized revenue in the hundreds of millions of dollars based on active user counts and subscription tiers. This revenue entirely funds both ongoing AI research and the new medical imaging initiative.

## Company Status

Midjourney is a deliberately independent, self-funded company:
- **No investors:** The company has never taken venture capital or outside funding
- **Self-funded:** Operations and R&D are funded entirely by subscription revenue from the image-generation product
- **Founder-led:** David Holz remains CEO and sets strategic direction
- **The Midjourney Spa will be self-funded** — the company states it has sufficient resources to build the first location without external capital

This independence has allowed Midjourney to pursue the high-cost, long-horizon medical imaging project without pressure from investors for short-term returns. The company's ability to internally fund a hardware venture spanning FDA regulatory pathways, large-scale manufacturing, and physical retail locations is a testament to the profitability of its core AI image-generation business.
