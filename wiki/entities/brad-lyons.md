# Brad Lyons

Brad Lyons is the founder of **Crossover Research**, a sell-side research firm focused on software and enterprise technology. He specializes in vertical SaaS analysis, moat theory, and the impact of AI on enterprise software business models. His research combines traditional financial diligence with voice-of-customer (VoC) data and proprietary survey work.

## Key Contributions

### The SaaSpocalypse Thesis (April 2026)
In a widely-circulated Note Tweet (1,631 likes, 4,175 bookmarks, 821K impressions), Lyons analyzed why vertical SaaS companies were down ~50% in multiples and argued the decline was structural, not cyclical. His central thesis:

- The **engineering moat** that protected SaaS for 20 years has been upended by AI
- Four remaining moats: distribution, proprietary data, workflow breadth, and **regulatory insulation**
- Regulatory moats (Tyler Technologies, Veeva, Guidewire) show "multiples compressed, fundamentals intact"
- **Data entrenchment** (Palantir model) is the most scalable moat when combined with AI
- Most SaaS companies fail because they never built a "second business" — monetizing the installed base beyond seat ARR

### Palantir Long Thesis (May 2024)
Called Palantir at $13 based on data entrenchment and ontology analysis — before the AIP bootcamp-driven growth inflection.

## Research Methodology
- **VoC (Voice of Customer) data**: Survey-based analysis of buyer sentiment, mission criticality, and switching friction
- **Battery Ventures partnership**: Joint research on AI adoption in the Office of the CFO (129 finance leaders surveyed)
- Focus on regulatory surface area, compliance moats, and second-business monetization

## Notable Frameworks

### Three Diligence Questions for Vertical SaaS
1. What % of revenue comes from non-subscription sources? (Second business readiness)
2. How hard to recreate with AI today? (Moat durability)
3. What % of stickiness is regulatory, and which way are rules moving? (Moat direction)

### Moat Taxonomy
- **Built moats**: distribution, proprietary data, workflow breadth
- **Captured moats**: regulatory insulation (most resistant to AI disruption)
