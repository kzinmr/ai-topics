# Clay Bavor

Co-founder of [[entities/sierra|Sierra]], former Vice President at Google where he led the company's AR/VR efforts including Google Cardboard, Daydream, and Project Starline.

## Background

Clay Bavor spent over 15 years at Google, joining in 2005 and rising to become one of the company's top product leaders. He is known for bridging ambitious technical projects with practical product execution.

## Career

### Google (2005–2023)
- Joined Google in 2005, working on early versions of Google Apps (now Google Workspace)
- Led the design and launch of **Gmail**'s major redesigns, including the Inbox by Gmail product
- Founded and led Google's **AR/VR division**, overseeing:
  - **Google Cardboard** — the low-cost VR viewer that introduced millions to virtual reality
  - **Google Daydream** — a platform for high-quality mobile VR
  - **Project Starline** — a breakthrough 3D telepresence system using light-field displays
- In 2021, Google merged its AR/VR efforts into a new **Labs** division under Bavor's leadership, focusing on forward-looking projects
- Left Google in early 2023 after the company's restructuring of its hardware and experimental products divisions

### Sierra (2023–present)
- Co-founded Sierra with [[entities/bret-taylor|Bret Taylor]] in 2023
- Serves in a product and technology leadership role, focusing on Sierra's Agent OS platform and the agent architecture
- Helped architect Sierra's approach to conversational AI agents that integrate with enterprise systems
- Co-authored Sierra's product philosophy around outcome-based pricing and long-horizon agent capabilities
- Bavor and Taylor have stated that Sierra's Horizon platform (announced July 2026) represents "the most significant expansion of Sierra since we launched in 2024"

## Key Contributions

At Sierra, Bavor brings deep expertise in:
- **Human-computer interaction**: From Gmail UX to 3D telepresence, Bavor's career has focused on making technology feel natural
- **Platform design**: His experience building developer ecosystems at Google informs Sierra's Agent OS architecture
- **Hardware-software integration**: From Cardboard to Starline, Bavor understands the full stack — relevant as AI agents increasingly operate across modalities (voice, vision, text)
