# Mo Bitar

Mo Bitar is a software developer and commentator known for satirical takes on corporate AI adoption patterns and the "Ralph Loop" phenomenon in enterprise technology.

## The "Ralph Loop" Commentary (May 2026)

Bitar published a widely-circulated satirical guide on how to exploit the corporate AI hype cycle for personal career advancement. The piece, titled around the concept of the **"Ralph Loop"**, describes a pattern where:

1. An employee claims to be experimenting with AI automation ("Ralph Looping")
2. They publicly "automate" colleagues out of existence
3. By the time management realizes nothing substantive was actually delivered, the employee has already received promotions and equity bumps
4. The cycle repeats because "nobody knows what they're doing" in the AI space

> "Now, if your CEO has never heard the phrase Ralph Loop, oh man, you are less than 30 days away from your next promotion. I'm not even exaggerating. Walk into his office, close the door, and say, hey chief, been experimenting with something. It's called Ralph Loops. And I think it could change literally everything. And he's gonna say, what's a Ralph loop? And you will say, give me $18,000 worth of API credits and I'll show you. Now you won't actually do anything, because you can't do anything. Because nobody can, because nobody knows what they're doing. But by the time he figures that out, you'll have a new title, and equity bump."

The piece highlights the fundamental disconnect between AI marketing hype and actual technical delivery, suggesting that corporate AI adoption is driven more by FOMO and narrative positioning than by demonstrated capability.
