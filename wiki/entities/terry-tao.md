## Overview

Terence "Terry" Tao is an Australian-American mathematician whose work spans an unusually broad range of pure and applied mathematics, including harmonic analysis, partial differential equations, combinatorics, number theory, and random matrix theory. A child prodigy who completed his PhD at Princeton at age 20 and won the Fields Medal at 31, Tao has been a professor at UCLA since 1999.

Since 2023, Tao has become one of the most visible voices in the mathematical community advocating for the integration of AI tools—particularly large language models (LLMs) and proof assistants—into mathematical research workflows. Rather than framing AI as a threat, he characterizes it as a "co-pilot" that augments and transforms the work of mathematicians, changing the job description itself.

## AI and Mathematics

Beginning in 2023–2024, Tao began systematically experimenting with AI tools for mathematical work, using models including GPT-4 and Claude for various tasks:

- **Proof strategies**: Using LLMs to brainstorm possible approaches and proof sketches for open problems, treating them as a source of suggestions rather than authoritative answers.
- **Translating formalisms**: Converting mathematical statements between different subfield notations and frameworks, which historically requires deep cross-domain expertise.
- **LaTeX generation**: Accelerating the mechanical aspects of mathematical writing, freeing up cognitive bandwidth for conceptual thinking.
- **Literature search and synthesis**: Leveraging AI to navigate the vast mathematical literature and identify relevant prior results.
- **Brainstorming**: Using conversational AI as a sounding board to explore ideas, much as a colleague would.

Tao describes AI as a **"co-pilot"** for mathematicians—augmenting rather than replacing human researchers. His core thesis is that AI will shift the division of labor in mathematics: routine verification and mechanical translation can be offloaded to machines, allowing mathematicians to concentrate on high-level conceptual insights and problem formulation.

## Formal Proof Verification (Lean)

Tao is a leading advocate for the [[formal-verification-llm-agents|Lean proof assistant]] and the broader formal verification ecosystem in mathematics. He believes the combination of AI with proof assistants represents a transformative development:

- AI can help translate informal mathematical proofs into formal, machine-checkable code.
- Proof assistants like Lean (from lean-lang.org) can verify that proofs are logically sound, eliminating the "reproducibility crisis" in complex mathematical arguments.
- The emerging workflow envisions mathematicians writing proofs at a high level, with AI handling the translation to formal code and the proof assistant performing verification.
- This reduces the burden of verification on human referees and collaborators and makes mathematics more collaborative and transparent.

Tao has written extensively on his blog and in public talks about his experiments with Lean and his vision for how AI-assisted formal verification could reshape the practice of [[formal-methods|formal methods]] in mathematics.

## Views on Open-Source AI

Tao has expressed strong concern about concentration of power in AI development. In an October 2024 interview with El Pais, he stated:

> "It's not good for something as important as AI to be a monopoly held by one or two companies."

His advocacy for [[open-source-ai|open-source AI]] stems from the belief that:

- Mathematics is a fundamentally open and collaborative discipline, and AI tools for mathematics should reflect those values.
- A diverse ecosystem of AI models and tools prevents lock-in and ensures that researchers at smaller institutions and in the Global South are not excluded.
- Open models allow for customization and specialization to mathematical domains, which proprietary models may not prioritize.

This position aligns him with the broader movement for open-weight and open-source AI models in scientific research and [[coding-agents|AI-assisted scientific work]].
