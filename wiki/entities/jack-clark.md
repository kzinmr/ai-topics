# Jack Clark

## Overview

Jack Clark is a co-founder of **[[entities/anthropic]]** and the former Policy Director at OpenAI. He currently serves as Anthropic's Head of Policy and writes the influential weekly newsletter **Import AI**, one of the longest-running AI-focused newsletters covering research, policy, and industry trends. His writing is known for combining technical depth with clear-eyed strategic forecasting.

## Background

- **Co-founder, Anthropic** (2021–present) — Head of Policy
- **Policy Director, OpenAI** (2016–2021)
- **Import AI newsletter** — Weekly analysis of AI research and industry developments
- Previously a journalist covering AI at Bloomberg and The Register

Import AI is one of the most widely-read AI newsletters in the world, known for:

## Key Ideas

### Recursive Self-Improvement (May 2026)
In Import AI 455, Clark made a landmark prediction: **60%+ probability of autonomous AI R&D by end 2028**, where AI systems build their own successors without human involvement. Key evidence:
- **SWE-Bench saturation**: Claude Mythos Preview at 93.9%, up from 2% (late 2023)
- **Time horizons**: METR tasks expanded from 30 seconds (GPT-3.5) to ~12 hours (Opus 4.6)
- **Training optimization**: Claude Mythos Preview achieved 52x speedup on CPU-only training
- **Compounding alignment risk**: 99.9% alignment accuracy drops to 60.5% over 500 generations

See: [[concepts/recursive-self-improvement]]

### Alignment as Compound Risk
Clark frames AI alignment not as a binary problem but as a compounding one — even near-perfect alignment decays exponentially across recursive self-improvement generations. This makes automated alignment research (AAR) both critical and inherently risky.

### The Amdahl's Law Economy
As AI accelerates digital work, physical-world bottlenecks (drug trials, manufacturing) become the primary constraints on economic growth — what Clark calls "Amdahl's Law for the economy."

## Writing Style
