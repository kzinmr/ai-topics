# Sara Hooker

**Role:** Researcher at Google DeepMind (formerly Google Brain). Former VP of Research at Cohere.

**Known for:**
- Authoring **"The Hardware Lottery"** — a foundational essay on how hardware availability determines AI research direction
- Leading research in model interpretability, robustness, and fairness
- Contributions to sparse models, modular AI systems, and evaluation frameworks
- Advocate for cross-disciplinary collaboration between hardware, systems, and ML communities

## Key Papers
- **The Hardware Lottery** (2020/2021) — arXiv:2009.06489, published in Communications of the ACM
- **The Grand Illusion: The Myth of Software Portability and Implications for ML Progress** (NeurIPS 2023) — with Fraser et al.

## Research Philosophy
Hooker advocates for reducing the cost of exploring new hardware-software-algorithm combinations. Her work highlights that the performance of algorithms is inseparably tied to the hardware they run on — a critical insight for AI infrastructure engineering.
