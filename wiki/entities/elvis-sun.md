## Core Philosophy & Frameworks

### The Generalist Founder
Elvis spent 8 years at Google building Firebase before becoming a generalist founder who can build the entire business — product, code, ops, marketing, sales. In the AI era, he argues one person can build and compete with a team of 50.

> "The question isn't 'what's easy to build?' anymore. It's 'what's worth building?'"

### Business Serves Life
His personal ethos: businesses should serve the founder's life goals, not the other way around. This led him to step back from PressPulse.ai when it conflicted with family priorities (high-risk pregnancy/second child).

### Platform Risk Warning
> "Never build your castle on someone else's land."

PressPulse.ai lost ~10% MRR overnight when HARO shut down. Lesson: diversify traffic and revenue sources immediately.

### Bootstrapping Lessons
> "If your side project pays less per hour than your day job and feels like a second job you dread, it's the wrong project."

**4 Critical Mistakes framework:**
1. Platform dependency (building on someone else's land)
2. Two-sided marketplace bootstrapping (requires VC or deep industry access)
3. Building a business you don't enjoy (audit hourly effective rate)
4. Solving too small a problem (validate TAM early)

## Key Ideas & Frameworks

### Context Lake System (Claude Code + Obsidian)
Built a 3-tier personal knowledge architecture:
1. **Level 1 (Raw Input):** Stream-of-consciousness notes, customer conversations, business decisions — no AI, no formatting
2. **Level 2 (Generated Derivatives):** Claude Code processes L1 into Brand Voice Guide, Business Strategy Doc, ICP Research, Content Pillars
3. **Level 3 (Output Layer):** Mix-and-match L2 docs to generate specific deliverables

> "Nobody else has my Level 1. So nobody else can get my Level 3." — Elvis Sun

### Vibe-Launching
Iterate in real-time during viral moments instead of code freezes. AI enables instant deployment based on live user behavior.

> "Ship fast enough that 'launch' is just the first commit."

**Case study: ismypitchshit.com** — AI pitch roaster that achieved viral coefficient > 1.0 through engineered shareability:
- Profane domain name as organic URL sharing driver (stolen from motherfuckingwebsite.com)
- Brutal AI feedback as pattern interrupt (screenshots shared)
- Self-deprecating results (users voluntarily share bad scores)
- 6 real-time fixes shipped in <10 minutes each using AI + session recordings
- Leaderboard with timestamp/locations for social proof
- Hot lead capture after 3+ submissions (20+ leads from free tool)

**The Science of Virality:**
- Viral coefficient (VC) > 1.0 = exponential growth
- VC < 1.0 = product slowly dies
- Every feature should be a "share trigger in disguise"
- Turn haters into users (PR people used the tool to prove they know better than spammers)

### Attention Flywheel
Marketing is fundamentally **one unified game: earning attention from people trained to ignore almost everything.**

**Cross-Channel Amplification Framework:**
1. **Test & Validate:** Post on high-velocity platform (X), track raw engagement
2. **Leverage Social Proof:** Repurpose to adjacent platforms (Reddit) with baked-in metrics
3. **Convert to PR/Outbound:** Use validated traction to pitch journalists

**Real Results:**
- X post → 340k impressions → Reddit screenshot → 512k views → Journalist retweet → Warm DM relationship
- X comment → 100 likes / 10.8k views → Pitched 9 journalists → Featured on Mashable → 2x DR91 backlinks earned organically

### Newsjacking (Viral Content Strategy)
Instead of creating momentum, steal it. Find something already trending and attach yourself to it.

**5 Psychological Triggers Behind Viral Comments:**
1. **The Polarizing Promise:** Use words like "never" to create curiosity and in-group feeling
2. **The Contrarian Insight:** Challenge conventional wisdom with data-backed takes
3. **Pattern Interrupt:** Break expectations (profane domain, brutal AI feedback)
4. **Social Proof Baking:** Built-in metrics signal value before content is consumed
5. **Self-Deprecation:** Wins look like bragging, losses look relatable

### AI-Powered Cold Email System
**Core Philosophy:** Cold email isn't inherently spammy; it fails due to poor targeting and generic messaging.

**The Framework:**
- Start with the list, not the copy
- Identify the core dream/pain of your niche
- Optimize the inbox preview (From Name, Subject Line, Preview Text)
- Use a low-friction CTA
- Track positive reply rate (aim for >10%, not ~0.26% industry standard)

**Tech Stack:**
| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Clay** | Data enrichment, AI personalization, workflow orchestration |
| **PhantomBuster** | No-code scraping (LinkedIn, Twitter DMs) |
| **Apify** | Directory/launch platform scraping |
| **Prospeo / Findymail** | Email discovery & verification |
| **ZeroBounce** | Email validation before sending |
| **Instantly / Smartlead** | Email scheduling & warmup |
| **Coldsire** | Automated domain/mailbox setup |

**Deliverability Rules:**
- Never send cold emails from your main domain
- Max 30 emails/mailbox/day (2026 limit, down from 50)
- 2-week warmup period required
- Use GSuite + Outlook 50/50 split for maximum inbox placement

**AI Advantage:**
- GPT-4 outperforms most human assistants in research and personalization
- Tasks that took a team a week now take ~30 minutes with AI
- Out of 20k contacts, only 3 suspected AI generation

### Solo Founder with AI
With Claude Code, Codex, and Gemini agents, solo founders can now build products that compete with well-funded startups. The constraint shifts from technical capability to problem selection.
