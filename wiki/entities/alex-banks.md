## Overview

Alex Banks is a British AI educator, creator, and entrepreneur who bridges the gap between cutting-edge AI research and practical, everyday application. He is best known for writing **The Signal**, a weekly Substack newsletter with over 55,000 subscribers and a remarkable 46% average open rate, which curates AI developments with actionable tutorials and philosophical reflections.

Banks operates across multiple ventures simultaneously: **Harley** (AI-powered analytics for retailers), **GrowHub** (AI-powered LinkedIn growth for professionals), and **Tribescaler** (AI writing assistant). He is also an instructor on DataCamp, where his course on Understanding Prompt Engineering has reached millions of learners.

His approach to AI education is distinctive: rather than chasing every announcement, he focuses on practical workflows that busy professionals can implement immediately. His audience of over 300,000 across platforms includes decision-makers at companies like Microsoft, Anthropic, Oracle, IBM, Adobe, HP, Intel, Capgemini, Morning Brew, HubSpot, DataCamp, Notion, and Synthesia.

## Core Ideas

### Signal Over Noise

Banks's core philosophy is captured in his newsletter's name: in a world of constant AI announcements, the value is in **curation**, not volume. The Signal delivers one carefully crafted issue per week, filtering what matters from what doesn't. His readership of 55,000+ professionals trust him to separate genuine advances from marketing hype.

> "The Signal is exactly that. It gives you signal in a sea of noise every Sunday."

### AI Literacy Through Prompt Engineering

Banks believes that effective AI use starts with fundamentals — understanding how to communicate with language models:

> "AI literacy fundamentally starts with getting the most out of the current systems on the market. And there's no better way to do that than learning prompt engineering."

His DataCamp course teaches that the quality of AI outputs depends entirely on the quality of inputs — a principle he applies across all his content creation.

### Start Then Learn

One of Banks's most influential ideas is the "grab a shitty rod and start fishing" philosophy — bias toward action over preparation. He contrasts two approaches to learning: the person who reads everything and buys the best equipment before starting, versus the person who starts immediately with basic tools and iterates based on feedback.

> "Start then learn. Not the other way around. Get going, then get good. Put nothing off."

This philosophy underpins his approach to AI adoption: don't wait for the perfect model or framework — start building with what's available now.

### Skills Over Prompts

Banks has been an early advocate for moving beyond one-off prompts to persistent, reusable AI capabilities. His work on Claude Skills — structured instruction sets that Claude reads automatically — represents his evolution from teaching prompt techniques to teaching system design.

> "Prompts are instructions you give once. Skills are instructions that persist forever."

### People Over Technology

Despite working in AI, Banks consistently returns to the human element:

> "Working with great people is one of the best parts of life. If there's one thing I've learned this year — it's all about the people. A small, product-focused team with an intense focus on what's important is the most powerful thing."
