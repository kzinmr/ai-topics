## Overview

Mira Murati is one of the most prominent engineering leaders in the AI industry. At OpenAI, she rose from VP of Applied AI to CTO, overseeing the launch of **GPT-4**, **ChatGPT**, **DALL-E**, and **Sora**. She briefly served as Interim CEO during the November 2023 board crisis, stabilizing the company during a period of intense internal turmoil.

In September 2024, she resigned from OpenAI and founded **Thinking Machines Lab**, a public benefit corporation backed by a16z and valued at $12B (July 2025). Her new venture emphasizes "collaborative AI" and "human-in-the-loop" safety, reflecting her long-standing advocacy for responsible deployment and regulation.

## Early Life and Education

Born in Vlorë, Albania (1988). Moved to Canada at 16 to attend Pearson College UWC.
- **2011**: BA Mathematics, Colby College.
- **2012**: BE Mechanical Engineering, Dartmouth College.
- Goldman Sachs intern (Tokyo, 2011).

## Career Timeline

### Tesla & Leap Motion (2013–2018)
- **Tesla (2013–2016)**: Senior Product Manager for the Model X program. Contributed to falcon-wing doors, structural design, and early Autopilot integration.
- **Leap Motion (2016–2018)**: VP of Product and Engineering. Led teams in hand-tracking sensors and AR/VR software.

### OpenAI (2018–2024)
Murati joined OpenAI in 2018 as VP of Applied AI and Partnerships. She was promoted to CTO in May 2022.

- **Product Leadership**: Oversaw the transition of research into products: GPT-3.5, ChatGPT, GPT-4, DALL-E 2/3, and Sora.
- **Nov 2023 Crisis**: Appointed Interim CEO after the board fired Sam Altman. Spoke out publicly in support of Altman's reinstatement, helping to resolve the 5-day crisis.
- **Safety vs Speed**: Internally, Murati often advocated for caution. Reports suggest she delayed features of GPT-4o and pushed back on the premature rollout of the o1 model, clashing with commercialization pressures.

> "I believe in a future where AI is a collaborative tool that empowers humans, not replaces them." — *On founding Thinking Machines Lab*

### Thinking Machines Lab (2024–Present)
Founded in Dec 2024, announced Feb 2025.
- **Mission**: Develop AI systems that align with human values through collaboration.
- **Structure**: Public benefit corporation. "Open-science practices" including publishing research and code.
- **Funding**: $1B seed (Jul 2025) led by Andreessen Horowitz; $12B valuation.
- **Team**: ~20 engineers, many ex-OpenAI.

## Core Ideas & Philosophy

### AI Safety and Governance
Murati is a vocal advocate for government regulation. In a June 2024 Dartmouth commencement speech, she called for oversight on high-risk AI applications. She argues that safety and innovation are not mutually exclusive, stating that rigorous evaluation enables sustainable progress.

### AGI Timelines
Murati believes AGI is achievable but likely "decades" away. She rejects hype-driven timelines, emphasizing the need for breakthroughs in long-term reasoning, agency, and robustness.

### Collaborative AI
Her new venture, Thinking Machines Lab, focuses on tools that incorporate human feedback and guidance, contrasting with the trend toward fully autonomous agents. She emphasizes accessibility for diverse industries beyond just coding.

## Key Quotes

> "AI will eventually match human performance across diverse tasks, though the exact pathway remains uncertain." — *Dec 2024 Interview*

> "We need to bridge the divide between rapid AI advancements and broader societal understanding." — *On Thinking Machines Lab*

## Controversies

- **2023 Ouster**: Her brief role as Interim CEO highlighted deep rifts between safety-focused board members and product-focused executives.
- **o1 Rollout**: Internal reports indicated staff viewed the o1 preview as premature, with Murati advocating for more testing before Altman overrode concerns.
- **Brain Drain**: Her departure in Sep 2024 was seen as a significant loss for OpenAI, compounding talent attrition fears.
