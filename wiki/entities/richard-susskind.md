# Richard Susskind

**Professor Richard E. Susskind CBE KC** is one of the world's leading thinkers on the intersection of technology, law, and society. He is President of the **Society for Computers and Law**, **Special Envoy for Justice and AI** to the Secretary-General of the Commonwealth, and from 1998 to 2023 served as **Technology Adviser to the Lord Chief Justice of England and Wales**.

## Background

- **Education:** Law graduate of Glasgow University; DPhil on AI and law from Balliol College, Oxford (mid-1980s)
- **Fellowships:** Royal Society of Edinburgh, British Computer Society
- **Publications:** 11 books, translated into 16 languages
- **Speaking:** Invited to speak in over 50 countries
- **Founded:** Remote Courts Worldwide

## Key Book: "How To Think About AI: A Guide For The Perplexed" (2025)

Published by Oxford University Press (March 2025), this non-technical guide challenges readers to think differently about AI's role in society and personal cognition.

### Structure

| Part | Chapters | Theme |
|------|----------|-------|
| **One: Understanding AI** | 1-2 | The Summer of AI, On Technology |
| **Two: Thinking Differently** | 3-5 | Process vs. Outcome Thinking, Confusions, We Don't Have the Words |
| **Three: Making AI Work** | 6-7 | Automation, Innovation, Elimination; Radical Structural Change |
| **Four: Confronting Risks** | 8-10 | Categories of Risk, Harnessing AI, Coming Soon, The Great Schism |

### Key Frameworks

**Automation → Innovation → Elimination:**
- **Automation:** Applying technology to existing human tasks (backward-looking, boosts productivity)
- **Innovation:** Using technology to achieve previously impossible outcomes
- **Elimination:** Deploying AI to make entire problems disappear, so automation and innovation are no longer needed

**Process-Thinking vs. Outcome-Thinking:**
Susskind argues that we often focus too much on how tasks are done (process) rather than what outcomes we want to achieve (outcomes). AI enables outcome-focused approaches that bypass traditional process constraints.

**AI Governance Model:**
Just as we don't allow doctors to be the final arbiters of medical ethics, we shouldn't assume computer scientists and tech titans should make the rules for AI. Susskind suggests the interdisciplinary approach with public consultations — used in medical ethics — as a model for AI governance.

## Influence on AI Discourse

Susskind's work resonates with the "How to think using AI" discourse (e.g., Sufyan Maan's May 2026 article) that explores AI as cognitive augmentation rather than mere automation. His framing of AI's evolution — from tools that automate existing processes to systems that enable radical structural change — provides a philosophical foundation for understanding the agent engineering revolution.
