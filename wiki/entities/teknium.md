# Ryan (Teknium)

## Overview

Ryan, known online as **Teknium** (@Teknium1), is the **Co-founder and Head of Post-Training** at **Nous Research**. He is the lead architect behind the **Hermes model family** (Hermes 2, Hermes 3, Hermes 4) — among the most influential open-source instruct-tuned LLMs. His work spans post-training, synthetic data generation, RL environments, and open-source AI development.

Nous Research is a $1B-valued AI research lab (Series A, 2025, led by Paradigm) focused on decentralized, open-weight AI models. Teknium is one of four co-founders alongside Jeffrey Quesnelle (CEO), Karan Malhotra (Head of Behavior), and Shivani Mitra.

## The Hermes Model Family

The Hermes series is Nous Research's flagship line of instruct-tuned LLMs:

### Hermes 4 (2025)
- **Hybrid reasoning model** combining structured multi-turn reasoning with broad instruction-following
- ~5 million training samples, ~19 billion tokens
- Outperforms Meta's own instruct variant on MATH by ~60 points
- Published technical report with open weights on Hugging Face
- Key innovation: data synthesis strategy for reasoning + non-reasoning data mix

### Hermes 3 (2024)
- **Neutrally-aligned generalist** instruct and tool-use model
- Strong reasoning and creative abilities
- Fine-tuned on Llama 3.1 (8B, 70B, 405B)
- Features: XML structured output, scratchpad reasoning, internal monologues, Mermaid diagrams, step-labeled reasoning
- 390M tokens across 100% curated instruction data (Tool Use, Content Generation, Steering & Alignment)
- DPO (Direct Preference Optimization) for alignment

### Hermes 2
- Available on Llama 2, Mistral, Yi-34B, and Mixtral-8x7B backbones
- Notable: Nous-Hermes-2-Mixtral-8x7B, OpenHermes 2.5 (Mistral 7B)
- OpenHermes became one of the most popular open-weight models in its class

### Previous Work
- **GPT4-x-Vicuna-13b**: Early Vicuna model built on GPT-4
- **Nous-Hermes-Llama2-13b**: Hermes model on Llama 2
- **Replit-Instruct 3B**: Doubled code performance on Replit's code model

## Post-Training Philosophy

Teknium's approach to post-training at Nous Research follows a rigorous pipeline:

1. **Data Engineering**: Finding, curating, cleaning, creating, and sourcing the right kind of data for target tasks
2. **Infrastructure**: Sourcing compute, managing distributed training across GPUs
3. **Testing & Evaluation**: Comprehensive benchmarks, documentation, and ablations

He emphasizes that post-training is highly effective when you have a specific use case in mind, but models generalize best with diverse, high-quality datasets. Nous Research has consistently kept pace with and often outperformed Meta's Llama instruct models despite having far fewer resources.

## Core Ideas

### Open-Source First
Teknium is a strong advocate for open-weight models. Hermes 4 weights are published publicly on Hugging Face. He believes open models allow for specialization, auditing, and innovation that closed models cannot match.

### Post-Training Is the Differentiator
> "If you want a specialist model, yes — or if you have at least some ideas of end use cases you want to support. The models do generalize, but they generalize best when given a good, diverse dataset."

His philosophy is that post-training quality is determined by data quality and curation, not just compute scale.

### Neutral Alignment
Hermes models are described as "neutrally-aligned" — they attempt to faithfully respond to the system prompt rather than imposing their own worldview. This makes them more steerable for specialized use cases.

### Build in Public
Teknium is known for rapid, transparent development — merging 95 PRs in 2 days for Hermes Agent v0.6.0 and answering community questions directly on Discord.
