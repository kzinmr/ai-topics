# Recursive Superintelligence

**Recursive Superintelligence** is an AI company founded by Richard Socher with the explicit goal of building recursive, self-improving superintelligence at scale. The company represents one of the most direct attempts to pursue [[concepts/recursive-self-improvement|recursive self-improvement]] (RSI) as a primary business objective.

## Mission

The company's stated goal: *"Build truly recursive, self-improving superintelligence at scale — where the entire process of ideation, implementation, and validation of research ideas would be automatic."*

This means creating a closed loop where AI systems:
1. Generate research ideas
2. Implement experiments
3. Validate results
4. Use learnings to generate better ideas

All without human intervention.

## Founder: Richard Socher

Richard Socher is a well-known AI researcher and entrepreneur. He previously founded You.com and served as Chief Scientist at Salesforce. His pivot to RSI reflects a broader industry recognition that the next frontier in AI may be automation of AI research itself.

## RSI: The New AGI

TechCrunch characterized RSI as "the new AGI — and it's just as hard to pin down" (May 28, 2026). Like AGI before it, RSI has become a buzzword with contested definitions. In basic terms, RSI refers to an AI system that can continuously upgrade itself — once AI systems manage the upgrade cycle better than humans, the process becomes a closed loop limited only by compute.

Recursive Superintelligence is part of a growing RSI ecosystem:

## Cautions and Critiques

- **Sundar Pichai**: *"In the way people describe RSI, that would represent a next level of acceleration... but we aren't quite there yet."*
- **Helen Toner (CSET)**: Argues that "using AI for AI research isn't enough to qualify as RSI"
- Current systems remain limited to incremental improvements on small-scale models (GPT-2 level for Karpathy's work)
