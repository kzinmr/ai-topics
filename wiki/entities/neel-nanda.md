## Overview

Neel Nanda is a leading researcher and educator in **mechanistic interpretability (MI)** — the study of reverse-engineering neural network internals into interpretable circuits and features. He has created the most comprehensive set of educational resources for entering the field, and his work has been instrumental in building the MI research community.

## Key Resources (cited in GenAI Handbook)

- **"A Comprehensive Mechanistic Interpretability Explainer & Glossary"** — The definitive reference for MI terminology and concepts
- **"An Extremely Opinionated Annotated List of My Favourite Mechanistic Interpretability Papers"** — Curated reading list by topic
- **"Mechanistic Interpretability Quickstart Guide"** — Practical entry point for new researchers
- **"How useful is mechanistic interpretability?"** — Community discussion on MI's practical value
- **"200 Concrete Problems in Interpretability"** — Open problems spreadsheet for the field
