| **Bio** | George Hotz (geohot) is a well-known hacker who first gained fame for being the first person to carrier-unlock the iPhone (2007) and later for hacking the PlayStation 3. He founded comma.ai (self-driving car technology) and is the creator of **tinygrad**, a minimalist deep learning framework. Known for his iconoclastic views on AI, technology, and culture. |

## Core Work

### tinygrad

Creator of **tinygrad**, a minimalist deep learning framework designed to be simple enough to understand fully while still being functional. The project embodies a "less is more" philosophy — contrasting with the increasing complexity of frameworks like PyTorch and JAX.

### comma.ai

Founded comma.ai, developing open-source advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and self-driving technology. The company's approach emphasizes practical, incremental autonomy rather than full self-driving promises.

## Philosophy and Commentary

### "Punk, or Why I Don't Stream Anymore" (May 2026)

In a May 2026 blog post titled "[Punk, or why I don't stream anymore](https://geohot.github.io//blog/jekyll/update/2026/05/03/punk-or-why-i-dont-stream.html)", Hotz articulated a critique of the modern internet culture and AI's role in it:

- **Spectacle vs. participation**: "What killed the hacker culture I grew up in was spectacle. You can consume it without participating, and even worse it has signaling value."
- **Wireheading**: Streaming and AI-assisted consumption are "wireheading, aka 'felt completion without world contact'" — you feel like you're steering but you aren't.
- **AI homogenization**: "The machine takes your culture and sells a shitty version of it back to them."
- **On AI capability**: "ChatGPT told me to end it there. I'm still smarter than it, but by less and less each year."
- **Information war**: "The new war demands your inner reality. The new war will be weird in all sorts of new ways we can't even imagine yet."

This post reflects Hotz's broader skepticism about passive AI consumption and the loss of genuine agency in an AI-mediated world.
