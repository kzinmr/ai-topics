## Embra (2023–2026)

Embra was an AI platform that built a **Graph Context Engine** to power B2B AI applications. The product created a "Business Memory Engine" that learned from users, prospects, and internal team interactions — ingesting information to detect patterns, cluster insights, and automate work. Embra was **acquired by Notion** in January 2026.

## Key Ideas

### AI Context & Data Apps (April 2026)

Tratar articulated a critique of vertical SaaS fragmentation:

> "You shouldn't have to have a 'meeting notes app.' You should have an 'AI context & data app' that happens to have great meeting notes. Don't overpay for things."

This reframes the SaaS landscape from **function-specific tools** (meeting notes, CRM, project management) to **unified context platforms** — a single AI-native application that ingests all organizational data and surfaces what's needed contextually, rather than requiring separate subscriptions for each function.

### Context Engineering

Tratar has been focused on the challenge of **context engineering** — building systems that can understand, organize, and leverage the full breadth of organizational knowledge — long before it became an industry buzzword. Ivan Zhao (Notion CEO) specifically cited this in announcing the Embra acquisition.
