# Ivan Burazin

**X/Twitter:** @ivanburazin
**Company:** Daytona (Co-Founder & CEO)
**Location:** New York, United States

## Background

Ivan Burazin is the Co-Founder and CEO of [[entities/daytona-io]], a company building **composable computers (sandboxes) for AI agents**. He previously built and sold a SaaS startup, and has a track record of open-source contributions (Daytona was open-sourced in March 2024).

## Daytona

Daytona provides fast, stateful composable computing environments — popularly called "AI sandboxes" — that let AI agents run code, commands, and computer-use workflows with full control over CPU, RAM, disk, and OS, provisioned in under 60 milliseconds. Key milestones:

- **Nov 2023:** Raised $2M pre-seed
- **Jun 2024:** Open-sourced Daytona
- **Jun 2024:** Raised $5M Series A led by Upfront Ventures
- **Oct 2025:** 1M+ sandboxes per day
- **Jun 2025:** Launched macOS sandboxes (early access)

## Key Ideas

### Headless SaaS (April 2026)

Burazin coined the term **Headless SaaS** to describe traditional software products rebuilt with agent-first APIs as the primary interface:

> "New category emerging: Headless SaaS. Not infrastructure as a service / platform as a service. Traditional software (Photoshop, Slack, Jira) rebuilt with agent-first APIs. No UI. Programmatic access. Essentially the same product with different interface. Entirely new business model."

### Agent-Majority Computing

In his HumanX 2026 interview, Burazin predicted a structural shift:

> "We're going to go from people-majority to agent-majority. It's already happening. It's going to be like 90%."

This means every agent needs its own computing environment — not just API endpoints, but full machines with CPU, RAM, disk, and OS access matched to the task.

### Composable Computers, Not Just Sandboxes

Burazin argues that the market term "sandbox" undersells the concept. Humans use different computers for different jobs (MacBook Air for light work, Linux box for development, Windows for 3D rendering). Agents need the same thing: access to different computing environments.
