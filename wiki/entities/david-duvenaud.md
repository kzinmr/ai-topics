# David Duvenaud

**David Duvenaud** (@DavidDuvenaud) is a professor and AI researcher at the University of Toronto, known for contributions to neural architecture search, Gaussian processes, generative modeling, and deep learning.

## Academic Background

David Duvenaud is a faculty member in the Department of Computer Science at the **University of Toronto**, affiliated with the **Vector Institute for AI**. His research spans probabilistic machine learning, differentiable optimization, and generative models.

## Key Work

### Talkie (April 2026)
Co-creator of **Talkie**, an open-weight 13B LLM trained exclusively on pre-1930 historical text data. Built alongside [[entities/alec-radford]] and @status_effects, Talkie represents a novel approach: instead of training on the full modern internet corpus, it constrains training to a specific historical period, creating a model that has never seen modern concepts. The project received significant community interest (1.3M+ impressions, 2223 bookmarks) for its controlled experiment in temporal knowledge boundaries.

See [[entities/talkie]] for full project details.

### Neural Architecture Search & Differentiable Structure Learning
Pioneered methods for learning neural network architectures through gradient-based optimization, enabling automatic discovery of efficient model designs.

### Gaussian Process Applications
Contributions to scalable Gaussian process inference and applications of probabilistic models in deep learning.
