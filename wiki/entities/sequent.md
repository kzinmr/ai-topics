# Sequent

**Sequent** is a nonprofit research organization founded in June 2026 by former members of the UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) Alignment team and researchers from **Timaeus** (an alignment theory startup). The organization was announced by Jack Clark in Import AI 461 with the thesis that "alignment is not on track."

## Founding Thesis

The founders argue that:
> "Artificial superintelligence (ASI) may be developed in the next few years. It is unclear whether alignment is on track to be ready on the same timeframe. At a minimum, the empirical programs at AI labs are unlikely to deliver a priori confidence, before training ASI, that things will go well."

> "In an ideal world, we would develop an approach to building superintelligence together with a theoretical proof that it was safe, and then build it. In this world, we probably have to settle well short of this ideal."

## Research Portfolio

Sequent plans to pursue a portfolio of under-resourced research bets in three areas:

1. **Scalable Oversight**: Methods for supervising AI systems that are more capable than humans
2. **Learning Theory**: Theoretical foundations for understanding how AI systems learn and generalize
3. **Heuristic Argument**: Informal reasoning frameworks for building confidence in AI safety claims

## Organization Structure

- **Initial funding target**: $100-150M
- **Growth plan**: 40-80 full-time employees within 2-3 years
- **Type**: Nonprofit research organization
- **Founded**: June 2026

## Background

The organization emerged from two sources:
- **UK AI Safety Institute (AISI) Alignment team**: Government researchers who concluded that current alignment efforts are insufficient
- **Timaeus**: An alignment theory startup focused on formal approaches to AI safety

The formation of Sequent represents a significant development in the AI safety landscape — experienced researchers from both government and startup backgrounds concluding that alignment is not progressing fast enough and forming a new organization to address the gap.

## Significance

Sequent's founding signals a growing consensus among alignment researchers that:
1. The timeline to ASI may be shorter than the timeline for alignment solutions
2. Current lab-led empirical approaches are insufficient for a priori safety confidence
3. A new organizational structure is needed to pursue under-resourced but critical safety research
