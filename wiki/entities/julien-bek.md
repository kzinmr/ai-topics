# Julien Bek

**Julien Bek** is a Partner at [[entities/sequoia-capital|Sequoia Capital]], based in London. He is known for articulating the "Service-as-Software" thesis — the argument that the next trillion-dollar company will sell AI-executed work (outcomes), not AI-powered tools (software). His March 2026 essay "Services: The New Software" became one of the most-circulated venture theses of 2026.

## Background

Julien Bek comes from a family of entrepreneurs. He had a successful startup in high school and a less successful one during his graduate program at Cambridge. He spent a summer at a VC firm where he saw early pitches for UiPath and Revolut — experiences that shaped his "people filter" for identifying exceptional founders.

He joined Sequoia Capital as a Partner in September 2023, focusing on London and the European ecosystem. He is fluent in both English and French.

## Key Investments

Bek's investments span fintech, enterprise SaaS, and AI-native service companies:

- **Revolut** — Angel investor (2016), now one of Europe's most valuable fintechs
- **Rillet** — AI-powered ERP for the accounting space
- **Magentic** — AI-driven supply chain procurement optimization
- **Dust** — LLM-powered enterprise productivity
- **Tacto** — Procurement intelligence for industrial SMEs
- **Attio** — Next-generation CRM
- **Auctor** — (Sequoia partnership)

## The "Services: The New Software" Thesis

Bek's most influential contribution to the 2026 AI discourse is his essay **"Services: The New Software"** (March 5, 2026), published on Sequoia's blog. The core thesis:

- **Intelligence vs. Judgement**: AI has crossed the threshold to perform "intelligence" work autonomously (complex rules, debugging, testing). "Judgement" (taste, instinct, strategy) remains human. But the boundary is shifting.
- **Copilots → Autopilots**: The business model shifts from selling productivity tools to professionals → selling completed outcomes to end-customers. The budget shifts from software spend (1x) to labor/services spend (6x).
- **Outsourcing as the Wedge**: The ideal entry point is tasks that are already outsourced — replacing an outsourcing contract is a "vendor swap," not a "reorg."
- **Opportunity Map**: Insurance brokerage ($140-200B), Accounting ($50-80B), Healthcare Revenue Cycle ($50-80B), IT Managed Services ($100B+), Recruitment ($200B+).
- **Innovator's Dilemma**: Copilot companies from 2025 will struggle to become Autopilot companies because selling work directly cuts their own professional customers out.

The thesis was covered by Fortune, Forbes, and sparked widespread debate in venture circles.

## On the Lookout For

Bek describes himself as "an entrepreneur at heart" who gets passionate about applications solving everyday problems. He is bullish on European AI talent density and sees Europe's maturing ecosystem as a key competitive advantage.
