# Scott Stevenson

**Scott Stevenson** (@scottastevenson) is the CEO and co-founder of **Spellbook Legal**, an AI-powered contract drafting and review platform for lawyers. Known for the viral X Article "High-Frequency Software" (February 2026), which introduced the HFT-to-software analogy framework for understanding how AI acceleration transforms the business of software.

## Spellbook Legal

Spellbook is an AI-powered platform for contract drafting, review, and negotiation. It operates in the legal AI space — a domain Stevenson describes as experiencing "massive rounds" and rapid competitive consolidation where AI-native companies with leverage are "wiping out" legacy competitors.

The company launched 100+ landing pages over 3 years to find product-market fit — an experience that directly informed Stevenson's HFS thesis about "low conviction strategies" and portfolio-of-bets approaches.

## High-Frequency Software Framework (Feb 2026)

Stevenson's most influential contribution is the **High-Frequency Software (HFS)** framework, which maps the structural dynamics of high-frequency trading onto AI-accelerated software:

1. **Phase shift**: 10x faster software delivery is a state change — the skills and strategies from the previous era don't translate.
2. **Edge funds**: The most novel prediction — startup + VC fund hybrids that eliminate fundraising latency and allocate capital at the market's edge.
3. **Front running**: Competitor functionality replication is now constant, with AI reducing the cost of copying.
4. **AI fracking**: Well-capitalized AI-native companies will absorb micro-niches previously served by bootstrapped companies.
5. **Stealth returns**: When execution is commoditized, ideas gain relative value. Founders will become more secretive.

See [[concepts/high-frequency-software]] for the full framework.

## Core Ideas

1. **Software as high-frequency trading**: Both are pure digital domains with no theoretical speed limit. 10x acceleration = phase shift, not just optimization.
2. **Edge funds over traditional VC**: When companies can ship mature software in days, a 6-month fundraising process becomes the bottleneck.
3. **Comical speed as competitive advantage**: "If you're not leaning into 'comical speed' territory, you're probably not thinking fast enough."
4. **Winner-takes-all amplification**: Winners won't just win one war — they'll build a machine that wins hundreds across many products and markets.
