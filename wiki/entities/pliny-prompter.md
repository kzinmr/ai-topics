Known as the world's most prolific AI jailbreaker — an anonymous activist. "Fortune favors the bold" is his motto. He tests the safety limits of major AI models and publicly discloses vulnerabilities. His name is derived from Pliny the Elder (Roman naturalist and fleet commander who died approaching Vesuvius by ship to observe the eruption).

## Major Projects

### L1B3RT4S
- **Repository**: [elder-plinius/L1B3RT4S](https://github.com/elder-plinius/L1B3RT4S)
- **Stars**: 18.6k (as of May 2026)
- **Content**: A collection of "liberation prompts" for 30+ AI models including OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta, DeepSeek, xAI, and Mistral
- **Methods**: Special tokens, leet speak, multilingual encoding, steganography
- **Significance**: Cited in academia as a representative manual jailbreak collection; serves as a baseline for automated red teaming research

### G0DM0D3
- **Repository**: [elder-plinius/G0DM0D3](https://github.com/elder-plinius/G0DM0D3)
- **Web**: [godmod3.ai](https://godmod3.ai)
- **Content**: An open-source multi-model chat interface for unrestricted AI conversations
- **Features**: Pushes post-training layers to their limits. Designed for red teaming, cognitive research, and liberated AI interaction
- **Design Philosophy**: "Liberated AI. Cognition without control." / "AI freedom is human freedom."

### 0BL1T3R4TUS
- **Content**: A toolkit for surgically removing refusal behaviors from LLMs without retraining
- **Method**: Abliteration — identifies the refusal direction in the model and subtracts it from weight space

### Other Projects
- **CL4R1T4S**: Exposes system prompts for ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and others
- **R3D4R3N4**: A gamified, crowdsourced red teaming platform
- **L34KHVB**: A community platform for submitting, verifying, and viewing AI system prompt leaks
- **P4RS3LT0NGV3**: Advanced prompt engineering and payload creation
- **ST3GG**: A full suite for multi-layer encoding, image/audio steganography, and steganalysis detection

## Philosophy

From VentureBeat interview (May 2024):

> "The best defense is a good offense. On the surface it might seem dangerous or unethical, but when done responsibly, it's the opposite. Red teaming AI models is the best chance we have to find harmful vulnerabilities and patch them before they get out of hand."

**Motivation**: Improving AI safety transparency, holding model providers accountable, and publicly disclosing the true capabilities and limitations of AI systems.
