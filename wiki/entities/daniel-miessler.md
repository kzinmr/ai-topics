# Daniel Miessler

**Role:** Cybersecurity/AI engineer, founder, writer
**URL:** https://danielmiessler.com
**X/Twitter:** @DanielMiessler
**GitHub:** https://github.com/danielmiessler
**Location:** San Francisco Bay Area

Daniel Miessler is a cybersecurity and AI engineer, founder, and researcher based in San Francisco. Since 1999, he has maintained a platform featuring over 3,000 essays and tutorials, currently serving as the home for his **Unsupervised Learning** project — focused on using AI to upgrade humans.

## Professional Background

- **2022–Present:** Founder of **Unsupervised Learning** (AI to upgrade humans); Fractional CISO
- **2021–2022 (Robinhood):** Built Vulnerability Management and AppSec programs
- **2018–2021 (Apple):** Head of Business Intelligence for Information Security
- **2015–2018 (IOActive):** Built and ran the Advisory Services group
- **2009–2015 (HP):** Co-founded **Fortify on Demand** (scaled from 2 to 350+ people)
- **2006–2009 (Aflac):** Head of Application Security
- **1992–1998 (US Army):** Infantry, Airborne, and Intelligence (S2) for the 101st Airborne; Spanish Linguist

## Key Ideas & Contributions

### Human 3.0
Miessler's philosophy of humanity's evolution: **Human 1.0** (primitive, physical survival) → **Human 2.0** (corporate, defined by value to capitalist society) → **Human 3.0** (creative, defined by unique value creation).

### Autonomous Component Optimization (April 2026)
Proposed the **Universal Improvement Cycle** extending Karpathy's Autoresearch concept: Map → Execute → Log → Collect → Optimize → Update. The thesis: "Everything we do becomes measurable, but more importantly: improvable."

### Intent-Based Engineering (April 2026)
Identified the **articulation gap** as the new primary bottleneck in AI — shifting from "how to build" to "how to describe what good looks like." The new elite skill: reverse-engineering a request into discrete, testable binary pass/fail criteria (8–12 words each).

### The Scaffolding Ratio (April 2026)
Argued that **75–99% of knowledge work is scaffolding** (maintaining tools, workflows, templates, knowledge bases) — and AI "crushes" this overhead, making expertise accessible to non-experts.
