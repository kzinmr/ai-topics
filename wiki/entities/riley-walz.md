## Overview

Riley Walz is an American software engineer and internet artist whose work sits at the intersection of programming, culture reporting, and public intervention. His projects follow a consistent pattern: identify a public data seam (a stream of information that's technically public but practically invisible), build a thin, fast interface to surface it, and launch with a sharp public hook that generates institutional or cultural reaction.

_The New Yorker_ describes his work as existing within "a lineage of prankster art that used the Internet both as a medium and as a venue." _Wired_ profiled his "nostalgic sites" in late 2024, noting that his web experiments frequently tap into collective memory of earlier internet eras.

In February 2026, Walz joined **OpenAI Labs** under Joanne Jang, where he prototypes new interfaces for human-AI collaboration. This move signals OpenAI's recognition that the future of AI interfaces requires the same kind of creative data-surfacing instincts that Walz has demonstrated across his independent projects.

## Creative Approach

Walz's work follows a recognizable creative pattern:

1. **Public data seam** — Identify a stream of information that is technically public but practically invisible (parking citation IDs, Shazam-able street audio, forgotten YouTube uploads)
2. **Thin interface** — Build the simplest possible web surface to make that data visible and browsable
3. **Sharp public hook** — Frame the project with a clear, memorable concept that captures public attention (Bop Spotter as play on ShotSpotter, Jmail as "logged in as Epstein")
4. **Public/institutional reaction** — Let the project generate debate, media coverage, and sometimes institutional response
5. **Methodology/archive** — Document how it was built and preserve the work for others to study

This pattern treats projects as **interventions rather than durable products** — many of his projects are ephemeral by design, existing only as long as the underlying data source remains accessible.

> "I did not make that GitHub account, I did not go to Stanford, I'm not a CS guy" — @rtwlz, March 2026

Walz studied business in college before dropping out to work in tech. He won a WWDC scholarship in 2019 and previously worked as a software engineer before his independent projects gained widespread attention.

## Key Projects

### Bop Spotter (2024)
A solar-powered Android phone and microphone installed on a Mission District street pole. The device captures audio clips, runs them through Shazam, and publishes an ongoing playlist of recognized songs. The title is a playful nod to urban "ShotSpotter" gunshot surveillance systems. Covered by KQED, Mission Local, and _The New Yorker_.

### IMG_0001 (2024)
A website that cycles through five million YouTube clips uploaded directly from early iPhone models with default filenames (e.g., IMG_0001). Filters for short, low-view videos from roughly 2009–2012. _Wired_ called it "a gut punch" of internet nostalgia. Exhibited at Kunstkerk in Dordrecht (2025) in collaboration with the Dordrechts Museum.

### Find My Parking Cops (September 2025)
A real-time map that deduced the near-real-time locations and routes of San Francisco parking-enforcement officers by scraping predictable citation IDs from the city's online payment portal. Went viral within hours. San Francisco MTA officials disabled the underlying data source four hours after launch. Covered by _Wired_ and local outlets, sparking public debate over enforcement transparency.

### Jmail (November 2025)
Co-created with Luke Igel. A browser-based archive of public emails released under the Epstein Files Transparency Act, presented through a Gmail-style interface as if viewed from Jeffrey Epstein's personal inbox. Incorporates "Jemini," an AI search tool using Gemini for OCR. Attracted ~18.4 million visits by late November 2025. Expanded into JPhotos, JFlights, Jamazon, Jacebook, Jmessage, Jotify, and Jdrive.

### Other Projects
- **Library Spy** — Surveillance of library usage patterns
- **Amtrak Right Now** — Real-time visualization of Amtrak train locations
- **Payphone Go** — Mapping and documenting remaining payphones
- **Andrew Walz** (2020) — Fictional Republican congressional candidate Twitter account to test platform verification; briefly verified before suspension
- **Mehran's Steak House** (2023) — Fictional restaurant that began as a Google Maps listing and staged a one-night pop-up in the East Village

## OpenAI Role

In February 2026, Walz joined **OpenAI Labs** under Joanne Jang. The Labs division is OpenAI's experimental product arm, responsible for prototyping new interfaces beyond the core ChatGPT assistant. Walz's role involves designing and building new interaction paradigms for human-AI collaboration — drawing on his demonstrated ability to create interfaces that make complex systems visible and navigable.

This hiring reflects a broader industry recognition that the next frontier in AI is not just model capability but **interface design** — how humans and AI systems interact, collaborate, and co-create.
