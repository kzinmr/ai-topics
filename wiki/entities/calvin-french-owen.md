## Background

Calvin French-Owen is a startup founder and engineer best known for founding **Segment**, the customer data platform acquired by Twilio. He joined OpenAI in May 2024 and departed in July 2025, having been deeply involved in the **Codex** launch from February to May 2025.

His deep-dive blog post *"Reflections on OpenAI"* (June 2026) provides one of the most detailed firsthand accounts of OpenAI's organizational culture during its hypergrowth phase.

## Key Writings

### Reflections on OpenAI (June 2026)

See [[concepts/openai/reflections-on-openai]] for the comprehensive concept page covering his account of OpenAI's culture, the Codex development sprint, Azure infrastructure challenges, and observations on organizational dynamics.

Key themes:
- **Rapid growth**: OpenAI went from ~1,000 to ~3,000 employees during his tenure; top 30% tenure after just 1 year
- **Slack-centric culture**: ~10 emails received during entire tenure; all communication via Slack
- **Bottom-up innovation**: Quarterly roadmap "doesn't exist"; good ideas come from anywhere
- **Fast iteration**: Agent application teams could ship in under 2 weeks; Python monorepo with CI/CD
- **Codex launch**: Built in 7 weeks from idea to production
