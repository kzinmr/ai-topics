# Dean W. Ball

## Overview

Dean W. Ball is an AI policy analyst and writer of the **Hyperdimensional** newsletter on Substack. His work focuses on the governance challenges posed by advanced AI systems, particularly the intersection of cybersecurity, national security, and frontier model deployment. He advocates for structured, evaluation-based regulatory frameworks that avoid both blanket deregulation and heavy-handed state control.

## Background

- **Hyperdimensional newsletter** — Analysis of AI policy, governance, and institutional design
- Fellow at the **Mercatus Center** at George Mason University
- Research focuses on AI governance, cybersecurity policy, and institutional responses to transformative technologies

## Key Ideas

### The "Mythos" Moment (May 2026)
Ball's analysis of Anthropic's non-public **Mythos** model — which demonstrated unprecedented autonomous vulnerability discovery — forms the basis of his governance framework:

- **The Capability Shift**: Mythos can string multiple bugs into coherent exploits, a task previously reserved for elite human hackers. This dramatically changes the cost structure of vulnerability discovery.
- **The Current Impasse**: The U.S. government has informally asked Anthropic not to release Mythos broadly while allowing OpenAI's GPT 5.5 — an ad-hoc "informal licensing" regime without legal basis.

### "Aviate, Navigate, Communicate"
Ball adapts the pilot's emergency mantra as a framework for AI policy:

1. **Aviate** (Current Stage): Keep the aircraft (society/technology) under control
2. **Navigate**: Determine the long-term direction for AI governance
3. **Communicate**: Explain the situation to the broader public

### The State Conflict of Interest
Ball identifies a fundamental tension in government-controlled AI security:
> "Governments have an incentive for the world's software to be less secure in a way that no other legitimate actor does... [they] sometimes keep vulnerabilities to themselves for espionage."

### Independent Verification Organizations (IVOs)
As an alternative to both corporate self-regulation and state control, Ball proposes IVOs:
- Publicly overseen, privately managed, nonpartisan bodies
- Licensed to verify safety claims and procedures
- Federally authorized to preempt state-level regulatory patchwork (CA, NY)

### Three-Part Governance Framework
1. **Prudential**: Delay release for models significantly beyond open-weight cyber-capability floor
2. **Dissemination**: Expand public-private vulnerability sharing (modeled on Project Glasswing)
3. **Long-term Security**: Fund provably secure software via Focused Research Organizations (FROs)
