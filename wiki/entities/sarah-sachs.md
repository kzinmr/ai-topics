# Sarah Sachs

**Sarah Sachs** (LinkedIn: [sarahmsachs](https://www.linkedin.com/in/sarahmsachs), X: [@sarahmsachs](https://x.com/sarahmsachs)) is an engineering leader at **[[entities/notion|Notion]]**, where she manages the Core AI capabilities and infrastructure team (~50 people). She previously worked at Robinhood, giving her deep experience in compliance and security-first engineering culture.

## Overview

Sachs joined Notion to manage the AI engineering team, bringing expertise in building low-ego, high-velocity engineering organizations capable of rapid iteration on frontier AI capabilities. Her leadership philosophy centers on objective-setting over idea ownership, and building teams comfortable with deleting their own work when better approaches emerge.

## Core Topics

### AI Engineering at Notion

Sachs leads Notion's AI infrastructure engineering, overseeing:
- **Core AI capabilities and infrastructure** (~50 people)
- **Eval framework development** — regression tests, launch-quality evals, frontier/headroom evals
- **Model Behavior Engineer** role definition and hiring
- **Agent dev velocity** platform — enabling every team to maintain their own evals

### Three-Tier Eval Philosophy

Sachs articulates Notion's evaluation approach:
1. **Regression tests** — CI-integrated, must pass before deployment
2. **Launch-quality evals** — report card requiring 80-90% pass rate on user journeys
3. **Frontier/Headroom evals** — intentionally designed to pass only ~30% to identify where model capabilities are heading

> "We have a whole org dedicated to agent platform velocity so that you can build your own eval and then maintain it once you ship it."

### Engineering Culture

Key principles Sachs promotes:
- **"Demos over memos"** — prototypes must be working feature flags
- **Low-ego teams** — comfortable deleting their own work and rebuilding
- **Security-first approach** — bring security reviews in early, not as afterthought
- **Objective-setting over idea ownership** — leadership ensures everyone understands the goal, not the specific solution

## Background
