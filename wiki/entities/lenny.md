## Overview

Lenny Rachitsky is one of the most influential voices in product management and technology, known for making complex product and growth strategies accessible and actionable. He writes **Lenny's Newsletter** (the #1 business newsletter on Substack with 1.2M+ subscribers and 45K+ paid subscribers) and hosts **Lenny's Podcast** (a top 10 technology podcast globally with 300+ episodes). His work reaches a massive audience of product leaders, founders, and ambitious builders through deeply researched, no-nonsense advice on product, growth, and career development.

He began his career as the founder and CEO of **Localmind**, a location-based Q&A startup that was acquired by **Airbnb** in 2012. At Airbnb, he spent seven years as Product Lead for Supply Growth, leading teams focused on scaling host onboarding and platform supply, conversion, trip quality, and community. After leaving Airbnb in 2019, he launched his Substack newsletter, which has since grown into a full media ecosystem including a podcast, community, job board, courses, and AI tools.

Lenny is also an active **angel investor** with investments in notable startups including Figma, Vanta, Webflow, and others, as well as a startup advisor. He created **Lennybot**, an AI-powered assistant trained on his writing and podcast insights.

## Lenny's Newsletter

## AI & Podcast Coverage of AI Evals

Lenny's Podcast has become an important platform for discussions about **AI evaluation and product development**. The most prominent AI-related episode is:

### "Why AI Evals Are the Hottest New Skill for Product Builders" (September 2025)

**Guests:** [[hamel-husain]] and [[shreya-shankar]] — co-creators of the world's most popular AI evals course on Maven (trained 2,000+ PMs and engineers, including teams at OpenAI and Anthropic)

**Episode Details:**
- **Views:** 101.5K+ on YouTube, 1.6K+ likes
- **URL:** https://open.substack.com/pub/lenny/p/why-ai-evals-are-the-hottest-new-skill
- **Duration:** ~1h 40min

**Core Topics Covered:**

1. **What Are Evals?** — Systematic methods to measure AI product quality, replacing subjective "vibes-based" assessment with measurable, reproducible checks.

2. **Error Analysis First** — Before writing any eval, manually review real user interaction traces. The highest ROI activity involves looking at actual user data, which most teams neglect.

3. **Open Coding & Axial Coding** — A rigorous methodology: review 100 production traces, write freeform notes on errors, use LLMs to categorize into actionable failure modes, continue until "theoretical saturation."

4. **The "Benevolent Dictator" Principle** — Assign one domain expert with product taste to conduct error analysis rather than forming committees, maintaining consistency in taxonomy development.

5. **Code-based Evals vs. LLM-as-Judge** — Code evals for deterministic checks (fast, cheap); LLM-as-judge for subjective outputs; use binary pass/fail, not Likert scales.

6. **Evals Are the New PRDs** — Well-crafted eval prompts become living product requirements documents that continuously test AI in real time.

7. **Minimal Ongoing Investment** — After initial setup (~30 min/week to maintain).

**Key Quote (Shreya Shankar):** *"To build great AI products, you need to be really good at building evals. It's the highest ROI activity you can engage in."*

**Key Quote (Hamel Husain):** *"Evals are the most important thing to get an agent working, yet no one is doing it."*

This episode also addressed the Claude Code "no evals" controversy, the distinction between dogfooding and systematic evaluation, and the importance of evaluating AI products beyond subjective "vibes."
