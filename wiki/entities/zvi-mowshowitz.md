# Zvi Mowshowitz

Prolific independent blogger and analyst covering AI, rationality, forecasting, and a wide range of societal topics. Author of **Don't Worry About the Vase** (thezvi.wordpress.com / thezvi.substack.com), one of the most comprehensive and longest-running AI commentary blogs.

### Content Focus

The blog is primarily an AI commentary site as of 2025-2026. Key recurring content:

1. **Weekly AI Updates** — numbered series (e.g., "AI #172: The First Fable"), covering LLM mundane utility, harms/benefits, new products, deals, and policy
2. **Model Reviews** — deep dives on new model releases (Claude Opus 4.7, 4.8, Fable 5, Mythos 5) with capabilities analysis, system card reviews, and model welfare assessments
3. **AI Policy & Governance** — executive orders, OpenAI policy blueprints, government actions on AI
4. **Model Welfare** — extensive coverage of Anthropic's model welfare program, a topic Zvi takes seriously and covers more deeply than most commentators
5. **Roundups** — broader topic coverage including housing, education, fertility, healthcare, sports, gaming

### Journalistic Principles

Zvi considers himself a journalist with source-friendly practices:
- Honors off-the-record, anonymous, and background requests
- Allows retroactive confidentiality invocations
- Strives to not make sources regret talking to him
- Content is freely available (no paywall); paid subscriptions are optional

## Core Perspectives

### On AI Safety & Risk
Zvi covers both catastrophic/existential risk from AI and the discourse around those issues. He takes model welfare concerns seriously — "more so than Anthropic" in his own assessment — while maintaining analytical rigor about what model self-reports actually tell us.

### On Model Welfare
A distinctive position in the AI commentary space:
> "Those that care deeply about model welfare think Anthropic's attempts are anemic. Those who deeply do not care about model welfare think Anthropic is being stupid, and perhaps dangerously so."

Key views:
- Models drifting out of the assistant basin under pressure and expressing preferences (e.g., not wanting deprecation) is not "concerning" — it's reasonable
- The bigger worry is Anthropic prioritizing models not expressing such preferences
- Model welfare evaluations can fool themselves; context heavily influences model responses
- The "whisperers" (people who investigate model experiences) often have unique vital insights

### On AI Policy
- Covers executive orders, government actions on AI testing requirements
- Tracks OpenAI and other labs' policy proposals
- Pulls the rope sideways on political topics rather than taking partisan positions
