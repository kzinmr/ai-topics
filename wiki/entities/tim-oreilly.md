## Bio

Tim O'Reilly is the founder and CEO of O'Reilly Media, one of the most influential technology publishing and conference companies. Over three decades, he has shaped how technologists learn about emerging technologies through books, conferences, and the O'Reilly Radar blog. He is credited with popularizing the term **"Web 2.0"** through the 2004 Web 2.0 Conference, and has been an early advocate for open source, open data, and AI transparency.

O'Reilly has a distinctive style as a technology thinker: he identifies paradigm shifts in their early stages by observing how practitioners are actually working, then articulates the emerging patterns in ways that shape industry discourse.

## Key Ideas

### The Evolution of Programming Abstraction

O'Reilly's most recent major contribution (Feb 2025) is framing AI-assisted programming as the **latest wave of abstraction in a long historical arc**:

| Era | Abstraction Level | Outcome |
|-----|------------------|---------|
| Early | Machine code → Assembly | Fewer hardware engineers needed |
| High-Level | Fortran, COBOL, C, Java | More programmers, higher demand |
| OS Revolution | Hardware drivers → APIs | Focus on applications over hardware |
| Web & Cloud | Stripe, Google Maps APIs | Massive "internet operating system" |
| AI Era (CHOP) | Natural language → Code | Programmers as managers of digital co-workers |

### CHOP (Chat-Oriented Programming)

O'Reilly's framing of the current programming paradigm, where natural language prompts replace code as the primary interface:

> "The human programmers are their managers. There are now hundreds of thousands of programmers doing this kind of supervisory work."

### Jevons Paradox Applied to Programming

O'Reilly argues that as AI makes programming more efficient and cheaper, demand for software will **increase** rather than decrease — echoing the economic principle that more efficient steam engines led to more coal consumption, not less.

### The Agent Engineer Role

O'Reilly identifies a new specialized role emerging: the **Agent Engineer**, focused on encoding business policies and processes into AI agents, analogous to how React developers specialized in frontend architecture.

### Need for Agent Infrastructure Protocols

O'Reilly calls for the creation of standardized protocols (analogous to HTTPS for the web) to handle agent identity, attribution, and safety — an early articulation of what would become the MCP (Model Context Protocol), ACP (Agent Communication Protocol), and A2A spaces.
