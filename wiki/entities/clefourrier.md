## Overview

Clémentine Fourrier is a research scientist who, between 2023 and 2025, **led Hugging Face's evaluation efforts and collaborations** on LLMs and AI agents. She is one of the most influential figures in the open-source AI evaluation ecosystem, responsible for building and maintaining the tools, benchmarks, and leaderboards that the broader ML community relies on to measure model capabilities.

Her team's most famous projects include **LightEval** (all-in-one LLM evaluation toolkit), the **LLM Evaluation Guidebook** (comprehensive open-source reference), and the **Open LLM Leaderboard** (community-standard ranking). She also co-created the **GAIA benchmark** with Meta for general AI assistants.

Before LLMs, she researched **historical word reconstruction using neural methods** (PhD, PSL/Sorbonne), worked on predicting neurodegenerative diseases, and investigated graph ML. She contributed to **EtymDB** (LREC 2020) and **CopperMT** (ACL 2021 Findings).

In October 2025, she was named to **H2O.ai's "AI 100"** and received the **2025 "French Innovators" Award** from *Le Point*. She is currently on sabbatical until December 2026.

## Core Ideas & Philosophy

See [[clefourrier--core-ideas]] for a deep dive into Fourrier's key ideas, including:

- **Reproducible Evaluation Is Non-Negotiable** — Few-shot prompt ordering can shift scores by 3 points; scripts must be re-runnable
- **Rankings vs. Scores** — Neither automated benchmarks nor human-judged arenas provide stable rankings under minor perturbations
- **Three Pillars of LLM Evaluation** — Automated benchmarking, human judges, and model-as-a-judge
- **We Cannot Evaluate "General Capabilities" Yet** — The field hasn't defined what that means
- **GAIA: Real-World Tasks** — Benchmark where humans score 92% but GPT-4 with tools only ~15%
- **CO₂ Transparency** — Analyzing emissions of 3,000+ evaluated models
- **Smol Training Playbook** — Open-source guide to training small language models
