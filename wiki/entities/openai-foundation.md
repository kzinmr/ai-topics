# OpenAI Foundation

The OpenAI Foundation is the nonprofit arm of [[entities/openai|OpenAI]], established to ensure artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity. It operates philanthropic programs, grant-making initiatives, and AI safety research funding. The Foundation was formally announced as a distinct entity in March 2026 with Bret Taylor as Chair of the Board of Directors.

## Mission

"Our mission is to ensure artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity." The Foundation aims to enable AI solutions for humanity's hardest problems while working with partners to prepare for new challenges and build societal resilience.

## Key Programs

### People-First AI Fund
A **$50 million** fund launched in September 2025 to support nonprofits and mission-driven organizations at the intersection of innovation and public good. First wave (December 2025): **$40.5 million** in unrestricted grants to **208 community-based organizations** across the US. Second wave (June 2026): **$9.5 million** for AI-driven impact in health, journalism, and community services. An additional **$50 million** committed for 2026, with applications re-opening June 15, 2026. See [[concepts/people-first-ai-fund]].

### AI for Alzheimer's
More than **$100 million** in grants across **six research institutions** to accelerate Alzheimer's research using AI. Five-layer research stack: (1) causal mapping using AI (Arc Institute), (2) AI-assisted drug design (Institute for Protein Design, David Baker), (3) biomarker discovery, (4) clinical collaboration (UCSF), (5) off-patent treatment testing (Harvard, Bruce Yankner).

### Economic Futures
**$250 million** initial commitment to building secure and economic futures. Three pillars: understanding the shift (measurement infrastructure), supporting the transition (worker resourcing, government capacity), building long-term economic security (taxation reform, sovereign wealth, distribution mechanisms). Led by Divya Siddarth and Wojciech Zaremba.

### AI Resilience
More than **$130 million** in grants through the AI Resilience program. Four focus areas: biosecurity (pathogen-agnostic prevention/detection/defense), cybersecurity, AI model safety (independent evaluation, alignment science), and AI impact on young people.

## Broader Financial Commitment
- **$1 billion** across several programs over the next year
- **$25 billion** long-term commitment

## Related Grantees

- **Dollar For** (Jared Walker) — Hospital financial assistance, $150M+ medical debt eliminated
- **Every Cure** (Dr. David Fajgenbaum) — AI-assisted drug repurposing
- **CareMessage** — Patient engagement for low-income populations, 22M people reached
- **OCHIN** — Digital tools for community healthcare, 2,200+ sites across 42 states
- **Lenfest Institute for Journalism** — AI training for local newsrooms (with Axios Media)
- **Arc Institute** (Patrick Hsu, Silvana Konermann) — Alzheimer's causal mapping
- **Institute for Protein Design** (David Baker, Nobel Laureate) — AI protein design for Alzheimer's
- **UCSF** (S. Andrew Josephson) — Clinical Alzheimer's research
- **Harvard Medical School** (Bruce Yankner) — Lithium orotate research for Alzheimer's

## Relationship to OpenAI

The Foundation is legally separate from [[entities/openai|OpenAI Inc.]] but closely connected. It was established as part of OpenAI's restructuring from a capped-profit to a public benefit corporation structure. The Foundation receives resources from OpenAI and operates independently with its own board and leadership.
