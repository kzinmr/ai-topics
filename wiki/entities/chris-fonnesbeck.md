## Overview

Chris Fonnesbeck is a **Bayesian statistician, probabilistic programming expert, and veteran sports analytics consultant**. He is a core developer of **PyMC** (the leading probabilistic programming framework in Python) and a Principal Quantitative Analyst at **PyMC Labs**, the Bayesian consulting firm co-founded with Thomas Wiecki.

His career spans academia, industry, and professional sports. At **Vanderbilt University Medical Center**, he teaches advanced statistical computing and biostatistics. In **Major League Baseball**, he has served as a quantitative analyst for the **Philadelphia Phillies**, **New York Yankees**, and **Milwaukee Brewers**, applying Bayesian methods to player evaluation, player development, pitch design, and defensive positioning.

Fonnesbeck is a prolific educator: his open-source course materials on Bayesian computing, MCMC, and Gaussian processes (published on GitHub) have thousands of stars and have been used by data scientists worldwide. He is a regular speaker at PyData conferences, teaching hands-on tutorials on probabilistic programming with PyMC.

## Bio

### PyMC & Probabilistic Programming

Fonnesbeck is one of the original core developers of **PyMC**, having co-authored the foundational papers:

- *"PyMC: Bayesian stochastic modelling in Python"* (Patil, Huard, Fonnesbeck, 2010, *Journal of Statistical Software*)
- *"Probabilistic programming in Python using PyMC3"* (Salvatier, Wiecki, Fonnesbeck, 2016, *PeerJ Computer Science*)
- *"PyMC: a modern, and comprehensive probabilistic programming framework in Python"* (Abril-Pla et al., 2023)

He continues to contribute to PyMC's development and teaches probabilistic programming workshops internationally.

### Sports Analytics

Fonnesbeck has applied Bayesian statistical methods across multiple MLB organizations. His talk *"Beyond Moneyball: The Role of Data Science in Baseball Operations"* (UVA School of Data Science, 2023) outlined how modern baseball R&D uses statistical modeling for:

- Player evaluation and draft decisions
- Player development trajectory modeling
- Pitch design optimization
- Defensive positioning
- In-game strategic decisions

His work spans the **Phillies** (current), **Yankees**, and **Brewers**, making him one of the most experienced quantitative analysts in professional sports.

### Academic Teaching

At Vanderbilt, Fonnesbeck teaches graduate-level courses in statistical computing and biostatistics:

- **Bios8366**: Advanced Statistical Computing — 550+ GitHub stars
- **Bios6301**: Introduction to Statistical Computing
- **Bayes_Computing_Course**: Bayesian computing methods

His open-source teaching materials are widely used beyond Vanderbilt and have become de facto references for learning Bayesian methods in Python.

## Key Philosophy

Fonnesbeck brings a **statistical rigor** perspective to computational work. His Bayesian background emphasizes:

- **Uncertainty quantification** — point estimates are insufficient; posterior distributions matter
- **Prior elicitation** — thoughtful specification of prior knowledge, mirroring his Socratic review approach
- **Assumption checking** — the ease of getting answers from computational tools (including agents) masks the difficulty of verification

He is skeptical of black-box approaches and advocates for workflows where assumptions are explicit and verifiable.

## Show Us Your (Agent) Skills Episode 4 (2026-05-29)

Appeared as a guest on the Season 1 finale of Hugo Bowne-Anderson and Thomas Wiecki's live series. Fonnesbeck brought Bayesian rigor to the agent workflow discussion:

- **Agents are seductive and dangerous**: The ease of getting an answer from an agent masks the difficulty of verifying it. Statistical thinking — quantifying uncertainty, checking assumptions — is more critical with agents, not less.
- **Switched to Pi TUI**: Adopted Pi, a terminal user interface agent that can rewrite its own source code. The self-modifying capability fundamentally changes the trust model — the tool you're using can change itself.
- **Socratic Review / Grill-Me Skill**: Pi's "Grill-Me" skill performs Socratic dialogue on the author's code, asking probing questions that expose weak assumptions. This mirrors Fonnesbeck's Bayesian approach: prior elicitation through structured questioning.
- **Zed as agent multiplexer**: Uses the Zed editor as the coordination hub for multiple agents, integrating marimo notebooks, vim keybindings, and Rust tooling.
- **Left VS Code for Zed**: The shift was driven by agent workflow needs — Zed's speed and multi-agent support matter more than VS Code's extension ecosystem.
- **Half-flat distributions live demo**: Demonstrated a Bayesian prior distribution (half-flat) live — showing the statistical machinery that underpins his agent-assisted decision-making.
