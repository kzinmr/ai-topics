# Yoav Goldberg

Yoav Goldberg is a prominent NLP researcher, known for his work on the theoretical foundations of neural language models, syntax in neural networks, and practical NLP education. He is the author of the widely-used textbook *Neural Network Methods for Natural Language Processing* (2017, Morgan & Claypool). He is best known for the "Learning LLMs in 2025" curated curriculum guide.

## Key Contributions

- **Neural Network Methods for NLP** — The standard textbook bridging classical NLP and modern neural approaches
- **[Learning LLMs in 2025](https://gist.githubusercontent.com/yoavg/95bbc5768cacd2bf07187779fada4867/raw/bcb8ff287e03a7e49f7ae5daedc03d04c11d8a71/llm-materials-2025.md)** — Curated curriculum of academic materials for learning LLMs beyond ML algorithms
- Research on provable limitations of learning meaning from ungrounded form (with Merrill, Schwartz, Smith)
- Research on syntactic knowledge in neural network language models

## Curriculum Curation

Yoav's "Learning LLMs in 2025" guide is a meta-knowledge resource that collects academic course materials focused on the **non-ML-algorithmic aspects** of LLMs — architecture, data, alignment, evaluation, safety, and human-centered design. The guide targets learners who already understand transformers and basic ML/DL.
