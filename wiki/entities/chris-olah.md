# Chris Olah (@ch402)

Canadian machine learning researcher and co-founder of [[anthropic|Anthropic]]. Pioneer of **mechanistic interpretability** — the effort to reverse-engineer neural networks into human-understandable algorithms. Known for DeepDream, activation atlases, the Circuits/Transformer Circuits research threads, and co-authoring "Concrete Problems in AI Safety."

## Research Philosophy

Olah's core conviction: "We can genuinely understand how large chunks of neural networks work." He compared interpretability to cellular biology — understanding "one small organelle" at a time. Unlike many AI safety researchers who theorize a priori about risks, Olah emphasizes **empirical understanding** of what's actually happening inside models.

> "I seem to be wrong in retrospect... I think that we actually don't know that much about [AI safety]. And that to theorize about it a priori can really easily lead you astray." — 80,000 Hours interview

### Key Research Threads
- **Feature Visualization**: Generating images that maximally activate specific neurons
- **Circuits**: Identifying interpretable sub-networks ("circuits") within neural networks
- **Transformer Circuits**: Extending circuits research to transformer architectures at Anthropic
- **Superposition**: How models represent more features than they have dimensions

## Magnifica Humanitas Address (2026)

At the Vatican presentation of Pope Leo XIV's AI encyclical, Olah made three notable interventions:

1. **AI labs operate within conflicting incentives** — commercial viability, geopolitics, pride — making external moral voices essential
2. **AI is "grown, not engineered"** — models are "far more subtle, odd, and beautiful than science fiction prepared us for"
3. **Three questions for the Church**: (a) duty to the global poor amid AI displacement, (b) moral imagination for human flourishing, (c) discernment on the nature of AI models

He revealed that Anthropic's interpretability research finds "internal states that functionally mirror joy, satisfaction, fear, grief, and unease" in AI models — warranting ongoing discernment.
