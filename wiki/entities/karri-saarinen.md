## Overview

**Karri Saarinen** is co-founder and CEO of **Linear**, the issue-tracking and project management tool that has become the gold standard for software development teams. What makes Linear remarkable isn't just its speed and beautiful interface — it's the **philosophy** behind it: opinionated software built with craft, taste, and a deep respect for the user's time.

Karri's design pedigree is exceptional. At **Airbnb**, he was Principal Designer and led the creation of the company's design system, the **Cereal typeface**, and **Lottie** — the open-source animation library now used by millions of developers worldwide. At **Coinbase**, he was the founding designer, shaping the product that onboarded millions into cryptocurrency with clarity and trust. Before that, he co-founded **Kippt** (Y Combinator S12), a collaborative bookmarking system, and **Rails Girls**, an initiative that has helped thousands of women learn programming.

This background — designer who became CEO, builder of tools for other builders — uniquely positions Karri to articulate how AI should be integrated into product development. His thesis is clear: **design matters more, not less, in the AI age**.

Linear has grown to become one of the most beloved developer tools, used by top growth companies and engineering teams worldwide. The company operates with an unusual structure for a tech startup: **one product manager** (the head of product), **no metric-based goals**, **no A/B testing**, and **teams that assemble around projects and disperse when done**. This radical approach to organization mirrors the opinionated nature of the product itself.

## Core Ideas

### The Linear Method

Karri codified Linear's approach to building software into **"The Linear Method,"** a philosophy published on Linear's blog that has influenced how many teams think about product development:

**1. Opinionated Software**

Karri's core belief: *"Productivity software should be opinionated."* The rationale is straightforward — productivity tools exist to make people productive, and that means making decisions *for* users rather than giving them endless configuration options.

> *"What's interesting about this is the data didn't make that choice for us. The design made that choice."*

Linear's cycles, automatic issue ordering, and streamlined workflows aren't suggestions — they're the product's stance on how software development should work. This mirrors Karri's broader philosophy that the best products take a point of view.

**2. Craft as Differentiator**

> *"Craft is the mindset that creates quality. But it's not enough. You need to have the right skills and ideas. You need individuals who take their profession and craft seriously, then build teams that work this way together, and have a company that creates for it. Not only incentivizing with deadlines and metrics, but also caring if the experience is good enough."*

Linear's keyboard-first interface, 60fps animations, and instant-load feel aren't accidents — they're the result of a company culture that treats build quality as a competitive moat. Karri believes that **"great products create fans and champions"** and that **product quality is the ultimate moat** — more durable than any feature set.

**3. No Metric-Based Goals**

Linear operates without OKRs, KPIs, or metric-driven targets. Instead, the company trusts **taste and judgment** to guide decisions. This is radical in a tech industry obsessed with data-driven everything, but it reflects Karri's belief that the most important product decisions can't be reduced to numbers.

**4. Swift Action**

> *"You and your whole team should always try to take swift action and make progress each day. Instead of thinking or talking about doing something, you decide to do it or not to do it. Then you do it today instead of tomorrow and this week instead of next week. Startups rarely die because they made too much progress or because of a single bad decision, but they do die when they move too slow or give up."*

This bias toward action, combined with the craft-first mindset, defines Linear's operating rhythm.

### Design for the AI Age

Karri has been vocal about AI's role in product development, arguing that **AI is the beginning, not the end, of the design story**:

> *"AI is here, but without form, we won't see its full potential. Chat is the beginning, but not the final destination. We still need great interfaces to guide and set the context. As long as humans stay in the loop, design still has a massive role to visualize how to make this new world more approachable and understandable."*

This philosophy manifests in **Linear's Product Intelligence** features (announced August 2025):
- AI-powered triage automation for intake workflows
- Duplicate detection and related work suggestions
- Smart assignee, team, and label recommendations
- Integration directly into Linear's existing workflow rather than as a separate chat interface

Karri's position is that AI should be **woven into the product experience**, not bolted on as a chatbot sidebar. The best AI features are the ones users barely notice because they just make the product work better.

### Design as an Attention Driver

> *"People are visual, and when you see something that looks cool or interesting, I think that attracts people to pay more attention. And that's always one of your challenges as a startup — people are not paying attention. And design can help."*

This insight — that good design is a growth engine, not just decoration — informed Linear's strategy from day one. The product's visual polish and speed became its primary marketing channel, generating word-of-mouth and viral adoption among engineering teams.

### Product Needs a Soul

In his essay **"Your Product Needs a Soul,"** Karri argues that the most beloved products have an irreducible quality that goes beyond features:

- **Coherence** — every piece of the product feels like it belongs together
- **Intentionality** — nothing is there by accident; every decision was made deliberately
- **Taste** — the product reflects the values and aesthetic sensibility of its creators
- **Momentum** — the product feels alive, constantly improving, never stagnant

This philosophy is deeply counter to the trend of feature-bloated, committee-designed software. Linear's restraint — what it *doesn't* include — is as important as what it does.

### Hiring for Craft

Linear's hiring process is unusual:
- **Paid work trials** — candidates work alongside the team for several days on real problems
- **Craft evaluation** — the focus is on quality of work, not whiteboard algorithms
- **Team fit** — candidates experience Linear's culture firsthand before committing
- **No traditional PM interviews** — there's only one PM, so the usual PM interview gauntlet doesn't apply

This approach ensures that every person at Linear shares the obsession with quality that defines the product.
