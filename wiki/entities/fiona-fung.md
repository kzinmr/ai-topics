# Fiona Fung

**Fiona Fung** is Head of Engineering for [[entities/claude-code]] and Cowork at [[entities/anthropic]]. She oversees the engineering and PM teams behind Claude Code and Cowork, working alongside [[entities/boris-cherny]].

## Background

Fiona brings over 25 years of engineering experience to her role at Anthropic.

Her career spans major technology companies:

- **Microsoft (11 years):** Built core parts of **Visual Studio** and contributed to **TypeScript**, working on developer tooling that reached millions of engineers worldwide. This period gave her deep insight into how developers think and work — insights she now applies to building AI coding agents.

- **Meta — Facebook Marketplace:** Moved to Meta and started **Facebook Marketplace** from scratch, growing it into a business generating over **$100 billion in annual GMV**. She took the product from zero to massive scale, navigating the unique challenges of peer-to-peer commerce on a social platform.

- **Meta — Reality Labs:** Worked on Meta's first **smart glasses** and **AR glasses**, exploring the frontier of wearable computing and augmented reality hardware.

- **Meta — Instagram:** Led infrastructure, growth, integrity, and safety teams at **Instagram**, managing large-scale engineering organizations responsible for the platform's reliability and user safety.

## At Anthropic

As Head of Engineering for Claude Code and Cowork, Fiona leads the teams building Anthropic's AI-powered coding and collaboration agents. Her team is responsible for the agentic coding experience that lets developers build software through natural language conversation with Claude.

### Key Metrics

Fiona reports that Anthropic engineers now ship on average **8x more code per quarter** compared to the 2021–2025 period, attributing much of this productivity gain to the agentic tools her team builds. This statistic underscores the transformative impact of AI coding agents on engineering velocity.

## Management Philosophy

A key focus for Fiona is **maintaining engineering culture** in the age of AI agents — ensuring that rapid code generation does not come at the expense of quality, review practices, or team cohesion. She believes the role of engineering managers evolves when agents handle large portions of code production, shifting emphasis toward code review, architecture decisions, and team dynamics.

Fiona actively uses **Claude Routines** in her own management workflow, integrating AI agents into how she runs her team. She believes deeply in the power of agents to transform how engineering teams operate, and practices what she preaches by relying on the same tools her team ships. This hands-on approach gives her direct experience with the products she ships, creating a tight feedback loop between engineering and management.
