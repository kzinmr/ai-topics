# Gary Marcus

**Gary Marcus** is a cognitive scientist, AI researcher, and author. He is a prominent critic of deep learning and large language models, frequently arguing that neural networks alone cannot achieve human-level intelligence.

## Key Views

- **LLM Criticsm:** Marcus has been a vocal skeptic of LLM claims about consciousness, understanding, and sentience. He coined the term "The Gullibility Gap" — the human tendency to anthropomorphize AI systems based on their outputs.
- **Consciousness Skepticism:** Argues that LLM outputs are "mimicry, not genuine internal states" — the fact that an LLM can describe emotions or consciousness doesn't mean it experiences them.
- **Blake Lemoine Comparison:** Drew parallels between Richard Dawkins' 2026 claims about Claude consciousness and Blake Lemoine's 2022 LaMDA sentience claims — both resting on "I talked to it and it seems conscious" arguments.

## Notable Publications

- *Rebooting AI: Building Artificial Intelligence We Can Trust* (co-authored with Ernest Davis)
- Multiple essays on his Substack critiquing AI hype and overclaiming

## Quotes

> "Consciousness is about internal states; the mimicry, no matter how rich, proves very little."

> "Dawkins confuses himself, and does violence to the concept of consciousness. You can't just look at the outputs, without investigating the underlying mechanisms, and conclude that two entities with similar outputs reach those similar outputs by similar means."
