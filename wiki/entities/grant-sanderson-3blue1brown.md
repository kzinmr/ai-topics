# Grant Sanderson (3Blue1Brown)

**Grant Sanderson** is a mathematician and math educator, best known as the creator and host of the popular YouTube channel **3Blue1Brown** (8.3M+ subscribers, 747M+ total views, 235+ videos). He is a Stanford graduate in mathematics and computer science, and the creator of **Manim** (Mathematical Animation Engine), an open-source Python library for programmatic math visualization.

## Overview

Sanderson studied math and computer science at Stanford University. After graduating, he worked for Khan Academy producing videos, articles, and exercises—primarily focused on multivariable calculus. Since the end of 2016, his primary focus has been on 3Blue1Brown and its associated projects.

The channel name "3Blue1Brown" is a self-reference to the color of Sanderson's right eye, which has sectoral heterochromia — blue with a wedge of brown.

In 2020, Sanderson co-created and lectured for an MIT course on **Introduction to Computational Thinking** alongside Alan Edelman, David Sanders, James Schloss, and Benoit Forget. The course uses the Julia programming language and Manim-based animations to cover convolutions, image processing, COVID-19 data visualization, epidemic modelling, ray tracing, climate modelling, and ocean modelling.

He has also contributed to a Netflix documentary about infinity and writes for Quanta Magazine.

### Neural Networks Coverage

Sanderson's visual breakdown of **backpropagation** and **gradient descent** (2017) remains one of the most accessible explanations of these foundational ML algorithms. In 2025–2026, he has given public lectures connecting high-dimensional geometry to modern machine learning — explaining the linear algebra and multidimensional calculus that allow neural networks to navigate abstract representation spaces and predict the next token.

## Manim (Mathematical Animation Engine)

**Manim** is a cross-platform, free and open-source animation engine released under the MIT License, initially developed by Grant Sanderson in early 2015. It provides a Python library for creating precise, programmatic animations of mathematical concepts.

The **Manim Community** ([ManimCommunity/manim](https://github.com/ManimCommunity/manim)) forked the original project and maintains a better-documented, more beginner-friendly version that has become the standard for educational math animations.

Manim is used not only for 3Blue1Brown videos but has been adopted by educators worldwide for creating animated math lessons.
