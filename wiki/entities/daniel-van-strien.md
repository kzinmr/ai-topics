# Daniel van Strien — Machine Learning Librarian at Hugging Face

**Daniel van Strien** (handles: `@vanstriendaniel` on X, `davanstrien` on GitHub/HuggingFace) is a **Machine Learning Librarian at Hugging Face**, where he focuses on datasets and building bridges between machine learning and the cultural heritage sector. He is the world's first "Machine Learning Librarian" — a role that bridges the gap between library practice and ML engineering.

## Overview

Van Strien's career uniquely spans traditional librarianship, digital humanities, and modern ML engineering. He started in libraries and digital humanities, working as a **Digital Curator at the British Library** on the **Living with Machines** project — an interdisciplinary collaboration using data science to study the impact of mechanization in 19th-century Britain. He later supported open access in academic libraries before moving into AI.

At Hugging Face, van Strien focuses on the **foundation of ML: data and datasets**. He works on improving the Hugging Face Hub as a platform for sharing datasets, building tools for dataset curation, and helping libraries/archives/museums (GLAM sector) use AI on their actual collections.

## Key Contributions

### Datasets and Data Curation at Hugging Face

At Hugging Face, van Strien works on the core infrastructure for datasets — improving how the Hub handles dataset discovery, curation, and quality:

- **Datasets library contributor** — Improving dataset loading, processing, and sharing
- **Dataset curation tools** — Building tooling for creating and maintaining high-quality datasets
- **Synthetic datasets** — Created `awesome-synthetic-datasets`, a curated list of synthetic text datasets (330+ stars)
- **Data for fine-tuning LLMs** — Resources and guides for dataset creation in LLM fine-tuning pipelines

### GLAM + AI Bridge

Van Strien's most distinctive contribution is his work connecting **GLAM (Galleries, Libraries, Archives, Museums)** institutions with modern ML/AI:

- **"AI Design Patterns for Information Professionals"** — A comprehensive guide for cultural heritage professionals on applying AI to collections at scale
- **fastai4GLAMS** — A study group for applying the fastai deep learning course to GLAM settings
- **Living with Machines** — Digital curation at the British Library for one of the largest digital humanities projects in the UK

## Philosophy

> "The tools are built from among developers, not over them."

Van Strien's approach is defined by his unique position as a librarian-turned-ML-engineer:
- **Practical sustainability**: ML tools for GLAM must be maintainable, not one-off projects
- **Collection-scale thinking**: ML approaches must work at the scale of real collections, not toy datasets
- **Cross-disciplinary bridge**: Effective AI adoption requires translating between domain experts and ML practitioners
- **Open data ethos**: Datasets should be shared, curated, and documented like scholarly resources
