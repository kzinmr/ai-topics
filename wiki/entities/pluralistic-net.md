## Core Ideas

Cory Doctorow is one of the most influential tech-policy thinkers of his generation. His work connects **antitrust theory, copyright law, digital rights, and platform economics** into a unified critique of how technology concentrates power. His core methodology is the **Memex Method** — maintaining 50,000+ public notes that coalesce into essays, books, and novels. He practices **POSSE** (Publish on Own Site, Syndicate Everywhere), refusing to let any single platform control his audience.

### Enshittification

Doctorow coined the term **"enshittification"** in January 2023 to describe a three-stage pattern of platform decay:

1. **Surplus to users** — Platforms are "good to their users" to build network effects and lock-in. Think early Facebook, Amazon, or Google.
2. **Surplus to business customers** — Once users are locked in, the platform degrades the user experience to attract and lock in business customers (advertisers, sellers, developers).
3. **Surplus to shareholders** — Once business customers are locked in, the platform raids them too, harvesting all remaining value for executives and shareholders, leaving a "giant pile of shit."

The key insight: enshittification is not inevitable market failure. It is the **predictable outcome of platforms that can unilaterally change terms** without user consent — a power enabled by regulatory capture and anti-circumvention laws.

> *"Enshittification is not the result of your failure to grasp that 'if you're not paying for the product, you are the product.'"*

### Adversarial Interoperability

A central pillar of Doctorow's theory. **Adversarial interoperability** occurs when competitors build products that interoperate with dominant platforms *without permission*. This is how markets stayed competitive before: third-party ink cartridges, compatible printer paper, alternative social media clients.

Tech monopolies killed this through:
- **DMCA §1201** (anti-circumvention) — criminalizes bypassing access controls on devices you own
- **ToS enforcement** — non-compete and tortious interference claims
- **IP maximization** — wrapping products in overlapping layers of patents, copyrights, and trademarks

> *"All these former pirates have turned admiral and use it to stop anyone from doing unto them as they did unto anyone else."*

### Chokepoint Capitalism

Developed with Rebecca Giblin, **chokepoint capitalism** describes markets where intermediaries position themselves as unavoidable bottlenecks between creators and audiences, workers and employers, buyers and sellers. They extract rents by controlling the chokepoint, not by adding value.

Examples: Amazon's book marketplace, Spotify's music streaming, Apple's App Store, YouTube's creator economy. The chokepoint holder can unilaterally change terms, suppress prices paid to creators, and block competitors.

### Anti-Circumvention & Right to Repair

Doctorow views anti-circumvention law as the **epicenter of the enshittogenic policy universe**. DMCA §1201 and its international equivalents make it a crime (up to 5 years prison, $500,000 fine) to modify code behind an access control — regardless of whether you're infringing copyright.

This enables:
- **Parts pairing / VIN locking** — manufacturers prevent independent repair
- **Jailbreaking bans** — users cannot modify devices they own
- **Right to repair obstruction** — legislation is toothless without anti-circumvention repeal

> *"Anticircumvention is a way for legislatures to outsource law-making to corporations... It's a way for a corporation to threaten you with prison if you don't use your property in the way they want you to."*

### Disenshittification

The counter-strategy to enshittification. Doctorow argues that **disenshittification** requires:

1. **Legalizing circumvention** — repeal DMCA §1201 and equivalent laws
2. **Building interoperable alternatives** — adversarial interoperability as competitive strategy
3. **Self-hosting / POSSE** — retain control over your own publishing and distribution
4. **Coalition building** — unite digital rights advocates, technologists, national security hawks, and labor

> *"Disclosure is the difference between knowledge and ignorance. Openness is the difference between dying of mercury poisoning and discovering medicine."*

### AI & Understaffing

Doctorow views current AI deployments not as technological progress but as **enshittification of labor**. His analysis connects AI adoption to systematic understaffing:

- Companies reduce headcount, shift costs to customers and remaining workers
- AI customer service is the logical endpoint: avoid paying for human labor entirely
- *"We did this with AI"* has become synonymous with *"We don't care if this is done well"*
- The AI bubble is fueled by a fantasy of bypassing workers, ignoring that technical breakthroughs don't guarantee functional products


### AI Pascal's Wager & ドゥーマー議論

2026年4月、DoctorowはAIドゥーマー（AI存在論的リスク論者）に対する批判的立場を明確化した。

#### Pascal's WagerとしてのAI安全性支出
- **$1.4T**が既に「AI超知能」追求に費やされた
- Sam Altmanはさらに**$2-3T**を要求（米国連邦予算の約6ヶ月分）
- Elon Muskはダイソン球構築（太陽系解体）を提案
- **「どうやって敗北を知るのか？」** — AI神が出現しない場合、支出に終わりがなく自己永続化する

#### Lawzeroとの部分的共通点
Yoshua Bengio（チューリング賞受賞者）が設立した**Lawzero**イニシアチブ：
- 透明で監査可能な「デジタル公共財」としてのAI開発
- Doctorowは目標は尊重するが脅威ベクトルに同意しない
- 将来のAIリスクではなく、**現在の企業独占**が実存的脅威

#### 真の脅威：企業という「人工生命体」
> *「私を心配させる人工生命体は仮説的なものではない — すでに我々の中に存在する。それらは有限責任株式会社と呼ばれる」*

- 7社がS&P 500の時価総額の**35%**を支配
- AIバブル崩壊→財政緊縮→政治的ファシズムの連鎖リスク
- 地理的キルスイッチ：米国プラットフォーム（Office365, iOS, Android）の停止で他国を機能停止可能
- **「AIは文明の壁に塗り込むアスベスト。子孫が何世代もかけて除去することになる」**

#### ポスト・アメリカン・インターネット
Doctorowが提案する解決策：
1. 現在の企業テック独占を解体するオープン・監査可能・透明なプラットフォーム構築
2. 「エンシッティフィケーション耐性」のあるファームウェアとデバイス開発
3. 米国中心の「エンシッティネット」からの体系的移行
4. ドゥーマーへの「サイドベット」提案：現在の脅威に対処するインフラが将来のAIリスクにも使える


### The Memex Method & Open Licensing

Doctorow publishes **all content under CC BY 4.0**, encouraging republication by anyone, including commercial outlets. Wired republished his enshittification essay. This is intentional: spread ideas widely, retain message integrity, refuse platform dependency.

His **Memex Method** — maintaining 50,000+ public notes in a searchable database — serves as both personal knowledge management and public intellectual work. Notes are intentionally readable by others, enforcing rigor and enabling others to build on his ideas.
