## Overview

Doug Turnbull (known online as **@softwaredoug**) is a **search relevance engineer, author, and consultant** who has been one of the most influential voices in the information retrieval and search engineering community since ~2013. He is the co-author of two landmark books: ***Relevant Search: With Applications for Solr and Elasticsearch*** (Manning, 2016) and ***AI-Powered Search*** (Manning, 2025).

His career spans the full arc of modern search evolution — from traditional lexical search engines (Solr, Elasticsearch) through learning-to-rank, vector/embedding search, and into agentic AI-powered retrieval. He has held senior engineering roles at **Reddit** (Principal Engineer), **Spotify** (Staff Relevance Engineer), **Shopify**, and **Daydream** (Principal Engineer), and spent ~8 years as **CTO of OpenSource Connections**, a search relevance consultancy.

His defining philosophy is that **search relevance is an engineering discipline, not magic** — it can be systematically improved through measurement, test collections, and iterative refinement.

## Core Ideas & Philosophy

See [[doug-turnbull-core-ideas]] for a deep dive into Turnbull's key ideas, including:

- **Search Relevance as Engineering** — systematic measurement and iteration over mysticism
- **Judgment List Methodology** — curated query/relevance pairs as test suites, training data, and alignment tools
- **From Keyword to Agentic Search** — tracking the industry through four paradigm shifts
- **Learning to Rank** — ML-based ranking with the Elasticsearch LTR plugin
- **RAG Isn't a Vector Search Problem** — classical IR as the missing piece in modern RAG systems
- **Query Understanding > Ranking** — domain expertise and manual rules outperform pure algorithmic approaches
- **Agents Need Simple Search** — thin APIs enable transparent agent reasoning
- **Semantic Search Without Embeddings** — hierarchical taxonomies as an alternative to vector search
- **Bayesian BM25** — probabilistic score calibration for hybrid retrieval
- **The Business Case for Search** — relevance as a revenue driver
