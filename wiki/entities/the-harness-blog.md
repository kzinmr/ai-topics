# The Harness Blog

**The Harness** is a blog focused on harness engineering, agentic AI, and AI applications in Architecture, Engineering, and Construction (AEC). Written by [[entities/theodoros-galanos|Theodoros Galanos]], it serves as a "working notebook" on the harder middle layer between model capability and reliable real-world work.

## Overview

The blog positions itself at the intersection of agentic AI and domain-specific engineering, arguing that AI capability in engineering lives not in the model alone but in harness engineering — the tools, verifiers, orchestration, and process design that make agentic work reliable.

## Relationship to Agent Harness Research

The Harness blog is one of the most focused publications on AI agent harnesses in the engineering domain. Unlike general AI agent blogs, it grounds every claim in real AEC domain tasks with concrete metrics. Its key contributions to the [[concepts/harness-engineering]] field include:

- **Lambda-RLM** — A deterministic pipeline architecture for recursive language models
- **AEC-Bench** — A domain-specific benchmark for agentic systems
- **Harness Self-Improvement** — The "Third Axis" of autonomous harness evolution
- **The Harness is All You Need** — Domain-specificity thesis backed by empirical AEC data
