# Mark McQuade

**Mark McQuade** is the Founder and CEO of [[entities/arcee-ai|Arcee AI]], a U.S.-based open-model startup. Previously, he worked at **Hugging Face** on monetization and at **Roboflow**.

## Background

Mark McQuade brings a commercial perspective to the open-model space. His time at Hugging Face (monetization team) gave him insight into how open-weight models can generate revenue, and his experience at Roboflow (computer vision platform) shaped his understanding of enterprise AI deployments.

## Arcee AI Leadership

As CEO of Arcee AI, Mark:
- Led the company's pivot from post-training services to frontier model pretraining
- Articulated the business case for open-weight models: $20M total training cost for Trinity Large, competitive with what frontier labs spend "just to keep the lights on"
- Positioned Arcee as one of the key responders to [[entities/nathan-lambert|Nathan Lambert]]'s **ATOM Project** call for U.S.-built open models
- Advocated for enterprise on-prem deployments as the primary monetization path for open models

## Key Philosophy

Mark has stated that **"open models built in the U.S."** are strategically important because:
1. China is currently leading in open model releases (Qwen, Kimi, GLM, MiniMax)
2. U.S. companies need sovereign AI infrastructure
3. Open weights + enterprise deployment is a viable business model (unlike pure research labs)
