## Overview

Eugene Yan (Ziyou) is a practitioner-scholar and one of the most influential voices on building production ML systems and applying LLMs at scale. His unconventional journey — psychology graduate who self-taught programming, rose through IBM → Lazada/Alibaba → healthtech startup → Amazon (Principal Applied Scientist) → **Anthropic (MTS)** — defines his work's practical orientation.

His blog (eugeneyan.com) features 209+ posts, 31 talks, 19 prototypes, and 420K+ words, bridging cutting-edge research with production engineering. His GitHub repositories **applied-ml** (28.7K stars) and **open-llms** (12.7K stars) are essential community resources. His newsletter reaches 11,800+ readers.

## Core Ideas & Frameworks

See [[eugene-yan--core-ideas]] for comprehensive coverage of Yan's frameworks, including:

- **Seven Patterns for LLM-Based Systems** — Evals, RAG, Fine-tuning, Prompting, UX, LLM-as-Judge, Cascade
- **Eval-Driven Development (EDD)** — Write evals first, build systems that pass them
- **The Scientific Method for AI Products** — Observe, Annotate, Hypothesize, Experiment, Measure, Iterate
- **LLM-as-Judge Survey** — Cross-Examination, Panel of LLMs, SelfCheckGPT, Fairer Preferences
- **AlignEval** — Open-source eval tool with 4-step workflow (upload, label, evaluate, optimize)
- **Product Evals in Three Steps** — Label data, align evaluator, run harness
- **Long-Context Q&A Evaluation** — Faithfulness vs. Helpfulness; dataset construction methods
- **Ghost Knowledge of Applied ML** — Practical, unwritten lessons from production
- **RecSys in the Age of LLMs** — Semantic IDs, LLM-RecSys hybrids for steerable recommendations
- **Simplicity Over Complexity** — Well-maintained simple systems over elegant complex ones
- **Self-Learning as Core Competency** — Psychology grad to Principal Applied Scientist
- **Principal IC Leadership** — Advice for senior individual contributors
