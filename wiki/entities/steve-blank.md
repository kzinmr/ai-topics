# Steve Blank

**Steve Blank** (born 1953, New York City) is an American serial entrepreneur, educator, author, and speaker who created the **Customer Development** methodology and launched the Lean Startup movement. He has spent over 30 years in the high technology industry, founding or working at eight startups — four of which went public.

## Overview

Blank's Customer Development framework fundamentally changed how startups are built, shifting the focus from execution toward hypothesis testing and iterative learning. His work is credited with launching the Lean Startup movement alongside Eric Ries. He is a consulting associate professor of entrepreneurship at Stanford University and lectures at UC Berkeley's Haas School of Business, Columbia University, and Caltech.

Before his entrepreneurial career, Blank served in the United States Air Force during the Vietnam War era.

## Career Timeline

### Entrepreneur (1978–2002)
Blank worked at eight technology startups, founding or co-founding four. His most notable venture was **E.piphany** (co-founded 1997), a marketing automation software company that went public.

### Educator & Author (2005–present)
- **2005**: Published *The Four Steps to the Epiphany* — introduced Customer Development methodology
- **2009**: Started teaching at Stanford, developed Lean LaunchPad curriculum (used by NSF I-Corps)
- **2011**: Customer Development formally credited as the foundation of the Lean Startup movement
- **2012**: Co-authored *The Startup Owner's Manual* with Bob Dorf
- **2012**: Published *Not All Those Who Wander Are Lost* (memoir/essays)
- **2010s–present**: Lectures at Stanford, UC Berkeley (Haas), Columbia, and Caltech

### Government & Defense Innovation
Blank has been deeply involved in defense innovation, co-founding the **Hacking for Defense** (H4D) program and advising on efforts to bridge Silicon Valley with the U.S. Department of Defense. His work includes involvement with **NSIN** (National Security Innovation Network) and **DIUx** (Defense Innovation Unit Experimental).

## Key Contributions

### Customer Development Methodology
A four-step framework for building startups: Customer Discovery, Customer Validation, Customer Creation, and Company Building. This replaced the traditional "build it and they will come" approach with a systematic process for finding and validating a repeatable business model.

### Lean Startup Movement
Blank's Customer Development framework, combined with Eric Ries's Lean startup principles (build-measure-learn, MVP), created the foundational toolkit for modern entrepreneurship education and practice.

### Lean LaunchPad
An experiential entrepreneurship curriculum adopted by Stanford, NSF, and over 200 universities. The NSF I-Corps program, based on this model, has trained thousands of scientists in commercialization.

### Hacking for Defense
A course and methodology teaching teams to apply Lean Startup principles to national security and public sector challenges.

## Writing & Philosophy

Blank writes prolifically at **steveblank.com** on entrepreneurship, innovation, startup methodology, and defense innovation. His essays blend memoir, practical advice, and systems thinking. Notable recurring themes include:

- **"Get out of the building"** — The imperative to test hypotheses with real customers, not research
- **"No business plan survives first contact with customers"** — The unpredictability of startups
- **"The Secret History of Silicon Valley"** — Revealing the Cold War military origins of Silicon Valley's innovation culture (widely viewed Google Tech Talk)
