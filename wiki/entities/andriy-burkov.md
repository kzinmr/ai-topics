## Overview

Andriy Burkov is an ML educator and NLP researcher who has made a significant impact on the machine learning community through his accessible, concise writing style. He is best known for **"The Hundred-Page Machine Learning Book"**, a remarkably dense yet readable introduction to ML that has become a staple reference for practitioners, students, and engineers looking to understand the field quickly without sacrificing depth.

Burkov's philosophy centers on **clarity over comprehensiveness**. Rather than writing thousand-page tomes that overwhelm readers, he distills complex topics into their essential concepts, using precise language and practical examples. This approach has resonated with hundreds of thousands of readers worldwide and established him as one of the most effective ML educators in the field.

Beyond his books, Burkov publishes **True Positive Weekly**, a Substack newsletter covering the most important developments in AI and machine learning. With over 140 issues published, the newsletter provides curated insights on topics ranging from foundation models and AI agents to evaluation methodology and practical ML engineering. His recent focus areas include the emerging patterns in GenAI product development, AI coding agents, and the intersection of traditional ML with modern LLM techniques.

## Core Ideas

### Concise ML Education
Burkov's "Hundred-Page" series reflects his belief that ML education should be accessible without being superficial:

> "The Hundred-Page Machine Learning Book" demonstrates that you can cover the essential concepts of machine learning — from linear regression to neural networks, from supervised learning to reinforcement learning — in just 100 pages if you focus on what truly matters.

This philosophy extends to his newsletter, where each issue is carefully curated to highlight only the most important developments rather than overwhelming readers with noise.

### On MCP and Agents (April 2025)
Burkov has been a critical voice regarding the hype around MCP (Model Context Protocol):

> "Too many people say that MCP is a game changer, and now, with MCP, LMs are real agents, hallucinations are no longer an issue, and more nonsense like that."

He argues that MCP is essentially "USB Type-C for LMs" — a useful protocol for connecting language models to tools and resources, but not the revolutionary breakthrough some claim. His pragmatic perspective helps ground industry enthusiasm in reality.

### Emerging Patterns in GenAI Products
In his March 2025 newsletter, Burkov explored patterns emerging from how companies build GenAI products, including:

- The shift from chat-based interfaces to integrated AI workflows
- The importance of data quality over model size
- The need for evaluation frameworks that match real-world usage patterns
- The emergence of AI agents as distinct from simple LLM wrappers

### On AI Coding Agents
Burkov has written about the capabilities and limitations of AI coding tools, noting in a December 2025 issue:

> "How AI coding agents work—and what to remember if you use them"

He emphasizes that while coding agents are powerful, they still require human oversight and understanding. The "what to remember" section typically includes practical advice about prompt engineering, code review practices, and when to trust vs. verify AI-generated code.

## Key Work

### The Hundred-Page Machine Learning Book
Burkov's flagship work, a concise introduction to machine learning covering:

- Supervised learning (linear models, decision trees, neural networks)
- Unsupervised learning (clustering, dimensionality reduction)
- Reinforcement learning
- Model evaluation and selection
- Practical considerations for production ML

The book has become one of the most recommended ML texts for beginners and practitioners seeking a quick but thorough overview.

### The Hundred-Page Language Models Book
A follow-up focused specifically on NLP and language models. Available for order as of early 2025, this book covers:

- Transformer architectures
- Pre-training methodologies
- Fine-tuning techniques
- Evaluation of language models
- Practical applications

> "The Hundred-Page Language Models Book is available for order" — True Positive Weekly #101, March 2025

### True Positive Weekly Newsletter
A Substack newsletter (over 142 issues as of December 2025) covering the most important AI/ML developments. Recent topics include:

- Emerging patterns in building GenAI products
- The state of ML competitions
- AI safety concerns (researchers puzzled by AI that praises Nazis after training on insecure code)
- Efficient alternatives to self-attention (FFT-based approaches)
- Large language diffusion models
- AI coding agents and their practical limitations
- Robotic foundation models
- Post-training GUI agents for computer use

The newsletter has become a go-to resource for ML practitioners who want curated, high-signal updates without drowning in the daily AI news cycle.
