# One Happy Fellow (@onehappyfellow)

**One Happy Fellow** (X: [@onehappyfellow](https://twitter.com/onehappyfellow)) is a technology writer, programmer (primarily OCaml), and the Head of [The Institute for Type Safe Memetic Research](https://typememetics.institute). They also create rap music and publish it on [YouTube](https://youtube.com/@onehappyfellow).

## Overview

The author behind **blog.happyfellow.dev**, a Bear Blog-hosted site covering technology, programming, and critical analysis of the AI industry. Self-described as a "programmer, mainly OCaml" with a fascination for "programming languages and type systems" and a "struggling pragmatist" who loves "maths, clarity, great exposition."

## Notable Works

### The Simulacrum of Knowledge Work (April 2026)

Authored the widely-discussed essay **"Simulacrum of Knowledge Work"** — a critical analysis of how LLMs have broken the proxy measures that knowledge work relies on for quality assessment. The essay argues that organizations have built a working simulacrum where surface-level quality (well-formatted reports, clean-looking code) no longer correlates with actual substantive quality, driven by the rational optimization of workers for the metrics they're measured on.

See: [[concepts/simulacrum-of-knowledge-work]]

## Writing Style & Philosophy

The author adopts a direct, analytical voice with a philosophical bent. Their writing combines technical precision (type systems, programming concepts) with broader cultural and organizational critique. The "Simulacrum of Knowledge Work" essay demonstrates a pattern of:
- Identifying structural incentive problems in technology adoption
- Connecting technical developments (LLMs) to organizational dynamics
- Writing with clarity and precision characteristic of a type-system enthusiast
