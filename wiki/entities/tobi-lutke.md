# Tobi Lütke

**Tobi Lütke** (@tobi) is the CEO and co-founder of **Shopify**, the e-commerce platform powering millions of online stores in 175+ countries. A self-taught programmer from Germany who never completed university, Lütke built Shopify from a frustrated attempt to sell snowboards online into a $200B+ publicly traded company. He is a Ruby on Rails core team member, avid open-source contributor, race car driver, and a prominent voice on AI agents and the future of software craftsmanship.

## Biography

### Early Life

Lütke was born in Koblenz, Rhineland-Palatinate, West Germany in 1981. His father was a clergyman and his mother a school principal. He received a Schneider CPC (German variant of the Amstrad) at age 6 and began rewriting game code by age 11–12 as a hobby. 

At 16, he dropped out of school and entered an apprenticeship program (Fachinformatiker) at the Carl-Benz-School in Koblenz, affiliated with a Siemens subsidiary (BOG Koblenz). The German dual-education system combined work as a junior employee with 60 days/year of vocational school over three years. There, a mentor named Jürgen taught him programming in Delphi and instilled the principle that "ego shouldn't be tangled in code."

In 2002, Lütke moved to Canada to join his girlfriend (now wife) Fiona McKean, arriving without a work visa. He supported himself through remote programming for a German company until the contract ended, which pushed him toward entrepreneurship.

### Snowdevil & Shopify

In 2004, inspired by a shared love of snowboarding, Lütke teamed with **Scott Lake** (a family friend of the McKeans) and **Daniel Weinand** (a German designer friend) to launch **Snowdevil**, an online snowboard shop. Working from a Bridgehead coffee shop in Ottawa, Lütke built the e-commerce platform from scratch using Ruby on Rails — a new web framework that had just been open-sourced by David Heinemeier Hansson.

> "I tried to find the right software to use for this business, and I was stunned that I couldn't find anything. It was so clear that no one who's ever run a retail business had any part in building these pieces of software."

When Snowdevil's first order came in via email, Lütke described it as a pivotal moment that transformed him from programmer to entrepreneur. Soon, people became more interested in the underlying e-commerce infrastructure than the snowboard gear itself. The team pivoted in 2006, renaming the platform **Shopify**.

Initial investment came from Lütke's father-in-law Bruce McKean, a mining investor. The company's first office was above a coffee shop. Lütke served as CTO initially and became CEO in 2008.

### Public Company & Scale

Shopify went public in 2015 at $17/share. By 2025, it had an ~$11.6B annual revenue, 11,700+ employees, and powered over 10% of all U.S. e-commerce. Lütke owns approximately 7% of Shopify with ~40% voting interest through a two-class structure.

Since March 2020, Lütke has drawn a $1 annual salary, tying his compensation entirely to long-term stock-based awards. He has been the 11th richest Canadian.

### Race Car Driving

Lütke holds a FIA Bronze racing license and competes in the **IMSA SportsCar Championship** driving in LMP2 for Era Motorsport. He pursued professional racing alongside his CEO duties — a reflection of his philosophy of going "wide" across disciplines rather than narrow specialization.

## AI & Software Philosophy

### River: Shopify's Internal AI Agent

In May 2026, Lütke revealed **River**, an AI agent living in Shopify's Slack, designed around the **apprenticeship model**:

> "I learned to be a programmer by watching them. By making them coffee. By hanging around long enough that their judgment seeped into mine."

River is mentioned in Slack like a teammate — performing engineering tasks, answering questions, and absorbing institutional knowledge. The design philosophy emphasizes:
- **Learning through proximity**: AI agents absorb craft knowledge the way apprentices learn from masters
- **AI as teammate, not tool**: Participation in existing communication channels as peers
- **Organizational memory**: Institutional knowledge that compounds over time

### AI as Baseline Expectation (April 2025)

In an internal memo at Shopify, Lütke declared that **reflexive use of AI** was now a "baseline expectation" for every employee — including himself. This positioned Shopify as one of the first major companies to embed AI usage into every role's standard practice rather than treating it as a specialist tool.

### The Apprentice Programmer

Lütke's 2013 essay "The Apprentice Programmer" remains his most personal philosophical statement. It argues that the German dual-education system's combination of work and vocational school produces stronger programmers than pure academic routes. He advocates for:
- **Apprenticeship over degrees**: "Experiencing and learning things quickly is the ultimate life skill"
- **Mentorship**: Being pushed slightly outside the comfort zone is the most effective learning
- **Systems thinking**: "Most people think about cause and effect, but the world works in systems — it is loopy, not linear"

### On Human Excellence in the AI Era

In his 2018 essay "The Future Role of Human Excellence," Lütke used the Kasparov–Deep Blue chess story to argue that AI doesn't diminish human achievement — it recontextualizes it. Chess popularity grew after computers beat humans because "we appreciate other humans pushing limits."

## Key Beliefs

1. **Organizational moats**: In an era of converging technology, how a company organizes itself is the last durable advantage
2. **Platform thinking**: "Put small things together to act big" — build virtuous cycles where everyone wins
3. **Compass metrics**: Choose one metric that guides every decision, like Shopify's focus on empowering merchants
4. **Maker mindset**: "You earn your job not by knowing what to do. You earn it by making great decisions when you don't know what to do."
5. **Canadian swagger**: Advocates for Canada to "dial up the swagger" about its technological achievements
