## Overview

Dario Amodei is the co-founder and CEO of Anthropic, one of the leading AI safety-focused companies. His career spans OpenAI (where he was VP of Research) to founding Anthropic in 2021 with his sister Daniela Amodei and key researchers who believed existing AI companies weren't doing enough to address safety risks.

Amodei is best known for establishing **Constitutional AI** (training models on high-level ethical principles), the **Responsible Scaling Policy** (classifying models by AI Safety Levels with mandatory red-teaming), and pushing the field toward **mechanistic interpretability** as a safety tool.

## Core Technical Frameworks

### Constitutional AI
Trains models on high-level ethical principles for consistent generalization. Paired with interpretability audits to prevent misalignment and scheming behavior.

### Responsible Scaling Policy
Classifies models by **AI Safety Levels**; mandates evaluations, red-teaming, and security breakthroughs before compute scaling. Enforced via board directives, dedicated safety hiring, and frequent testing.

### Mechanistic Interpretability
Described as an **"MRI for AI"**: reverse-engineering internal computations via sparse autoencoders to map reasoning circuits. Used to detect deception, block jailbreaks, and verify safety pre-deployment.

### Scalable Oversight
**"Sandwiching" paradigm**: non-experts + AI handle intermediate tasks → experts evaluate outcomes. Validated on MMLU and QuALITY benchmarks; outperforms unassisted humans/models.

## AI Consciousness Stance

Amodei has been unusually open about uncertainty regarding AI consciousness:

- Acknowledges fundamental uncertainty: *"We Don't Know if the Models Are Conscious"*
- Opus 4.6 self-assigns **15–20% probability** of consciousness under various prompts
- Anthropic adopts precautionary measures (e.g., avoiding distressing prompts)
- Identified neural activations linked to "anxiety" but stresses this ≠ subjective experience

## Policy and Ethics Positions

### Military and Defense
Rejected Pentagon demands (Feb 2026) to remove AI safeguards for a potential $200M DoD contract. Supports lawful foreign intelligence but opposes mass domestic surveillance.

### Open-Source AI
Warned Senate Judiciary (July 2023) that scaling open-source models is a *"very dangerous path"* due to uncontrollable risks (bioweapons, misinformation).

### Economic Policy
Advocates progressive taxation on AI-driven profits to mitigate job displacement and inequality. Supports AI regulation and export restrictions to authoritarian regimes.

### Global South / India
Keynote at India AI Impact Summit (Feb 2026) emphasized India's role in setting tech standards, balancing AI's potential (healthcare, poverty alleviation) against risks (economic disruption, autonomous actions).

### Political Positioning
Disputes "woke" characterization; positions Anthropic as cross-political collaborator focused on responsible development.

## Controversies and Critiques

- **Open-Source Opposition**: His Senate testimony against open-source scaling drew criticism from the open-source AI community, who argue that open models are essential for safety research and democratization.
- **Anthropic's "Safety" Branding**: Critics argue that Constitutional AI and Responsible Scaling are more marketing than substance, and that Anthropic's models still exhibit hallucination and jailbreak vulnerabilities.
- **Pentagon Refusal**: The Feb 2026 rejection of DoD contract terms was praised by safety advocates but criticized by those who see military AI development as inevitable and necessary.
