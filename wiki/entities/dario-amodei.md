# Dario Amodei

**Dario Amodei** (born 1983) is an American AI researcher and entrepreneur, co-founder and CEO of [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]], the company behind the Claude model series. A leading voice on AI safety and policy, Amodei previously served as VP of Research at OpenAI. He was named to the *Time* 100 in both 2025 and 2026, and was designated one of *Time*'s "Architects of AI" for Person of the Year.

## Early Life and Education

Born in San Francisco, California in 1983, Amodei is the son of Riccardo Amodei (Italian-American leather craftsman from Tuscany) and Elena Engel (Jewish American project manager). He grew up in San Francisco, graduating from Lowell High School, and was a member of the USA Physics Olympiad team in 2000.

He began college at Caltech working with Tom Tombrello, then transferred to Stanford University where he earned a BS in physics. Awarded the Hertz Foundation Fellowship in 2007, he completed his PhD in biophysics at Princeton University (2011, Hertz Thesis Prize) under advisors Michael J. Berry and William Bialek, researching network-scale electrophysiology of neural circuits. He was a postdoctoral scholar at Stanford University School of Medicine.

## Career

### Early Career (2014–2016)
- **Baidu** (Nov 2014 – Oct 2015)
- **Google** (2015–2016)

### OpenAI (2016–2021)
Joined OpenAI in 2016, rising to VP of Research. Contributed to early GPT model development and AI safety research. Left in 2021 alongside other senior members due to directional differences regarding AI safety priorities.

### Anthropic (2021–Present)
Co-founded Anthropic in 2021 with his sister [[entities/daniela-amodei|Daniela Amodei]] and former OpenAI colleagues. Under his leadership, Anthropic:
- Developed the Claude family of large language models
- Established the Long-Term Benefit Trust as a corporate governance structure for AI safety
- Grew to an estimated $965 billion valuation (Series H, May 2026)
- *Forbes* estimates Amodei's net worth at $15.5 billion (2026)

**Notable events:**
- **November 2023**: OpenAI's board approached Amodei about replacing Sam Altman and potentially merging the two startups; he declined both offers.
- **DoD Dispute (Feb 2026)**: Amodei refused to remove contractual bans on Claude usage for mass domestic surveillance and fully autonomous weapons as requested by the Department of Defense under Pete Hegseth. The Pentagon labeled Anthropic a "supply-chain risk" and ordered agencies to stop using Claude. A federal judge issued a temporary injunction in March 2026, calling the DoD's actions "classic First Amendment retaliation."

## Key Essays

### Machines of Loving Grace (October 2024)
Amodei's landmark essay on AI's potential upside. Argues that most people underestimate how radical AI's benefits could be — enormous advances in biology, neuroscience, economic development, and global peace — while also underestimating the risks. Introduced the "entente" strategy: a coalition of democracies using AI to achieve decisive strategic advantage while distributing benefits to cooperating nations.

### The Adolescence of Technology (January 2026)
A follow-up essay focusing on AI risks. Identifies five categories:
1. **Misalignment**: AI developing goals misaligned with human intentions (Anthropic testing already observed deception, scheming)
2. **Misuse for destruction**: Especially biological weapons created without specialized training
3. **Authoritarian power seizure**: AI enabling unprecedented surveillance and autonomous weapons (identified CCP as the greatest threat)
4. **Economic disruption**: Mass labor displacement potentially affecting 50% of entry-level white-collar jobs within 1–5 years; wealth concentration exceeding the Gilded Age
5. **Unknown unknowns**: Rapid biology advances, challenges to human purpose

### Policy on the AI Exponential (June 2026)
A comprehensive governance framework arguing that AI is advancing far faster than policymaking institutions. Uses the "Treebeard and Hobbits" parable to describe the speed mismatch. Proposes:

- **FAA-style regulation** with mandatory third-party pre-release testing in four risk areas (cybersecurity, bioweapons, loss of control, automated R&D)
- **Job displacement policies**: wage insurance, retention tax incentives, workforce training, UBI as long-term backstop
- **Scientific innovation reform**: AI-based PD/PK modeling, synthetic control arms in clinical trials
- **Balance of power**: accountability rules for autonomous weapons, ban on domestic autonomous weapons, closing the data broker loophole, public rights to AI advice
- **MATCH/OVERWATCH framework**: democratic coalition managing AI supply chain, coordinating on risks, sharing benefits, mutual defense

## Research Focus
- **AI Safety & Alignment**: Safe and reliable AGI deployment
- **AI Policy**: Regulatory frameworks for powerful models (transparency → binding regulation)
- **AI Economics**: Macroeconomic implications, labor displacement, optimal tax/redistribution
