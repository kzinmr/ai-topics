# Ashe Magalhaes

Founder of **Hearth AI** (the first agentic CRM), former ML engineer at Airbnb, and a builder whose career spans solar car racing, NASA satellite work, and frontier AI products. Known for her philosophy that **the builder is an artist** — creating across mediums from code to hardware to poetry. Currently building agentic personal operating systems and sharing her workflow publicly on X.

## Bio

Ashe Magalhaes has lived at the intersection of frontier technology and creative building. While at Stanford, she spent two years on the **solar car team**, building a car end-to-end and racing it in the Bridgestone World Solar Challenge across the Australian outback. She then worked on **satellites at NASA** and rode the deep learning wave as an **ML engineer at Airbnb**.

In **2022**, she founded **Hearth AI** — the first agentic CRM, built on GPT-3.5 when the infrastructure was still fragile and non-deterministic. She gave early feedback to OpenAI's API team, pushing for structured outputs and JSON schema retrieval before those features existed as products.

Her current work focuses on **agentic personal operating systems** — personal AI agents that enable deeper presence, connection, and intentionality. She is an advocate for **building in public**, sharing her process and letting serendipity emerge from open creation.

## Core Ideas

### Builder as Artist

> *"I view the builder archetype as quite close to the artist archetype. I'm happiest viewing myself as like a conduit for creativity."*

Ashe refuses the engineer/artist binary. For her, engineering is a **tool for creating and incarnating** — whether the medium is satellites, cars, code, poetry, or videos. The common thread is curiosity and the desire to bring things to life.

### Agentic Personal Operating System

Ashe runs multiple AI agents as a **personal OS** — agents that handle context, memory, and follow-through so she can be more present in creative and relational domains. This is not about replacing human connection but **enhancing it**: offloading administrative overhead to agents frees up attention for what matters.

### Build in Public

> *"Let it go out into the world, let it breathe, and allow there to be a surprise in what could happen."*

Rather than waiting for polish, Ashe advocates sharing work early. Building in public creates serendipity — unexpected connections, feedback, and collaborations that wouldn't emerge from private development.

### Frontier Rigor

Her solar car experience instilled a visceral understanding of safety-critical engineering: **"How do you make safe decisions and also really push the bounds of what's technically possible?"** This mindset — rigorous without being rigid — carries through to her AI work.
