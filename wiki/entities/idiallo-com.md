# Ibrahim Diallo (idiallo.com)

**Ibrahim Diallo** is a software developer and technical writer who blogs at idiallo.com. He is known for his reflective writing about the developer experience, particularly around AI-assisted workflows, tooling choices, and the human side of software engineering.

## Overview

Diallo writes about practical developer topics with a focus on the **human experience** of coding and creating. His blog covers:

- LLM-assisted writing workflows and their pitfalls
- Developer tool choices (auto-update policies, CLI tools)
- The philosophy of building "useless" tools as learning experiences
- The tension between AI efficiency and authentic human voice

## Core Topics

### LLM-Assisted Writing (2025-2026)

Diallo has been a vocal practitioner of AI-assisted writing, documenting both the benefits and challenges:

- **The Voice Problem** - He noted that LLM-generated content often sounds generic and lacks the author's distinctive voice, making it difficult to quote one's own work
- **The Editing Workflow** - Developed a multi-stage process: use AI for structure, then aggressively edit to restore personal voice and authentic anecdotes
- **Case Study: "Why Developers Build Unfinished Calculators"** - Documented the evolution from AI draft (Feb 2025) to human-edited final version (May 2026), concluding that while functional, AI output "wasn't my human experience with the subject"

### Developer Philosophy

- **Learning by Building** - Advocates for the "monkey bars" approach: developers learn through play and experimentation, not just structured study
- **Tool Autonomy** - Writes about maintaining control over one's development environment (e.g., disabling auto-updates to preserve workflow stability)
- **Iteration over Perfection** - Views abandoned projects as valuable learning experiences rather than failures

## Writing Style & Philosophy

Diallo's writing is characterized by:

- **Personal narrative** - Uses specific memories and anecdotes to ground abstract concepts
- **Self-reflection** - Openly discusses his own failures and learning processes
- **Practical wisdom** - Focuses on actionable insights rather than theoretical frameworks
- **Human-centered approach** - Consistently emphasizes the human experience over technical efficiency
