# Future of Humanity Institute (FHI)

## Overview

The **Future of Humanity Institute** was a multidisciplinary research institute at the University of Oxford, founded in 2005 by [[entities/nick-bostrom]]. FHI focused on existential risks, future technologies, and long-term human flourishing. It was one of the most influential institutions in the AI safety and existential risk movements.

FHI **closed in April 2024** after Oxford University declined to renew its operational arrangements, citing administrative and fundraising challenges.

## Key Research Areas

### AI Safety and Strategy
- **Nick Bostrom**: *Superintelligence: Paths, Dangers, Strategies* (2014) — the foundational text on AI existential risk
- **Eric Drexler**: *Reframing Superintelligence* (2019) — the [[concepts/comprehensive-ai-services]] framework (FHI-TR-2019-1)
- AI governance frameworks, race dynamics, and strategic analysis

### Existential Risk
- Nuclear war, pandemic risk, climate catastrophe
- The "vulnerable world hypothesis" (Bostrom)
- Risk from advanced nanotechnology and biotechnology

### Decision Theory and Ethics
- Anthropic reasoning (the "sleeping beauty problem")
- Moral uncertainty and population ethics
- Longtermism — the idea that the long-term future matters enormously

## Closure (April 2024)

FHI's closure marked a significant loss for the AI safety research ecosystem:
- Oxford University chose not to renew the institute's arrangements
- Staff dispersed to other institutions (many joined other AI safety organizations)
- The closure reflected broader tensions between academic freedom and institutional risk management
- Some researchers moved to the Centre for the Governance of AI (GovAI), also at Oxford

## Legacy

Despite its closure, FHI's intellectual legacy remains foundational:
- *Superintelligence* (2014) shaped the entire AI safety discourse
- *Reframing Superintelligence* (2019) offered the most comprehensive alternative to agent-centric AI risk models
- FHI researchers populated the leadership of subsequent AI safety organizations
- The institute demonstrated that rigorous academic analysis of existential risk was viable
