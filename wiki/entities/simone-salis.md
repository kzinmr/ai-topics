## Bio

Background: Storytelling at Scale
Before I began automating media production, I spent a decade mastering the craft of storytelling across every medium—from broadcast television to silicon valley accelerators.
I don't believe you can automate what you don't understand. My engineering systems are built on a foundation of “doing the work”:
TV & Satire:
I worked as a Senior Correspondent for
Comedy Central
and produced segments for
Discovery
, reaching millions of viewers with satirical content (shows like
Trumplandia
,
Lercio
).
The Viral Era:
As a pre-algorithmic YouTuber, I generated over
1M organic views per video
through pure narrative structure, long before “growth hacking” existed.
Performance:
I spent years on stage teaching improvisation (coming from the iO Theater and The Second City) and behind the mic as a voice actor for anime franchises like
Neon Genesis Evangelion
,
Gundam
, and
Code Geass
.
The Bridge:
I am a graduate of
YCombinator’s Startup School
and studied Architecture and Industrial Design, before moving to the US to study Marketing and Media Production. This is where the messy world of art collided with the rigid world of systems.
Career: Industry 5.0
Today, I apply that diverse background to a single mission:
Creative Engineering.
I spent the last 10 years in the US tech sector (most notably at
GoHealth and other start-ups
in Chicago) building systems that allow creative teams to scale. I realized that by using AI to handle the “boring” parts of production
