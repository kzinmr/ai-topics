# Enterprise AI Operating Model

A framework introduced by IBM at Think 2026 (May 5, 2026) for running AI at enterprise scale. Addresses the defining challenge: many enterprises have invested heavily in AI, but few believe it's paying off.

## The Four Pillars

IBM's model posits that enterprise AI requires four integrated systems:

### 1. Agents — Orchestration at Scale
- **watsonx Orchestrate**: Agentic control plane for the multi-agent era
- Deploy agents from any source with consistent policy enforcement and accountability
- Designed for thousands of agents built by different teams on different platforms
- From "building agents" to "governing agents in near real time"

### 2. Data — Real-Time Foundation
- **IBM Confluent**: Real-time, AI-ready data foundation
- Governed, connected information that agents need to act with speed
- Shared view of what's happening across the enterprise

### 3. Automation — End-to-End Infrastructure
- **IBM Concert**: Intelligent operations platform
- Automated workflows scaling across processes
- Infrastructure, security, and operations connected across hybrid environments

### 4. Hybrid — Sovereignty & Governance
- **IBM Sovereign Core**: Operational independence
- Governance and sovereignty controls for AI at scale
- Consistent controls across deployment environments

## Developer Tools
- **IBM Bob**: Agentic development partner (GA) — AI-assisted enterprise development
- Designed to help enterprise developers build, test, and deploy with AI assistance
- Integrated into the watsonx platform

## Significance
IBM's framework is the most structured enterprise AI operating model proposed by a major vendor. It addresses the gap between AI investment and ROI by treating AI not as a set of tools but as a **new operating system for the enterprise** — with governance, data, automation, and agents as integrated layers.

This contrasts with the "agent-first" approaches of OpenAI and Anthropic, which focus more on model capability than enterprise integration.
