# AI Pre-Release Government Testing

The emerging framework where governments evaluate frontier AI models for national security risks before public release. A major policy shift in May 2026, reversing the earlier hands-off approach to AI regulation.

## CAISI Agreements (May 5, 2026)

The [[entities/caisi]] (Center for AI Standards and Innovation) within NIST signed voluntary agreements with **Microsoft, Google (DeepMind), and xAI** to conduct pre-deployment evaluations. These join existing 2024 agreements with [[entities/openai]] and [[entities/anthropic]].

### Evaluation Scope
- **Cybersecurity** risks (primary driver)
- **Biosecurity** and chemical weapons
- **National security** implications
- Post-deployment monitoring also included

### Catalyst: Claude Mythos
[[entities/anthropic]]'s Claude Mythos (April 2026) was described as "far ahead" of other models in cybersecurity capability. Anthropic restricted access to approved organizations only, and briefed senior US government officials. This alarmed governments, banks, and utilities, triggering the policy shift.

## Policy Evolution

| Era | Administration | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| 2024 | Biden | Voluntary standards, definitions, testing at US AISI |
| 2025 | Trump (early) | Hands-off, deregulatory stance |
| 2026 (May) | Trump (post-Mythos) | Voluntary pre-release agreements; considering mandatory reviews via executive order |

## International Dimension
- **UK**: Microsoft signed similar agreement with UK AI Security Institute (May 2026)
- **CAISI mandate**: Represent US interests internationally to guard against burdensome foreign regulation while ensuring US dominance of AI standards

## CAISI Evaluation Track Record
- 40+ model evaluations completed
- DeepSeek V4 Pro evaluated (May 1, 2026)
- AI agent security red-teaming competitions
- Collaboration with OpenMined for secure evaluations
