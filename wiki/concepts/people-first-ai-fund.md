# People-First AI Fund

The People-First AI Fund is a philanthropic grant-making program operated by the [[entities/openai-foundation|OpenAI Foundation]]. Launched in September 2025 with an initial **$50 million** commitment, it supports nonprofits and mission-driven organizations working at the intersection of AI innovation and public good.

## Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Sep 2025 | Fund launched with $50M commitment; open call for applications |
| Dec 2025 | First wave: $40.5M in unrestricted grants to 208 organizations |
| Jun 4, 2026 | Second wave: $9.5M announced; additional $50M committed for 2026 |
| Jun 15, 2026 | Applications re-open for 2026 round |

## Grant Focus Areas

### Health
Health emerged as the strongest theme in the first wave. Grants address:
- Hospital financial assistance and medical debt elimination (Dollar For)
- AI-assisted drug repurposing (Every Cure)
- Patient engagement for underserved populations (CareMessage)
- Digital health tools for community and rural providers (OCHIN)

### Local Journalism
Support for local media emerged as a core theme. Grant to **Lenfest Institute for Journalism** to partner with Axios Media on AI training for journalists in underserved communities, focusing on responsible AI use that augments human editorial responsibility.

### Community Organizations
208 community-based organizations across the US received unrestricted grants to strengthen local communities and expand the opportunity of AI.

## Scale

- Nearly **3,000 organizations** applied in the initial open call
- 208 organizations funded in first wave
- Part of broader **$1 billion** commitment over the next year
- Additional **$50 million** committed for 2026

## Philosophy

The fund reflects the Foundation's belief that "AI should be shaped by the communities, educators, workers, patients, and families who can benefit from these tools in practical, tangible ways." Grant decisions are guided by people-first values rather than purely technical or commercial considerations.
