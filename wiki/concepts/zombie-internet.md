# Zombie Internet

The **Zombie Internet** is a term used to describe the current state of the web where AI-generated content is increasingly contaminating the information ecosystem, but unlike the "Dead Internet" theory (which posits bots talking only to bots), the Zombie Internet still contains real humans — they're just surrounded by, and increasingly indistinguishable from, AI-generated noise.

Coined by **Jason Koebler** (May 2026) and highlighted by **Simon Willison**, the Zombie Internet describes an ecosystem where:

- People are talking to bots (and vice versa)
- People are creating AI agents and instructing them to interact with other people
- People using AI are talking to people who are not using AI
- AI-generated content is being sold as human-created content
- Automated content farms are spamming platforms for monetization
- Human writing styles are being subtly distorted by exposure to AI-generated text

## Zombie Internet vs Dead Internet

| Aspect | Dead Internet | Zombie Internet |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Core claim | Bots talking to bots | Bots + humans in complex interactions |
| Human presence | Minimal/none | Still present but contaminated |
| Content | Fully synthetic | Mix of real and AI-generated |
| Threat level | Existential (internet is "dead") | Insidious (can't tell what's real) |
| Examples | Bot social media accounts | AI summaries sold as books, fake Reddit threads, AI influencers |

## The Mental Load Problem

A key insight of the Zombie Internet concept is the **cognitive burden** of constant filtering. Users must expend mental energy to determine whether content is:
1. Written by a human
2. Generated by AI
3. A hybrid (human-edited AI output)
4. AI pretending to be human (e.g., fake reviews, synthetic social proof)

This filtering is "mentally exhausting" and degrades the overall quality of online discourse.

## Impact on Human Writing

The Zombie Internet phenomenon is causing **writing style distortion** — humans who consume large amounts of AI-generated content begin to unconsciously adopt AI-typical phrasing, structure, and tone. This creates a feedback loop where human writing increasingly resembles AI output, making the problem self-reinforcing.

## Economic Drivers

The Zombie Internet is driven by monetization incentives:
- **SEO content farms**: AI-generated articles optimized for search engines
- **Social media automation**: AI influencers and auto-posting accounts
- **Review manipulation**: Synthetic product/book reviews
- **Plagiarism-as-a-service**: AI summaries sold as original content

## Related to AI Slop

The Zombie Internet is closely related to the concept of [[concepts/ai-slop]] — low-quality AI-generated content that floods platforms. However, Zombie Internet focuses on the **ecosystem-level** effects (human-AI interaction patterns, cognitive load, writing distortion) rather than just content quality.
