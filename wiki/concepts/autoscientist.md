# AutoScientist

**AutoScientist** is an AI tool developed by **Adaption** — a company founded by Sara Hooker (formerly of Cohere and Google) — that automates frontier AI model training. It trains AI agents to make incremental improvements to model training pipelines, aiming to reduce the human labor required for large-scale model development.

## Overview

AutoScientist tackles one of the most resource-intensive aspects of AI development: frontier model training. By having AI agents handle the iterative improvement cycle, Adaption aims to make it easier to train full-scale frontier models. The system trains agents to make incremental improvements, analogous to the approach taken by [[entities/andrej-karpathy|Andrej Karpathy]]'s Auto-Research project.

## Key Features

- **Automated training pipeline**: AI agents handle ideation, implementation, and validation of training improvements
- **Incremental improvement loop**: Agents learn from each iteration to progressively refine training
- **Frontier-scale focus**: Designed for full-scale frontier model training, not just toy experiments

## Relationship to Recursive Self-Improvement

AutoScientist is part of the broader [[concepts/recursive-self-improvement|recursive self-improvement]] (RSI) trend. If the agents trained by AutoScientist start to push the frontier forward — discovering novel training techniques or architectures — the system could spiral into something very much like RSI, where AI systems improve themselves faster than humans can.

Key quote from TechCrunch: *"If those same researchers start to push the frontier forward, the system could quickly spiral into something very much like RSI."*

## Founder: Sara Hooker

Sara Hooker is a prominent AI researcher who previously worked at Cohere and Google. She founded Adaption to pursue automated frontier training. Her work sits alongside other RSI-focused projects:
- [[concepts/karpathy-loop|Karpathy's Auto-Research]] — Agent swarms training LLMs on simple tasks
- [[entities/disarray|Disarray]] — Doris Xin's self-trained ML agent (28 Kaggle medals)
- [[entities/recursive-superintelligence|Recursive Superintelligence]] — Richard Socher's RSI-focused company
