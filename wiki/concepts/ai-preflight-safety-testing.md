# AI Preflight Safety Testing

**AI Preflight Safety Testing** is a proposed regulatory framework — modeled on aviation preflight checks and FDA drug approvals — requiring that frontier AI models pass mandatory, transparent, and independent safety evaluations before public deployment.

## Hassabis Proposal

On July 14, 2026, [[entities/demis-hassabis|Demis Hassabis]] (Google [[entities/deepmind|DeepMind]] CEO) publicly endorsed preflight safety testing. His specific proposal:

1. **Voluntary initial phase**: Frontier labs voluntarily share models with a Standards Body for review up to 30 days before release.
2. **Formalization**: Once the assessment protocol proves effective, frontier models would be required to pass before deployment in the US market.
3. **Post-release**: Labs work with the Standards Body on critical post-release vulnerabilities.
4. **Governance**: A FINRA-style model (industry-funded self-regulatory organization with government oversight), involving independent technical experts.

## Significance

This marks a major shift: a top AI leader endorsing **mandatory** (not voluntary) pre-deployment testing. [[entities/gary-marcus|Gary Marcus]] called it a "turning point," noting he had advocated for this since his 2023 Senate testimony.

The proposal would bake 30-day minimum review periods into development cycles and establish a FINRA-style self-regulatory organization with government oversight. Marcus emphasizes the need for genuine independence and transparency in the evaluation process.
