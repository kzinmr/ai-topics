# OpenAI Foundation: AI for Alzheimer's Initiative

The OpenAI Foundation's $100+ million initiative to accelerate Alzheimer's research through AI-powered approaches across six research institutions.

## Overview

The OpenAI Foundation is finalizing **more than $100 million in initial grants** across **six research institutions** to support and accelerate Alzheimer's research. The initiative focuses on generating new data, helping design new drugs, and expanding possible paths to treatment.

## Why Alzheimer's?

Alzheimer's has resisted treatment because it is the quintessential complex disease:
- **Hundreds of genetic and environmental risk factors**
- **Interaction across cell types over decades**
- **Complex disease mechanisms**
- **Limited understanding of causal pathways**

## Five-Layer Research Stack

### 1. Causal Map of Alzheimer's Using AI
- **Goal**: Validate targets for intervention
- **Method**: Map full network of causal factors to pinpoint effective nodes for therapeutic intervention
- **Collaborator**: Arc Institute
- **Additional work**:
  - Creation of new open datasets with nonprofits like Focused Research Organisation
  - Expansion of longitudinal/epidemiological datasets
  - Opening up existing datasets from biotech companies

### 2. AI-Designed Drugs Tested in Lab
- **Goal**: Design and test new drugs using AI
- **Collaborator**: Institute for Protein Design at University of Washington
- **Focus**: AI-driven protein design models
- **Achievement**: Successfully engineered molecules that engage, modify, and degrade targets critical to Alzheimer's disease progression

### 3. AI-Assisted Biomarker Discovery
- **Goal**: Create new diagnostics
- **Method**: Use AI to identify biomarkers
- **Application**: Early detection and monitoring
- **Impact**: Improved diagnosis and treatment tracking

### 4. Clinical Collaboration
- **Goal**: Translate findings to patient care
- **Collaborator**: UCSF (University of California, San Francisco)
- **Focus**: Clinical implementation
- **Integration**: Connect research with clinical practice

### 5. Off-Patent Treatment Testing
- **Goal**: Test existing treatments using AI
- **Method**: Use AI to analyze anonymized patient data and online experiences
- **Target**: Lithium orotate and other off-patent compounds
- **Approach**: Data-driven treatment discovery

### Arc Institute
- **Patrick Hsu, PhD** — Co-Founder and Core Investigator

- **Silvana Konermann, PhD** — Co-Founder and Executive Director

### University of Washington
- **David Baker, PhD** — Nobel Laureate, Director of Institute for Protein Design
  - "Using our newest AI-driven protein design models, we have successfully engineered molecules that engage, modify, and degrade targets critical to Alzheimer's disease progression."

### UCSF

  - "With AI helping us integrate these insights and make sense of tremendous complexity, we have an opportunity to accelerate discoveries."

### Harvard Medical School

  - "My hope is that the ability of physiological dose lithium orotate to reverse pathology and restore memory in Alzheimer's disease mouse models will translate to the aging human population."
