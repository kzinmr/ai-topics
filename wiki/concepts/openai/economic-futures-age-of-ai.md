# OpenAI Foundation: Economic Futures in the Age of AI

The OpenAI Foundation's $250 million initiative to build secure and abundant economic futures in the age of AI.

## Overview

The OpenAI Foundation is committing an initial **$250 million** to building secure and economic futures. The initiative is led by **Divya Siddarth** and **Wojciech Zaremba**.

## Core Thesis

Economic systems exist, in principle, to give people security, autonomy, and the ability to build purposeful lives. AI is going to lead to huge economic changes as it makes previously scarce capabilities far more widely available. The breadth of possibilities makes this an extraordinary opportunity to build systems that enable better lives. The current pace of change means the window to get this right is shorter than we're used to.

## Three Pillars

### 1. Understanding the Shift
- **Goal**: Create clearer picture of AI's impacts on the economy
- **Method**: Invest in independent measurement and forecasting infrastructure
- **Challenge**: "We still don't have good ways to answer fundamental questions about how AI is changing and will change the economy."
- **Approach**: Measurement that tracks what people can actually do and access, not just what they earn

### 2. Supporting the Transition
- **Goal**: Resource workers and communities through near-term disruption
- **Scope**: More than reemployment — also interested in approaches that give workers agency over AI deployment and citizens real voice in institutions shaping economic change
- **Focus**: When work provides meaning, purpose, and satisfaction
- **Government**: Invest in capacity of governments and public institutions to deliver
- **Tool**: AI itself may be a powerful tool for accelerating state capacity
- **Equity**: Especially interested in making AI work well for people least served by existing systems

### 3. Building for Long-term Economic Security
- **Urgency**: Cannot afford to waste time waiting for certainty about pace and scale of change
- **Approach**: Help move promising approaches from ideas into testable designs
- **Revenue side**: 
  - Shifting taxation from labor toward capital and economic rents
  - Windfall or excess-returns mechanisms
  - Public or sovereign wealth funds (Norway Government Pension Fund, Alaska Permanent Fund models)
- **Distribution side**: 
  - Durable claims on broad economic growth through income, capital, public goods, essential services, jobs, access to compute, or new forms of data governance
- **Participation**: "The goal is not only to support people through economic change after decisions have already been made, but to give them a stake and a voice in shaping how that change unfolds."
