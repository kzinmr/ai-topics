# Newsjacking Framework

**Origin:** Elvis Sun (@elvissun), January 2026  
**Core Insight:** *"Instead of creating momentum, you steal it."*

## Definition

Newsjacking is the practice of identifying already-trending topics, conversations, or content waves, and strategically inserting yourself into them with a unique angle. Rather than building attention from zero, you ride existing attention.

> *"On January 4th, I wrote a post. Put some thought into it. Hit publish. 77 views. The next day, I went hunting for content that was already getting attention. Result: 5 viral tweets in one day, 602k views total."* — Elvis Sun

## The 5 Psychological Triggers

### 1. Polarizing Promise
Use absolute or provocative language ("never", "dead", "wrong") to create curiosity and stop scrolling.

**Example:** "I never hit the 5hr limit" (Claude Code context window bypass hack) → 339k views, 4.8k bookmarks.

**Mechanism:**
- "Never" creates a curiosity gap: "Wait, really?"
- Forces a mental stop → "Is this person lying or is there something here?"
- Generates both agreement and disagreement → both drive engagement
- As Justin Welsh notes: *"For content to spread, it needs to be a little polarizing."*

### 2. Contrarian Insight
Challenge conventional wisdom with data-backed arguments. Not just "hot take" but "hot take + proof."

**Example:** Most people think you need original ideas to go viral. Elvis proved the opposite: riding existing waves beats originality every time.

### 3. In-Group Signaling
Use specialized knowledge, jargon, or references that create an "insider" feeling.

**Example:** Not saying "Claude Code" but "the 5hr limit" — only power users know what this means. Creates a secret club effect.

**Mechanism:**
- When someone "gets it," they feel smart
- They engage to signal membership
- Screenshotting becomes identity expression ("I'm the kind of person who understands this")

### 4. Pattern Interrupt
Break the visual or structural expectations of the feed.

**Examples:**
- Code screenshot instead of text (forces visual stop)
- Profane domain name (ismypitchshit.com vs ismypitchgood.com)
- Unusual topic from a known source (Karpathy writing about biology)

### 5. Self-Deprecation
Share failures, bad scores, and losses rather than wins.

> *"Wins look like bragging. Losses look relatable."*

**Example:** Users of ismypitchshit.com voluntarily shared their terrible AI-roast scores. The tool's brutal honesty ("Absolute Garbage") became the shareable artifact.

## Why It Works (3 Mechanisms)

1. **The audience already exists** — You're not shouting into the void. Thousands are already watching the conversation you're joining.

2. **Algorithm rewards you** — Replies and quote tweets to viral content get pushed further because the original post is already "approved" by the algorithm.

3. **Lower creative burden** — You don't invent the topic. You just need a smart take on what's already happening.

## Application to Knowledge Curation (Hermes Wiki)

### Reader Perspective (Triage Filter)
When processing RSS scans, newsletter articles, and social media content:
- **Score articles** by newsjacking potential (0-5)
- **Prioritize** pieces that ride existing waves (new model releases, viral debates, platform changes)
- **Flag contrarian takes** from trusted sources for immediate wiki ingestion
- **Detect pattern interrupts** — when a known expert writes about something unexpected

### Writer Perspective (Daily/Weekly Reports)
When generating reports and wiki updates:
- **Lead with wave-riding content** — what's already hot in the community
- **Use polarizing framing** — "X is wrong about Y" performs better than "X discusses Y"
- **Create in-group resonance** — reference shared knowledge, use precise technical terms
- **Include contrarian angles** — when multiple sources agree, find the dissenting voice
- **Self-deprecate when appropriate** — "Here's what I got wrong last week" builds trust faster than "Here's what I know"

## The Attention Flywheel Connection

Newsjacking is one gear in the larger **Attention Flywheel**:

```
Test on X (high velocity) 
    → Validate engagement metrics 
        → Repurpose to Reddit (social proof baked in) 
            → Pitch journalists (warm outreach) 
                → Build product features from feedback 
                    → Product generates its own attention 
                        → Repeat
```

The flywheel's ultimate state is **product-driven virality** — when the product itself generates and distributes attention automatically (Polymarket, PhotoAI, ismypitchshit.com).
