# RPG (Regularized Policy Gradient)

**RPG (Regularized Policy Gradient)** is a unified theoretical framework for deriving and analyzing KL-regularized policy gradient methods in online reinforcement learning for Large Language Models, introduced by Zhang et al. (ICLR 2026). It clarifies the relationship between different KL divergence variants (forward vs. reverse, normalized vs. unnormalized) and their gradient estimators, resolving fragmentation in the RLHF literature.

The framework is significant because it identified a **critical importance-weighting mismatch** in the popular GRPO algorithm, and proposes the **RPG-REINFORCE** algorithm with **RPG-Style Clip** — a stable, scalable RL method that outperforms DAPO on mathematical reasoning benchmarks.

## Core Idea

The RPG view provides a compact, unified derivation for optimizing KL-regularized objectives in off-policy settings. Its key insights:

1. **k₃ Penalty Identity** — The widely-used k₃ penalty (used in GRPO and other methods) is mathematically equivalent to **unnormalized KL** divergence, not standard KL.
2. **Gradient Equivalence Conditions** — Specifies exact conditions under which REINFORCE-style losses (using `stop-gradient`) are gradient-equivalent to fully differentiable surrogates.
3. **Off-Policy Importance Weighting** — Clarifies what weighting each KL variant requires so the surrogate yields the exact gradient of the intended KL-regularized objective.

### RPG Design Surface

The framework characterizes algorithms across three dimensions:

| Dimension | Variants |
|-----------|----------|
| **KL Direction** | Forward KL (FKL) vs. Reverse KL (RKL) |
| **Normalization** | Normalized (KL) vs. Unnormalized (UKL) |
| **Estimators** | k₁, k₂, k₃ estimators from RLHF literature |

## GRPO Correction

A key practical contribution of the RPG framework is identifying an off-policy importance-weighting mismatch in GRPO's KL term. Under the off-policy setting (where completions generated by an older policy are reused), GRPO's KL penalty does not correctly account for the importance sampling ratio, leading to biased gradient estimates. The RPG view provides the corrected formulation.

## RPG-REINFORCE Algorithm

The proposed algorithm consists of three pillars:

### A: KL-Correct Objective
Use the RPG derivation to ensure the surrogate loss matches the intended KL-regularized gradient, avoiding the GRPO mismatch.

### B: RPG-Style Clip (Clipped Importance Sampling)
A clipped-importance-sampling step within the REINFORCE framework that:
- Prevents extreme gradient updates from off-policy samples
- Maintains mathematical integrity of the KL-regularized objective
- Similar in spirit to PPO's clipping but applied to importance weights

### C: Iterative Reference-Policy Updates
Periodically updates the reference policy to maintain stability during long-horizon reasoning tasks, preventing reward hacking and KL collapse.

## Relationship to Other Methods

| Aspect | RPG View | GRPO | PPO | DAPO |
|--------|----------|------|-----|------|
| **KL direction** | Unified (FKL/RKL) | Reverse KL | Reverse KL | Reverse KL |
| **Normalization** | Both analyzed | Normalized | Normalized | Normalized |
| **Importance weighting** | Correct off-policy | ❌ Mismatch in KL term | Correct | Correct |
| **Reward model** | Not required | Not required | Required | Not required |
| **Clip mechanism** | RPG-Style Clip | Group-based normalization | PPO clip | Dynamic sampling |
| **Theoretical rigor** | Full gradient derivation | Empirical | Empirical | Empirical |
