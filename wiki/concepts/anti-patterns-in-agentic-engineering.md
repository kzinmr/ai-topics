# Anti-patterns in Agentic Engineering

Common failure modes, risks, and anti-patterns observed by practitioners of agentic engineering.

## AI Psychosis / Agent Addiction (Van Horn, June 2026)

[[entities/matt-van-horn|Matt Van Horn]] identified a concerning pattern among early adopters of agentic engineering:

> "Agents were supposed to do all the work for us. Instead, every friend I have is working the hardest they ever have in their lives. ... Building with agents is the greatest video game ever made, and the loop is that good."

**Symptoms:**
- Working longer hours than ever before, despite agent automation
- Being so lit up by the ability to build anything that nothing else gets done
- Launching products with zero users -- vanishing into the build and losing social connections
- Agent-driven development as a dopamine loop: each interaction delivers reward, reinforcing the behavior

**Mitigations:**
- Take breaks and touch grass
- Talk to loved ones about the addiction risk
- Ask whether anyone actually wants the thing being built
- Accept that tools built "just for you" are valid -- not every project needs an audience
- Follow the "Gary Vaynerchuk path": start posting into the ether, build audience incrementally

**Context:** This anti-pattern is distinct from burnout -- it's specifically about the addictive quality of the agentic workflow loop, where rapid creation capability eliminates the natural friction that once served as a pacing mechanism. The same capability that enables shipping at unprecedented speed also removes the pauses that historically allowed for social connection and reflection.
