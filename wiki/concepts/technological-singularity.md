# Technological Singularity

The hypothetical future point at which technological growth — specifically, recursively self-improving artificial intelligence — becomes uncontrollable and irreversible, resulting in unforeseeable changes to human civilization. Coined in its modern sense by Vernor Vinge (1993), the term was derived from the mathematical singularity where a function goes to infinity in finite time.

## Core Mechanism: The Intelligence Explosion

Per [[entities/eliezer-yudkowsky|Eliezer Yudkowsky]] (1996), the mechanism is straightforward:

1. Computing power doubles every ~18 months (unaided human research)
2. Once computers can do AI research, they're added to the pool of researchers
3. Now computing power doubles every 2 **subjective** years — but subjective time shrinks as computers get faster
4. 2 years → 1 year → 6 months → 3 months → 1.5 months → **Singularity**

This "Transcended doubling sequence" produces a hyperbolic curve: y = 2/(2-x). When x=2, y = infinity. This is a true mathematical singularity.

## Why "Beyond" Comprehension

Yudkowsky's core argument is not just that things will change fast, but that post-Singularity intelligence will be **qualitatively incomprehensible** to humans. Key analogies:

- **Chimpanzee → human**: A chimp can't understand integral calculus, no matter how hard it tries
- **Human → Power**: The gap between human and superintelligence is larger still
- **Graham's number**: Even numbers describable by the human mind can be so vast they shatter our conception of "infinity" — and the Singularity is beyond *that*

## Perceptual Transcends (PTs)

Yudkowsky's proposed unit of post-Singularity progress. One PT occurs when all things that were comprehensible become obvious-in-retrospect, and semantic structures of one generation become the semantic primitives of the next. The whole of human knowledge becomes perceivable in a single flash of experience — as we now perceive an entire picture at once, rather than pixel-by-pixel.

## Multiple Paths

Per Yudkowsky:
1. **Artificial Intelligence** — Coding a true AI that can improve itself
2. **Nanotechnology** — Building computers atom-by-atom, brains neuron-by-neuron
3. **Human Enhancement** — Algernon's Law notwithstanding, bootstrapping via mild enhancements
4. **Unanticipated breakthroughs** — e.g., the Scanning Tunneling Probe advancing nanotech by a decade overnight

## Key Terminology (from Vinge's *A Fire Upon The Deep*)

- **Power**: Entity from beyond the Singularity
- **Transcend**: The act of recursively self-improving into superintelligence
- **Beyond**: The grey area between human and Power — entities smarter than human but not yet capable of direct self-reprogramming

## The Stable State Argument

"A planet scoured of life and a superintelligence are the only two stable states." Life As We Know It is unstable — it will end one way or the other. The Singularity is thus not optional but inevitable, barring extinction.

## Timing Estimates

- Original estimate (1996): ~2035
- Revised (1999): closer to 2025 (based on 55%/year computing growth vs 40%)
- "2035 is just a wild guess, and it might as well be next Tuesday"
- Yudkowsky's personal "target date": 2005 (if funded with $1B/year)

## Practical Implications

Per Yudkowsky, once you internalize the Singularity:
- You can't keep a straight face when someone talks about "a hundred years down the road"
- 50-year bonds and retiring in 2030 become absurd
- Our **sole responsibility** is to produce something smarter than we are
- Post-Singularity problems are not ours to solve — "a fish designing a working airplane out of algae and pebbles"

## The Minefield

The pre-Singularity period is a minefield, not a playground. Three "deadlines" threaten to destroy humanity before reaching Singularity:
1. **Nanotechnology** — Most urgent; complete molecular control makes deliberate extinction too easy
2. **Nuclear war** — Would leave survivors but alter the balance between AI and nanotech unfavorably
3. **Economic collapse** — Slows AI more than nanotech (AI needs Internet + thousands of programmers; nanotech needs a single lab)

## Techno-Optimism as Religion

The Singularity functions as a secular eschatology within transhumanist thought. The parallels with religious narratives are striking: an "end of history," transcendence of mortal limitations, Powers that are beyond comprehension, and a moral imperative ("Interim Meaning of Life") to bring about this transformation. [[concepts/techno-optimism|Techno-optimism]] in this framework becomes a form of faith — a point that [[concepts/techno-pessimism|techno-pessimists like Curtis Yarvin]] push back against.
