# Pentagon AI Contracts (May 2026)

On May 5, 2026, the Pentagon signed AI contracts with 8 companies: SpaceX, [[entities/openai]], Google, [[entities/microsoft]], [[entities/nvidia]], AWS, Oracle, and Reflection. [[entities/anthropic]] was notably excluded.

## The Exclusion

Anthropic's exclusion likely stems from its public stance on AI safety and military applications — the company has been more cautious than competitors about weapons-related AI use. The exclusion is reportedly under litigation.

## Fallout

- **Google DeepMind unionization**: On May 9, UK staff voted 98% to unionize specifically over classified Pentagon AI contracts — the first union at any top AI lab
- **US govt model vetting**: On May 8, vetting agreements were confirmed with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI
- **UK AISI evaluation**: GPT-5.5 matches Anthropic's restricted Mythos model on cyber benchmarks — raising questions about whether safety restrictions affect competitiveness

## Strategic Implications

The Pentagon's exclusion of Anthropic despite its leading technical capabilities raises questions about whether AI safety stances carry a financial penalty at the government contracting level. The Google DeepMind unionization vote signals growing internal tension within AI labs about military applications.
