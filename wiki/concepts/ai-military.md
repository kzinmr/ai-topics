# AI in Military Wargames

Coverage of AI systems (particularly Claude) outperforming US military teams in wargame simulations, and the subsequent Pentagon-Anthropic dispute over military use of AI.

## Key Events

### Claude Defeats US Military Team
- Gary Marcus reported on Claude outperforming trained military personnel in wargame scenarios
- AI demonstrated superior strategic reasoning in simulated conflict environments
- Raised questions about AI decision-making capabilities in high-stakes scenarios

### Pentagon-Anthropic Dispute
- Washington Post reported on Pentagon leveraging AI in Iran strikes planning
- Anthropic disputed the military use of Claude, citing usage policy violations
- Trump administration appealed ruling that blocked Pentagon action against Anthropic
- AP News covered the ongoing legal battle over AI military applications

## Implications

- **Governance Gap**: No clear framework for AI use in military decision-making
- **Corporate Responsibility**: AI companies struggling to enforce usage policies post-deployment
- **Strategic Advantage**: Nations may gain significant military edge through AI adoption
- **Escalation Risk**: AI systems in wargames could lead to real-world deployment pressures

## Pentagon AI Deals (May 2026)

On May 1, 2026, the Pentagon signed agreements with **7 AI companies** to deploy their technology on classified IL6/IL7 networks for "any lawful use":

| Company | Role |
|---------|------|
| **SpaceX** | Merged with xAI; Grok models |
| **OpenAI** | ChatGPT, GPT-5.5 on classified networks |
| **Google** | Gemini on classified networks |
| **Nvidia** | Nemotron open-source models |
| **Reflection AI** | Startup, no public model yet, seeking $25B valuation |
| **Microsoft** | Azure cloud infrastructure |
| **AWS** | Cloud infrastructure |

**Anthropic excluded**: The company was blacklisted as a "supply chain risk" in February 2026 after refusing the "any lawful use" clause due to concerns about autonomous weapons and domestic surveillance. However, the NSA is reportedly using Anthropic's [[entities/claude-mythos|Mythos]] model on classified networks despite the broader dispute, for vulnerability assessment.

**Budget**: $54B requested for autonomous weapons; $961.6B total 2026 defense budget. GenAI.mil platform serves 1.3M personnel.

### Reflection AI
A 2-year-old startup founded by former Google DeepMind researchers, backed by Nvidia and 1789 Capital (Donald Trump Jr. partner). Mission: open-source models to counter Chinese AI firms like [[entities/deepseek|DeepSeek]]. No public model released as of May 2026. See [[entities/reflection-ai]].

### Key Dynamics
- **Pentagon leveraging competition**: Signing Anthropic's rivals aims to pressure the company back to negotiations
- **Mythos exception**: DOD CTO Emil Michael called Mythos a "separate national security moment" — the model is too valuable to exclude despite the blacklist
- **Anthropic lawsuits**: Company suing in San Francisco and Washington, D.C. to reverse blacklisting
- **OpenAI opportunistic timing**: Struck its deal hours after Anthropic was blacklisted; Sam Altman later called it "opportunistic and sloppy"
