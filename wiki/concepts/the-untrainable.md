# The Untrainable

## Definition

**The Untrainable** refers to frontier work whose correctness exists only in private data, walled off from external evaluation. This concept, articulated by Sarah Guo in her 2026 essay, describes the category of AI applications that cannot be commoditized because their value is derived from private, organization-specific knowledge that cannot be captured in public benchmarks.

## The 2x2 Framework

Sarah Guo proposes a framework for evaluating AI work along two dimensions:

| | **Public Answers** | **Private Answers** |
|---|---|---|
| **Saturated Tasks** | Commodity tokens (open models win) | **The Untrainable** (custom models win) |
| **Frontier Tasks** | Labs win (eval is free) | **The Untrainable** (custom models win) |

### Key Insight
The prize is the last corner: frontier work whose correctness exists only in private. In inference clouds hosting AI-native pioneers, the vast majority of tokens are generated by custom models, not generic open ones.

## The Absorption Frontier

The expanding boundary where labs pull model scaffolding into the weights:
- Retrieval
- Routing between cheap and expensive calls
- Tool use
- Reasoning policy

All the apparatus that used to wrap a model is being pulled into the weights, until the wrapper is the model.

## Legible vs Illegible Work

### Legible Work (What's Leaving)
- Anything you can put on a leaderboard
- Anything you can train against
- Anything measurable is already on its way to commodity
- Example: Generic coding tasks, standard benchmarks

### Illegible Work (What's Valuable)
- Correctness that exists only inside someone's data
- Private ground truth
- Organization-specific knowledge
- Example: A decade-old codebase with undocumented reasons, a bank's production systems

## Permission & Accountability

Intelligence is not the bottleneck. **Permission** and **accountability** are:

- **The Lock (Environment)**: You only get to verify whether AI did something useful inside a system once you're trusted inside it
- **The Deadbolt (User)**: Trust is built slowly, on relationships, with user's acquiescence
- **Accountability**: Someone has to put their name on what the model does; the model cannot be sued

## Real-World Examples

### Legal (M&A Practice)
- Top white-shoe law firm runs ~1,000 deals/year
- Signal lives at the level of the deal (NDA, term sheet, diligence, purchase agreement, ancillaries, closing checklist)
- Each practice area has its own shape; lawyers and tools don't interchange
- Transforming a firm isn't a single eval — it takes an operator to moneyball it

### Medical (OpenEvidence)
- Majority of American doctors use OpenEvidence daily
- No amount of compute buys that adoption
- Lab can train flawless medical model tomorrow and still have no way into physician's habit

### Customer Service (Sierra)
- Charges when agent resolves customer's issue
- Nothing when it kicks to human
- Price becomes the evaluation
- Works because Sierra owns definition of "resolved"

### Software (Cognition/Devin)
- "Performance guarantee" you can only offer for outcomes in a system you're trusted inside

## Economics of the Untrainable

### Token Economics
- Token answering generic question: worth almost nothing (anyone's model can answer)
- Token reasoning over company's data: worth much more (does what you actually want)

### Inference as Non-Commodity
- Best AI-native companies concentrate serving on one or two providers
- Cost per token commoditizes on schedule
- Reliability under real traffic and guaranteed access to scarce compute do not
- Price is the only part of inference that acts like a commodity

### Lab Competition
- Model layer is not a single-player game
- Three-and-a-half-way death match with international players
- Customers want competition among suppliers
- Labs want market share more than any one application dead

## Defense & Offense

### Defense
- On narrow task with private data and own evals: train to frontier, beat general models
- Specialized model becomes part of moat
- Competing on general model: capital war you lose to whoever owns most compute

### Offense
- Choosing what to build in the first place
- Model can't tell you what's worth pointing it at
- Can't benchmark that, so can't train it
- Incumbents keep ground they have; next thing comes from someone who finds a use first
- "Maybe intent is an even scarcer input than compute"

## The Despair (and Why It's Half Right)

**The despair**: Nothing is investable except Anthropic and Nvidia; every company is a thin wrapper waiting to be absorbed.

**Why it's half right**: The thin wrapper layer really is being absorbed.

**Why it's wrong**: Intelligence keeps getting cheaper, and value keeps sliding toward the few places a model can't reach.

## Key Quotes

> "The untrainable is value with history. So get inside one, do the unglamorous translation, and start writing down what good means there, because someone is going to."

> "The most cited benchmark score of the year is a map of territory about to be worthless, and a notice of who is about to lose the right to say what counts as good."

> "A benchmark is a thing you can measure, and a thing you can measure is a thing you can train against."

> "Passing the test never told you the change was the right one for a decade-old codebase with three undocumented reasons that module exists and a deploy pipeline held together by a cron job no one will admit to writing."
