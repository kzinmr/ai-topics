# Frontier Safety Blueprint

OpenAI's June 2026 policy proposal for democratic governance of frontier AI systems. Published alongside a broader OpenAI public policy agenda, the blueprint outlines a regulatory framework centered on mandatory safety evaluations and expanded institutional oversight.

## Core Proposals

### CAISI Expansion
The blueprint advocates for expanding the **Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI)** — a proposed regulatory body that would oversee frontier AI development:

- **Mandatory evaluations** — All "frontier" AI systems would require pre-deployment safety evaluations
- **Standard-setting authority** — CAISI would define what constitutes frontier AI and establish testing standards
- **Democratic oversight** — Governance mechanisms designed to ensure public accountability

### Regulatory Framework
- Pre-deployment safety testing requirements
- Ongoing monitoring of deployed frontier models
- Centralized institutional authority for AI standards

## Criticism and Debate

The proposal has drawn significant skepticism from the AI community:

### Regulatory Capture Concern
Critics argue the blueprint functions as **regulatory capture** — creating barriers that advantage incumbent companies (OpenAI and Anthropic) while disadvantaging open-source and smaller competitors. By requiring mandatory government evaluations, the framework could:
- Create a moat around well-resourced companies that can afford compliance
- Stifle open-source AI development
- Centralize control over AI innovation

### Missing Elements
Notable omissions from the proposal:
- **Open source AI** — No mention of open-source development paths or community governance
- **Privacy protections** — Individual privacy rights not addressed
- **Freedom and access** — No framework for ensuring broad access to AI capabilities

### Skepticism About Motives
Community commentators suggest OpenAI's push for regulation may be driven by competitive concerns rather than genuine safety considerations, noting that both OpenAI and Anthropic face increasing competition from open-weight models.

## Broader Context

This blueprint arrives amid intensifying AI governance debates:

- **EU AI Act** implementation continuing
- **California SB 1047** (and successor legislation) debated
- **International governance** proposals at UN and G7 levels
- **Voluntary commitments** from major AI labs proving insufficient for critics
