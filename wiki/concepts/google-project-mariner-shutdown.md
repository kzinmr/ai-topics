# Google Project Mariner Shutdown

**Date**: May 2026

Google has quietly shut down **Project Mariner**, its Chrome-browsing AI agent designed to complete tasks on users' behalf. The project was originally highlighted on stage at **Google I/O 2025**.

## Background

Project Mariner was Google's entry into the browser automation agent space, competing with Anthropic's **[[entities/anthropic-computer-use]]** and other agentic browsing solutions. The vision was an AI agent that could navigate websites, fill forms, and complete multi-step tasks on behalf of users.

## Shutdown Reasons

The shutdown appears to be due to:
- **Poor performance**: Low task completion rates in real-world usage
- **High operational costs**: Expensive to run at scale
- **Technical limitations**: Browser-based agents face fundamental challenges with reliability and security

## Context

This shutdown is part of a broader pattern in 2026 where early browser automation agent experiments are being reevaluated:
- [[entities/browserbase]] continues to operate in this space
- [[entities/anthropic-computer-use]] faces similar reliability challenges
- [[concepts/agentic-browsing]] as a category is proving harder than expected

The shutdown suggests that **agentic browsing** may require fundamentally different approaches than the initial "navigate and click" paradigm.
