# Content Engine

An AI-powered system for automating content creation, curation, and distribution at scale — combining LLM generation, editorial workflows, and multi-channel publishing into a unified pipeline.

## Definition / Core Idea

A content engine is the application of [[agentic-engineering]] principles to content production: instead of humans writing, editing, and distributing content manually, AI agents handle the full pipeline from research → drafting → editing → publishing → analytics, with humans in supervisory roles.

## Core Components

### 1. Research & Ingestion
- RSS/feed monitoring (see [[blogwatcher]])
- Trending topic detection
- Source aggregation and deduplication

### 2. Drafting & Generation
- LLM-powered article/blog generation
- Style adaptation to match brand voice
- Multi-format output (blog, social, newsletter, video scripts)

### 3. Editing & Quality Control
- AI-assisted editorial review
- Fact-checking against source materials
- SEO optimization

### 4. Distribution & Analytics
- Multi-channel publishing automation
- Performance tracking
- Feedback loop for content optimization
