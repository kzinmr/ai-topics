# Open Model Consortium

## Overview

The **Open Model Consortium** is a proposed organizational structure for funding and developing frontier-level open-weight AI models through shared investment from multiple companies, rather than relying on a single well-funded lab. The thesis is that open models are a public good that requires collective funding to remain competitive with closed-source frontier labs.

## Key Players

### NVIDIA Nemotron Coalition
NVIDIA launched the **Nemotron Coalition** — a global collaboration of model builders and AI labs working to advance open, frontier-level foundation models through shared expertise, data, and compute. Inaugural members include:
- Black Forest Labs
- Cursor
- LangChain
- Mistral AI
- Perplexity
- Reflection AI
- Sarvam
- Thinking Machines Lab

Nathan Lambert (Interconnects AI) views this as NVIDIA's attempt to "bankroll and bootstrap this approach within a single wealthy company" but argues a true multi-company consortium is the only long-term stable path.

### Marin (Stanford)
Led by Percy Liang, Marin is an **open lab for building foundation models — together**. They're training powerful models from scratch and sharing every step programmatically: code, data, experiments, mistakes — all in real-time.

### Arcee AI
A startup taking the most pragmatic approach to monetizing open models. With experience in post-training open models for specific customer domains, they realized they needed to both prove themselves and fill a niche by pretraining larger, higher-performance open models built in the U.S.A.

## The Business Case for Open Models

2025 was the year companies started taking open models seriously as a path to influence in the AI ecosystem — massively accelerated by **DeepSeek R1's breakout success**. Most of this is being done as a mission of "hope, principle, or generosity." Very few businesses have a real monetary reason to build open models.

Nathan Lambert identifies the emerging pattern:
> *"There will eventually be a consortium of companies funding a foundational set of open models used across industry. It's not clear when this'll emerge, and Nemotron Coalition is Nvidia's attempt to bankroll and bootstrap this approach within a single wealthy company, but a consortium is the only long-term stable path."*

## Connection to Open-Source Philosophy

George Hotz (geohot) argues from a different angle:
- **Closed Source AI = Neofeudalism** — API access without weight sharing is feudalism
- **Share the science** — "Science never credits the first guy who came up with an idea, it credits the guy who published"
- Open-source AI is "anti-feudal," not anti-safety
