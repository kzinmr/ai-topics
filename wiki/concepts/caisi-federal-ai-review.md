# CAISI Federal AI Review

The **Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI)** is the U.S. federal government's primary agency for pre-deployment evaluation of frontier AI models, housed within the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) under the Department of Commerce. It represents the institutionalization of government AI safety testing — transitioning from ad-hoc informal arrangements to formal, agreement-based pre-deployment review.

## What CAISI Is

- **Full Name**: Center for AI Standards and Innovation
- **Parent Agency**: NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology), Department of Commerce
- **Director**: Chris Fall
- **Commerce Secretary**: Howard Lutnick
- **Established**: August 2024 (as US AI Safety Institute); renamed/restructured under Trump administration
- **Designated Role**: Industry's primary point of contact within the U.S. government for AI testing, collaborative research, and best practice development

## What CAISI Does

### Pre-Deployment Evaluation
Tests AI models **before public release** for national security risks:
- **Cybersecurity**: Autonomous vulnerability discovery, exploit generation
- **Biosecurity**: Enabling biological weapons development
- **Chemical weapons**: Enabling chemical weapons development

### Post-Deployment Assessment
Ongoing testing after models are publicly deployed to catch emergent capabilities and safety issues.

### Testing in Classified Environments
Evaluations can occur in classified settings. Developers frequently provide models with **reduced or removed safeguards** to enable thorough capability assessment.

### TRAINS Taskforce
An interagency group of experts focused on AI national security concerns. Participates in evaluations and provides regular feedback through CAISI-convened coordination.

## Track Record

- **40+ evaluations completed** as of May 2026, including on state-of-the-art unreleased models
- Agreements are drafted with flexibility to rapidly adapt to continued AI advancements

## Why It Matters

### The Mythos Catalyst
Anthropic's [[concepts/claude/mythos|Claude Mythos]] model demonstrated unprecedented autonomous vulnerability discovery capabilities in early 2026. This created an ad-hoc "informal licensing" situation where the government informally asked Anthropic not to release broadly — highlighting the absence of a formal evaluation framework.

### From Informal to Institutional
CAISI transforms ad-hoc government-industry AI safety coordination into formal agreement-based evaluation. This represents a middle path between:
- **Laissez-faire**: No government AI oversight (risks missing catastrophic capabilities)
- **Heavy regulation**: Licensing regime (risks stifling innovation)

### International Dimension
Microsoft's simultaneous agreement with the UK AI Security Institute (May 5, 2026) suggests a pattern of bilateral evaluation agreements between AI labs and allied governments. The U.S. and UK evaluation frameworks may converge or interoperate.

### White House Escalation
The New York Times reported that the White House is separately considering its own AI vetting system and potential executive order, indicating that CAISI may be just one piece of a broader federal AI governance architecture.
