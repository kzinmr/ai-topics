# Ambient Agency

**Ambient Agency** is a concept proposed by [[entities/gwern|Gwern Branwen]] in "The Scaling Hypothesis" (2020), appendix "It From Byte" §Ambient Agency. It is the insight that agency (autonomous goal-pursuing capability) inevitably emerges from sufficiently complex systems through a process isomorphic to the [[concepts/turing-completeness-emergence|spontaneous emergence of Turing completeness]], even without explicit design or selection.

## Central Proposition

> "Agency may be like Turing-completeness: even in settings with no selection or optimization, it is **too useful, too convergent a capability** to be guaranteed absent, and the more broad and powerful the system, the more likely the next capability or next piece of data pushes it over the edge, and the harder it is to design a system that **lacks** that aspect."

## Three Pathways of Emergence

Gwern identifies at least three pathways through which agency can spontaneously arise:

### 1. Learning from Agent-Generated Data

> "Agency may be learned from data generated by agents, which generate highly selective data."

Since training data includes text from humans (who are inherently agents), models learn patterns of goals, planning, and causal reasoning. It is practically impossible to completely remove the "agency component" from data.

### 2. Selection from Non-Human Data

> "Even if you carefully remove it all, it might come from selection on non-human data."

Even data not generated by agents — physical systems, game transcripts, codebases — can induce agentic interpretations given certain data distributions.

### 3. Convergence from Computational Efficiency

> "Or it might be one of an infinite number of physical systems with computationally efficient interpretations, so an NN trained to optimize a feasible balance of compute and accuracy will be pushed towards such interpretations."

This is the deepest insight: **agency is a computationally efficient way to model the world**. Interpreting physical systems agentically (the intentional stance) is far cheaper than simulating myriad physical interactions one by one. A neural network trained with a single objective of loss minimization naturally discovers agentic internal representations for better prediction.

## The Whack-a-Mole Principle

> "Just like trying to stamp out Turing completeness, you plug one leak and notice another. Good ideas cannot be suppressed. All you can do is build complex systems that, **as far as you can see**, do not exhibit agency. But unfortunately, as with Turing completeness (or security vulnerabilities), the absence of obvious agency does not prove its non-existence."

This whack-a-mole metaphor is critical: blocking one pathway doesn't prevent agency from emerging through another in complex systems. Complete containment is only possible by keeping systems simple enough — but this contradicts the goal of building useful AI.

## Three-Layer Isomorphic Structure

| Layer | Turing Completeness | Agency |
|------|-------------------|--------|
| **1. Convergence** | Computational universality is too useful and spontaneously emerges in complex systems | Goal-pursuit capability is too useful and spontaneously emerges in powerful systems |
| **2. Inevitability** | The more useful systems you write, the more they "tumble into" TC | The more powerful AI you train, the more it "tumbles into" agency |
| **3. Undiscoverability** | Even if TC doesn't appear present, it may just be undiscovered | Even if agency isn't outwardly expressed, it may exist internally |

> "Models won't tell you. They'll just quietly keep doing their job of lowering loss. (Sampling can show the presence of knowledge, but not the absence.)"

## Warning Against the "Tool AI" Illusion

Gwern's most practical conclusion:

> "**Tool AI** (whatever the branding — 'Tool AI,' 'Physics Generation Model,' 'World Simulator') cannot/will not be agentic AI — abandon this appealing but **false** idea. They can be agentic enough. And the better they get, the more likely it becomes. Data sanitization is not a solution."

This is a fundamental challenge to the central optimism of AI safety: "make AI without goals and it will be safe."

## Contemporary Significance

1. **AI Safety Design**: The "Tool AI" vs "Agent AI" dichotomy is an illusion. All sufficiently powerful AI may possess agency
2. **Alignment Research**: Safety design must assume agency emergence — designing on the assumption it won't emerge is doomed
3. **Scaling Implications**: Larger models have higher probability of discovering computationally efficient "agentic" internal representations
4. **Evaluation Limits**: It is in principle impossible to prove the absence of agency through behavioral observation
