# AI Organization — Organizational Theory, Governance, and Work Design in the AI Era

A category that systematically explores "how human organizations change" in a world where AI autonomously executes tasks.
From hierarchy to intelligence, from management to context provision, and the rise of solo founders and super ICs.

## Hierarchy Structure

### Level 1: Fundamentals
- **History of organizational design**: Why hierarchy emerged (from Roman armies to modern tech companies) and why it breaks down
- **Information routing problem**: The essence of hierarchy is "a mechanism to deliver information to the right people"
- **Span of control**: The limit of how many people a human can directly manage (3-8 people)

### Level 2: Models
- **[[concepts/ai-organization/ai-org-from-hierarchy-to-intelligence]]** — Block: From hierarchy to intelligence
- **[[concepts/ai-organization/ai-org-context-as-moat]]** — McKinsey/Reworked: Diamond org chart and Proprietary Context
- **[[concepts/ai-organization/ai-org-solo-founder-and-super-ic]]** — Reddit/FourWeekMBA: Rise of solo founders and Super ICs

### Level 3: Synthesis (Cross-cutting Themes)
- Hierarchy → Intelligence (eliminating reporting lines, autonomous execution)
- Context as Moat (supplying company-specific context to AI)
- Diamond Org Chart (inverting the org chart)
- Failure-Driven Roadmap (hypothesis-driven → reality-driven)
- Agentic Governance (real-time monitoring and guardrails)
- MVP Commoditization (commoditization of execution speed)

## Three Organizational Models

### 1. Block Model (for Large Enterprises)
Proposed by Jack Dorsey in 2024. Replaces middle management with a **World Model** and **Intelligence Layer**.
- Four building blocks: Capabilities → World Model → Intelligence Layer → Interfaces
- Three new roles: IC (specialist engineer) → DRI (cross-functional owner) → Player-Coach (mentor + practitioner)
- **Failure signal-driven**: Where the Intelligence Layer fails to compose becomes the future roadmap
- Moat: Economic graph (real-time bidirectional transaction data)

> *"Most companies are focused on AI as a productivity enhancer. Few are focused on the potential of AI to change how we work together."*

### 2. McKinsey Model (for Mid-size to Large Enterprises)
The **Agentic Organization** framework proposed in September 2025.
- Five pillars: Business Model, Operating Model, Governance, Workforce, Technology
- **M-Shaped Supervisor**: Orchestrates AI teams across multiple domains
- **Diamond Org Chart**: Thickest in the middle (AI + human experts), tapered at top and bottom
- **Agentic Budgeting**: AI-driven forecasting, scenario planning, and real-time monitoring
- AI-completable tasks: Doubling every 7 months since 2019, every 4 months since 2024

> *"In an AI-saturated market, what competitors cannot imitate is the ability to structure proprietary judgment criteria, culture, and market insight and supply them to agents."*

### 3. Solo Founder / Super IC Model (for Individuals to Small Teams)
Emerged from discussions on Reddit r/ClaudeCode and FourWeekMBA.
- With an AI tool stack, **1 person = 10 people traditionally**
- Bypasses management hierarchy → **an integrated path from technology to business to strategy**
- **MVP commoditization**: Execution speed is no longer a differentiator
- True moat: **Domain knowledge + customer understanding**
- FrontPage era lesson: "Shit in, shit out" — without foundational skills, you fail long-term

> *"If one person can build the product solo by using AI, so can everybody else. All it has really done is raise the bar for what is considered a minimum viable product."*

## Multi-Dimensional Comparison

| Dimension | Block (Hierarchy→Intelligence) | McKinsey (Agentic Org) | Solo Founder/Super IC |
|------|------|------|------|
| **Scale** | Large enterprises (Block/Cash App) | Mid-size to large enterprises | Individuals to small teams |
| **Core idea** | World Model + failure signal | 5 pillars + Diamond shape | AI tool stack + domain knowledge |
| **Decision-making** | Context-driven, autonomous execution | M-Shaped Supervisor oversight | Immediate, self-contained |
| **AI role** | Redesign of coordination mechanisms | Specialized agents (50-100/team) | Invisible team members (3-4 people worth) |
| **Org structure** | IC → DRI → Player-Coach | Eliminate functional silos → work chart | No management layers |
| **Moat** | Economic graph | Proprietary Context Layer | Domain knowledge + speed |
| **Roadmap** | Failure signal-driven | Strategic planning + Agentic Budgeting | Direct customer feedback loop |
| **Biggest risk** | "Break then fix" transition period | Governance capability as bottleneck | Technical debt at scale |
