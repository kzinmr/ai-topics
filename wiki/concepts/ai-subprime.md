# AI Subprime Crisis

Ed Zitron's analysis drawing parallels between the 2008 subprime mortgage crisis and the current AI industry economics.

## Key Thesis

The AI industry mirrors the 2008 housing crash through **deceptive pricing, easy debt, and inflated valuations**:

- **Teaser Rates**: AI subscriptions mask actual compute costs (token burn) behind flat monthly fees
- **Universal Credit Expansion**: Subsidies span consumers, startups, and enterprises
- **Illusion of Value**: VC and cheap debt artificially inflate AI demand and valuations
- **Regulatory Denial**: Industry leaders dismiss warnings about unsustainable economics

## Critical Financial Data

| Entity | Key Metric | Status |
|--------|------------|--------|

| US Data Centers | $178.5B Debt Raised (2025) | 200GW projects announced, only 5GW built |
| AI Industry Revenue | ~$65B (2025) | Entirely subsidy-dependent |

## 2008 Parallels

1. **Adjustable Rate Mortgages** → Flat AI subscription pricing
2. **Housing Price Inflation** → AI valuation inflation
3. **Credit Expansion to All Buyers** → AI subsidies across all segments
4. **Regulatory Dismissal** → Industry denial of economic unsustainability
5. **Eventual Rate Hikes** → AI companies implementing punitive rate limits and price increases

## Implications

- AI companies face similar structural risks as 2008 mortgage lenders
- When subsidies end, demand may collapse dramatically
- Infrastructure debt ($178.5B in data center financing) at risk
- Potential for cascading failures across AI ecosystem
