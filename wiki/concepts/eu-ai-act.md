# EU AI Act

The **EU AI Act** (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) is the world's first comprehensive AI regulation, establishing a risk-based framework for artificial intelligence systems in the European Union.

## Timeline

| Date | Milestone |
|---|---|
| August 1, 2024 | Regulation entered into force |
| February 2, 2025 | Prohibited AI practices and AI literacy obligations applied |
| August 2, 2025 | GPAI model obligations and governance rules applied |
| **August 2, 2026** | **Majority of rules come into force (high-risk AI systems, transparency)** |
| December 2, 2027 | Certain high-risk AI obligations (Digital Omnibus provisional) |
| August 2, 2028 | Product-integrated high-risk AI systems (lifts, toys, etc.) |

## Key Provisions

### Risk Classification
- **Unacceptable risk**: Banned outright (e.g., social scoring, real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces with limited exceptions)
- **High risk**: Strict obligations (biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment, migration, law enforcement)
- **Limited risk**: Transparency obligations (chatbots, deepfakes, emotion recognition systems)
- **Minimal risk**: No obligations (spam filters, video games)

### Penalties
- Up to **€35 million or 7% of global annual turnover** for most serious infringements
- Tiered structure based on violation severity

### GPAI Model Obligations
- General-Purpose AI model providers must comply with transparency and documentation requirements
- Applies to [[entities/openai|OpenAI]], [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]], [[entities/google|Google]], [[entities/mistral-ai|Mistral]], etc.

## Digital Omnibus Simplification (May 2026)
Political agreement reached May 7, 2026 to simplify implementation:
- Certain high-risk AI obligations postponed to December 2027
- Product-integrated high-risk rules postponed to August 2028
- Still requires formal adoption and publication

## Compliance Status (May 2026)
- **50%+ of organizations** lack even a basic AI system inventory
- Compliance becoming enterprise sales requirement, not just legal function
- UK taking lighter-touch approach via existing regulators (ICO, FCA, CMA)
