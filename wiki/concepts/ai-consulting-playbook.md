# The AI Consulting Playbook

A comprehensive framework for building and scaling an AI consulting practice, created by **Jason Liu** (@jxnlco), founder of [[jason-liu|567 Studios]] and creator of the Instructor library. The playbook codifies the transition from employee/IC to independent consultant, covering positioning, pricing, sales, and scaling.

## Core Thesis: The Consulting Flywheel

The central concept is a self-reinforcing loop:

> **Content → Calls → Proof → Content**

You write about problems you're solving. People read it and reach out. You help them. Their success becomes testimonials and case studies. Those generate more content and more inbound leads. The goal is to build a system where **you're never answering the same question twice**.

## The 7-Part Structure

### Part 1: Foundations — The Mindset Shift

**Vendor vs. Partner positioning:**

| Vendor Mindset | Partner Mindset |
|---|---|
| "How many hours will this take?" | "What is this worth to the client?" |
| Executes defined tasks | Shapes strategy and drives outcomes |
| Competes on price | Competes on value delivered |
| Focuses on deliverables | Focuses on business results |

**The Value Equation:**

$$Value = \frac{Dream\ Outcome \times Probability\ of\ Success}{Time \times Effort}$$

- **Dream Outcome:** Articulate their vision better than they can
- **Probability of Success:** Case studies, testimonials, guarantees
- **Time:** Compress timeline to results
- **Effort:** Make implementation "easy" for their team

**The Hierarchy of Business Focus:**
1. **Level 1: Trading Time for Money** — Individual contributor mindset
2. **Level 2: Delegation** — Team leader mindset
3. **Level 3: Capital Allocation** — Investor mindset; these are the target clients

**The 20/80 Axiom:** 20% of what you deliver drives 80% of the client's willingness to pay. That 20% is often the easiest for you. Lead with it.

### Part 2: Content & Lead Generation

**The "Never Answer Twice" Principle:**
| Instead of... | Do this... |
|---|---|
| Answering the same email question | Write a blog post, send the link |
| Giving the same advice on calls | Create a guide, use as pre-work |
| Explaining the same concept | Build a course module |
| Repeating yourself in Slack | Document in your knowledge base |

**Content as Strategic Friction:** Content acts as an automated filter. By the time a prospect reaches out, they already understand your perspective and have self-selected as a good fit. Adjust friction level based on availability:
- Low friction: Direct Calendly link
- Medium friction: Survey form before booking
- High friction: Prominent minimum engagement prices

**The Content Creation System:** Content is the "sawdust of expertise-based consulting" — a byproduct of your actual work.

Flywheel workflow: **Record → Extract → Test → Expand → Scale**
1. Record calls (Circleback, Granola)
2. Extract insights with AI (Claude/ChatGPT)
3. Post on social media (Low Investment)
4. Turn high-performing posts into blog articles (Medium Investment)
5. Turn validated winners into courses or books (High Investment)

**Writing as Sales Practice:** Writing is "cheap reps" for sales. Crafting headlines = opening calls, explaining complex ideas = articulating value, handling objections in text = responding to pushback live.

### Part 3: Pricing — Hourly to Value-Based

**Three Stages of Pricing Evolution:**
1. **Hourly Rates** ($150–$250/hr) — Easy to sell but caps income
2. **Retainers with Minimums** ($5K–$12K/mo) — Predictable income
3. **Value-Based Pricing** ($25K–$100K+) — Decoupled from time, based on outcomes

**The Power of Minimum Engagements:** Setting a floor ($7K–$10K to start) filters small projects, communicates scale, and increases deal size. Increase minimum by $1K–$2K every time a client says yes. If acceptance rate >80%, minimum is too low.

**The Efficiency Calculation:** Anchor price to the value of payroll/revenue you're optimizing. Example: 10 engineers @ $200K = $2M payroll. 20% efficiency gain = $400K value. Your fee: $40K (10% of value created).

**Risk Mitigation:** "I would rather work for free or work for a lot of money." Offer refunds as a feature (50% if expectations not met, 30-day guarantee). Refunds are rare but willingness to offer them justifies premium pricing.

### Part 4: Sales & Discovery

**Qualifying Buyers:**

| Qualified Buyer | Unqualified Buyer (Warning Signs) |
|---|---|
| Has real purchasing power | Focuses primarily on costs |
| Understands business metrics | Needs to "check with boss" |
| Controls resource allocation | Concentrates on technical details only |
| Discusses ROI naturally | Cannot discuss ROI meaningfully |
| Makes decisions independently | Wants free work "to prove yourself" |

**The Klarna Lesson:** Initial conversations with PMs and data scientists focused on technical details — when presented with a proposal, they disappeared (not decision-makers). Months later, Klarna partnered with OpenAI at a much higher price point because real decision-makers saw different value: $100M potential savings, PR benefits, strategic positioning.

**Navigating Technical Co-founders:** They focus on technical elegance, not business impact. Solution: Speak with the CEO first, then get reintroduced to the technical team with business context already established.

### Part 5: Proposals — The Situational Assessment

**Why Situational Assessments Work:**

| Traditional Proposal | Situational Assessment |
|---|---|
| Leads with services | Leads with client situation |
| Price feels arbitrary | Price feels justified |
| Client thinks "is it worth it?" | Client thinks "they understand me" |
| Comparison-shopped on features | Differentiated by understanding |

**Five Components:**
1. **Situation Analysis** — Current state, pain points, costs
2. **Objectives** — Specific, measurable outcomes, timeline
3. **Values and Benefits** — Business and emotional outcomes
4. **Methodology and Accountability** — Your approach, responsibilities, milestones
5. **Options and Investment** — Multiple service tiers, fee structures, guarantees

**Co-Creating the ROI Model:** Never show up with an ROI model after the fact. Build it together during discovery. When clients agree on inputs, they rarely contest the outputs.

### Part 6: Engagement & Scaling

Engagement models, client retention strategies, and scaling beyond yourself. Covers how to structure ongoing relationships, expand within accounts, and build systems that don't require your direct involvement for every engagement.

### Part 7: Business Operations

Legal foundations, financial infrastructure, and practical getting-started guidance for establishing a consulting practice.
