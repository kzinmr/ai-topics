# Company AI-Pilled

A framework by **Khe Hy** (Khemaridh Hy, formerly of Rad Reads, now at Latour AI) for moving organizations beyond superficial AI usage into deep cultural and operational transformation. The term distinguishes between "using AI" (surface-level chatbot interaction) and being "AI-pilled" (fundamental operational change).

## Definition / Core Idea

There's a difference between "using AI" and being AI-pilled. One uses a chatbot on the web. The other changes how a company fundamentally operates.

## The AI-Pilled Maturity Model

### Level 1: AI User
- Employees occasionally use ChatGPT or other web interfaces
- No systematic integration into workflows
- Ad-hoc, individual experimentation

### Level 2: AI Adopter
- Company provides AI tool licenses (Cursor, Copilot, etc.)
- Some team-level standardization
- Still tool-focused, not process-focused

### Level 3: AI-Pilled
- AI usage is reflexive and baseline (see [[reflexive-ai]])
- Workflows redesigned around AI capabilities, not just augmented
- Hiring and role design assumes AI collaboration
- [[solo-founder-stack]] patterns applied internally

### Level 4: AI-Native
- Company structure and operations designed from ground up with AI
- New roles and organizational patterns emerge
- AI as core competitive advantage, not productivity multiplier

## Key Signals of Being AI-Pilled

1. **No AI tool spend limits** — friction removal for adoption
2. **AI-first hiring** — prove AI can't do it before hiring
3. **Cross-functional adoption** — support and revenue teams are fastest adopters, not just engineering
4. **Cultural shift** — "using AI well is a skill that needs to be carefully learned" (Tobi Lütke)
