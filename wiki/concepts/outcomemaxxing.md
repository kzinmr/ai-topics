# Outcomemaxxing

Outcomemaxxing is a business strategy and pricing philosophy championed by [[entities/sierra|Sierra AI]], advocating that enterprise AI companies should price by **outcomes delivered** rather than by consumption (tokens/API calls) or seats (per-user licenses). The term was coined by Elliot Greenwald at Sierra in a June 3, 2026 blog post, building on Sierra's December 2024 introduction of outcome-based pricing.

## Core Thesis

The central argument is that AI fundamentally disrupts both technology AND business models:

1. **Cost of raw intelligence is falling**: This is the clearest trend in technology. Software priced on top of compute will compress with it.
2. **Outcomes don't compress**: A customer who did not churn, a home sold, or an insurance claim processed has the same value regardless of what the model costs.
3. **Incentives drive behavior**: Seat-based pricing incentivizes adding seats; consumption pricing incentivizes driving usage. Both tie you to a product surface that the underlying technology can erode.
4. **Outcome-based pricing aligns incentives**: The vendor's incentive is simply to get the job done — the architecture, model, or implementation can change freely.

## The Four-Quadrant Pricing Framework

Based on Ramanujam's *Charging for Intelligence*, Sierra uses a 2×2 matrix:

| | **Low Attribution** | **High Attribution** |
|---|---|---|
| **High Agency** | API/Infrastructure pricing (OpenAI, AWS) — usage-based, natural fit | **Outcome-based pricing** — Sierra's quadrant |
| **Low Agency** | Seat-based SaaS — classic model of last 15 years | Hybrid seats + consumption (Cursor) |

## Operational Implications

Outcome-based pricing rewires the entire company:

- **Sales, development, product, support, marketing** all focus on the job to be done
- **Failure is baked into the P&L**: If you don't deliver, attrition and margin take a hit in ways consumption/seat pricing don't expose
- **Complexity is real**: More complex operationally, contractually, and accounting-wise than seat/consumption models
- **Prerequisites**: Only works where the software is highly autonomous AND the outcome is cleanly attributable

## The Saaspocalypse Context

Since Sierra's first outcome-based pricing post (December 2024):
- **S&P 500**: Up ~30%
- **WCLD (WisdomTree Cloud Computing ETF)**: Down ~15%

This divergence reflects the market internalizing that the future is not productivity tools for teams but AI agents that deliver outcomes. The "Saaspocalypse" describes the collapse of the traditional SaaS business model as AI agents replace human users as the primary consumers of enterprise software.

## The Gold Rush Analogy

The current AI boom is compared to the 1849 Gold Rush: the gold (undifferentiated AI compute) won't be the enduring part. The enduring businesses will be those built to serve the rush — like Levi Strauss (dry goods for miners) and Wells Fargo (moving gold and mail), which survived because they created value around the rush, not by mining gold themselves.
