# High-Frequency Software

**High-Frequency Software (HFS)** is a framework proposed by Scott Stevenson (@scottastevenson, Spellbook Legal) that uses the shift from floor stock trading to high-frequency trading as an analogy to predict how the business of software will evolve when AI enables 10x faster software delivery.

## The Phase Shift

When a game speeds up by 10x, it's not the same game anymore — a phase shift occurs (like water turning to vapour). The skills that made you successful at the original game often don't translate.

> "The traits that made someone successful in 'the pit' trading stocks at the NYSE in the 1980s are a different universe from the prototypical quant building market-making algorithms."

## Ten Parallel Predictions

| HFT Dynamic | Software Equivalent |
|---|---|
| **Low conviction strategies** | Software companies become portfolios of bets, not singular missions. 100+ landing pages in weeks, not years. |
| **Latency optimization** | Respond to market within hours, not quarters. Prototype immediately on new customer request. "Comical speed" territory. |
| **Front running** | Detect competitor success and replicate 10x faster. Ramp executed this against Brex pre-AI. Now constant across AI software. |
| **Leverage as weapon** | Large rounds to immediately wipe out competitors. Already visible in legal AI (Spellbook's domain). |
| **Edge funds** | Startup + VC fund hybrids with radically low roundtrip latency. Broad mandate: "make money." |
| **New infrastructure** | "AWS for the AI era" — managing hundreds of apps, AI sales teams, AI agents interpreting market signals. |
| **Flash bubbles** | Runaway copycat feedback loops. 1 success → 100 copycats → competing for same ads, same customers. |
| **Order flow** | Who sees the "order flow" of opportunities first? Currently: large funds with portfolio visibility. |
| **Stealth returns** | When execution is 10x easier, ideas gain relative value. Founders become more secretive. NDAs return. |
| **Black box algorithms** | AI chip design, chess engines, trading algorithms — all illegible but trusted through backtesting. |

## Edge Funds: Startup + VC Hybrids

The most novel prediction: traditional VC fundraising (months) becomes the bottleneck when companies can output mature software in days. The solution is **edge funds** — entities that combine startup execution with fund capital allocation:

- Significant capital earmarked for unknown future opportunities
- Broader mandate than traditional startups: "make money"
- Radically lower roundtrip latency than VC funds (hours, not months)
- Able to identify and respond to market opportunities in minutes
- May operate with extreme privacy (like Renaissance Technologies' Medallion Fund)

The traditional VC model remains competitive in deep tech and hardware where development time dwarfs fundraising latency. But in pure software, edge funds represent a structural advantage.

## AI Fracking Kills Bootstrapping

Just as HFT algorithms "frack" alpha from every market crevice, well-capitalized AI-native companies will do the same to software niches:

- Previously, bootstrapped companies thrived in crevices too small for VC-backed companies
- AI enables VC-backed companies to deploy "100 AI tentacles" into micro-segments
- Real example: a company used AI to spin up over 1,000 integrations in a month — niches that previously supported entire bootstrapped businesses
- "Every SaaS company will become a threat to every other SaaS company"

## Backtesting in Software

Financial backtesting (testing strategies against historical data) has a software equivalent: testing AI product managers against historical customer feedback to see if they would have caught opportunities before humans did. Stevenson's team ran this experiment — in some cases, the AI PM beat them.

## Winner-Takes-All Acceleration

The VC power law amplifies: winners build a machine that wins hundreds of wars across many products and markets. The winners will look like Renaissance Technologies' Medallion Fund.

## Actionable Takeaways

- Set up automated product development workflows that identify opportunities and prototype them
- Build marketing infrastructure for simultaneous multi-experiment execution
- Start backtesting AI systems against historical information flow
- Focus on coding "the machine that builds the machine" — not individual product commits
