# Experience is Now a Tax

The thesis that traditional experience and seniority in knowledge work is becoming a liability rather than an asset in the AI era. Experienced workers may be more resistant to AI adoption, locked into legacy patterns, or over-optimizing for approaches that AI has made obsolete.

## Core Arguments

1. **Legacy Pattern Lock-in:** Senior engineers carry entrenched mental models that may not apply to AI-augmented workflows
2. **Unlearning Cost:** The "tax" of needing to unlearn old patterns vs. starting fresh with AI-native approaches
3. **Adaptability Premium:** Junior/novice approaches may be more adaptable to new AI tools and workflows
4. **Resistance to Change:** Experience correlates with comfort in existing systems, creating friction for AI adoption

## Evidence

The concept went viral in April 2026 (5,792 bookmarks, 2.83M impressions), indicating strong resonance with current debates about AI's impact on professional skill valuation. This aligns with [[reflexive-ai]] at Shopify, where Tobi Lütke advocates for "beginner's mindset" and hiring more beginners.

## Relationship to Reflexive AI

The "experience tax" is the individual-level manifestation of the organizational-level shift toward [[reflexive-ai]]. When AI becomes the baseline, those with the most to unlearn (senior workers) face the highest adoption cost.

## Critiques

- Experience still matters for system architecture, judgment, and edge cases
- The "tax" may be temporary during the transition period
- Some experienced workers adapt faster than juniors due to stronger foundational knowledge
