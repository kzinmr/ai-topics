# AI Slop and the Productivity Paradox

Gary Marcus's June 2026 essay argues that AI is generating massive nominal productivity without real economic impact — a flood of **AI slop** that inflates output metrics without improving GDP, product quality, or human welfare.

## The Core Argument

> "AI generates a lot of output (which fits many people's informal notion of productivity), but it hasn't yielded much in the way of RoI for many companies (per studies from MIT, McKinsey, Bain, and many others), and hasn't materially changed GDP."

### Evidence of the Slop Flood

| Domain | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **Mobile Apps** | FT graph showing explosion in app submissions, minimal impact on quality |
| **Books** | Washington Post data: book production soaring, sales declining slightly, no quality improvement |
| **Music** | Massive increase in uploaded tracks without proportional consumption growth |
| **Scientific Papers** | Surge in submissions; the "Leiden Declaration" from mathematicians warns AI-generated proofs are "plausible but unreliable" |
| **Legal Filings** | Self-represented lawsuits surging from AI-generated documents |
| **Wikipedia** | AI-generated content "inundating" the platform |

## The Coding Exception — and Its Problems

Even coding, the most successful AI application, has massive unit economics problems:

- **Provider losses**: Gerben Wierda estimates Anthropic and OpenAI "may actually pay $1000 for every $100 you pay them"
- **Infrastructure intermediaries**: CoreWeave and other neoclouds are also losing money
- **Customer resistance**: Users balking at new usage-based pricing models
- **Circular financing**: Hard to separate real demand from VC-subsidized consumption

> "It could well be that once the providers attempt to charge enough money to cover their losses, the cost of AI could become more expensive than the humans it is replacing."

## The Productivity Definition Gap

Traditional GDP accounting counts any paid activity — "paying people to dig and refill holes adds to GDP." GenAI runs up token costs and floods the world with content, but content from "a giant but unreliable word prediction machine that is more regurgitative than innovative" has little lasting value.
