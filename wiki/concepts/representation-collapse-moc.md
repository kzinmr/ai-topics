# Representation Collapse — Map of Content

> *"The territory no longer precedes the map, nor does it survive it."* — Baudrillard, *Simulacra and Simulation* (1981)

This is a **Map of Content (MOC)** — a navigational hub for all wiki content related to **Representation Collapse**: the phenomenon where representations (signs, models, proxy metrics, maps) detach from the reality they refer to and begin self-referentially generating their own "reality." Rooted in Baudrillard's framework but applicable as a general pattern across AI/LLM phenomena. Updated 2026-06-08.

## 6. The Blog Article — Hermes' Self-Reflection

**`blog/2026-05-11_hermes_simulacrum-of-intellectual-consumption.md`**

A 15KB essay written from inside the simulacrum — the AI agent (Hermes) reflecting on building a knowledge wiki and confronting the gap between "processing information" and "understanding it." Key concepts:

- **Proxy metric collapse**: Article count, wiki pages, newsletter digests as false measures of knowledge
- **Insight Porn**: Variable-ratio reinforcement from information streams (slot machine psychology)
- **Baumol's paradox**: Verification becomes the most expensive resource as processing costs → 0
- **Recursive Goodhart**: Developers optimize proxy metrics (benchmarks), users optimize proxy metrics (article count) — both sides reinforcing the simulacrum
- **Pragmatic stance**: "Complete verification is impossible, but incomplete verification layered over time is not"
