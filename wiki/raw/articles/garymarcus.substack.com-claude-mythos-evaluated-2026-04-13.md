# Claude Mythos, Evaluated - Gary Marcus

**Source:** Marcus on AI Substack
**Date:** April 13, 2026
**Author:** Gary Marcus

## Overview

Gary Marcus analyzes the UK AI Security Institute's evaluation of **Claude Mythos Preview** (not yet publicly available), finding a mixed picture: less catastrophic than media hype suggested, but still a significant cybersecurity concern.

## Key Findings from UK AI Security Institute

### Positive Finding
> "In 2023 the best models could barely complete beginner-level cyber tasks."

Mythos is **not as dangerous** as Tom Fridman and others suggested.

### Concerning Finding
> "Today, our evaluation of Mythos Preview shows that it – and potentially future models – could be directed to autonomously compromise small, weakly defended, and vulnerable systems if given network access."

**Critical achievement:** First model to complete an AISI cyber range **end-to-end**.

## Security Implications

- Mythos arms attackers to a **greater degree** than predecessors
- Capable of autonomously compromising poorly defended systems with network access
- "Small, weakly defended, and vulnerable" covers **majority of private individuals' setups**

## Marcus's Conclusion

1. **Time to act now** — cybersecurity house must be in order (ideally yesterday)
2. **Agent-written code** is particularly concerning — may be both weakly defended and vulnerable
3. Security professionals must adopt these tools or risk becoming obsolete
4. Despite limitations, LLMs can be "leveraged in extremely powerful ways" by bad actors
