# Companies Are Just a Graph of Algorithms

**Author:** Daniel Miessler
**Date:** May 6, 2024
**Source:** [danielmiessler.com](https://danielmiessler.com/blog/companies-graph-of-algorithms)

---

## Core Thesis
All businesses can be understood as interconnected collections of algorithms (steps/processes). AI will soon be used to map, analyze, and continuously optimize these algorithm graphs, dramatically reducing human labor and waste—whether companies are ready or not.

---

## Key Excerpts

> "Explainability is the new currency."

> "AI excels at both discrete task execution and determining how things fit together, and every single one of your company's workflow components becomes ripe for optimization or elimination."

> "You've probably never seen your company in this way, but AI soon will."

> "No matter how special the product or company, it still operates as a pipeline of steps."

> "The only way out of this is through."

---

## 1. Everything Is an Algorithm
- **Business as a sequence:** Even a simple company (e.g., *Memories*: upload → scan/repair → stylize → caption → download/ship) is a series of steps—an algorithm.
- **Nested algorithms:** Each step can be broken down further (e.g., "Upload" itself contains sub-steps), creating a fractal of algorithms.
- **Beyond core operations:** The full business graph includes hiring, taxes, marketing, support, etc.

---

## 2. Graph of Algorithms as a Mental Model
- Visualizing a company as a **graph** (nodes = processes, edges = handoffs) reveals:
  - All components and their relationships
  - Opportunities for optimization or elimination
- Even pre‑AI (2022), such mapping gave competitive advantage.

---

## 3. AI + Transparency = Optimization
- **AI's dual strength:**
  1. Execute discrete tasks
  2. Understand how components fit together
- **Transparency is fuel:** Once all workflows are made explicit, AI can:
  - Detect waste
  - Spot redundant teams
  - Identify processes that can be eliminated or merged
  - Continuously monitor and improve

> "Transparency opens the door for optimization."

---

## 4. The Coming Consultancy Wave
Firms like **Accenture, KPMG, McKinsey** will pitch AI‑driven transparency/optimization:

1. Exhaustive automated + manual interviews across the business
2. Map all workflows (automated and human)
3. Find waste, redundancy, ineffectiveness
4. Propose a leaner company with fewer humans

Result: a permanently **smaller, tighter** organization.

---

## 5. Continuous Optimization Loops
After initial mapping, AI systems will perpetually interrogate each department:

- "How many humans? How many emails? Why monthly idea cycles? Why so long from idea to campaign? Who writes the copy? Who sends the emails?"

This applies to **every department**—marketing, support, HR, hiring, etc.

---

## 6. No Exceptions
- Complexity only means a **larger graph**—not a different kind of problem.
- AI excels at explaining and navigating large graphs, so no business model is immune.
- **Any human process** can be broken into steps ready for AI optimization.

---

## 7. Emotional Fallout and Advice
- **Acknowledge the shift:** It's not about scaring you—it's about preparation.
- **Upside:** Businesses become vastly more efficient → more output, new innovations, easier for newcomers to start companies.
- **Downside:** Many easily automatable jobs will be lost.
- **Action:** Start viewing your own company (or job) as a graph of algorithms. Anticipate what AI will recommend before it does.

> "The only way out of this is through."

---

## Summary of Key Takeaways

1. **Business = Graph of Algorithms**
   Every company can be decomposed into interconnected steps.

2. **Mapping Enables Optimization**
   Once articulated, waste and improvement opportunities become obvious.

3. **AI Accelerates Everything**
   AI will make this mapping and continuous optimization standard practice; companies can't resist the efficiency gains.

4. **Prepare Now**
   Business owners and employees alike must understand their algorithmic footprint and adapt.

5. **Think Ahead**
   Model your organization as a graph and anticipate AI‑driven recommendations to stay competitive.
