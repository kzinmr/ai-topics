# Three Observations

- **Author:** Sam Altman
- **Source:** https://blog.samaltman.com/three-observations
- **Published:** 2025-02-09
- **Topics:** AGI, Scaling Laws, AI Economics, AI Agents, Societal Impact

## Summary

Sam Altman outlines three core economic observations about AI development: (1) intelligence scales logarithmically with resources, (2) AI costs drop ~10x every 12 months, and (3) the value of linearly increasing intelligence is super-exponential. He discusses implications for AI agents, the economy, and society.

## Three Observations

### 1. Logarithmic Scaling Law
> "The intelligence of an AI model roughly equals the log of the resources used to train and run it."

- Resources include training compute, data, and inference compute
- Gains are continuous and predictable over many orders of magnitude
- Scaling laws hold true and you can spend arbitrary amounts for predictable gains

### 2. Hyper-Deflationary Costs
> "The cost to use a given level of AI falls about 10x every 12 months, and lower prices lead to much more use."

- GPT-4 (early 2023) → GPT-4o (mid-2024): ~150x price drop per token
- This pace far exceeds Moore's Law (2x every 18 months)
- Lower prices drive dramatically more usage

### 3. Super-Exponential Value
> "The socioeconomic value of linearly increasing intelligence is super-exponential in nature."

- Justifies exponentially increasing investment in AI
- No reason to expect investment to stop in the near future

## AI Agents Vision

- Starting to roll out AI agents as "virtual co-workers"
- Software engineering agent: capable of tasks typical of a junior engineer (up to few days)
- Impact comes from deploying thousands or millions across knowledge work
- Economic parallel: AI as the transistor — foundational, invisible, pervasive

## Societal Implications

- Human agency becomes more valuable (resilience, determination, adaptability)
- AGI as "the biggest lever ever on human willfulness"
- Scientific progress expected to be the most transformed area
- Cost of many goods falls dramatically; land and luxury goods rise
- Policy tension: individual empowerment vs authoritarian control
- Suggests "compute budget" for every person on Earth
- 2035 vision: everyone can marshal intellectual capacity equivalent to everyone in 2025

## Key Quotes

> "Anyone in 2035 should be able to marshall the intellectual capacity equivalent to everyone in 2025; everyone should have access to unlimited genius to direct however they can imagine."

> "AGI will be the biggest lever ever on human willfulness, and enable individual people to have more impact than ever before, not less."
