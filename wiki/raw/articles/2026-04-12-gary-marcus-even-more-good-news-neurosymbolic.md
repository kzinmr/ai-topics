# Even more good news for the future of neurosymbolic AI

Gary Marcus (Apr 12, 2026)

## Key Quotes

> "What the Apple paper shows, most fundamentally, regardless of how you define AGI, is that LLMs are no substitute for good well-specified conventional algorithms."

> "On the 3-block task, the neuro-symbolic model achieves 95% success compared to 34% for the best-performing VLA. The neuro-symbolic model also generalizes to an unseen 4-block variant (78% success), whereas both VLAs fail to complete the task."

> "LLMs are an efficient way to pattern recognition where perfect results are not required, but an inefficient way to reason a plan. Different tools for different jobs."

> "What we have now are lights into the future, not full answers."

> "As a society we could spend another trillion dollars pursue LLMs, or a trillion dollars seeking new, hybrid approaches. I for one know which I would pick."

## Research Cited

1. **Apple (2025)** — "The Illusion of Thinking": LLMs pass small-scale Tower of Hanoi but fail as disk count increases
2. **Tufts (Feb 2026)** — Duggan, Lorang, Lu, Scheutz: Extended findings to Vision-Language-Action (VLA) robotics models
   - Neurosymbolic: 95% (3-block), 78% (unseen 4-block)
   - Best VLA: 34% (3-block), 0% (4-block)
   - Energy efficiency: ~100x better for neurosymbolic
3. **The Mirage of Reasoning** — Stanford's taxonomy of LLM reasoning errors
4. **The Illusion of the Illusion** — AI-generated flawed counter-critique of Apple's paper

## Marcus's Position

- Current AI (e.g., Claude Code v4.6) is impressive but error-prone
- Best treated as assistive tools, not AGI
- Architecture-task matching: LLMs for pattern recognition, symbolic planners for logic/reasoning
- Society should pivot investment from pure LLM scaling to neurosymbolic hybrids
