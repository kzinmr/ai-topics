# Together AI: Introducing Provisioned Throughput (July 8, 2026)

## Summary

Together AI introduced Provisioned Throughput, a new inference form factor offering reserved capacity for frontier open models with token-based pricing and a 99% uptime SLA. Costs run up to 90% below Claude Opus 4.8 at list price.

## The Problem

Open weight models have become essential for companies wanting an abundant AI future. But historically, using open weight models meant choosing between:
- **Serverless inference**: Best-effort, convenient, but unpredictable for production workloads
- **Dedicated inference**: Guaranteed capacity but requires managing GPU-hour math, capacity planning, and serving stack configuration

What teams actually want: the same token-based capacity agreement they have with closed-model providers, pointed at frontier open models.

## What Provisioned Throughput Offers

- **Token-based pricing** with reserved capacity (not GPU-hour math)
- **99% uptime SLA** — production-grade reliability for open models
- **Pre-optimized models** — no serving infrastructure to configure
- **Global availability**: Capacity in North America, EMEA, and beyond
- **One-month minimum term**
- **Available for MiniMax M3 and GLM-5.2** initially

## Market Context

- Together AI's token volume grew from **30 billion to 400+ trillion tokens per month** in nine months
- Companies report **6-20x lower inference costs** on open models vs proprietary alternatives
- "A substantial share of that traffic is workloads that used to run on closed APIs"
- "Inference spend has become a line item the board asks about"

## Strategic Significance

This product bridges the gap between the convenience of serverless and the reliability of dedicated inference. As companies move to multi-model, multi-harness setups, open models are powering more everyday agents for coding, finance, marketing, and workflow automation.

The move signals maturation of the open-model inference market — from "best effort" to "production SLA" — and positions Together AI as an infrastructure layer for the open-model AI economy.
