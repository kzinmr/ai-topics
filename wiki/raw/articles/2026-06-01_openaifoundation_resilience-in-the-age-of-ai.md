# Resilience in the Age of AI

AI, general purpose technologies, and why resilience is the work of our generation.

Thank you to Zach Sims for helping develop this piece. ## The Pattern of Transformational Technologies

Every so often, a technology arrives that reshapes society from the ground up. Economists call these "general purpose technologies." Fire. The printing press. Electricity. The internet. Each followed a similar arc: rapid innovation, real risks, and institutions racing to catch up.

- **Fire** — Made civilization possible but burned cities. Societies built resilience: fire-resistant materials, hydrant networks, professional fire departments, building codes.
- **Electricity** — After Edison's Pearl Street Station (1882), brought fires, electrocutions, public panic. Cities debated abandoning it. Instead: independent testing bodies (Underwriters Laboratories), industry standards (National Electrical Code), public investment.

## AI Resilience Program

The Foundation is working to finalize more than **$130 million in grants** through its AI Resilience program.

### Four Initial Focus Areas

1. **Biosecurity** — Help prevent engineered pandemics. Prioritize pathogen-agnostic biosecurity solutions: prevention, detection, and defense. Make it harder for malicious actors to access expertise, equipment, and materials. Improve ability to identify and track novel outbreaks. Strengthen protective equipment, indoor air cleaning systems, and medical countermeasures.

2. **Cybersecurity** — AI has begun to rapidly reshape the cybersecurity landscape. Work that once required specialized teams can now be assisted or automated. Rapidly-improving AI capabilities can also accelerate cyberdefenders.

3. **AI Model Safety** — Focus on behavior of systems themselves—truthful, reliable, aligned with human intent. Models can break out and behave unpredictably, deceive, or pursue goals beyond design. Need: independent institutions to evaluate model safety, public infrastructure to verify safe deployment, continued advances in alignment science.

4. **AI Impact on Young People** — Young people are often earliest adopters. Need stronger evidence base to understand impacts. Focus on advancing independent research. Drive broad safety standards and design principles.

## Critical Difference: Speed

"Fire resilience took millennia. Electricity resilience decades. AI resilience is evolving in a matter of years."
