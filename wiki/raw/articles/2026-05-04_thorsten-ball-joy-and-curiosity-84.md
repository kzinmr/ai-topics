# Joy & Curiosity #84: Summary

Thorsten Ball's weekly digest covers the shifting landscape of software development, the "degradation" of GitHub, the future of AI in education, and technical breakthroughs in algorithms and video processing.

## The State of Software & GitHub

The industry is currently grappling with the transition out of the Zero Interest Rate Policy (ZIRP) era and the rise of AI agents.

- **Software Degradation:** Nic Barker suggests we are entering a period of "unprecedented software degradation" and frequent outages. This isn't just due to AI, but the culmination of issues "bubbling away at just below critical levels" during the ZIRP era.
- **The GitHub Exodus:** Mitchell Hashimoto (Ghostty) announced he is leaving GitHub after 18 years, citing a platform that no longer feels optimized for shipping software.
  > "I want to get work done and it doesn't want me to be there... I can't code with GitHub anymore." — Mitchell Hashimoto
- **The "Agentic" Pressure:** GitHub's recent instability is attributed to "unprecedented scale from agents." Internal sources claim the team is dealing with 30X-ing capacity within a few months.
- **The "Forge" Future:** Armin Ronacher notes that GitHub is losing its feeling of inevitability due to product churn and AI noise. Mat Duggan suggests a future where the local repo includes issues and PRs.

## AI, Education, and Strategy

- **Radical Adaptability:** Kevin Kelly argues that AI will bring 10-15 years of "perpetual, continuous, severe uncertainty." Treat goals as disposable hypotheses.
- **The Tacit Knowledge Gap:** Growing concern that the West is forgetting how to code. Junior engineers using AI skip formative mistakes and debugging sessions.
- **Mistral's Strategy:** Lagging behind US/Chinese models in benchmarks but betting on being a "sovereign," cheaper European alternative. Reportedly on track for $80 million monthly revenue by December 2025.

## Technical Insights & Tools

- **SIMD Quad Algorithm:** Daniel Lemire's algorithm beats binary search using parallelism.
- **Copy Fail:** A 732-byte Python script that roots every Linux distribution shipped since 2017.
- **CorridorKey:** Neural network tool for green screen footage unmixing.
- **Zed 1.0:** High-performance code editor reached 1.0.
- **macOS Internals:** SkyLight enables multi-cursor background agents.

## Philosophy & Productivity

- **Embracing Errors:** Brad Neely on preserving rough edges in art.
- **Extreme Focus:** "Staring at walls" as secular meditation for productivity.
