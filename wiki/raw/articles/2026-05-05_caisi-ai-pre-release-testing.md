# US Government AI Pre-Release Testing Agreements

On May 5, 2026, the Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) within NIST / Department of Commerce signed agreements with Microsoft, Google, and xAI to evaluate frontier AI models for national security risks before public release.

## Key Details
- **CAISI Director**: Chris Fall
- **Scope**: Pre-deployment evaluations for cybersecurity, biosecurity, chemical weapons risks
- **Signatories**: Microsoft, Google (DeepMind), xAI (Anthropic and OpenAI had agreements from 2024)
- **Trigger**: Anthropic's Claude Mythos (April 2026) — described as "far ahead" in cybersecurity, sparked concerns among governments, banks, utilities. Anthropic restricted access to approved organizations only.

## CAISI Background
- Established June 2025 under Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick
- Serves as primary US government hub for commercial AI testing
- Completed 40+ AI model evaluations
- Coordinates with DoD, DOE, DHS, OSTP, Intelligence Community
- Represents US interests internationally on AI standards

## Policy Context
- Under Biden (2024): focused on voluntary standards, definitions, testing at US AISI (later CAISI)
- Under Trump (2026): voluntary agreements with tech firms; considering mandatory reviews via executive order
- White House rethinking hands-off approach after Mythos raised cybersecurity alarm

## Related Evaluations
- CAISI evaluated DeepSeek V4 Pro (May 1, 2026) — open-weight model assessment
- Ongoing work: AI agent security red-teaming, evaluation methods, procurement standards with GSA
