# The Causal Backbone Conjecture
**Author:** tailcalled | **Date:** 2024-08-17 | **Platform:** LessWrong | **Tag:** AI | **Points:** 26  
**Context:** Follow-up to *[Rationalists are missing a core piece for agent-like structure]*, Related to *[Linear Diffusion of Sparse Lognormals]*, Response to *[Fixing The Good Regulator Theorem]*. Proposes an `"energy"-orientation` to build efficient models that `"zoom in on what really matters."`

---

## 🔑 Key Excerpts
> *"For events where there are causes which point in contradictory directions, the cause with more resources tends to win out."*

> *"The universe has a 'universal backbone' of causal dynamics that really matter, in the pathways of the resources, and the entities that can shift those pathways."*

> *"One needs to model the parts of this universal backbone that are directly relevant to oneself."*

> *"The assumption of a sufficiently rich set of tasks is just extremely disastrously wrong and will make you crash into information overload."*

> *"While it cannot be necessary to model everything that is going on, it will at least be necessary to model the biggest aspects of the flow of these resources, since that influences the big dynamics that do matter to you."*

## 📖 Comprehensive Summary

### Foundational Premises
- **Limited Resources:** The universe operates on finite constraints (mass, energy, space, time, money/capital). Resource abundance dictates causal dominance in conflicting scenarios.
- **Heterogenous Fluctuations:** Complex dynamics emerge from resource interactions. While most entities have negligible impact on long-term resource distribution, influence follows a **long-tailed distribution**. High-leverage actors include: the sun, Earth, biological life, humanity, society, capitalism, NVIDIA, Berkshire Hathaway, Saudi Aramco, Elon Musk, Eliezer Yudkowsky, and Sam Altman.

### The Conjecture
- **Resource Shifts & Selection:** Fluctuations trigger massive redistributions of resources. Entities dependent on outdated distributions that fail to adapt face extinction or severe decline.
- **The Universal Backbone:** A core network of causal pathways governs resource flow and high-leverage entities. Agents must model the backbone segments directly relevant to their survival/goals.
- **Interconnected Modeling Incentive:** Universal modeling of the backbone tightly couples its components. This creates a systemic pressure to model the **"top" of the backbone** (highest-leverage dynamics/entities) rather than isolated local branches.

### Distinction from Existing Theory
- **Critique of *The Gooder Regulator Theorem*:** Rejects the premise that regulators must model a `"sufficiently rich set of tasks"` to handle a system. This assumption is computationally flawed and leads to informational collapse.
- **Resource-Constrained Alternative:** Instead of exhaustive environmental modeling, agents should use **resource flow constraints** as a natural filter. Tracking major resource pathways is both necessary and sufficient for navigating high-impact dynamics.

---

## 🎯 Actionable Insights
- **Prioritize High-Leverage Nodes:** Focus modeling efforts on top-tier causal entities and major resource flows, not granular low-impact details.
- **Avoid Information Overload:** Reject exhaustive system modeling; use resource scarcity as a heuristic for relevance.
- **Dynamic Adaptation:** Continuously update models to track shifting resource distributions. Failure to align with the causal backbone leads to obsolescence.
