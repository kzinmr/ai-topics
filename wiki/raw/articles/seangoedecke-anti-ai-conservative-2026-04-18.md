# Many anti-AI arguments are conservative arguments

- **Source:** Sean Goedecke (seangoedecke.com)
- **Date:** 2026-04-18
- **URL:** https://seangoedecke.com/many-anti-ai-arguments-are-conservative/
- **Tags:** [ai-criticism, politics, ai-autonomy-debate]

## Summary

Sean Goedecke argues that the substance of most anti-AI rhetoric historically aligns with conservative/right-wing worldviews, despite being framed in progressive language. This is an inversion of the expected political coding.

## Core Thesis

Conservative-coded anti-AI arguments:
| Argument | Traditional Left Stance | Current Anti-AI Stance |
|----------|------------------------|------------------------|
| Copyright/IP | Historically anti-IP; views copyright as corporate control | Demands strict copyright enforcement against AI training |
| Art & Human Spirit | Embraces experimental/tech-driven art | Claims AI art is "corrosive to the human spirit" |
| New Tech Art | Champions innovation | Dismisses AI art as inhuman/cheap/degenerate |
| Job Preservation | Supports disruptive transitions | Opposes AI to protect existing jobs |

## Why Left-Coded Anti-AI Sentiment Exists

1. **Tech CEO Shift (2024):** Big tech's right-wing turn coincided with ChatGPT's emergence, tagging AI as "right-wing"
2. **Crypto Hangover:** AI viewed as continuation of pro-crypto mania
3. **Trump Backlash:** Trump's pro-AI stance triggered anti-Trump sentiment into anti-AI rhetoric
4. **Environmental Concerns:** Left focuses on AI's energy footprint
5. **Economic Anxiety:** Left-leaning groups blame economic hardship on new tech
6. **Workforce Demographics:** Left-wing over-represented in tech jobs threatened by AI
7. **European Skepticism:** Europe slower on AI adoption, Europe sentiment is left-coded

## AI's Political Bias
- Frontier LLMs consistently reflect left-wing biases in outputs
- Attempts to engineer right-wing AI (e.g., Grok) have struggled
- Training data naturally encodes progressive viewpoints

## Author's Position
Left-wing, agnostic on AI's net impact. Rejects copyright/human-soul arguments but concerned about environmental impact and job displacement.
