# Economy Chapter Summary

From the Stanford HAI 2026 AI Index Report. Analyzes economic footprint of AI across private sector and implications for labor markets.

## 1. Global Corporate AI Investment
- Investment more than doubled in 2025: private investment grew **127.5%**, now 60% of total
- Generative AI grew **200%+**, capturing nearly half of all private AI funding
- Newly funded AI companies: +71%; billion-dollar funding events nearly doubled

## 2. U.S. Investment Dominance
- U.S. committed **23× more than China** in private AI investment
- In generative AI, U.S. exceeded China + Europe combined
- China's government guidance funds have deployed ~$184B into AI firms (2000-2023) — understates China's total

## 3. Revenue Growth & Infrastructure Costs
- Leading frontier companies reaching meaningful revenue scale quickly
- Google reported **>$150B annual capex** in 2025
- Compute costs / infrastructure spending at record levels

## 4. Consumer Surplus
- Estimated U.S. consumer surplus: **$172B annually** (early 2026), up from $112B
- Median value per user **tripled** year-over-year
- Most tools remain free or close to free

## 5. Organizational Adoption
- **88%** of surveyed organizations adopted AI
- GenAI used in at least one business function at **70%** of organizations
- AI agent deployment: single digits across nearly all business functions

## 6. Generative AI Adoption Rate
- **53%** in 3 years — faster than PC or internet
- Singapore: 61%, UAE: 54%, U.S.: 28.3% (ranked 24th)
- Adoption correlates strongly with GDP per capita

## 7. Labor Market Effects
- Software developers ages 22-25: employment fell nearly **20%** from 2024
- **1/3** of employers expect workforce reductions in coming year
- Reductions highest in: service operations, supply chain, software engineering

## 8. Productivity Gains
- Customer support: **14-15%** gains
- Software development: **26%** gains
- Marketing output: **50%** gains
- Gains smaller in tasks requiring deeper reasoning
- Heavy AI reliance may carry long-term learning penalties

## 9. Industrial Robots
- China: **54%** of global industrial robot installations (up from 51.1%)
- Taiwan: highest YoY growth at 33%
- U.S., Germany, Italy: saw declines
