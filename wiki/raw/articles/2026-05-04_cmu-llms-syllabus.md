# CMU LLMs: Methods and Applications (Spring 2026)

**Institution:** Carnegie Mellon University, Language Technologies Institute (LTI)
**Focus:** Practical applications of LLMs covering the full lifecycle from prompting to deployment.

## Assignments
| Date | Assignment | Topics |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Jan 17 | #1 Released | Prompting, Finetuning, Retrieval, Embeddings |
| Feb 5 | #2 Released | Chatbots, Agents, Assistants |
| Mar 30 | #3 Released | Language Models beyond Natural Language |
| Apr 30 | Final Report Due | Project presentations |

## Lecture Modules

### Phase 1: Foundations & Core Techniques (January)
- Origins & Theory (Bengio 2003 → modern NLU vs Generation)
- Prompt Engineering: science of prompting, spurious feature sensitivity
- Finetuning: when to finetune vs other techniques
- Representations: embeddings and knowledge storage

### Phase 2: Retrieval & Advanced Interaction (February)
- RAG (3-part series): storing knowledge, deep research, advanced architectures
- Dialogue Systems: task-oriented, tool-use, persona, chitchat
- Creative AI: writing assistants, ideation
- Evaluation: LLMs as judges, synthetic data, simulation
- Multi-agent architectures

### Phase 3: Safety, Ethics & Specialized Domains (March)
- Safety & Security: harms, attacks (red teaming)
- Code: guest lecture (Zora Wang)
- Multimodal: image generation, vision-language
- Multilingual: non-English, non-American cultures (Shaily Bhatt)
- World Models (Mingkai Deng)

### Phase 4: Beyond Text & Deployment (April)
- Scientific: biology (Lei Li), music (Chris Donahue), math reasoning
- Physical AI: robots, embodied AI (Leena Mathur)
- Engineering: deployment strategies
