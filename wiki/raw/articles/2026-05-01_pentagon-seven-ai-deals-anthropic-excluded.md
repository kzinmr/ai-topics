# Pentagon AI Deals — 7 Companies for Classified Military Work

**Source:** The Guardian, CNBC, Reuters, NYT | **Date:** May 1, 2026
**URLs:**
- https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/may/01/pentagon-us-military-pairs-with-spacex-google-openai
- https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/01/pentagon-anthropic-blacklist-mythos-michael.html

## Summary

The Pentagon signed agreements with 7 AI companies (SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Nvidia, Reflection, Microsoft, AWS) for classified military AI work. All agreed to "any lawful use" clause. Anthropic excluded over its refusal to accept unrestricted military use terms.

### Seven Approved Companies
1. SpaceX (merged with xAI)
2. OpenAI
3. Google
4. Nvidia
5. Reflection AI (2-year-old startup, open-weight models, no public release yet, seeking $25B valuation)
6. Microsoft
7. Amazon Web Services

### Anthropic's Status
- Blacklisted as "supply chain risk" in February 2026
- Refused "lawful use" clause due to concerns about autonomous weapons and domestic surveillance
- Mythos model is a "separate national security moment" — DOD still wants access
- Dario Amodei met with White House; Trump says deal is "possible"
- Anthropic has sued the Pentagon in two courts
- Models reportedly used in Iran war and Maduro operation despite blacklist

### Budget Context
- $54B requested for autonomous weapons
- $961.6B total 2026 defense budget
- GenAI.mil: 1.3M personnel already using Pentagon's AI platform

### Reflection AI
- Founded ~2024 by former Google DeepMind researchers
- No public model released yet
- Seeking $25B valuation
- Backed by Nvidia and 1789 Capital (Donald Trump Jr. is partner)
- Mission: open-source models to counter Chinese AI (DeepSeek)
