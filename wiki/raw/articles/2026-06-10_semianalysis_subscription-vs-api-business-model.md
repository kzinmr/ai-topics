# SemiAnalysis — Subscription vs API Business Model for AI Labs

**Source:** X thread by [@SemiAnalysis_](https://x.com/SemiAnalysis_/status/2064815042374074396) (June 10, 2026)

## Thread (4 parts)

### 1/4
What's the better business model for an AI lab, subscription or API?

### 2/4
Recently, we purchased one of each Anthropic/OpenAI subscription plan and randomly ran long horizon coding tasks until we exhausted the weekly limit. It's widely believed that a $200/month plan maxes out at ~$2000/month worth of tokens (assuming API pricing). However, we found that the subscriptions are actually far more generous.

## Key Findings

- SemiAnalysis purchased **every tier** of both Anthropic and OpenAI subscription plans
- Ran **long horizon coding tasks** until weekly limits were exhausted
- Common belief: $200/month plan maxes at ~$2000/month in API-equivalent token value
- **Actual finding:** Subscriptions are "far more generous" than the $2000 assumption
- If both labs have **75% API gross margins**, subscription margins are significantly worse (implied negative or near-zero for heavy users)
- Labs face a dilemma: nerfing subscriptions → public backlash; but current utilization → unsustainable margins

## Strategic Implications

1. **Feature/model gating > usage capping**: Labs are more likely to withhold new models/features from subscription tiers rather than explicitly reduce usage limits
2. **Rapidly falling inference costs**: Opus 4.8-level models will be profitable at $20/month "in the near future"
3. **Mythos (Anthropic)** may launch as API-only — a test case for this strategy
4. **Subscription plans are loss leaders** for ecosystem lock-in, not direct profit centers
