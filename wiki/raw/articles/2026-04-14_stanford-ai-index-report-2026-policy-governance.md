# Policy & Governance Chapter Summary

From the Stanford HAI 2026 AI Index Report. Examines global AI regulation, public investment, and AI sovereignty trends.

## 1. Global Expansion of National AI Strategies
- In 2024, over **50% of newly adopted AI strategies** from emerging economies
- New strategies being developed across **sub-Saharan Africa, Central Asia, Middle East** (as of 2025)

## 2. AI Sovereignty Rise
- State-backed AI supercomputing clusters growth (2018-2025):
  - Europe & Central Asia: **3 → 44 clusters**
  - Middle East & North Africa: 8 clusters
  - Latin America: 3 clusters
  - South Asia: 2 clusters

## 3. Data Localization Divergence
- East Asia & Pacific: **77 data localization measures** (highest)
- Sub-Saharan Africa: 71 measures
- Europe/Central Asia: 66 measures
- North America: only **3 measures** (prefers open cross-border flows)

## 4. U.S. Legislative Trends
- AI witnesses in congressional hearings: **5 (2017) → 102 (2025)** — 20× growth
- Industry witnesses: **13% → 37%** (largest group, up from 13%)
- Academic witnesses: fell to **15%**

## 5. Public vs Private Investment
- U.S. public: ~$20.4B in contracts/grants (2013-2024 total)
- U.S. private: **$285.9B** in 2025 alone
- Europe public commitments (2013-2024): UK $1.6B, Germany $505M, France $320M
