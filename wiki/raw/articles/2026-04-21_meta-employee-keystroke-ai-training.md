# Meta to start capturing employee mouse movements, keystrokes for AI training data

**Exclusive** by Katie Paul and Jeff Horwitz, Reuters, April 21, 2026

Meta is installing new tracking software on U.S.-based employees' computers to capture mouse movements, clicks and keystrokes for use in training its artificial intelligence models, part of a broad initiative to build AI agents that can perform work tasks autonomously.

### Key Details

- **Tool name:** Model Capability Initiative (MCI)
- **Scope:** Runs on work-related apps and websites; takes occasional screenshots
- **Purpose:** Improve AI models in areas they struggle with — dropdown menus, keyboard shortcuts, human-computer interaction patterns
- **Broader initiative:** Agent Transformation Accelerator (ATA), re-branded from "AI for Work"

### Meta CTO Andrew Bosworth's Vision

> "The vision we are building towards is one where our agents primarily do the work and our role is to direct, review and help them improve."

Bosworth stated Meta would step up internal data collection as part of ATA efforts.

### Organizational Context

- Meta has been moving aggressively to integrate AI into workflows and reshape its workforce
- Employees urged to use AI agents for daily tasks
- Plans for 10% global layoffs announced
- Meta SuperIntelligence Labs team (MCI memo posted by staff AI research scientist)

### Privacy Concerns

- Experts warn employee surveillance raises privacy concerns
- Meta says safeguards in place, data will not be used for performance reviews
- Part of broader trend: companies using employee data as training material for AI agents
