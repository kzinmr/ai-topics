## Summary

David Rosenthal's blog post examines the growing gap between AI platform revenue and costs, documenting what he calls the "AI Affordability Crisis." The post argues that AI platforms have been massively subsidizing usage to drive adoption, but are now being forced toward price sanity as their burn rates become unsustainable.

## Key Arguments

### The Drug-Dealer's Algorithm

AI platforms have been running "the first one's free" — massively subsidizing usage to generate overwhelming demand, justifying massive investments in the hope that users would become addicted and later pay vastly higher prices.

### Subsidy Scale

Estimates vary, but typically AI platforms cost $8 to $14 to generate $1 in revenue. Key data points:

- **SemiAnalysis test**: A $200/month ChatGPT subscription can burn $14,000 in tokens; a $200/month Anthropic subscription can burn $8,000 in tokens
- **Implied subsidy**: Anthropic subsidizes enterprise customers up to 40x, OpenAI up to 70x
- **Gross margin impact**: With current subsidies, a user only needs to use 25% of their rate limit to have negative 25% gross margin

### The Transition to Token-Based Pricing

- **GitHub Copilot**: Microsoft paused new signups for student/paid individual tiers, moving toward token-based billing. Week-over-week costs nearly doubled since January.
- **Anthropic, OpenAI, Microsoft**: All three have now transitioned customers from subscriptions to token-based pricing.
- **Claude Code to Copilot CLI**: Microsoft reportedly canceling most Claude Code access for engineers, shifting toward Copilot CLI to rein in internal AI coding costs.

### IPO Context

US equity markets face three AI company IPOs (SpaceX, Anthropic, OpenAI), each losing tens of billions per quarter. After going public, they'll need to charge enough to generate returns on enormous capital investments.

### Comments of Note

- **Dan Davies** ("tokenalysis and john henry"): When large companies tell employees to use AI tokens wisely, "the game is up" — John Henry won against the steam hammer. If you need a human to decide on AI token allocation, mass redundancy predictions are gone.
- **Michael Dale**: Open weight models (like GLM 5.2) are closing the gap at a fraction of the cost — nothing prevents running the best open models on a Mac mini or AMD machine at energy cost after the first month.
