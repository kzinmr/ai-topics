# A Three- and a Four- Body Problem

Source: https://www.johndcook.com/blog/2026/04/08/artemis-1-apollo-12/

Last week
I wrote about the orbit of Artemis II. The orbit of Artemis I was much more interesting.
Because Artemis I was unmanned, it could spend a lot more time in orbit. The Artemis I mission took 25 days while Artemis II will take 10 days. Artemis I took an unusual path, orbiting the moon the opposite direction of the moon’s orbit around earth.
This video
by Primal Space demonstrates the orbit both from the perspective of earth and from the perspective of the Moon.
Another video
from Primal Space describes the orbit of the third stage of Apollo 12. This stage was supposed to orbit around the sun in 1971, but an error sent it on a complicated unstable orbit of the earth, moon, and sun. It returned briefly to earth in 2002 and expected to return sometime in the 2040s.
