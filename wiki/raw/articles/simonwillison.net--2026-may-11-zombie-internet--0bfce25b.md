# Your AI Use Is Breaking My Brain

**Simon Willison**, May 11, 2026

Link blog post referencing Jason Koebler's piece on the "Zombie Internet" — how AI writing online is becoming impossible to avoid, filtering it is mentally exhausting, and it's distorting regular human writing styles.

## Key concept: Zombie Internet

A term coined to describe the internet that is not just bots talking to bots (Dead Internet) or bots talking to people, but a more complex, insidious ecosystem:

- People talking to bots
- People talking to people
- People creating "AI agents" and instructing them to interact with people
- People using AI talking to people who are not using AI
- People using AI talking to other people using AI
- Influencer hustlebros teaching each other how to make AI influencers
- Automated YouTube channels and blogs spamming for money
- AI summaries of real books being sold as the books themselves
- Fake Reddit posts and comment threads with heartfelt advice from marketing firms

The "Zombie Internet" is distinguished from the "Dead Internet" theory by the presence of real humans still participating, but in an environment increasingly contaminated by AI-generated content and AI-mediated interactions.

## Impact on mental load

Filtering AI content from human content is becoming mentally exhausting. The boundary between human and AI writing is blurring, and even human writing styles are being distorted by exposure to AI-generated text.
