Why transcribe a YouTube video?
A YouTube video transcript transforms audio and video into a searchable, editable text script. For some users, that might mean they can read along with their favorite videos to enhance accessibility. For others, it might give you a great base to then develop other forms of content, like long-form articles or written how-to guides.
No matter if it’s for personal or professional use, there are a number of reasons you might transcribe a YouTube video:
Accessibility:
Highly accurate YouTube transcripts make video content significantly more accessible for people who are deaf or suffer from auditory loss. For content creators, uploading a transcript of your content as subtitles also means it becomes accessible to users, even when they watch without audio or in a noisy environment.
Searchability:
If you’re looking for specific quotes from a YouTube video or small elements, then you can use a full transcript to Ctrl-F exact phrases. Instead of skipping back and forth through a video trying to find the exact moment a user mentioned something, you’ll have a transcript to easily search through.
Content syndication and repurposing:
Marketing teams may want to reuse content from their company YouTube channels on other platforms. By downloading a YouTube transcript, you’ll have an entire text document to build out blog posts, newsletters, social media posts, technical documentation, or training materials from. It opens the door to scaled content repurposing.
Note-taking:
Students, researchers, journalists, or even professionals looking to learn can download a video transcript to highlight important sections and make notes. Being able to quickly convert a YouTube video into a transcript means you can organize written information however you’d like.
Many of these uses are about convenience. They all enable you to access a YouTube video in a different format, opening the door to alternative use cases from studying to finding quotes for a newspaper article.
An important note is that regardless of the actual use case of YouTube transcription you use, they all rely on highly accurate and detailed transcription. Final drafts that miss sections or fail to correctly write what someone said in a video quickly make this less effective. Always look to work with tools that offer the highest possible transcription accuracy.

How to transcribe a YouTube video: Step-by-step
For many years, manual transcription was the only way you could access video transcripts on YouTube. After YouTube created and enabled the video transcript feature, you could read through a transcript within YouTube. However, this only works for videos where creators have enabled captions. It also doesn’t offer a download option, reducing what you can do with the transcription.
AI transcription is the easiest and most consistent way of producing a high-quality YouTube transcript. You’ll be able to produce an entire script that you can download and then reuse for other purposes.

Let’s break these down in more detail.
Method 1: Transcribe a YouTube video with YouTube’s built-in transcription feature
For any video that has captions enabled, you’ll be able to generate a full transcript from the YouTube video’s description box.
Here’s how to do that on desktop and mobile.
On desktop
Below is a step-by-step process of transcribing audio from YouTube videos via YouTube on your desktop.
Open video:
Navigate to the YouTube video you want to create a transcript for.
Expand description box:
Click on ‘...more’ on a video’s description box to expand it to its full size.
Show transcript:
Click on the ‘Show transcript’ button within the bolded ‘Transcript’ section to automatically generate a YouTube transcript.
At the top of your screen, you’ll notice a Transcript box that progresses alongside the video. You can search within this and hop around to the specific moment where something was said. The only problem is you can’t download the transcript, meaning you’ll have to stay within the YouTube desktop window when using this feature.
On mobile
You can quickly generate a YouTube video transcript via the YouTube app for a video that has captions enabled. The process is almost identical to the desktop approach, with the only difference being you’ll stay inside the YouTube app the whole time.
Open video:
Inside the YouTube app, click on the video you want to watch and transcribe.
Expand description box:
Click on ‘...More’ within the description box, right below the video, to expand the entire description.
Transcribe:
If captions are enabled, you’ll see a ‘Transcription’ section with a button to ‘Show transcript’. Click that and an auto-generated transcript will appear just below your video.
Just like with the desktop version, you can jump around to find specific moments using the transcript.

Method 3: Transcribe a YouTube video to text manually
While the low WER and high accuracy of ElevenCreative mean you can automatically transcribe YouTube videos, some users may still want to manually transcribe their video clips. Especially if you’re working with short clips or want more flexibility in the transcription’s format, you can always transcribe by hand.
Here are some tips to help you manually transcribe:
Play the video at a lower speed:
Working at 0.5x speed gives you more time to type without having to constantly pause.
Split your screen:
Splitting your screen between a YouTube video on one side and the open word processing software on the other lets you easily write without switching back and forth.
Use keyboard shortcuts to pause and go back:
The J, K, and L keys let you rewind, pause, and skip forward, respectively. Use these (especially J) to skip around and make sure your transcription is correct.
While the exact amount of time this takes will depend on how quick you are, it’ll likely take about 3-4x the total length of the video.

YouTube transcription guide FAQ
Can you transcribe a YouTube video without captions?
While you can’t generate a YouTube transcript from their in-app tool if the video doesn’t have captions, you can use a third-party AI transcription tool. As long as you have a link to the YouTube file, you’ll be able to produce a transcription.
How accurate are YouTube’s automatic transcripts?
YouTube’s automatic transcripts work well for production-grade videos with high-quality audio but struggle with low-quality audio, background noise, accents, and industry-specific terminology. For the highest accuracy, use a dedicated speech to text tool instead.
Can ChatGPT transcribe a YouTube video?
You can’t transcribe a YouTube video with ChatGPT directly, as the platform can’t access the contents of a YouTube video solely from its link. You would have to use a work-around transcript extractor or upload the video as a file directly to ChatGPT.
Is there a free way to transcribe a YouTube video?
YouTube’s built-in transcription feature is free to use but only works on videos with captions. ElevenCreative offers a free tier that you can use to transcribe videos instead. You can get started for free and play around with 10,000 credits per month.
How do I download a YouTube video transcript?
One method to download a YouTube video transcript is to copy and paste the auto-built transcript into a Google Docs file. However, this will likely need a cleanup and some editing before it’s usable. Alternatively, use a third-party AI transcription tool to create a proper file and then export it in whichever format you’d like.
