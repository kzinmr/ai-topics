The biggest open question around enterprise AI is no longer about what AI can do—it's about whether you can trust AI to perform reliably.
After hundreds of conversations with business leaders who are eager to adopt modern AI solutions, one thing is clear: companies don’t want to deploy AI systems they can't understand.
At Decagon, we envision transparent AI as the foundation for widespread enterprise AI adoption. From day one, we’ve prioritized building better visibility tooling and robust feedback mechanisms so that you have complete confidence and control. AI agents should never be black boxes — they should be fundamentally trustworthy and transparent.
Why Transparent AI Matters
While enterprise leaders recognize the transformative impact that AI can have, one concern is consistently top-of-mind:
How can we ensure AI won't go rogue?
We’ve entered a fundamentally new era of AI where LLMs are capable of increasingly complex tasks, yet
many of today’s AI-powered products are still black boxes
. They offer no insight into how their inner processes work like how different decisions are made.
Even worse, AI can sometimes "hallucinate"— instances where an LLM generates plausible but inaccurate information with apparent confidence. It’s a well-documented phenomenon by AI labs like OpenAI and Anthropic and there’s a lot of research going into this topic.
That's why it’s up to applications at the agentic layer, like Decagon, to design AI systems with built-in transparency and explainability. This means creating tools that not only show what AI is doing, but also why it makes certain decisions and how it reaches its conclusions.
Without such visibility, enterprise AI becomes a liability rather than an asset. In customer-facing environments, every single interaction carries weight—one mishandled conversation can permanently damage brand perception and customer trust, directly impacting revenue and top-of-funnel.
As enterprises increasingly rely on AI for critical functions like customer support, transparency isn't just a feature—it's a necessity.
The companies that embrace this approach will be the ones that successfully navigate the AI transformation ahead.
Our Vision at Decagon
At Decagon, we've rebuilt the enterprise AI playbook around a core principle: transparency.
We believe that for enterprise AI adoption to reach its full potential, transparency cannot be an afterthought—it must be fundamental to how these systems operate.
Our AI product suite has transparency built into the entire AI agent lifecycle, from pre-deployment testing to continuous monitoring post-deployment. Here's how we're building toward our mission of transparent AI:
Decision-making visibility.
We believe you should have clear insight into how AI tools reach their decisions. This means visibility into which specific data sources are being referenced and why—as well as how certain conclusions are drawn based on that data. Our Admin Dashboard allows you to easily examine specific user conversations and see exactly what information was used for each response.
‍
User-friendly feedback systems.
We offer easy-to-use mechanisms to provide feedback and make real-time adjustments to AI behavior. Our natural language feedback tools make understanding, monitoring, and guiding AI systems accessible to everyone, especially non-technical team members.
Proactive improvements.
Lastly, it would be awesome if your AI could proactively suggest improvements so that it is evolving over time based on past performance and human input. In our case, we enable the AI agent to proactive write new knowledge content and workflows based on ongoing conversations. For instance, it will continuously detect knowledge gaps and draft new articles to fill them in!
This isn't just about providing visibility into AI workflows—it's about creating a new operational paradigm where AI systems are true partners that can be understood, trusted, and refined by the teams that use them.
By making AI systems more transparent, we're building the foundation for widespread enterprise AI adoption that delivers real value while maintaining the trust of both organizations and their customers.
As the AI landscape continues to advance, we expect transparency to become an industry standard. The era of opaque, black-box AI solutions is coming to an end. Enterprise leaders are right to demand more visibility and control over their AI systems, and we're committed to leading the way in making this the new standard.
We strongly believe this is a necessary component of widespread enterprise adoption when it comes to AI agents, and it should be a top evaluation criteria for anyone considering initiatives in the space.
