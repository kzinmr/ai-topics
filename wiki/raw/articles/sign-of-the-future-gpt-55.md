# Sign of the Future: GPT-5.5

**Author:** Ethan Mollick
**Source:** One Useful Thing, Apr 23, 2026

## Summary

GPT-5.5 represents a significant step in frontier AI capability. Frontier ability remains jagged but has extended dramatically.

## Key Demonstrations

### Coding
Prompt: "build me a procedurally generated 3D simulation showing evolution of a harbor town 3000 BCE to 3000 AD"
- Only GPT-5.5 Pro modeled an *evolving* town
- Speed improvement: GPT-5.4 Pro 33min → GPT-5.5 Pro 20min

### Image Generation (GPT-imagegen-2)
- High-quality text rendering
- Any described picture on demand
- Practical: PowerPoint slides, product mockups, websites

## Framework: Models, Apps, Harnesses

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Models | The AI engine (GPT-5.5 family) |
| Apps | Products (chatgpt.com, Codex) |
| Harnesses | Tools extending AI (computer control, code, images) |

## Combined Use Cases

### Academic Paper from Raw Data
- Codex + GPT-5.5 with hundreds of research files (STATA, CSV, Word)
- Four prompts → literature review + statistics + formatted paper
- "Would be happy if this was a 2nd year PhD project"
- Weakness: hypothesis not interesting despite sound stats

### Tabletop RPG
- Single prompt: 101-page PDF, fully illustrated
- Novel setting, coherent rules
- Fiction flaws persist (same as prior models)

## Limitations
- Long-form fiction remains flat
- Carwash Test (Horrocks): fluency ≠ real reasoning (6/29 pass rate)
- Capability gains appear to be accelerating
