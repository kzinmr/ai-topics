# Builders Unscripted: Ep. 1 — Peter Steinberger, Creator of OpenClaw

> *"Builders Unscripted spotlights the stories behind real projects and the mindset that makes them possible: you can just build things."*

The premiere episode of OpenAI's **Builders Unscripted** series. Romain Huet (Head of Developer Experience at OpenAI) interviews Peter Steinberger (creator of OpenClaw) prior to Steinberger joining OpenAI. The conversation covers OpenClaw's explosive viral growth, Steinberger's journey from burned-out CEO to AI-native solo builder, his workflow with Codex, and his philosophy on open source and agentic engineering.

## Core Thesis

**AI has fundamentally changed what it means to be a builder.** Steinberger argues that the most important shift is not the tools themselves, but the collapse of barriers — anyone can build anything, anywhere, and the only thing holding builders back is their willingness to approach the technology playfully.

## Key Topics

### 1. OpenClaw's Viral Moment (00:00–05:00)

- OpenClaw made the **Wall Street Journal** — unusual for an open-source project
- Steinberger describes "slight sensor overload" but pride: "When I started out this year, I was messing with AI, I wanted to inspire people, and I feel like this is the final form"
- **Claw Con**: A community-organized event in SF with ~1,000 people, spawned from a Discord channel he created for meetups
- Global reach: 300+ people signed up for a Vietnam meetup — "It's now worldwide, so now it's a phenomenon"

### 2. The Dopamine Hit of AI-Assisted Building (02:00–05:00)

- Steinberger started with Claude Code at ~30-40% success rate: "I got a dopamine hit every time it got something right"
- > *"I had this realization that now I can just build anything."*
- The entire toolchain is changing, and the definition of what it means to be a developer is shifting
- **Key insight**: "Usually you're so time-constrained because software is hard" — AI removes the time barrier

### 3. OpenClaw's Origin & Philosophy (05:00–15:00)

- Originally wanted something that could look at his WhatsApp messages
- Built a prototype but initially thought "all the big labs are going to build it" — they didn't, so he kept going
- Created a Discord, put his bot in without any security: "I just worked in the open"
- **Primitives over defaults**: OpenClaw's design philosophy — give users building blocks, not pre-baked solutions
- The community exploded: "People had much better use cases than I ever imagined"

### 4. Vibe Coding Is a Slur (15:00–20:00)

- Steinberger pushes back on the term "vibe coding": > *"I think vibe coding is a slur"*
- Argues that using AI effectively is a **skill** that requires practice: "Like you pick up the guitar, you're not going to be good at the guitar on the first day"
- Engineers who try AI once and dismiss it don't understand the learning curve
- The gap between casual users and skilled practitioners is widening

### 5. Building OpenClaw with Codex (20:00–25:00)

- Steinberger used **Codex** extensively to build OpenClaw itself
- > *"Codex is just much better at..."* understanding larger codebases and multi-step tasks
- Describes how Codex changed his workflow: less about writing code, more about **intention and architecture**
- PR review shifts from line-by-line code review to reviewing the **prompt and intention** behind the change
- > *"PRs sometimes take me longer than if I would approach it myself, because my trust in the model not being malicious is much higher than an external contributor"*
- **Most code is boring**: "Most code is boring. Like most of what we write, it's just CRUD, it's just boilerplate"

### 6. Open Source Philosophy (25:00–30:00)

- > *"That's the beauty of open source — people can embrace it and come up with ideas that you didn't even think about"*
- Steinberger's approach: if an open-source thing solves his problem to 70%, he'll just **build the remaining 30% himself**
- Contrasts with the enterprise expectation — people wanted a polished product, but for him it was "my little playground"
- Open source as a vehicle for **serendipitous innovation**: community contributions create unexpected use cases

### 7. Advice for Builders (29:00–31:00)

- > *"My first advice always is: approach it in a playful way. Build something that you always wanted to build."*
- References Jensen Huang's quote: > *"In the near term you're not going to be replaced by AI. You're going to be replaced by someone who uses AI."*
- > *"If your identity is 'I want to create things, I want to solve problems' — if you are high agency, if you are smart — you will be more in demand than ever."*
- Closes with prediction: **"2026 will be an interesting one."**
