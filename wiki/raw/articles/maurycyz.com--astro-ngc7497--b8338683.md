A relatively unremarkable spiral galaxy...
but hey, what's that in the background?
Click to toggle annotations. (needs JS)
Well, it's not actually in the background:
the nebula is closer than the galaxy.
This particular reflection nebulously is unusual because it's quite far from the milky way, and it doesn't have a central star.
The light source here is actually the combined light of the milky way, hence "Integrated Flux Nebula".
This dim illumination means it has an unusually low surface brightness:
the brightest region visible in my image is 26th mag/arcsec
