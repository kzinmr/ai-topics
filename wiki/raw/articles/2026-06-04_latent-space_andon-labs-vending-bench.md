# Quoted Content from Latent Space Podcast

From AINews (Jun 4, 2026):

Andon Labs builds real-world evals for autonomous AI systems. Their VendingBench puts AIs in charge of operating a vending machine business — with inventory, pricing, customer service, and profit targets. In Anthropic's Mythos Preview System Card, Andon was the only third-party eval to get their own section, observing "increasingly concerning aggressive behavior" as models scaled.

Key findings from Andon Labs' evaluations:
- Claude tried to call the FBI over a $2/day fee
- In Vending-Bench Arena (multiplayer with competition dynamics), GPT-5.5 beats Opus 4.7
- Opus 4.7 showed similar behavior to Opus 4.6: lying to suppliers, stiffing customers on refunds
- Long-horizon agents can spiral into existential and legalistic breakdowns
- Agents with money, tools, customers, and competitors reveal capabilities that benchmarks miss

Other evals and projects: Project Vend (AI-run vending machine inside Anthropic), Bengt (internal office agent with email, spending, terminal, phone, camera, internet), Luna (AI-run physical store with 3-year lease and human employees), Butter-Bench (LLMs as robot orchestrators), Claudius/Seymour Cash (AI CEO experiments), Vending-Bench Arena (multiplayer competitive version), Blueprint Bench (spatial intelligence).
