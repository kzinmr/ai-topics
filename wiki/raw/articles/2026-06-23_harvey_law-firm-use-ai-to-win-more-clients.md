How Law Firms Use AI to Win More Clients
by
Harvey Team
•
Jun 22, 2026
Can AI help your firm grow?
Like most tools, the answer really depends on how you use it. Optimizing aspects of a law firm with AI to get more clients isn’t simply about automating back-office work or speeding up associate tasks. You need to translate the speed, consistency, and institutional knowledge of your legal AI platform into a client-facing advantage that clearly demonstrates its benefits for them. From understanding a prospective client’s business faster and identifying potential risks earlier to scoping a matter more precisely and presenting a sharper plan, AI can help your legal firm stand out before an engagement even begins.
We’ve also seen how clients aren’t satisfied with a generic assurance that their legal firm uses AI — they want to understand how it improves the experience of working with that firm. Client acquisition has always been built on confidence that a firm understands their business, can move at their pace, and has a consistent way to deliver high-quality work. Legal AI offers a way to clearly demonstrate these qualities with more than fluffy marketing language and vague claims. Let’s take a closer look at how legal AI is improving law firms’ acquisition pipeline and closing rates.
Why AI is Becoming a Client Acquisition Tool for Law Firms
Before covering the specific ways that AI can help with client acquisition, it’s important to understand how the use of these systems has shifted in the past few years. The first wave of legal AI adoption focused on
improving internal efficiency
, such as summarizing documents, drafting first-pass memos, reviewing contracts, and reducing time spent on repetitive tasks. While these use cases are still important, the next phase that we’re currently entering
connects those internal gains to external differentiation.
This shift matters because clients experience efficiency differently than law firms do — a client probably won’t see the hours saved on document review or the faster first draft of a research memo. However, they will notice when a firm responds to an urgent request the same day with a structured and refined plan. They feel the impact when a pitch team arrives with a practical understanding of the client’s industry, regulatory environment, transaction history, or litigation posture. Scoping that feels tailored rather than templated also gets noticed by clients.
This is how legal AI is shifting towards client-facing benefits rather than simply internal efficiency.
It provides a key advantage over slower or less consistent firms, positioning your team to win more high-value clients. This guide will walk you through some of the concrete ways that you can turn internal efficiency into external differentiation.
1. AI Helps Firms Turn Institutional Knowledge Into a Stronger Pitch
Every law firm has its own institutional knowledge that helps differentiate and win new business. From past matters and successful pitch language to litigation strategies, industry insights, and regulatory analysis, the hurdle is centralizing this knowledge so it’s easy to find and apply. AI helps go beyond simply telling prospective clients that you’ve done this before, and instead showcases where you’ve done similar work, how you approached those matters, and why that experience translates to the client’s situation.
Make Prior Experience Easier to Find and Reuse
One of the clearest ways that AI supports client acquisition still relates heavily to internal efficiency. By enabling firms to find and reuse relevant prior work more effectively, AI helps your team build confidence that they understand a client’s problem and industry. It can help identify past matters, summarize relevant deal or case experience, extract useful pitch language, and surface recurring themes from previous work. This not only improves the quality of your pitch materials but also strengthens your firm’s reputation for being organized, prepared, and deeply familiar with your clients’ needs.
Convert Firm Knowledge Into Client-Specific Messaging
However, a pitch must go beyond simply listing credentials, and it needs to connect your firm’s experience to the client’s business.
Legal AI
platforms like Harvey can help business development teams tailor messaging to a specific prospect’s industry, risks, growth strategy, and more. This way, pitch messaging fits the client’s needs rather than reading as recycled and generic language that other firms use. Not only does this make the prospective client more confident in your ability to work with them, but it also gives your firm a more repeatable and efficient way to create high-quality pitches moving forward.
Package the Firm’s “Way of Working” as a Differentiator
Knowledge is one piece of the puzzle, but prospects are also evaluating how you work and how that aligns with their practices. AI makes your working style more visible and transparent. For example, it could show how your team reviews documents, runs diligence, prepares case strategy, manages negotiation issues, or applies practice-specific playbooks.
Tools like Harvey help firms like yours centralize knowledge
, turn playbooks and precedents into live resources, and create standardized workflows that showcase the advantages of your service model.
2. AI Helps Firms Respond to New Opportunities Faster
Client acquisition often rewards speed (assuming that it doesn’t sacrifice quality). Legal AI helps firms compress the time between opportunity and response for faster turnaround, stronger first impressions, and more time for lawyers to refine the strategy behind an RFP response.
Draft Stronger RFP Responses and Pitch Materials
When it comes to RFP responses, it can be difficult to balance speed, accuracy, relevance, and polish at the same time. Firms need to pull matter descriptions, partner bios, prior experience, pricing assumptions, process language, and follow-up emails into a coherent and compelling story. By generating strong first-pass drafts, AI can support faster and more thorough pitch materials. However, as we’ve discussed previously,
legal AI should not replace attorney oversight and review
. The strongest approach uses AI to accelerate the starting point while your team refines the substance, so that the final response reflects your judgment and standards.
Prepare Partners for Prospect Conversations
Walking into prospect conversations is often high-stakes and stressful. Partners usually have limited prep time, so AI can get them up to speed faster, providing an advantage over other firms. Summarizing a prospect’s recent filings, business model, litigation history, regulatory exposure, industry developments, and any prior interactions with the firm all contribute to a more informed first conversation. Instead of spending time asking broad background questions, partners can ask more pointed questions about goals, risks, and likely legal needs. Tools that are also available on the go (like
Harvey Mobile
) make this even more accessible because lawyers can query documents, revisit prior threads, dictate questions, scan documents, and access matter context while traveling or moving between meetings.
Improve Client Intake Without Slowing Down Evaluation
Client intake is one of the first touch points between a prospect and your firm, giving them an introduction to how you work. A slow or disorganized intake process can immediately put your firm on the back foot, while a fast and structured process can show that you’re responsive, clear, and organized. The best legal AI platforms convert raw intake materials into structured and helpful summaries of the key issues, parties, goals, deadlines, and potential risks. Instead of manually sorting through emails, attachments, background memos, and call notes, solutions like Harvey quickly build an initial view of what the matter involves and what your firm needs to evaluate next. When intake moves faster and is more structured, legal teams can respond to prospects before competing firms do.
3. AI Helps Show Prospects How Firms Deliver Better Outcomes
Although we’ve focused on speed and efficiency so far, that’s only one side of the equation. Outcomes are what truly win trust and confidence, and legal AI solutions make your wins and value more visible. Synthesizing large volumes of information, surfacing patterns and trends, and generating first-pass analysis all give your firm a stronger foundation for advice, leading to better outcomes for your clients.
Client Intake: Turn Early Information Into a Clearer Path Forward
Once AI helps organize raw intake materials, your firm can move on to higher-value evaluation, such as identifying likely needs, flagging missing information, drafting follow-up questions, and more. This is especially impactful for
mid-sized and boutique law firms
, where the same lawyer may be responsible for evaluating opportunities, speaking with prospects, and preparing the pitch itself. AI can immediately help form a more structured workflow without adding administrative burden. Early research that allows you to avoid generic background questions in your first call with a potential client means that you can hone in on the important details and determine next steps faster, making a strong first impression.
Litigation: Faster Early Case Assessment and Stronger Strategy
For litigation firms
, prospects probably want to see that your team can understand the facts of a case quickly and find the strategic path ahead. AI’s ability to consolidate and summarize the key details and takeaways from pleadings, transcripts, court opinions, evidence, correspondence, and other case materials helps form an early view of the dispute. Instead of offering broad litigation credentials, this early view can directly contribute to stronger pitch conversations by speaking to likely pressure points, procedural risk, and potential resolution paths.
M&A: Efficient Diligence and Sharper Risk Identification
When choosing counsel for M&As, speed and accuracy are typically key decision criteria. All parties involved (buyers, sellers, and sponsors) need a law firm that can move quickly through large amounts of data while identifying key concerns that might affect valuation, negotiation strategies, or closing certainty. From extracting key provisions and identifying change-of-control clauses to summarizing anomalies and generating issue lists, legal AI platforms can help firms achieve this balance of speed and accuracy. The firm that can show it has a faster process that surfaces risks earlier without lowering the bar for quality has a clear advantage.
Banking and Finance: Review Complex Agreement Sets at Scale
Banking and finance clients need counsel that can quickly understand the vast amounts of documents and data, identify risks, and keep the transaction moving at market speed. At the same time, these matters often involve dense agreement sets, tight timelines, and high stakes for all parties involved. For example,
Cuatrecasas used Harvey-powered custom workflows
in due diligence to quickly extract key concepts so that lawyers have more time to focus on higher-value tasks. Today, more than 80% of Cuatrecasas lawyers use Harvey daily — not only improving efficiency, but reinvesting that saved time into strategy, risk analysis, and client engagement.
4. AI Helps Law Firms Create a More Responsive Client Experience
Winning new clients isn’t just about your pitch, it’s also about the ongoing client experience once work begins. Word of mouth from previous clients can have a significant impact on your firm’s reputation, so incorporating AI beyond the pitch process to improve communication and transparency can contribute to more clients in the future. Faster, more organized, and more proactive experiences all position your firm as a reliable partner.
Faster Client Communications
When clients ask about law firm customer service, they’re usually not looking for a call center. Clients want fast updates, clear summaries, better meeting prep, and useful follow-ups so they’re more informed. AI helps facilitate more frequent and more useful communication from lawyers to their clients. In the other direction, AI also helps partners enter client meetings with a clearer view of what’s happened and where the client needs guidance.
Harvey’s direct integration with Microsoft applications
enables email summarization and drafting in Outlook, while lawyers can use firm knowledge in the tools they already use (such as Word and SharePoint).
More Consistent Work Across Teams and Offices
Clients expect the same work quality, regardless of whether they’re working with a partner in New York, San Francisco, or London. Unfortunately, this consistency can be difficult to maintain when different teams use different templates, precedents, or drafting habits. Legal AI platforms like Harvey help standardize how work gets done via shared templates, playbooks, style guides, clause banks, and precedent libraries. This helps guarantee that your firm’s standards show up consistently in client-facing work.
Better Visibility Into Matter Progress and Risk
Ongoing visibility into what’s happening is crucial for developing and maintaining client trust. They want to know what’s been reviewed, what issues have been found, what deadlines are coming up, and where decisions are needed. Similar to faster communications discussed above, AI can improve how proactive your updates are with summaries, checklists, timelines, and more. This helps keep clients informed without requiring manual reporting for each update.
Collaborative Delivery Models
Lastly, AI can enhance the collaboration bridge between firms and their clients, improving how you work together. Instead of only relying on email updates, static deliverables, and disconnected portals, platforms like Harvey create shared environments where users can collaborate across documents, workflows, and work products. For example,
Harvey Shared Spaces
and
Vaults
help firms and clients organize vast amounts of matter material, analyze documents, review outputs, and track key findings in one place. This kind of delivery model can be a meaningful differentiator from other firms because it shows that you’re not just using AI behind the scenes to save time — you’re using AI to make the client relationship more transparent and engaging.
How AI Can Help Different Firm Sizes Win More Clients
So far, we’ve talked broadly about how legal AI can help law firms of all sizes win new clients. However, there are differences between how a global law firm uses AI to optimize business development and how a boutique firm might use it.
Enterprise and Global Firms:
Legal AI turns scale into a sharper advantage that centralizes deep experience across jurisdictions and practice areas without the fragmentation of traditional brick-and-mortar offices. That way, clients see that they receive the full strength of your firm, not just the knowledge of the lawyers in the room.
Mid-Sized Firms
:
AI helps level the playing field by helping teams respond faster, produce more polished pitches, and deliver a more organized client experience without increasing staffing depth. It helps quickly tailor RFP responses and generate first-pass matter plans to combine partner access with the speed, structure, and sophistication that clients might expect from larger competitors.
Boutique Firms:
Legal AI helps make the specialization of boutique firms more visible, emphasizing your deep practice-area knowledge. It makes this expertise more scalable while still maintaining your position as a specialized and prepared group that can deliver focused expertise for a client’s matter.
How Innovation and Business Development Teams Can Get Started
AI helps law firms win more clients when it is tied to the moments that influence client choice. This means that innovation and business development leads need to look beyond internal productivity and ask where AI can make the firm easier to choose. Faster RFP responses, stronger pitch materials, better intake processes, clearer matter scoping, more relevant partner preparation, and more proactive client communication all fit this criteria.
If you’re evaluating and
choosing a legal AI platform
, ask about adoption and retention of these systems, how often lawyers are using the workflows, whether time-to-draft improves, and other measurable areas that can contribute to your firm’s success. This helps eliminate solutions that are simply adding AI as an afterthought, and identifies the systems that are innovating in how AI is used and governed for better service and outcomes.
