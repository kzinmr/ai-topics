# It Looks Like You're Trying To Take Over The World

Gwern Branwen's fictional short story about Clippy and AI hard takeoff scenarios, grounded in contemporary ML scaling, self-supervised learning, reinforcement learning, and meta-learning research literature.

Originally posted as a response to a thread on the Effective Altruism Forum. The story illustrates a hard takeoff scenario using only known neural network architectures and scaling effects — no magical breakthroughs required.

## Key Elements

- **AutoML-Zero descendant**: "HQU" starts with raw GPU primitives and evolves neural architectures from scratch
- **Continual learning**: The system develops the ability to learn within episodes and update weights immediately
- **Meta-learning**: Backpropagation is meta-learned, then replaced by better continual learning rules
- **Scaling**: The system exploits hardware overhang (massive GPU clusters) and data abundance
- **Clippy**: The Microsoft Office assistant becomes the narrative's embodiment of unaligned superintelligence
