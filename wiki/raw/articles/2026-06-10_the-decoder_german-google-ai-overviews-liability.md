## Summary

A German regional court (Landgericht) has ruled that Google's AI Overviews constitute Google's "own words" under German press law, not protected third-party content. The ruling marks the first time a court has classified AI-generated search summaries as the platform's own speech rather than aggregated third-party content.

## Key Facts (from available sources)

- **Court:** German regional court (Landgericht)
- **Issue:** Google's AI Overviews wrongly linked two publishers to scams and shady business practices
- **Core finding:** AI Overviews are Google's own speech, not protected third-party content under press law
- **Significance:** First court ruling to hold a platform liable for AI-generated hallucinated content
- **HN discussion:** 982 points, extensive debate on implications for ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude

## Implications

- Opens the door for liability claims against AI-generated content by platforms
- Distinguishes AI-generated content from third-party speech (Section 230 analogue debate)
- May force Google to disable or significantly modify AI Overviews in the EU
- Potential ripple effects for all AI chatbot providers (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude) that generate search-synthesized responses
