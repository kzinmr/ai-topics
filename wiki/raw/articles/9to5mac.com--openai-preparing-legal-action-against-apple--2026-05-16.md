# OpenAI preparing legal action against Apple over Siri partnership

Apple and OpenAI inked a deal to integrate ChatGPT with Siri as part of iOS 18 in 2024. OpenAI is displeased with how the partnership has played out and is considering taking legal action against Apple.

According to Bloomberg, OpenAI lawyers are working with an outside legal firm "on a range of options that could be formally executed in the near future." One possible outcome: OpenAI sends Apple a notice "alleging breach of contract without necessarily filing a full lawsuit at the outset."

Key claims:
- OpenAI believed the partnership would coax more users into subscribing to ChatGPT and expected deeper integration across more Apple apps with prime placement within Siri
- An unnamed OpenAI executive: "We have done everything from a product perspective. They have not, and worse, they haven't even made an honest effort."
- OpenAI expected the in-Settings ChatGPT subscription signup "could generate billions of dollars per year in subscriptions" — "hasn't come close to happening"
- Apple unwilling to share details when deal was made: "They basically said, 'OpenAI needs to take a leap of faith and trust us'"
- No money changing hands between companies, outside of Apple getting a cut of subscriptions

Apple is expected to announce Siri powered by Google Gemini at WWDC. iOS 27 will also reportedly let users integrate with other AI models including Claude.
