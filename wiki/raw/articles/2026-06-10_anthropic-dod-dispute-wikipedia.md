# Anthropic–United States Department of Defense Dispute

## Background

Since January 2026, a dispute has existed between AI company **Anthropic** and the **United States Department of Defense (DoD/Pentagon)** over the military use of Anthropic's Claude AI products, particularly regarding lethal autonomous weapons and domestic mass surveillance.

## Timeline

### 2024–2025: Seeds of Friction
- Anthropic CEO **Dario Amodei** endorsed Harris before the 2024 presidential election, calling Trump a "feudal warlord"
- January 2025: Criticized Trump administration's AI investment plan "Stargate" as "chaotic"
- Hiring of Biden administration officials by Anthropic drew backlash from Trump allies
- May 2025: Anthropic announced **Claude Gov** for national security customers
- September 2025: Contradictions surfaced regarding use in FBI, ICE, and Secret Service surveillance systems
- Trump AI advisor **David Sacks** labeled Anthropic as "AI doomers" (excessive regulation supporters)
- December 2025: Amodei met with Trump administration officials and senators to repair relations

### December 2025 – January 2026: Escalation
- December 2025: Defense Secretary **Pete Hegseth** announced military AI platform **GenAI.mil**. Google Gemini signed, followed by OpenAI ChatGPT
- January 2026: Hegseth announced xAI Grok for military use, criticizing "woke AI" (implicitly targeting Anthropic)
- Reuters reported Anthropic opposed use of its products in **lethal autonomous weapons (LAW)** and **mass surveillance**

### February 2026: Peak of Confrontation
- Pentagon used Claude in the 2026 Venezuela intervention (Axios report). Anthropic announced reassessment of the relationship
- Anthropic refused Claude use for "any lawful purposes" → Pentagon threatened contract termination
- Hegseth attempted to designate Anthropic as a **"supply chain risk"** (aiming to ban military contractors from using Anthropic)
- DoD Under Secretary for Research & Engineering **Emil Michael** told Anthropic to "cross the Rubicon"
- Estimated **$200 million** contract terminated
- Trump Jr.-linked VC **1789 Capital** withdrew hundreds of millions in planned Anthropic investment

### Late February – March 2026: OpenAI's Move & Legal Battle
- **OpenAI** hastily signed a contract with the Pentagon without the restrictions Anthropic had demanded (hours before the Iran war began)
- **March 9, 2026**: Anthropic filed suit in **U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California** (*Anthropic PBC v. Department of War*)
- **March 26, 2026**: **Judge Rita F. Lin** issued an **injunction** against the "supply chain risk" designation. Ruled: "Retaliation against Anthropic's media response violates the First Amendment"

### April 2026: Appeals Court
- **D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals** denied Anthropic's emergency motion to lift the FASCSA designation: "Cannot force dealings with unwanted vendors amid active military conflict"
- However, this order is not a final judgment; the main case continues

### May 2026
- Pentagon signed 7 AI companies (SpaceX/OpenAI/Google/Nvidia/Reflection/Microsoft/AWS) — Anthropic excluded
- DOD CTO Emil Michael said Mythos is a "separate national security moment"
- NSA reportedly using Claude despite the blacklist

## Status as of June 2026
- **Judge Lin's injunction remains in effect** (supply chain risk designation partially blocked)
- **D.C. Circuit denied lifting FASCSA designation** → Claude exclusion from DoD target systems proceeding on **180-day timeline**
- Anthropic cannot be used as a primary DoD contractor or subcontractor on DoD target systems
- **Litigation ongoing** — government procurement experts note: "Anthropic has strong legal arguments, but courts rarely overrule the White House on national security matters"
- **No settlement has been reached**
