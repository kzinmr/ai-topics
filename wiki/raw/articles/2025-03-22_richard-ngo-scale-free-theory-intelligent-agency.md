# Towards a scale-free theory of intelligent agency

Richard Ngo outlines his pursuit of a *scale-free theory of intelligent agency* — a unified mathematical framework describing both understanding the world and influencing it. The post has two parts: a critique of existing theories (expected utility maximization and active inference), and a proposal for a new approach called **coalitional agency**.

## Part 1: Existing Theories

### Expected Utility Maximization (EUM)
- Productive in simple settings (game theory, microeconomics)
- Fails for agents making sequences of choices over time while learning/updating concepts
- Treats goals and beliefs as separate — in practice, both are built from the same underlying concepts via deep learning
- Agents often act on learned heuristics (model-free RL), not explicit belief-goal calculations

### Active Inference
- Born from neuroscience: predictive coding models explain how beliefs and goals are built up over time
- Hierarchical networks reconcile bottom-up signals with top-down predictions
- Adds *active inference*: agents act to make their predictions come true (minimize prediction error)
- Limitation: still a "one-agent theory" — doesn't account for society-level intelligence or multi-agent coordination

## Part 2: Coalitional Agency

Ngo proposes that intelligent agency is inherently *multi-agent* — it arises from coalitions of sub-agents at multiple scales. Key ideas:

1. **Agency as coalition**: Each agent is composed of sub-agents that negotiate and coordinate
2. **Scale-free**: The same principles apply from neurons to individuals to organizations to civilizations
3. **Coordination mechanisms**: Language, markets, laws, and norms are mechanisms by which coalitions coordinate
4. **Beyond single-agent RL**: Real intelligence emerges from multi-agent dynamics, not just individual optimization

This directly relates to the scaling hypothesis — as AI systems scale, they may naturally develop coalitional structures (see also: [[concepts/agents-scaffolding-composition-inference-scaling-hypothesis]]).

## Relevance to AI Topics

- **AI Agents**: Directly relevant to multi-agent AI architectures and the "harness" paradigm
- **Alignment**: Scale-free alignment must work for coalitional agents, not just single EUM agents
- **Scaling**: Complements Gwern's scaling hypothesis with a multi-agent perspective
