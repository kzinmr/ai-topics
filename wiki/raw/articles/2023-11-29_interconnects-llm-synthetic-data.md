# Synthetic data: Anthropic's CAI, scaling, OpenAI's Superalignment, tips, types, and open examples

**Author:** Nathan Lambert (Interconnects)

Synthetic data has evolved from simple data augmentation to a core strategy for training advanced LLMs. As high-quality human data reaches its limit, synthetic data is becoming the primary accelerator for the next phase of AI development.

## Two Strategic Roles

- **Scaling for Closed Models:** OpenAI and Anthropic use synthetic data to bypass the "data wall." Training models 100x larger than GPT-4 requires data that doesn't exist in human-written form.
- **Efficiency for Open Models:** Boutique providers use synthetic data (often generated by GPT-4) to fine-tune Llama/Mistral at a fraction of human labeling cost.

> "The open and closed communities are pursuing very different end goals, yet synthetic data serves both of them."

## Anthropic's Constitutional AI (CAI)

Anthropic is the "king of synthetic data" due to its CAI framework powering Claude models:

1. **Principled Instruction Correction:** Model generates answers, critiques them against a "constitution" (e.g., "Is this truthful?"), and refines them. Model fine-tuned on this corrected data.
2. **Principle-Following RLAIF:** Model generates pairwise preference data by deciding which of two completions better follows a specific constitutional principle.

**Formula:** `CAI = principled instruction correction + principle-following RLAIF`

## Hierarchy of Synthetic Data Types

Difficulty and infrastructure requirements follow a progression:

- **Instructions (SFT):** "Self-Instruct" methods to create seed instructions and have an LLM expand them.
- **Preferences:** Ask LLM to score/rank multiple completions (e.g., UltraFeedback). Used for RLHF/DPO.
- **Critiques (Final Frontier):** Models provide detailed feedback on prompt-generation pairs. Requires high-capacity models (50B+ params).

## Practical Tips

- **Always use the best model for generation:** Quality capped by training data. Cost-benefit favors SOTA (GPT-4) even if it requires paying for API access.
- **Lock API Versions:** Model behavior in APIs changes (more critical, nonsensical scores). Pin specific model version for data consistency.
- **Robustness over Novelty:** Synthetic data helps models handle long-tail tasks by allowing obscure data points to be seen multiple times during training.

## Open-Source Examples

### Models & Datasets
- **OpenHermes (Teknium):** Synthetic instruction-trained models
- **Orca / Orca 2 (Microsoft):** GPT-4 augmented FLAN datasets
- **Starling (Berkeley):** RLHF on Nectar dataset (synthetic preferences)
- **Neural-chat (Intel):** Synthetic preferences for DPO

### Methods
- **Evol-Instruct:** Tree-of-rules for diverse high-quality instructions
- **SteerLM (Nvidia):** Attribute-based scores without OpenAI outputs
- **Prometheus (KAIST):** Open-source evaluator for AI feedback

## Superalignment

OpenAI's Superalignment team uses AI feedback to solve alignment for future superhuman models. Core assumption: as AI surpasses human capability, humans can't provide feedback — making AI-to-AI supervision (synthetic feedback) the only path forward.
