Australian web infrastructure company Sitecove has developed the Sitecove HyperCache Inference Protocol (SHIP), a new AI inference optimisation architecture designed to significantly improve how LLMs are served in production.

## Key Results
- Up to 91% reduction in GPU usage
- Speed improvements of up to 12x
- Gains in memory efficiency and cost per token
- Cost per million tokens reduced from $49 to $4

## Approach
SHIP takes a system-level approach to inference — optimising memory handling, cache behaviour, scheduling, and token generation as a unified system rather than isolated components. It reworks the entire inference lifecycle with a multi-layered architecture that compounds efficiency gains across memory, compute, and throughput.

## Origin
Built by a web infrastructure team, not AI researchers. Founder Adam Kerr: "We weren't trying to reinvent AI — just make it faster and more efficient."

## Significance
As AI scales, infrastructure — not models — is becoming the primary bottleneck. SHIP reflects a broader trend of impactful innovation coming from smaller, systems-focused teams.
