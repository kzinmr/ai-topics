# Pentagon Signs AI Deals — Anthropic Excluded (May 2026)

On May 5, 2026, the Pentagon signed AI contracts with 8 companies: SpaceX, OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, AWS, Oracle, and Reflection. Notably, Anthropic was excluded.

## Why Anthropic Was Excluded

Anthropic's exclusion likely stems from its public stance on AI safety and military applications. Anthropic has been more cautious than competitors about military and weapons-related AI use. The exclusion is reportedly under litigation.

## Related Developments

- May 8, 2026: US government AI model vetting agreements confirmed with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI
- May 9, 2026: Google DeepMind UK staff voted 98% to unionize specifically over classified Pentagon AI contract concerns — first union at any top AI lab
- UK AISI evaluation: GPT-5.5 matches Anthropic's restricted Mythos model on cyber benchmarks

## Context

This represents a significant shift in the AI-military relationship. The Pentagon is simultaneously deepening partnerships with major AI labs while certain labs resist on safety/ethical grounds. The exclusion of Anthropic — despite its leading technical capabilities — raises questions about whether AI safety stances carry a financial penalty at the government contracting level.

The Google DeepMind unionization vote over Pentagon contracts signals growing internal tension within AI labs about military applications, potentially foreshadowing broader industry conflict between commercial priorities and researcher values.
