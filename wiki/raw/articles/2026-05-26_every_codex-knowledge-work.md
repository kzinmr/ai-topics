## The Vision: A Day in the Life of an Agent-Pilled Knowledge Worker

> Picture a Monday morning: A request for a launch plan lands in your inbox. You forward it to Codex, which has its own email account, and close your laptop while Codex runs tasks in the cloud, or on a machine like a Mac Mini that you keep active. On your commute to the office, you get an email notification on your phone: Codex has read the relevant Slack threads, pulled customer notes out of Google Drive, checked last quarter's numbers in PostHog, and started a go-to-market plan in a shared Notion document. It just needs you to confirm one detail about timing, which you do with a thumbs-up. By the time you reach your desk, a draft is waiting for review.

This is built on **Codex**, OpenAI's agentic desktop app, which acts as a workspace for you and your AI agents, not just a coding tool.

## Part 1: Understanding Codex

### What Codex Is

> Codex is a workspace for you and your AI agents. Give Codex access to the files, apps, and tools it needs, and it gathers context, moves through the task across every surface it can reach—including your connected apps, the browser, and your computer.

**Core capabilities** (summarized):
- Works on multiple tasks in parallel alongside you
- Pulls context from connected apps and files
- Uses a supported browser and desktop automation when needed
- Checks its own work, revises, and persists
- Holds a **persistent goal** across a long session, not just one-off requests
- Turns repeatable tasks into recurring workflows
- Routes shared requests from Slack, email, or forms
- Allows phone-based steering and review (via ChatGPT mobile app while work runs on a Mac Mini / cloud machine)

**Two working modes:**
- **Delegate** — for predictable, repeatable, low-risk tasks with clear instructions; the agent runs autonomously and brings back finished work for review.
- **Collaborate** — for judgment-heavy, exploratory, or iterative work; you work alongside the model toward your vision.

### A Note on Goals (`/goal`)

A *Goal* is a persistent objective that shapes an entire session, not a one-off prompt.

> A Goal in Codex, initiated using the `/goal` command, is a persistent objective that shapes an entire session rather than living and dying with a single message. Instead of re-briefing the agent on every turn, you tell it what "done" looks like, how success gets checked, and which constraints to respect. Codex then keeps working toward that outcome across interruptions and session breaks.

**Test for using `/goal`:**  
> "If you'd type the same sentence into three prompts in a row—'cite every factual claim, match the house style, never send without my review'—make it a goal instead."

**Goals vs. Skills:**
- A **skill** is a reusable set of packaged instructions (sometimes with scripts) for a recurring task type.
- A **goal** is the specific objective for one stretch of work; it ends when the objective is met.

### Codex on Mobile

Codex can be controlled from your phone through the **ChatGPT mobile app**, remotely driving the machine where work runs. Use it for lightweight interactions: kick off a task, approve an action, answer a question, or review a draft. Heavier review still benefits from a full screen.

## Key Takeaway

> Expert Codex users know how to direct AI's capability without losing their personal judgment. They *ride* the models rather than being overwhelmed by them. This guide is about becoming one of those people—turning repeated work into durable systems that get better over time.

To begin, the two meta-skills are: choosing between delegating and collaborating, and turning repetitive multi-prompt instructions into persistent goals.
