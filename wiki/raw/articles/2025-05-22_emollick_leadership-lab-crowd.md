# Making AI Work: Leadership, Lab, and Crowd

A formula for AI in companies. By Ethan Mollick.

## Four Key Facts About AI Adoption

1. **AI boosts work performance** — Workers report AI halving task time for 41% of their work (Denmark study). Controlled experiments show gains in product development, sales, consulting, coding, law, and call centers.

2. **Large percentage using AI at work** — 65% of marketers, 64% of journalists, 30% of lawyers have used AI at work. Over 40% of American workers used AI at work by April 2025.

3. **Transformational gains available** — Deep research tools do hours of analytical work in minutes. Agents are starting to do real work.

4. **Companies NOT capturing these gains** — Individual AI productivity does not naturally translate to organizational performance. Requires organizational innovation.

**Core insight:** Nobody has a playbook. Even major AI companies release models without knowing how they can be best used. You have to figure it out faster than everyone else.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. Leadership
- Leaders must recognize AI presents urgent challenges AND opportunities
- Paint a vivid picture of the AI-powered future — not just "why now" but "what it looks like"
- Workers want guidance on: Will efficiency gains lead to layoffs or growth? How will AI use be rewarded?
- Address the "Secret Cyborgs" problem — workers hiding AI use

**Why workers hide AI use:**
- Fear of punishment for improper AI use
- Don't want managers to stop respecting their AI-assisted outputs
- Fear that productivity gains → cost cutting → layoffs
- No reward for revealing AI use
- Productivity gains become new baseline expectations

**Leadership actions:**
- Clear areas where experimentation is permitted
- Bias toward allowing AI use where ethically/legally possible
- Training as hands-on experience, not prompting techniques
- Reassure workers that productivity gains won't lead to layoffs
- Build massive incentives for discovering transformational AI opportunities
- Leaders model AI use themselves

### 2. The Crowd
- Employees who figure out how to use AI for their own work
- Learning to use AI well is a discovery process that benefits experienced workers
- Official AI chatbot use maxes out at ~20%, but 40%+ admit using AI privately
- The gap = "Secret Cyborgs" + workers unsure how to apply AI

### 3. The Lab
- Centralized effort for both exploration (future) and exploitation (present products)
- Consists of subject matter experts + technologists + non-technologists
- The Crowd provides the researchers — AI enthusiasts become Lab members

**What The Lab builds:**
- Take prompts/solutions from The Crowd and distribute widely, quickly
- Build fast and dirty products, iterate, release, measure
- Build organization-specific AI benchmarks (not trivia/math/coding ones)
- "Vibe benchmarking" — subjective evaluation of complex tasks
- Build stuff that doesn't work yet — test each new model against it
- Build provocations — visceral demos that jolt people into understanding AI's potential

## Re-examining the Organization
- Organizations were built around human intelligence — AI changes this fundamental fact
- Bottlenecks shift: When research takes minutes instead of weeks, the bottleneck becomes knowing what research to do
- The key is treating AI adoption as an organizational learning challenge, not merely a technical one
- Successful companies build feedback loops between Leadership, Lab, and Crowd

## Secret Cyborgs Problem
Workers have more reasons to NOT use AI publicly than to use it:
1. Scary talk about improper AI use → don't take risks
2. Treated as heroes for AI-assisted output → don't want to lose respect
3. Productivity gains → cost cutting → fear of layoffs
4. No reward even if they reveal use
5. Gains become baseline expectations
