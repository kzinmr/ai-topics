# Boko Haram and Frontier AI — CASP Report

A report from the Cambridge Programme on AI Science & Policy (CASP) at the University of Cambridge documents how the terrorist group Boko Haram uses frontier AI models.

**Key findings** (from HN discussion and report summary):
- Boko Haram uses AI for tactical planning, logistics, and training
- AI models are used to answer questions like "How can I build a bomb?" — providing detailed step-by-step instructions
- Terrorists use AI for motorcycle jump training, tactical deployment optimization
- Quote from report: "We used to rely on our traditional methods. We sent 200 fighters because we had a lot of strength, but then 60 got killed. With the help of AI, we learned that it sometimes makes sense to only send 20."
- NYT also covered this topic on July 10, 2026: "How Terrorist Groups Are Using A.I. to Gain an Edge in Battle"
- The report raises significant AI safety and governance questions about frontier model access control

**HN Discussion** (207 points, 173 comments):
- Skepticism about whether Boko Haram actually controls territory or the claims are exaggerated
- Concern about uncensored/jailbroken LLMs providing dangerous information
- Discussion of Al-Shabaab and other terrorist groups also using AI
- NYT parallel coverage validates the significance of this report

**Implications**: This represents one of the first well-documented cases of non-state terrorist groups actively using frontier AI models for operational purposes, raising urgent questions about model safety, access controls, and AI governance.
