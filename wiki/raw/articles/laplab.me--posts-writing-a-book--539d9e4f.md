# I'm writing a book!

Source: https://laplab.me/posts/writing-a-book/

Over the course of my career, I introduced a couple of engineers into the topic of query engines. Every time, I bumped into the same problem: query engines are extremely academic. Despite the fact that industry has over 40 years of expertise, reading foundational papers and then sort of just googling around is the only way to dive deep. Database courses and seminars from Andy Pavlo definitely help, but they are still targeted at academic audience and require
a lot
of extra reading to be useful.
This makes the topic hard to grasp for people that work in databases and completely unapproachable for everyone else. There are plenty of guides out there on how to write SQL, but almost nothing on how SQL works under the hood. Information is quite scattered and articles often assume existing knowledge of the topic.
All of this lead me to the idea of writing a book on query engines. I want to share what I know about this topic in an approachable way. This is not a university course or a comprehensive academic research. Instead, these are practical suggestions on how various parts of a query engine can be implemented.
This book is available to read for free
here
. I plan to sell e-book and printed versions eventually, but the online version will always be free. It’s still work in progress though! I will publish chapters as I finish them. If you want to be notified, feel free to subscribe:
Writing a book is also a scary and lonely endeavor. If you are excited about this book and want to see it finished, feel free to
tell me about it
. I would really appreciate it :)
