# Prediction: A Frontier Open Source LLM Will Be Released On 3rd December 2026

Jamie Dborin — June 22, 2026

Using Artificial Analysis benchmarks to predict when an open source LLM will be released that matches frontier LLMs.

## Key Findings

- The gap between open weights LLMs and closed source LLMs is measured by looking at the frontier of open-weight performance and comparing it to when the closed source frontier was at that level.
- On the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index (overall capability assessment):
  - Gap has been reliably shrinking since summer 2024
  - Line of best fit projects gap shrinks to 0 months around December 3, 2026 (6 months from writing)

## Multi-Benchmark Analysis

Analysis repeated across 18 different benchmarks from Artificial Analysis:

- **Coding benchmarks**: Gap has gone from 15 months behind to only 1-2 months behind
- **Most other benchmarks**: Moderate increase in gap over time
- **Overall average**: Almost completely flat, at just under 5 months gap for the entire period

This reveals the difficulty of measuring LLM quality — depending on the benchmark, you predict the open source singularity by Christmas 2026, or conclude open source LLMs are consistently ~5 months behind and the gap may be growing.

Benchmarks analyzed: AIME, AIME25, Artificial Analysis Agentic Index, Coding Index, Intelligence Index, Math Index, GPQA, HLE, IFBench, LCR, LiveCodeBench, MATH 500, MMLU Pro, SciCode, TAU2, TAU Banking, TerminalBench Hard, TerminalBench v2.1
