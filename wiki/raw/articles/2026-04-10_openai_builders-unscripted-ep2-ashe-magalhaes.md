# Builders Unscripted: Ep. 2 — Ashe Magalhaes, Founder of Hearth AI

> *"The builder archetype is quite close to the artist archetype. I'm happiest viewing myself as a conduit for creativity."*

The second episode of OpenAI's **Builders Unscripted** series. Romain Huet interviews **Ashe Magalhaes**, founder of **Hearth AI** — the first agentic CRM, built in 2022 on GPT-3.5 before the infrastructure was ready. Ashe brings a unique perspective spanning solar car racing, NASA satellite work, and ML engineering at Airbnb. The conversation explores building AI-native products when the underlying systems are still fragile, the philosophy of building in public, and how agentic personal operating systems enable deeper human presence and connection.

## Core Thesis

**Building is a creative act, not just an engineering one.** Ashe frames the builder as an artist — someone who brings things to life across mediums (code, hardware, poetry, video). AI agents, in this view, are not just productivity tools but instruments for **incarnating ideas** and being more present in what matters.

## Key Topics

### 1. Hearth AI: The First Agentic CRM (00:10–05:00)

- Founded in **2022**, Hearth AI was the first **agentic CRM** built on GPT-3.5
- > *"We were trying to kick off all these agentic workflows when the underlying systems were still pretty fragile. I was trying to wrangle you and other people."*
- Ashe gave early feedback to OpenAI's API team — wanted **structured outputs** and **retrieval with JSON schemas** before they existed as products
- Romain recalls: "You were so early. What was it at the time to build an AI?"
- The challenge: LLMs were non-deterministic, and the product had to feel stable despite that

### 2. The Solar Car, NASA, and Frontier Tech (01:26–03:15)

- **Stanford solar car team**: Ashe spent 2 years building a solar-powered car end-to-end — telemetry, CAD modeling, meshing, and autocross driving
- **Bridgestone World Solar Challenge** in Australia: Drove 6-hour shifts through the Australian outback over 5 days (Darwin to Adelaide)
- > *"I was very aware that I had spent 2 years building something with a team that I would put my body into."*
- Other teams crashed (Cambridge's team), making safety rigor visceral: "How do you make safe decisions and also really push the bounds of what's technically possible?"
- **NASA**: Worked on satellites — another frontier tech domain
- **Airbnb**: ML engineer riding the wave of deep learning commercialization

### 3. Builder as Artist (03:15–05:00)

- > *"I view the builder archetype as quite close to the artist archetype. I think I'm happiest viewing myself as like a conduit for creativity."*
- Engineering has always felt like a **tool for creating and incarnating** — whether building satellites, cars, code, poetry, or videos
- Curiosity and the desire to **bring things to life** drives everything

### 4. AI and Human Connection (05:43–08:44)

- Hearth AI's mission evolved from CRM to something deeper: **using AI to enhance human relationships**
- Agents as tools for being more **present, connected, and intentional**
- The insight: AI can handle the administrative overhead of relationships (remembering details, follow-ups) so humans can focus on genuine connection
- > *"Our users didn't care that LLMs were new. They wanted to see the flexibility of what was just then possible."*

### 5. Build in Public (08:44–12:17)

- Ashe advocates for **building in public** — sharing the process, not just the polished outcome
- The philosophy: let work "go out into the world, let it breathe, and allow there to be a surprise in what could happen"
- Building in public creates **serendipity** and unexpected connections
- Contrasts with the perfectionist urge to only share finished products

### 6. Ashe's Agentic Workflow (12:17–31:55)

- **Personal agentic OS**: Ashe runs multiple agents as a personal operating system
- Moves fast with **Codex** — the speed and capability of coding agents has transformed what's possible for a solo founder
- > *"Finding beauty in tools"* — the aesthetic dimension of well-crafted agentic workflows
- Agentic tools as instruments for **presence**: by offloading cognitive load to agents, builders can be more present in creative and relational domains
- The workflow integrates agents into daily life, not just coding tasks

### 7. Advice for Builders (31:55–33:38)

- > *"First, curiosity — and having fun. Really honoring that curiosity. If we have these desires, I think it's for a reason — let those pulls guide us and take that risk because you want to know what happens."*
- > *"Curiosity is such a magical part of the history of being an engineer. As everything's changing, that is the same pillar."*
- Romain notes the similarity to **Peter Steinberger's advice** in Ep. 1: approach playfully, follow curiosity
- Closing: "To be driven by curiosity, to bring something to life that is newly possible"
