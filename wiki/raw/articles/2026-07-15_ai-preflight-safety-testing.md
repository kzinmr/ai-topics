# Demis Hassabis Endorses Preflight Safety Testing for AI

## Summary

On July 14, 2026, Sir Demis Hassabis (Google DeepMind CEO) publicly endorsed mandatory preflight safety testing for frontier AI models — modeled on aviation preflight checks and FDA drug approvals. The proposal requires frontier models to pass mandatory, transparent, and independent safety evaluations before public deployment.

## Hassabis's Proposal

1. **Voluntary initial phase**: Frontier Labs voluntarily share models with a Standards Body for review up to 30 days before release.
2. **Formalization**: Once protocol proves effective, frontier models would be required to pass before deployment in the US market.
3. **Post-release**: Labs work with the Standards Body on critical post-release vulnerabilities.
4. **Governance**: A FINRA-style model (industry-funded self-regulatory organization with government oversight), involving independent technical experts.

## Endorsements

- Gary Marcus: Called it "genuinely exciting" and a "turning point" — had been advocating since 2023 Senate testimony.
- The Verge (Robert Hart, July 14): "Google's Demis Hassabis says it's time for a global AI watchdog — led by the US."

## Timeline

- 2023: Marcus advocates preflight testing in Senate testimony.
- Early 2026: "Mythos moment" prompts partial White House implementation.
- July 14, 2026: Hassabis publishes essay on X endorsing preflight testing.

## Implications

- Major shift: a top AI leader endorsing mandatory (not voluntary) pre-deployment testing.
- FINRA model suggests industry-funded SRO with government backstop.
- US-led global oversight envisioned.
- Would bake 30-day minimum review periods into development cycles.
- Marcus emphasizes need for genuine independence and transparency.
