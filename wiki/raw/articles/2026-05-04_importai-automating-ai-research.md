# Import AI 455: Automating AI Research

**By Jack Clark** | May 4, 2026

Jack Clark (Anthropic co-founder) lays out his case for why fully automated AI R&D — an AI system that can autonomously build its own successor — is likely by the end of 2028. He pegs the probability at **60%+**.

## Core Thesis

Clark assembles a mosaic of evidence from public sources (arXiv, bioRxiv, NBER, product deployments) to argue that all engineering components for automating AI development are now in place. If scaling trends continue, models may soon substitute for human researchers at identifying novel research paths.

## Evidence Presented

### The Coding Singularity
- **SWE-Bench**: From Claude 2's ~2% (late 2023) to Claude Mythos Preview's 93.9% — effectively saturating the benchmark
- AI systems now write code, tests, and verify results autonomously
- At frontier labs, most people code entirely through AI systems

### METR Time Horizons
- METR measures AI task complexity by how many hours a skilled human would take
- The trend shows AI systems handling longer-duration, more complex tasks over time

### Other Capability Trends
- AI systems are crossing key thresholds in science, reasoning, and long-horizon planning
- The aggregate trend across multiple benchmarks points toward rapid capability growth

## Timing
- Clark does NOT expect full automation in 2026
- But expects a proof-of-concept "model end-to-end trains its successor" within 1-2 years
- Non-frontier models likely automated first; frontier models are harder (more expensive, heavily human-driven)

## Implications
- Crossing this threshold represents a "Rubicon" into a nearly-impossible-to-forecast future
- Clark plans to spend most of 2026 working through the implications
- Society may not be ready for the pace of change implied by automated AI R&D

## Key Quote
> "I now believe we are living in the time that AI research will be end-to-end automated. If that happens, we will cross a Rubicon into a nearly-impossible-to-forecast future."
