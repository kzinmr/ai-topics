1. CIM Screening
Find the best opportunities aligned with your metrics, faster.
Problem:
CIM screening is slow and manual. Teams spend hours pulling revenue mix, customer concentration, margin trends, churn, geographic exposure, competitive dynamics, and key risks. Opportunities slip while teams hand-build summaries and early-stage scorecards.
How Hebbia Helps:
Hebbia’s flagship Matrix product ingests CIMs and generates structured insight instantly. Analysts can issue queries such as:
"Break out revenue, EBITDA, and margin trends for the past three years."
"Summarize customer concentration and top ten accounts by percentage of revenue."
"Highlight risks mentioned more than once across the CIM."
"Extract all operational KPIs and organize them by function."
Outputs return in structured tables and citation-linked summaries that map directly to the original CIM pages. Hebbia understands tables, section headers, footnotes, and embedded charts, ensuring accuracy even in documents with inconsistent formatting. Teams can triage opportunities faster, standardize screening materials, and focus their time on the highest conviction deals.
2. VDR First Pass
Automate the first sweep of a data room.
Problem:
Teams manually open, skim, and catalog hundreds of files. This slows diligence and introduces risk of oversight. There is often repeat work as more documents are added, and early signals get buried before they reach partners.
How Hebbia Helps:
Matrix ingests the full VDR and turns everything into an indexed, searchable library. Teams can ask:
"List every customer contract and pull out key terms including renewal dates, pricing, termination, and exclusivity."
"Extract all financial statements and roll them up into a year over year summary."
"Identify red flags related to regulatory exposure, litigation, or covenant breaches."
"Summarize KPIs across all operational dashboards."
Hebbia returns structured, review-ready summaries and tables that map directly to their source documents, allowing analysts to move from raw data to a comprehensive first-pass memo in hours. As the VDR updates, analysts can re-run queries instantly over the expanded document set. Teams can accelerate VDR organization, highlight issues earlier, and walk into partner meetings with clarity.
3. IC Memo Prep
Generate comps, positioning, and narrative support in minutes.
Problem:
IC prep is a scramble. Teams manually build comps, search filings for market structure commentary, hunt for expert quotes, and attempt to harmonize insights across dozens of sources. Valuable time is lost stitching together evidence and formatting outputs.
How Hebbia Helps:
Hebbia centralizes filings, transcripts, CIM summaries, expert calls, and internal notes in one workspace and Draft can create memos in branded templates with the click of a button. Teams can query:
"Generate a competitive landscape including market share, pricing strategy, and key differentiators."
"Summarize public filings on unit economics and margin profiles across peers."
"Pull quotes from expert calls about switching costs, buying behavior, and operational bottlenecks."
"Create slide-ready comps showing valuation multiples, revenue growth, and EBITDA trends."
Outputs are citation-linked so teams can verify every claim and export tables and narrative summaries directly into IC templates. Hebbia’s infinite context window connects all sources so analysts can form a defensible story supported by data, quotes, and historical evidence without manual cross-referencing.
4. Expert Call Synthesis
Turn dozens of transcripts into a single thesis.
Problem:
Synthesizing calls is slow. Teams scroll through transcripts manually, highlight quotes, and attempt to consolidate themes. Information becomes siloed and it becomes difficult to compare viewpoints or extract consensus.
How Hebbia Helps:
Investment professionals upload transcripts, call notes, surveys, and internal memos. Matrix indexes each document, preserves speaker attribution, and allows analysts to query across all expert materials at once. Teams can ask:
"Summarize insights on pricing power, renewal behavior, and sales cycle length across all expert calls."
"Extract all quotes describing competitive weakness or strengths."
"Identify consensus opinions versus outlier views across experts this quarter."
"Summarize operational risks repeatedly raised in the last six months."
Matrix returns structured, theme-based summaries with citations back to specific speakers. Hebbia consolidates fragmented insights into a single, defensible narrative that accelerates diligence and improves quality of internal discussions.
5. PortCo BoD Library
A searchable intelligence base for every portfolio company.
Problem:
Information becomes decentralized and hard to retrieve. Preparing partner briefings requires manual searching, re-downloading documents, and recreating materials that have already been produced.
How Hebbia Helps:
Matrix turns all PortCo materials into a centralized, living library. Teams can query across years of board decks, financials, management updates, and market research at once:
"Summarize fiscal year trends in revenue, EBITDA, churn, and cash conversion."
"Compare management performance against the previous year's budget and board commitments."
"Extract all commentary on operational issues raised in the last four board meetings."
"Pull all KPIs mentioned across dashboards and organize them into a single table."
Hebbia’s ISD preserves tables, charts, and footnotes, making PortCo documents fully searchable and citation-linked. Partners can instantly brief themselves before meetings or diligence discussions without digging through files.
6. Generate Presentations
From diligence findings to first-draft outputs in your firm's template.
Context:
Deal teams generate decks, memos, summaries, and model-ready tables continuously. Even after the analysis is complete, formatting and writing materials slows teams down.
Problem:
Drafting IC memos, red-flag summaries, operating reviews, and outreach materials requires manual synthesis and formatting. Analysts spend time building documents instead of uncovering insights.
How Hebbia Helps:
Draft, Hebbia’s generative presentation feature, converts analysis into first-pass drafts using your firm's templates. After teams run queries and synthesize findings, they can request:
"Draft a red-flag memo summarizing financial, legal, operational, and market risks with citations."
"Generate an IC draft section covering competitive landscape, KPIs, and valuation context."
"Create a board update summarizing month over month movements in KPIs and financials."
"Build a slide deck summarizing diligence findings grouped by workstream."
Draft pulls structured insights from your documents – or public information and integrations within Hebbia (Factset, CapIQ, PitchBook, ThirdBridge, Guidepoint, and more) -- and outputs narrative text, tables, and slides that align to the firm’s style standards. Every statement remains citation-linked so teams can verify accuracy and iterate quickly. Analysts move from raw data to partner-ready deliverables in minutes.
