## Summary

IBM and MIT launched the MIT-IBM Computing Research Lab, expanding their 2017 AI partnership to include quantum computing, algorithms, and hybrid computing systems.

### Research Pillars
| Area | MIT Lead | IBM Lead |
|------|----------|----------|
| AI | Jacob Andreas | Kenney Ng |
| Algorithms | Vinod Vaikuntanathan | Vasileios Kalantzis |
| Quantum | Aram Harrow | Hanhee Paik |

Co-Directors: Aude Oliva (MIT CSAIL), David Cox (IBM Research VP)

### Key Objectives
- Small, efficient, modular language models for enterprise
- Quantum-centric supercomputing toward 2029 fault-tolerant quantum computer
- Hybrid systems combining classical, AI, and quantum hardware
- Applications: materials science, drug discovery, finance, climate modeling

### Legacy
- 210+ projects, 1,500+ papers, 500+ students since 2017 predecessor lab
- Builds on MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab (est. 2017)
