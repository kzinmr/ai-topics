# 2024 Reflection | Nikita Lapkov

Source: https://laplab.me/posts/2024-reflection/

Holy shit, what a year that was. It was absolutely bonkers overwhelming. A lot of interesting stuff happened, but at the same time I took on so much more than I could handle.
Conferences
Speaking at RustLab 2024
During late 2023 and early 2024 I applied to a bunch of conferences. Previously, I didn’t have any luck of becoming a speaker at non-Russian conferences - all of my talk proposals were rejected (with a notable exception of Handmade Seattle, which happened late 2023). I was expecting at most one conference reaching out to me and inviting me to speak. Instead, I was very humbled (and overwhelmed!) to get accepted to
five
conferences this year. This was absolutely unexpected and I am very grateful to the organisers for trusting me on delivering a good talk. Due to logistical and financial reasons, I was only able to accept invitations to four conferences - which was still 4x of my best expectations.
I spoke at RustNation UK, P99 Conf, EuroRust and RustLab this year. All conferences had absolutely amazing organizers who made the process smooth - I got the smallest glimpse of the colossal effort which goes into this. That being said, I don’t recommend doing four conferences in one year. This was very exhausting (and expensive!) to pull off. Making the slides, practicing the talks, flying every few months somewhere - this sounds like a dream and I’m very privileged to have this, but it’s still hard. I will have a soft limit of one and a hard limit of two conferences per year - no matter if I’m speaking there or just attending.
I’ve met a lot of amazing folks and I was very happy to see familiar faces. I’m extremely grateful for this experience and will be taking some well-earned rest now.
Startup
Key-value that finally makes sense! …or does it?
I’ve tried to build a company! I failed!
A friend of mine referred me to a VC firm and after some talking they suggested to build a company. I was very excited (and a bit naive), so I jumped at the opportunity. I spent 9 months working in my free time (in addition to a full-time employment) building prototypes, talking with experts in the field and trying to see if my ideas have any chance of becoming a sustainable business.
This was a whole process on its own and probably deserves a separate blog post. In a nutshell, instead of focusing on the problems of users and trying to solve them with technology, I tried to focus on cool technology and then see if somebody needs it. This is a hard path in any industry, but I’m now convinced that it is absolutely impossible to pull of in databases and data processing. Unless users have a huge problem that your new database is going to solve, they are going to use Postgres, no matter how unsuitable it is for the problem. That’s fine! I think this year we saw a lot of posts claiming that Postgres is a solution to everything. This just seems to be the current industry consensus. I don’t agree with it, but I’m also not going to put down “convincing the entire industry that it’s wrong” as a requirement for success for my business.
In the end, I fully tested two ideas and confirmed that neither of them will result in a working business. I even presented one of these ideas on RustLab this year. However, this all came at a cost of me needing to take a sick leave for a month for medical reasons, indirectly caused by essentially working two jobs at the same time. All of my bravado that I will be fine and that I just need a few more months for the startup to fly got the best of me. I’m now convinced of two things: you need to dedicate 100% of your
working
time to the startup and you need to have a lot of
non-working
time (e.g. rest, hobbies) for this to be sustainable. I’m no longer a 19-year old that is able to drink rocket fuel, power through a 48-hour hackathon and then go to school fresh next day. I need to respect my body and its needs.
Changing jobs
View from Cloudflare’s London office
I changed jobs and got into Cloudflare! I have a multi-year history of interviewing with Cloudflare and then either deciding it’s not the best thing for me at the moment or just getting rejected, so finally getting here feels like closing a gestalt. I’ve only been here for a few months, but I like it so far!
This transition also marks the end of me working at small companies for almost two years. First, I worked in a small trading fund of 70 people and then at Prisma, which was also something like 100 people at the time. As with everything, there are good and bad things with working in such companies. I loved the pace of small companies and people who make it possible, but my capacity for work drama was exceeded 300%. Now I want to try working at small teams within larger organisation - which is partly the reason why I joined Cloudflare’s Developer Platform organisation.
Becoming a Dungeon Master
3D printed figurines of player characters
I became a Dungeon Master! It was a bit scary at first, but that faded away with time. I enjoy doing this a lot. Currently, I’m going through my “let’s build a huge multi-year homebrew” era, which from what I understand every DM goes through. We will see how it goes!
Just generally, I took a huge dive into role-playing games (RPG) community and it was very rewarding. I’m also very happy that I can share this hobby with my wife, who is a player at my table. We watch Dimension 20 every evening, we recently went to an RPG convention, my wife is DMing for the first time soon - so much interesting stuff is out there and so many talented people putting in amazing work to produce jewelry, story supplements, extensions, dice and much more! Truly, it seems like a neverending nerdy Christmas fare.

Wrapping up and thinking forward
This is just a glimpse of what 2024 was for me. There was also a lot of personal stuff - meeting new friends, losing old ones, trying to understand who even is Nikita Lapkov and what role his past plays in his current life. Is it even a “he” and not “they”? I don’t know :)
What I do know, however, is that 2025 is going to be a good year. It will be a year of gradual progress, of building on the foundations I layed out in the previous years and taking it a bit slower than usual. It will be a year of good friends, rewarding work and learning self-compassion.
Happy holidays and take care of yourself!
