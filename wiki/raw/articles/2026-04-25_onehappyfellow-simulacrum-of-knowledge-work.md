# Simulacrum of Knowledge Work

- **Author**: @onehappyfellow (blog.happyfellow.dev)
- **Date**: April 25, 2026
- **URL**: https://blog.happyfellow.dev/simulacrum-of-knowledge-work/

## Summary

A critical essay examining how LLMs have broken the proxy measures that knowledge work relies on for quality assessment, creating a "simulacrum of knowledge work" where the surface-level appearance of quality (well-written reports, clean code) no longer correlates with substantive quality.

## Key Concepts

- **Proxy Measures in Knowledge Work**: Quality is inherently expensive to evaluate, so organizations rely on cheap proxies (writing quality, formatting, presentation polish). These proxies historically correlated well enough with actual quality.

- **LLMs Broke Proxy Measures**: LLMs excel at simulating the *style* of high-quality work without reproducing the *substance*. A ChatGPT-generated market analysis looks like a top-tier consulting deliverable. AI-generated code passes a quick skim. AI-conducted code reviews find plausible issues.

- **Simulacrum of Knowledge Work**: The entire ritual of producing and reviewing work can now be performed by LLMs, with humans serving as a thin approval layer — "slap LGTM on it and open their 17th Claude Code session."

- **Incentive Alignment Problem**: Workers are rationally optimizing for the metrics they're measured on (surface quality). When LLMs can produce surface-quality output at negligible cost, the rational response is to maximize LLM-generated output.

- **Recursive Problem**: LLM training itself relies on proxy measures — next-token prediction optimizes for "looks like training data"; RLHF optimizes for "satisfies the judge." The same Goodhart dynamic applies to the models themselves.

- **Goodhart's Law Applied to AI**: "We've automated ourselves into Goodhart's law" — when a measure (surface quality) becomes the target, it ceases to be a good measure.

## Key Quotes

> "We have built a working simulacrum of knowledge work."

> "We spent billions to create systems used to perform a simulacrum of work. Companies are racing to be the first on the tokens-spent leaderboard. The more LLM output workers produce, the less time anyone spends on looking deeply at the output."
