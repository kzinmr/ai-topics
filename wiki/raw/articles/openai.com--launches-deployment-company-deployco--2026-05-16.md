# OpenAI Launches the OpenAI Deployment Company (DeployCo)

> Source: OpenAI Blog — May 11, 2026 + Axios reporting
> URL: https://openai.com/index/openai-launches-the-deployment-company/

OpenAI has launched the OpenAI Deployment Company (DeployCo), a new entity designed to help organizations build and deploy AI systems for day-to-day operations.

## Key Facts

- **Investment**: $4 billion initial investment at a $10 billion pre-money valuation
- **Structure**: Majority-owned and controlled by OpenAI
- **Lead investor**: TPG, with Advent International, Bain Capital, and Brookfield as co-lead founding partners
- **Other investors**: B Capital, BBVA, Emergence Capital, Goanna, Goldman Sachs, SoftBank Corp., Thrive, Warburg Pincus, WCAS
- **Consulting partners**: Bain & Company, Capgemini, and McKinsey & Company (also investors)
- **Total founding partners**: 19 global investment firms, consultancies, and system integrators
- **Investor terms** (per Axios): Guaranteed minimum 17.5% return with profits capped
- **Acquisition**: Tomoro, an applied AI consulting and engineering firm (~150 Forward Deployed Engineers and Deployment Specialists)

## Strategy

DeployCo is designed to bridge the gap between AI capability and operational impact. It provides Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs) who work inside client organizations to connect OpenAI's models with internal data, tools, controls, and core business processes.

> "AI is becoming capable of doing increasingly meaningful work inside organizations. The challenge now is helping companies integrate these systems into the infrastructure and workflows that power their businesses." — Denise Dresser, Chief Revenue Officer at OpenAI

## Context

Per Axios, Anthropic is reportedly pursuing a comparable enterprise AI services effort. Goldman Sachs is the only backer of both DeployCo and Anthropic's initiative. The move signals a shift from selling model access to providing implementation services — AI companies moving into territory traditionally held by consulting firms like Accenture and Deloitte.

The Tomoro acquisition brings experience from mission-critical workflows at Tesco, Virgin Atlantic, and Supercell.
