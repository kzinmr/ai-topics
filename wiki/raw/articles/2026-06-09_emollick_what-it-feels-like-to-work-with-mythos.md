# What it feels like to work with Mythos

Claude Fable represents another big jump in AI. Ethan Mollick had early access to the first Mythos-class AI model being released to the public, Claude 5 Fable.

## Key findings

- Fable outperformed basically every other public model by a considerable margin across experiments
- Capable across many problems and produced startling results — would work up to a dozen hours executing on multi-page specifications
- Made the most sophisticated academic social science paper yet seen from an AI from a single prompt and one piece of feedback
- Created a 10-page epic rhyming poem about a haircut where every word starts with the letter s
- Created games from vague prompts in Claude Code where Fable had to take prompts and generate something workable, followed by additional prompts with minor encouragement or feedback
- Claude cannot generate images, so every piece of art or 3D object was made with math alone, not using any external assets
- The guardrails around Fable essentially prevent it from being used for cybersecurity at all

## Implications

- Represents a very real leap over every model used before
- Suggests our relationship with AI is changing in drastic ways
- Much of the discussion of Mythos has centered on its impact on software security, but this testing focused on other domains
- The problem about communicating results is that many of the most impressive results are going to be interesting to only small portions of readers
