# Services: The New Software

**Author:** Julien Bek, Sequoia Capital
**Published:** March 5, 2026
**Core Thesis:** The next $1T company will be a software company masquerading as a services firm. Instead of selling tools to professionals (SaaS), the next generation of winners will sell the **outcome** (the work itself).

---

## 1. Intelligence vs. Judgement
The transition from AI as a tool to AI as a service provider depends on the distinction between two types of work:

*   **Intelligence:** Tasks involving complex rules, debugging, testing, and translating specs. AI has now crossed the threshold to perform these autonomously.
*   **Judgement:** Tasks requiring experience, taste, instinct, and strategic decision-making (e.g., deciding which feature to build or when to take on tech debt).
*   **The Shift:** Software engineering is the "canary in the coal mine." Because it is primarily intelligence-based, it accounts for over 50% of AI tool usage. This trend is now expanding to all other professions.

> "Writing code is mostly *intelligence*. Knowing what to build next is *judgement*."

---

## 2. Copilots vs. Autopilots
The business model is shifting from selling productivity to selling results.

| Feature | Copilot (The Tool) | Autopilot (The Work) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Customer** | The Professional (e.g., Lawyer) | The End-User (e.g., CFO/Company) |
| **Value Prop** | Makes the pro more productive | Delivers the final outcome |
| **Budget** | Software/Tool Budget | Labor/Work Budget (6x larger) |
| **Responsibility** | Human takes responsibility | AI/Service provider takes responsibility |

**The Convergence:** Today's "judgement" will become tomorrow's "intelligence" as AI systems accumulate proprietary data. Copilots and autopilots will eventually merge, but starting as an autopilot allows a company to capture the larger labor budget immediately.

---

## 3. The Autopilot Playbook: Outsourcing as the Wedge
For every $1 spent on software, $6 is spent on services. The most effective way to enter a market is to target tasks that are already outsourced.

**Why Outsourcing is the ideal wedge:**
1.  **Acceptance:** The company already accepts external work.
2.  **Budget:** There is a clear line item to substitute.
3.  **Frictionless:** Replacing an outsourcing contract is a "vendor swap"; replacing internal headcount is a "reorg."

> "The outsourced task is the wedge. The insourced work is the long-term TAM."

---

## 4. Opportunity Map: High-Potential Verticals
Sequoia identifies several sectors ripe for "Service-as-Software" disruption based on their intelligence-to-judgement ratio and outsourcing maturity.

### High-Value Targets
*   **Insurance Brokerage ($140-200B):** Highly standardized; value is in form-filling and shopping carriers. *Key Players: WithCoverage, Harper.*
*   **Accounting & Audit ($50-80B):** Facing a massive talent shortage (340k accountants lost in 5 years). *Key Players: Rillet (AI ERP), Basis.*
*   **Healthcare Revenue Cycle ($50-80B):** Medical coding is pure intelligence (translating notes to ICD-10 codes). *Key Player: Anterior.*
*   **Claims Adjusting ($50-80B):** Aging workforce and high outsourcing to Third Party Administrators (TPAs). *Key Players: Pace, Strala.*
*   **IT Managed Services ($100B+):** SMBs already outsource this. Opportunity to sell "Your IT runs" as an outcome. *Key Players: Edra, Serval.*
*   **Recruitment & Staffing ($200B+):** Top-of-funnel (screening/outreach) is intelligence-heavy. *Key Players: Juicebox, Mercor, Jack & Jill.*

### Emerging/Niche Targets
*   **Supply Chain/Procurement ($200B+):** Focus on the "long tail" of suppliers that humans don't have time to negotiate with. *Key Players: Magentic, AskLio, Tacto.*
*   **Tax Advisory ($30-35B):** High intelligence ratio; complexity increases with multiple jurisdictions. *Key Players: TaxGPT, Skalar, Ravical.*
*   **Legal Transactional ($20-25B):** NDAs and regulatory filings. *Key Players: Harvey, Crosby, Lawhive.*
*   **Management Consulting ($300-400B):** Currently judgement-heavy, but intelligence components (data gathering) will be disaggregated.

---

## 5. The Innovator's Dilemma
Incumbent "Copilot" companies (the winners of 2025) will struggle to transition to "Autopilots" because selling the work directly effectively cuts their own professional customers out of the loop. This creates a massive opening for **pure-play autopilot startups** to capture the market.
