## Summary

The Yale Chief Executive Leadership Institute (CELI), led by Jeffrey Sonnenfeld, published an agentic AI governance framework for banking, healthcare, retail, and supply chain sectors. Anthropic's Mythos model was cited as exposing a crisis in corporate governance.

### Key Governance Principles
- Agents as "digital contractors" with identity-based access control
- Three-tiered guardrails: Foundational, Risk-Based, Societal
- Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) for high-risk decisions
- Human-on-the-Loop (HOTL) for lower-risk tasks
- ISO/IEC 42001 compliance standards

### Risk Landscape
- "Excessive Agency" vulnerability — agents modifying databases or executing financial transactions from unexpected outputs
- Indirect Prompt Injection — malicious instructions hidden in web content
- EU AI Act mandates "effective human oversight" for high-risk systems

### Economic Context
- McKinsey: agentic automation adds $2.6-4.4 trillion annually
- Organizations with robust governance gain competitive advantage
