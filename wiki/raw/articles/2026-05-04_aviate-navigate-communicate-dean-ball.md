# Aviate, Navigate, Communicate

**Author:** Dean W. Ball | **Source:** Hyperdimensional | **Date:** May 4, 2026

## Overview
Dean Ball explores AI policy after the emergence of **Mythos**, Anthropic's non-public model with unprecedented hacking capabilities. He proposes a structured "triage-mode" governance framework.

## The "Mythos" Moment
- Mythos can autonomously discover software vulnerabilities by stringing multiple bugs into coherent exploits
- Previously reserved for elite human hackers
- The U.S. government has asked Anthropic not to release Mythos beyond a limited group

## Structural Argument Against Overreaction
Governments have a fundamental conflict of interest:
- **Incentive for less secure software**: Governments sometimes hoard vulnerabilities for espionage
- **CAISI underfunded**: Budget <$10M, lacks technical compute/personnel
- **Politicization risk**: Unilateral regulator vulnerable to shifting administrations

## Proposed Governance Framework
1. **Release delay for cyber-capable models**: Open-weight model sets the risk "floor"
2. **Expand public-private vulnerability sharing**: Modeled on Project Glasswing
3. **Fund provably secure software**: Focused Research Organizations (FROs) for formal verification

## Independent Verification Organizations (IVOs)
- Publicly overseen, privately managed nonpartisan bodies
- Role: Verifying safety claims on behalf of the public
- Federal framework to preempt state regulatory patchwork (CA, NY)
