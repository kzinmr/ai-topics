# EAGLE 3.1: Advancing Speculative Decoding — Collaboration Between EAGLE Team, vLLM, and TorchSpec

**Source**: https://vllm.ai/blog/2026-05-26-eagle-3-1
**Date**: May 26, 2026

## Overview

The EAGLE series (EAGLE 1, 2, 3) is among the most widely deployed speculative decoding families. EAGLE 3.1, jointly developed by the EAGLE, vLLM, and TorchSpec teams, brings major gains in robustness, efficiency, and practical deployability.

## Key Innovations

**Problem:** Speculative decoding often degrades under different chat templates, long contexts, or out-of-distribution prompts.
**Root cause:** *Attention drift* — as speculation depth grows, the drafter shifts attention away from sink tokens toward its own generated tokens.

Two underlying issues:
1. **Imbalanced fused input** – higher-layer hidden states dominate the drafter input.
2. **Magnitude growth** – hidden-state magnitude increases across steps (unnormalized residual path).

### Architectural Fixes
- **FC normalization** after each target hidden state, before the FC layer.
- **Feeding post-norm hidden states** into the next decoding step.

### Measured Improvements
- Better training-to-inference extrapolation
- Stronger long-context robustness
- Higher resilience to chat template / system prompt variation
- More stable acceptance length across diverse serving environments
- In long-context workloads, EAGLE 3.1 achieves up to **2× longer acceptance length** compared to EAGLE 3.

## Training with TorchSpec
TorchSpec now provides efficient training support for EAGLE 3.1. Draft model for Kimi K2.6 open-sourced: `lightseekorg/kimi-k2.6-eagle3.1-mla`

## vLLM Integration
Config-driven extension of existing EAGLE 3 implementation, fully backward-compatible. Available in vLLM v0.22.0.

## Performance (SPEED-Bench coding, Kimi-K2.6-NVFP4, vLLM TP=4, GB200)
- C=1: 2.03× speedup
- C=4: 1.71× speedup
- C=16: 1.66× speedup
