# ACP Joins Forces with A2A Under the Linux Foundation

**Source:** [i-am-bee GitHub Discussions](https://github.com/orgs/i-am-bee/discussions/5)  
**Date:** August 25, 2025  
**Announcement by:** Kate Blair (`geneknit`), Director of Incubation for IBM Research

## Executive Summary

IBM Research has announced that the **Agent Communication Protocol (ACP)** is officially merging with the **Agent2Agent Protocol (A2A)** under the **Linux Foundation** umbrella. This merger aims to create a single, unified standard for AI agent communication and collaboration, consolidating the expertise of major industry players.

## Key Organizational Changes

- **Consolidation:** ACP development is winding down; the team is now contributing directly to the A2A project.
- **Governance:** Kate Blair (IBM) joins the **A2A Technical Steering Committee (TSC)**.
- **Industry Alignment:** The A2A TSC now includes representatives from Google, Microsoft, AWS, Cisco, Salesforce, ServiceNow, SAP, and IBM.

## Impact on BeeAI Users

The BeeAI platform, which previously relied on ACP, is transitioning to A2A:

- **A2AServer Adapter:** Used to make agents built with the BeeAI framework A2A-compliant.
- **A2AAgent Integration:** Allows external A2A agents (regardless of their original framework) to be integrated into BeeAI applications.
- **Platform Update:** The BeeAI platform now uses A2A natively to support cross-framework agent interaction.

## Project Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|

| April 2025 | Agent2Agent (A2A) Protocol launched by Google |
| August 25, 2025 | Official merger of ACP into A2A announced |
