:Harvey: to Open Singapore Office in June
by
Harvey Team
•
Mar 12, 2026
Harvey is expanding to Singapore. Opening in June, our new office reflects accelerating growth across APAC and deepening partnerships with legal teams throughout the region.
Singapore plays an outsized role in global commerce and is one of the world’s most innovative markets. It is a centre for cross-border transactions, disputes, finance, and technology — and a meeting point for legal work that spans jurisdictions. As more firms and in-house teams across APAC leverage AI to transform the way they work, we’re investing locally to support that shift.
We entered the Singapore market with WongPartnership LLP as our first customer in South East Asia. Since then, our presence has expanded significantly to include leading law firms and legal teams, including Rajah & Tann Singapore LLP, Drew & Napier LLC, Braddell Brothers LLP, Lee & Lee, Oon & Bazul LLP, TSMP Law Corporation, organisations such as Airwallex and our strategic partnership with PwC, and the
Singapore Judiciary
.
Establishing a presence on the ground allows us to work more closely with these teams as they scale adoption and embed Harvey into mission-critical workflows across offices and jurisdictions.
“APAC is a major growth engine for us, and Singapore sits at the intersection of legal, financial, and tech innovation,” said Winston Weinberg, CEO and Co-founder of Harvey. “It consistently ranks among the most innovative economies in the world, which is a testament to its forward-thinking business environment and deep commitment to technology and legal-tech adoption.”
The Singapore office builds on our existing footprint in Sydney and Bengaluru, reinforcing support for customers across APAC with local expertise and global coordination.
“We’re seeing strong momentum from customers across the region adopting AI in real workflows,” said John Haddock, Chief Business Officer at Harvey. “Being here allows us to partner more deeply with legal teams as they scale Harvey across offices and across borders — in a market that’s truly leading in innovation and transformation.”
The office will include legal engineering, customer success, sales, and G&A roles. If you’re interested in helping shape the future of legal work in APAC, explore open roles on our
careers page
.
