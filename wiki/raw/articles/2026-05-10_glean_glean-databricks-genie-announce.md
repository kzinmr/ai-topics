AI Summary by Glean
Glean's partnership with Databricks enables users to discover and analyze complex data using natural language, leveraging Databricks AI/BI Genie's capabilities.
The integration allows users to securely and efficiently query structured data without needing additional resources, such as analysts or engineers.
Users can interact with both structured and unstructured data through a simple interface, maintaining permissions and security while enhancing enterprise data analysis.
We’re thrilled to announce today that Glean is partnering with Databricks to enable everyday users to discover and analyze tabular information with natural language, thanks to
Databricks AI/BI Genie
's advanced capabilities of processing structured data. This upcoming integration will enable end-to-end workflows involving complex datasets directly within Glean Assistant.
Grounded in Glean’s understanding of enterprise data and permissioning rules, all responses follow user permissions set within Databricks’ Unity Catalog. This ensures anyone can securely and efficiently query data without having to tap additional resources or team members for assistance, such as analysts and engineers.
"Databricks AI/BI provides a new paradigm for connecting business users with data and analytics," said Adam Conway, SVP of Products at Databricks. "We are excited to partner with Glean to enable data presented in Genie to be discovered and analyzed directly in Glean, allowing users to take advantage of AI to explore the full breadth of enterprise data."
No-hassle data discovery and analysis
Let’s walk through a quick example displaying how an everyday Glean user will be able to make the most of this new integration with Genie. For example, marketers can use this new integration to understand complex structured data without the help of engineers, analysts, or data scientists.
In this example, a marketing manager wants to identify which subscribers are churning in order to better target her promotional offers. The information she needs is distributed across multiple applications, including a MySQL database, Excel spreadsheets, and Powerpoint presentations. Normally, she would have to contact various team members for help. With Genie and Glean, she can complete the entire workflow herself in a matter of minutes. She starts by asking Glean, “What states and age groups have the highest churn rate on our website in 2024?”
Glean understands that Sarah is asking a question that can be answered by Genie, and invokes it to pull churn data relevant to the prompt. Based on the results, she finds that 18-24 year olds and 25-40 year olds in the US Pacific region are churning the most. Now she wants to understand why, and asks, “What are the top reasons that 18-40 year olds in the US Pacific provided for canceling their subscription?”
This enables her to confirm that pricing is the top reason for subscriber churn. However, she wants to further understand the competitive landscape to better calibrate her promotional offer. She asks Glean, “Show me the monthly subscription prices for our top competitors in California, ranked by market share.” Glean provides the price details, sourced from an Excel spreadsheet that the research team regularly updates.
Finally, Sarah checks Glean to see if the marketing team has previously sent any promotional emails to the younger demographics in the Pacific region. Glean points her to a similar initiative that was successful in September 2023, sourced from a campaign powerpoint. Now provided with all the right information, Sarah gets about to building her promotional campaign. If she’s interested in digging deeper on her own later, she can click on the link to the Genie Space to perform more advanced queries.
Enterprise-ready structured data analysis
We understand that getting the right answers regarding enterprise knowledge takes a horizontal approach to information search. Since the day we launched Glean Assistant, providing our users with the capability to interact with and analyze structured, tabular data has been a priority for us.
Soon, with the power of Glean's enterprise-ready platform combined with
Databricks AI/BI Genie
’s structured data capabilities, users can interact with a simple interface to chat with all company data—structured or unstructured—regardless of where it lives.
"Glean is excited about the release of Databricks AI/BI Genie, and the potential to integrate Genie as a native knowledge source within Glean," said Arvind Jain, Co-founder and CEO of Glean. "Integration of Glean and Genie will allow users to chat with the breadth of their enterprise data, uniting structured and unstructured data like never before while maintaining permissions and security."
