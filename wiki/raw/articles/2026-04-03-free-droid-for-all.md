# Free Droid for all.

Posted by @0xSero, April 3, 2026.

Factory AI announced "Free Droid for all" — making their autonomous coding agents (Droids) available to all developers. This marks a significant shift from paid-only enterprise access to a freemium/open model.

## Key Details

Factory AI's Droids are autonomous agents designed for long-horizon software engineering tasks. Unlike IDE assistants (GitHub Copilot, Cursor), Droids execute entire workflows: feature development, testing, code review, documentation, and deployment.

The announcement aligns with Factory AI's $150M Series C (April 16, 2026) at a $1.5B valuation, led by Khosla Ventures with participation from Sequoia Capital, Insight Partners, and Blackstone.

## Platform Capabilities

- **Missions:** Describe business outcomes in natural language; Droids plan, execute, and verify work over hours/days
- **Model-Agnostic Routing:** Route tasks to different LLMs (Claude 4.5, DeepSeek V3, etc.) based on requirements
- **Enterprise Integration:** Deep CI/CD, API, compliance tooling integration
- **Desktop App:** Supervised access to local filesystems, browsers, and command lines
- **Multi-Interface:** Works in IDE (VS Code, JetBrains, Vim), terminal, web browser, Slack, Jira

## Strategic Significance

The move to "Free Droid" suggests Factory is betting on network effects and developer adoption over immediate monetization — similar to how Vercel, Supabase, and other devtools grew. By lowering the barrier to entry, they aim to build a large developer base that can be upsold to enterprise features.

This mirrors industry trends seen with Mistral's Vibe Remote Agents and other companies making autonomous coding capabilities broadly accessible.
