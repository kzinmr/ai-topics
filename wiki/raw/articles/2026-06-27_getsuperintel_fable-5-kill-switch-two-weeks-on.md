# The Fable 5 Kill-Switch, Two Weeks On

Source: https://getsuperintel.site/p/the-fable-5-kill-switch-two-weeks-on
Author: Kim "Chubby" Isenberg, Super Intel newsletter
Date: June 27, 2026

## Summary

Two weeks after the Commerce Department letter forced Anthropic to take Fable 5 and Mythos 5 offline globally, this analysis examines the gap between competing narratives and the precedent being set.

## Key Points

### The Commerce Department Letter (June 12, 2026)
- Received at Anthropic at 5:21pm ET on a Friday
- Signed by Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick
- Required a license for "export, reexport, or transfer (in-country)" of Fable 5 and Mythos 5 "to all destinations worldwide and to all 'foreign persons' ... wherever located"
- Threatened "prompt criminal and civil penalties"
- Stays in force "until superseded by a subsequent letter"

### The "Deemed Export" Mechanism
- The license attaches to foreign persons, not to Americans
- Under the Cold War "deemed export" provision, giving a foreign national access to controlled technology — even on US soil — is treated as an export to that person's home country
- Because Anthropic cannot sort users by passport in real time across an API, the only way to comply was to pull the plug for everyone
- A rule targeting only foreign users could only be obeyed by taking the model away from all users

### Two Competing Narratives
1. **Capitol Hill version** (Senator Mark Warner, relaying NSA/Cyber Command head): Mythos "broke into almost all of our classified systems, not in weeks, but in hours"
2. **Anonymous official to AP** (12 days later): The model had "identified vulnerabilities within hours in an authorized test," which "did not mean the model was able to exploit them"

"One sentence describes a cyberweapon breaking into the nation's secrets. The other describes a security tool doing its job. They refer to the same week."

### Timeline (June 9-25, 2026)
Nine documented events from launch to worldwide shutdown in sixteen days.

### Key Question
Whether June 12 was "the opening of an era in which governments treat access to frontier AI as a lever of state power, or a clumsy one-off already being walked back."
