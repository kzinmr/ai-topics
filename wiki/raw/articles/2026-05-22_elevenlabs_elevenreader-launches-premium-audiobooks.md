Today, we’re expanding the
ElevenReader
library by introducing more than 200,000 premium audiobooks and eBooks. Available to subscribers globally, this brings listeners access to more titles, in more languages, for less. These audiobook and eBook titles are from a broad range of publishers, including Blackstone Publishing and Vinci Books.
ElevenReader Ultra subscribers pay just $11 per month to receive 20 hours of access to premium titles each month, in addition to the existing paid catalog, and across more than a dozen languages. A significant portion of these titles are bestsellers, and title availability spans every genre—from cozy mystery to historical romance—and includes Agatha Christie's
The Murder of Roger Ackroyd
, Karin Slaughter's
Cop Town
, Walter Isaacson’s
Kissinger
, as well as classics like Emily Brontë's
Wuthering Heights
, Fyodor Dostoevsky's
White Nights
, and Marcus Aurelius’
Meditations
. Listeners get 33% more audiobook hours in comparison to other streaming services—with no individual book credits required—and at a lower price.
More ways to listen
For a selection of titles in the app, listeners can choose their own narrator voice, powered by
ElevenLabs
’ AI voice technology, in 16 languages—personalizing how books sound with a wide selection of unique, high-quality voices that fit their preferences, subject to publisher allowance. This feature addresses a real need: 74% of audiobook listeners report stopping an audiobook in the last year—not because of the story, but because of the narrator’s voice.
In addition to its catalog, ElevenReader allows listeners to import their own PDFs, documents, and articles and have them read aloud in lifelike voices—making the app the ideal audio companion for productive listening on the commute, at home, or anywhere in between.
“We’re making premium audiobooks more accessible than ever, while giving listeners new ways to personalize their experience,” said Jack McDermott, Growth Lead at ElevenLabs. “Whether you’re listening to a bestseller from a world-class publisher or choosing your own narrator voice, ElevenReader puts more freedom and control in listeners’ hands.”
