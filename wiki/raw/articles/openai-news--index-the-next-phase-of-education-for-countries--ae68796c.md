# Summary: The Next Phase of OpenAI’s Education for Countries

**Published:** May 20, 2026 | **Source:** [OpenAI Blog](https://openai.com/index/the-next-phase-of-education-for-countries)

---

## Key Highlights

- **900 million** people use ChatGPT weekly; **4 million** use Codex.
- OpenAI emphasizes that responsible AI deployment in education requires **government-led research partnerships**, not afterthoughts.
- **Education for Countries** program launched at Davos earlier this year; now shares early results and welcomes **Singapore** to the cohort.
- First cohort: **Estonia, Greece, Italy’s CRUI, Slovakia, Trinidad & Tobago, Kazakhstan, UAE, Jordan**.
- Focus on three pillars: **research-driven deployment**, **localized AI tools**, and **teacher training**.

---

## Three Core Pillars

1. **Research-driven deployment**  
   - Uses OpenAI’s *Learning Outcomes Measurement Suite*.  
   - Governments and educators study AI’s impact on learners, adapt the tech, and build shared evidence.

2. **Localized AI tools for learning**  
   - System-wide access to secure, compliant **ChatGPT, Codex, and API platform**, tailored for education.

3. **Teacher training and enablement**  
   - AI literacy, professional development, and certifications for confident, responsible use.

---

## Progress from the First Cohort

### Estonia
- **ChatGPT Edu** reaches **20,000+ students** and **4,600 teachers** via AI Leap Foundation and Ministry of Education.
- Next phase: localisation, sovereign capability, real-world impact measurement.
- Collaboration with **University of Tartu** and **Stanford** to study learning outcomes publicly.
- **Presidential Codex Hackathon**: 150+ participants in 30 teams built tools like a *Maths Feedback Coach* and *AI STEM Tutor*.

> “AI Leap and OpenAI have brought global AI expertise into the Estonian education system while also researching how these tools influence learning, thinking, and long-term skill development. We believe the next generation of AI—including agents and tools like Codex—can support more personalized and proactive learning while still strengthening critical thinking, independent learning, and the role of teachers. We’re excited to help develop approaches that other education systems can build on.”  
> — **Laura Kalda, AI Leap, COO**

### Jordan
- **1 million+ students** and **100,000+ teachers** have used **Siraj**, an AI Education Assistant, as part of a national public-service AI initiative by the *National Council for Future Technology*.

### Greece
- **OpenAI for Greece AI Startup Accelerator** selected **21 AI-native startups** from 240 applications to retain talent and spur national innovation.

### Kazakhstan
- National **ChatGPT Edu** deployment across all **20 regions**.
- **84,000 educators** completed AI-readiness training.
- **9 in 10** surveyed educators find ChatGPT Edu useful; **44,000 active educators** sent **1.5 million prompts** in the first month.

### Slovakia
- Early university surveys: **>90%** of educators report higher productivity, saving **~5 hours/week**.
- Ministry of Education used **Workspace Agents** to draft revised teacher professional standards linked to the national AI competency framework—reducing **months of work to hours**.

## Singapore Joins the Program

- Singapore’s Ministry of Education (MOE) and GovTech are working with OpenAI to:
  - Build AI use cases for **personalized learning**, e.g., interactive mother tongue practice.
  - Run **OpenAI Academy** workshops and **Codex for Teachers hackathons**.
  - Ensure teacher-driven, responsible, and equitable AI adoption.
- **43% of ChatGPT usage** among 18–24 year‑olds in Singapore is already tied to learning and education.

## Looking Ahead

- OpenAI will soon launch **OpenAI Luminaries**, an educator engagement track focused on:
  - Co‑design with teachers
  - Practical classroom resources
  - Sharing teacher‑led examples across countries
- The program is now **selecting country partners** for the next cohort (announcement later this year). Interested governments can contact OpenAI to apply.
