## The Core Crisis: Deployment vs. Governance
A massive gap between AI agent deployment speed and governance maturity.

- **80% of Fortune 500** use active AI agents built with low-code/no-code tools
- Only **10%** have a clear strategy to manage them
- Enterprise risk is accumulating faster here than any other category in 2026

> "We found 47 autonomous agents running across six business units that we had never approved, never audited, and couldn't even name." — Fortune 100 CISO

## The Evolution of Shadow AI

| Feature | Traditional Shadow AI | Agentic Shadow AI (2026) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nature** | Human-initiated, single interaction | Autonomous, continuous, multi-step |
| **Access** | Copy-pasting data | API access to CRM, Email, Databases |
| **Visibility** | Manageable via DLP/Web filters | Often invisible; chains actions across services |
| **Scale** | One-off data exposure | 4 million prompts/week (observed in one firm) |

## Why Traditional Governance Fails
1. **Identity Model Mismatch:** Human IAM assumes stable roles. AI agents are ephemeral and dynamic.
2. **Permission Model Issues:** Organizations grant agents broad standing permissions "just in case."
3. **Speed Mismatch:** Agents make hundreds of API calls/sec. Human-speed approval can't keep up.

## Critical Data Points (2025-2026)
- 91% of organizations use AI agents; 88% reported security incidents
- 29% of employees use unsanctioned agents
- 223 data policy violations per month in average enterprise
- AI governance spending projected to hit $1B by 2030

## The 5-Capability Control Framework
1. **Registry:** Centralized inventory of every agent
2. **Access Control:** JIT provisioning with TTL-based identities
3. **Visualization:** Real-time dashboards for agent actions
4. **Interoperability:** MCP and A2A protocol support
5. **Security:** Runtime enforcement and behavioral anomaly detection
