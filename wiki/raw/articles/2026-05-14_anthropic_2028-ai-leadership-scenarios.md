# 2028: Two scenarios for global AI leadership

**Published:** May 14, 2026
**Source:** Anthropic Research

## Executive Summary

Anthropic's policy paper frames US-China AI competition through the lens of **compute** — the advanced semiconductors required to train and deploy frontier AI. The paper presents two scenarios for 2028 and argues that democracies must act now to maintain their lead.

**Key thesis:** Democracies currently hold a substantial compute advantage thanks to American innovation and bipartisan export controls. However, Chinese AI labs remain close on model intelligence through two workarounds: (1) illicit compute access via smuggling and offshore data centers, and (2) large-scale distillation attacks on US frontier models. If policymakers close these loopholes, a 12-24 month lead can be locked in by 2028. If they don't, the CCP could achieve near-parity.

## The Four Fronts of Competition

Anthropic identifies four fronts in the US-China AI competition:

1. **Intelligence** — which countries develop the most capable AI models (most important front)
2. **Domestic adoption** — which countries integrate AI most effectively across commercial and public sectors
3. **Global distribution** — which countries deploy the global AI stack on which the world economy runs
4. **Resilience** — which countries sustain political stability through the economic transition

## The Compute Gap

- Democracies are winning compute leadership today — Huawei will produce just 4% of NVIDIA's aggregate compute in 2026 and 2% in 2027
- China lacks EUV lithography technology and cannot manufacture high-bandwidth memory at scale
- If US tightens restrictions, one study estimates America will have ~11x more compute than China's AI sector
- China's own AI leaders confirm export controls are effective; top labs cite compute scarcity as their chief constraint

## How China Stays Competitive: Two Workarounds

1. **Illicit compute access**: Chip smuggling (e.g., $2.5B Supermicro case) and offshore data center access in Southeast Asia, where US export law covers chip sales but not remote access
2. **Distillation attacks**: China-based labs create thousands of fraudulent accounts to harvest outputs from US frontier models, replicating capabilities at a fraction of the cost

## The Mythos Preview Wake-Up Call

Mythos Preview (April 2026, Project Glasswing) signals an acceleration period. One PRC cybersecurity analyst wrote that China is "still sharpening our swords while the other side has suddenly mounted a fully automatic Gatling gun." Anthropic argues 2026 is a "breakaway opportunity" for American AI.

## Two Scenarios for 2028

### Scenario 1: Democracies Lead (Commanding Lead)
- US models 12-24 months ahead on intelligence, lead growing
- "Country of geniuses in a data center" reality across critical industries
- American AI becomes backbone of global economy
- Self-reinforcing cycle: lead → stronger coalition → stronger lead
- Democracy-led international order anchored through AI transition

### Scenario 2: CCP Neck-and-Neck (Near-Frontier)
- PRC models only months behind US models despite weak domestic chip production
- Rapid state adoption via "AI+" policies shifts balance of power
- PLA cyber force gains access to critical infrastructure globally
- China wins global adoption on cost via Huawei/Alibaba data centers with older chips
- Authoritarian AI norms shape global rules; automated repression at scale

## Policy Recommendations

1. **Close compute loopholes**: Tighten smuggling enforcement, close offshore data center access, strengthen SME controls
2. **Defend innovations**: Legislative clarification that distillation attacks are illegal, facilitate threat intel sharing between US labs and government
3. **Champion export of American AI**: Promote global adoption of trusted AI hardware and models shaped by democratic principles

## Key Arguments

- Compute advantage compounds into algorithmic advantage, which compounds into durable AI lead
- Neck-and-neck competition could disincentivize safety — only 3 of 13 top Chinese AI labs publish safety evaluations; none disclose CBRN risks
- Frontier AI is dual-use: accelerates military "intelligentization," cyber offense, and R&D in semiconductors/biotech
- Engagement on AI safety is most productive when US maintains a large capabilities advantage
- Window of opportunity to lock in lead is limited and may not remain open for long
