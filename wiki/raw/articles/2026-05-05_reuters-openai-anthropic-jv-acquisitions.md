# OpenAI, Anthropic ventures in talks to buy AI services firms

**Source**: Reuters, Milana Vinn, May 5, 2026
**URL**: https://www.reuters.com/world/openai-anthropic-ventures-talks-buy-ai-services-firms-sources-say-2026-05-05/

The joint ventures OpenAI and Anthropic separately created with private equity firms are in talks to acquire services companies that help businesses deploy AI. OpenAI's venture is in advanced stages on three deals.

Both are looking to incorporate hundreds of engineers and consultants to help companies put their AI models to work.

OpenAI is raising roughly $4 billion from 19 investors including TPG, Bain Capital, and Brookfield Asset Management. The vehicle, called The Deployment Company, will be announced later this week. Anthropic raised $1.5 billion from Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs.

Most capital raised through the JVs is expected to fund acquisitions of engineering services and consulting firms.

Key insight: what is often cast as a high-margin software business that could eliminate consultants still depends on labor-intensive, highly skilled services. Companies need deep technical help integrating AI into legacy systems and workflows.

This marks the AI race expanding from model development to model deployment.
