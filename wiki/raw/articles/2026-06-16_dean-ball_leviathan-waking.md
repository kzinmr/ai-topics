Dean Ball's long-form analysis of the Fable 5/Mythos 5 conflict as a watershed moment: the U.S. government asserting sovereign control over frontier AI.

Core analogy: FDA-drug analogy — government went from laissez-faire to incomprehensibly strict in weeks.

Key narrative:
- Anthropic released Fable (commercial Mythos with guardrails)
- Trump Admin became aware of a jailbreak, demanded de-deployment
- Anthropic did not de-deploy → government imposed worldwide export controls on Fable and Mythos
- Anthropic had to pull models globally due to inability to validate U.S. personhood

Key insight: The sheer opacity of everything. No written policy, no actual text to analyze. Game of he-said, she-said.

David Sacks critique: Initially downplayed Mythos, then contrasted OpenAI's GPT 5.5 openness vs Anthropic's caution. Contradicted by EO 14409 which explicitly disclaims mandatory licensing.

Core thesis: Making superintelligence is a profoundly political act. A model like Mythos implicates state sovereignty. Anthropic seen as making a move against the U.S. Government.

Solution: Not "FDA for AI" but technocratic institutions that mediate between political actors and private enterprise. Procedure, structure, predictability that constrains the state as much as the industry.

Critique of Fable's machine-learning safeguards: "safeguards" that silently degrade performance without user awareness create trust problems.
