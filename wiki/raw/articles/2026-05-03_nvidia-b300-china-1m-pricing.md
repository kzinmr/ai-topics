# NVIDIA B300 Server Hits $1M in China — Chip Scarcity Under US Export Curbs

**Source:** Reuters (Exclusive), April 30, 2026
**URLs:** https://www.reuters.com/world/china/prices-nvidias-b300-server-1-million-china-us-curbs-sources-say-2026-04-30/, https://thenextweb.com/news/nvidia-b300-server-1-million-china-export-controls

## Summary

NVIDIA's B300 AI servers are now selling for approximately 7 million yuan (~$1 million) each in China — nearly double the US list price of ~$550,000. The surge is driven by tightened US export controls, a crackdown on chip smuggling (following the March 2026 prosecution of Supermicro co-founder Yih-Shyan "Wally" Liaw), and voracious demand from Chinese AI companies.

## Drivers

1. **US Export Controls:** B300 GPUs restricted from direct sale to China
2. **Grey Market Crackdown:** Supermicro co-founder arrest choked key supply channels
3. **AI Demand Surge:** Chinese tech giants racing for cost-efficient inference hardware
4. **Token Economics:** Hardware is the key cost driver for AI model monetization

## Broader Implications

- **Split Market:** Same hardware trades at ~2x premium in China vs. US
- **Rental Market:** Monthly B300 rentals reaching 190,000 yuan on short-term contracts
- **MATCH Act:** US House Foreign Affairs Committee advanced legislation (April 22, 2026) requiring Netherlands/Japan to align chip equipment restrictions
- **DeepSeek Factor:** Chinese models gaining global usage share, increasing domestic compute demand
- **Huawei Ascend Alternative:** DeepSeek V4 confirmed running on Huawei Ascend 950 for inference
