# CAISI Signs AI Model Review Agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI

## Key Facts

On May 5, 2026, the **Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI)** at the Department of Commerce's National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) announced new agreements with **Google DeepMind**, **Microsoft**, and **xAI** to conduct pre-deployment evaluations and targeted research on frontier AI capabilities.

## Background

- **CAISI** was originally established as the US AI Safety Institute in August 2024 under the Biden administration, when it signed similar agreements with OpenAI and Anthropic
- Renamed and restructured under Commerce Secretary **Howard Lutnick** to reflect "America's AI Action Plan"
- Designated as industry's primary point of contact within the U.S. government for AI testing, collaborative research, and best practice development
- Director: **Chris Fall**

## Scope of Agreements

- Pre-deployment evaluation of AI models before public release
- Post-deployment assessment and ongoing research
- Testing in classified environments
- Information-sharing to drive voluntary product improvements
- Developers frequently provide models with reduced or removed safeguards for thorough evaluation
- Agreements drafted with flexibility to rapidly respond to continued AI advancements

## Results to Date

- CAISI has completed **more than 40 evaluations** of AI models, including on state-of-the-art unreleased models
- The **TRAINS Taskforce** — an interagency group of experts focused on AI national security concerns — participates in evaluations and provides regular feedback

## National Security Focus

- Cybersecurity
- Biosecurity
- Chemical weapons risks

## Context and Catalysts

The agreements come after **Anthropic's Claude Mythos** model demonstrated unprecedented autonomous vulnerability discovery capabilities, heightening concerns about AI's cybersecurity impact. The Mythos situation created an ad-hoc "informal licensing" regime (government informally asked Anthropic not to release broadly), highlighting the need for formal evaluation frameworks.

## Microsoft's Parallel UK Agreement

On the same day, Microsoft also announced an agreement with the UK's **AI Security Institute (AISI)** for model testing, emphasizing that "testing for national security and large-scale public safety risks necessarily must be a collaborative endeavor with governments" — Natasha Crampton, Microsoft Chief Responsible AI Officer.

## Industry Context

- The White House was separately reported (NYT) to be considering a formal AI review process and potential executive order
- The agreements reflect growing bipartisan consensus that frontier AI models require pre-deployment government evaluation for national security
- With Google, Microsoft, and xAI joining OpenAI and Anthropic, all five major U.S. frontier AI labs now have CAISI evaluation agreements
