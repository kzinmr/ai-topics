# Responsible AI Chapter Summary

From the Stanford HAI 2026 AI Index Report. Examines safety, fairness, transparency, governance, and measurement gaps.

## 1. Responsible AI Benchmarking Lag
- Almost all frontier model developers report capability benchmarks (MMLU, SWE-bench)
- But reporting on responsible AI benchmarks remains **sparse**
- AI incidents: **362** in 2025 (up from 233 in 2024)

## 2. Knowledge vs Belief Confusion
- New accuracy benchmark: hallucination rates across 26 top models: **22% to 94%**
- GPT-4o: accuracy dropped from 98.2% → 64.4% (when false claim presented as user belief)
- DeepSeek R1: fell from 90%+ → 14.4%

## 3. Organizational Responsible AI
- AI governance roles: **+17%** in 2025
- Businesses with no responsible AI policies: **24% → 11%**
- Main obstacles: knowledge gaps (59%), budget (48%), regulatory uncertainty (41%)

## 4. Regulation Mix Shifting
- GDPR: most cited but slipped 65% → 60%
- ISO/IEC 42001 (AI management standard): cited by **36%**
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework: **33%**
- Organizations citing no regulatory influence: 17% → 12%

## 5. Language Gaps
- AI works best in English; gap wider than benchmarks suggest
- On HELM Arabic: regionally developed model outscored GPT-5.1 and Gemini 2.5 Flash
- On Slovenian commonsense: leading models lost ~50% accuracy in regional dialect

## 6. Transparency Decline
- Foundation Model Transparency Index: rose 37→58 (2023-2024), then **dropped to 40 in 2025**
- Major gaps: training data, compute resources, post-deployment impact

## 7. Jailbreak Vulnerability
- AILuminate benchmark: frontier models scored "Very Good"/"Good" under normal use
- But safety performance dropped under adversarial/jailbreak prompts

## 8. Responsible AI Tradeoffs
- Training for one responsible AI dimension (safety, fairness, privacy) consistently degrades others
- Tradeoffs not well understood
