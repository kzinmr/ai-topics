## Summary

Report that Google Chrome silently downloads a 4GB Gemini AI model to users' machines without explicit consent. The model is ostensibly for privacy protection features, but the lack of user notification and the size of the download raise questions about AI model distribution practices, user consent, and the future of browser-integrated AI.

Key issues:
- **Silent installation**: No user consent or notification for a 4GB download
- **Privacy paradox**: AI model installed "to protect privacy" without consent
- **Browser as AI platform**: Signals Google's strategy to make Chrome an AI runtime, not just a browser
- **Disk space impact**: 4GB is significant for many users
- **Gemini Nano integration**: Likely related to Chrome's built-in Gemini Nano for on-device AI features
