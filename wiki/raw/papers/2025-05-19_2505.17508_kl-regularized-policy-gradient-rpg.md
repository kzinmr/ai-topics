# On the Design of KL-Regularized Policy Gradient Algorithms for LLM Reasoning

**Authors:** Yifan Zhang, Yifeng Liu, Huizhuo Yuan, Yang Yuan, Quanquan Gu, Andrew Chi-Chih Yao

**Published in:** ICLR 2026

**Links:** [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17508) | [GitHub](https://github.com/complex-reasoning/RPG)

## Abstract

This paper addresses the fragmented landscape of KL-regularized Policy Gradient (PG) algorithms used to enhance LLM reasoning. The authors introduce the **Regularized Policy Gradient (RPG)** view, a unified framework that clarifies the relationship between different KL variants (forward vs. reverse, normalized vs. unnormalized) and their estimators. The research identifies a critical importance-weighting mismatch in the popular **GRPO** algorithm and proposes **RPG-REINFORCE with RPG-Style Clip**, a stable, scalable RL algorithm that outperforms existing methods like DAPO and official instruct models on mathematical reasoning benchmarks.

## Key Contributions

1. **RPG View** — A compact, unified derivation for optimizing KL-regularized objectives in off-policy settings. Proves the widely-used k₃ penalty is mathematically equivalent to the **unnormalized KL** divergence.
2. **Gradient Equivalence** — Specifies exact conditions under which REINFORCE-style losses (using stop-gradient) are gradient-equivalent to fully differentiable surrogates.
3. **GRPO Correction** — Identified an off-policy importance-weighting mismatch in the KL term of GRPO and provided a corrected version.
4. **RPG-Style Clip** — Clipped-importance-sampling step within RPG-REINFORCE, preventing extreme gradient updates while maintaining mathematical integrity.
5. **Iterative Reference-Policy Updates** — Periodic updates to the reference policy maintain stability during long-horizon reasoning tasks.

## Technical Framework: RPG Design Surface

Three dimensions of KL-regularized algorithms:

1. **KL Direction:** Forward vs. Reverse KL
2. **Normalization:** Normalized vs. Unnormalized KL variants
3. **Estimators:** Analysis of k₁, k₂, k₃ estimators across RLHF literature

> Key Finding: "Under the off-policy setting, what weighting is required for each KL variant so that the surrogate we optimize yields the exact gradient of the intended KL-regularized objective?"

## Three-Pillar Implementation Approach

- **A: KL-Correct Objective** — RPG derivation ensures surrogate loss matches intended KL-regularized gradient
- **B: Clipped Importance Sampling** — RPG-Style Clip handles off-policy REINFORCE variance
- **C: Iterative Reference-Policy Updates** — Periodic reference policy updates for long-horizon reasoning
