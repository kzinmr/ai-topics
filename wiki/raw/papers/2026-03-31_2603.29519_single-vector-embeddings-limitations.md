# On Strengths and Limitations of Single-Vector Embeddings

**arXiv:** [2603.29519](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29519) | **Submitted:** 2026-03-31 | **Authors:** Archish S, Mihir Agarwal, Ankit Garg, Neeraj Kayal, Kirankumar Shiragur (Microsoft Research India)

> **TL;DR:** Refutes Weller et al. (2025)'s claim that dimensionality limits single-vector embeddings. Shows domain shift + misalignment are the real causes, multi-vector models are fundamentally superior, and single-vector models suffer catastrophic forgetting when fine-tuned.

---

## 1. Introduction & Motivation

Recent work (Weller et al., 2025) introduced **LIMIT** — a naturalistic dataset where popular single-vector embedding models suffer drastic retrieval failures. Weller et al. hypothesized that **limited dimensionality** was the primary cause. This paper **refutes that claim** through theoretical analysis and controlled experiments.

### The LIMIT Dataset (Weller et al., 2025)

- **Structure:** 1,000 queries, 50,000 documents
- Each document is a **list of 48 attributes** (e.g., "Jon Durben likes Quokkas and Apples")
- Each query asks **"Who likes {attribute}?"** (single-attribute query, length-1 set)
- **Ground-truth relevance** is set-theoretic: `rel_set(Q,D) = |Q ∩ D| / |Q|`
- Relevance is binary: a document is relevant iff it contains the queried attribute
- Each query has exactly **k=2 relevant documents**

---

## 2. Key Findings

### 2.1 Dimensionality is NOT the Bottleneck

**Observation 2.3:** For the LIMIT dataset (k=2 relevant docs per query), embeddings of dimension **d = 2k + 1 = 5** are sufficient to perfectly capture the relevance matrix.

> *Proof sketch:* Each row of the sign matrix has at most 2k sign changes → can be realized as the sign pattern of a degree-2k polynomial → resulting matrix has rank ≤ 2k+1.

**Observation 2.4:** If only **top-k retrieval** matters, embeddings of dimension 2k+1 suffice to make the top-k results relevant.

**Proposition 2.5 (Johnson-Lindenstrauss bound):** For LIMIT-like set-based datasets, assign each attribute a unit vector in ℝᵈ. The embedding of a query/document is the normalized sum of its attribute vectors. When `d = Ω(min(|U|, ℓ_Q² ℓ_D² log |U|))`, cosine ordering matches set-theoretic relevance.

> **Key quote:** "Low-dimensional single-vector embeddings for LIMIT-like datasets exist whenever at least one of the following quantities is small: the sparsity of the relevance matrix, the number of documents to be retrieved, the length of queries and documents, or the size of the attribute universe."

**Current models use dimensions >100 — so dimensionality alone cannot explain the failure.**

### 2.2 Tokenization and Linguistic Similarity

Created **Atomic LIMIT**: attributes replaced by single-token nouns, linguistically dissimilar.

| Model | Dataset | Recall@2 | Recall@10 |
|-------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Qwen3-Emb-0.6B (1024 dim) | LIMIT | 0.0075 | 0.0115 |
| Qwen3-Emb-0.6B (1024 dim) | Atomic LIMIT | 0.0215 | 0.0305 |
| GTE-ModernColBERT-v1 (128 dim) | LIMIT | 0.2740 | 0.3945 |
| GTE-ModernColBERT-v1 (128 dim) | Atomic LIMIT | **0.968** | — |

**Key insight:** Tokenization and linguistic similarity have **modest impact** on single-vector models (improvement: 1%→3% Recall@10), but **massive impact** on multi-vector models (27%→96% Recall@2). Multi-vector models are limited by bad tokenization; single-vector models have deeper, fundamental issues.

### 2.3 Domain Shift and Misalignment

- LIMIT documents are unnatural long unordered lists of attributes → **domain shift**
- **Finetuning** on LIMIT-like data improves single-vector significantly (Recall@10: ~1% → ~40%), but still far behind multi-vector (40% → 98%)
- **Catastrophic forgetting:** Single-vector finetuning drops MSMARCO Recall@100 by **>40%**; multi-vector forgetting is **minimal (~1%)**

> **Takeaway:** Finetuning mitigates domain shift but cannot close the gap. Single-vector models are fundamentally weaker and fragile.

### 2.4 The Drowning-in-Documents Paradox

As corpus size grows, relevant documents are "drowned out" by irrelevant ones due to noisy similarity scores.

**Goodness metric:** `G = (μ_+ - μ_-) / σ_-` where μ_+ is the mean score of relevant docs, μ_- of irrelevant, and σ_- is the standard deviation of irrelevant. Drowning probability ~ exp(-Θ(G²)).

**Toy model comparison:**
- **Single-vector:** `G ≈ √D × K / √(mn)` → requires high dimension D
- **Multi-vector:** `G ≈ Θ(K √(D / (m log n)))` → much larger for same D

> **Key result:** "Single-vector models are more susceptible to drowning effects than multi-vector models. The reason is that the scores of relevant and irrelevant documents are much better separated in multi-vector models."

---

## 3. Experimental Summary

- **Tokenization/linguistics** explain most of multi-vector's LIMIT failure but only a tiny fraction of single-vector failure
- **Finetuning** helps single-vector but causes catastrophic forgetting — a fragile band-aid
- **Multi-vector models** are fundamentally more robust across domain shifts, scale, and fine-tuning

## 4. Related Work

- **Weller et al. (2025):** LIMIT dataset; proposed sign-rank/dimensionality as the cause

- **Jacob et al. (2025):** Extended drowning analysis to modern architectures
- **Alon et al. (2016):** Sign-rank and polynomial representations (theoretical basis for Observation 2.3)
