# The Hardware Lottery

**Author:** Sara Hooker (Google Research, Brain Team)

## Abstract

Hardware, systems and algorithms research communities have historically had different incentive structures and fluctuating motivation to engage with each other explicitly. This essay introduces the term **hardware lottery** to describe when a research idea wins because it is suited to the available software and hardware and *not* because the idea is superior to alternative research directions. Examples from early computer science history illustrate how hardware lotteries can delay research progress by casting successful ideas as failures. These lessons are particularly salient given the advent of domain specialized hardware which make it increasingly costly to stray off the beaten path.

## Core Contributions

1. **The Hardware Lottery Framework** — A meta-framework for understanding why certain research directions succeed: it's often because of hardware/software compatibility, not algorithmic superiority
2. **The Anna Karenina Principle of AI** — Breakthroughs require multiple factors (algorithm, hardware, software) to align serendipitously
3. **Historical Analysis** — Case studies of neural networks (lost decades due to CPU incompatibility), symbolic AI (won via LISP/PROLOG), and GPU-driven resurgence
4. **Domain Specialization Warning** — As we move toward domain-specific accelerators (TPUs, NPUs), the hardware lottery intensifies because specialized hardware locks in current algorithmic paradigms
5. **Future Agenda** — Calls for reconfigurable hardware (FPGAs, CGRAs), profiling tools, domain-specific languages, and investment in non-standard computing (neuromorphic, optical, quantum)

## Key Data Points

- 2012: Google used 16,000 CPU cores to classify cats; 2013: same task with 2 CPU cores + 4 GPUs
- Capsule Networks (Sabour et al., 2017) perform poorly on GPUs/TPUs due to routing-by-agreement instead of standard matrix multiplies
- Human brain (85B neurons) runs on ~20W; training GPT-3 costs ~$12M

## Significance for AI Infrastructure Engineering

The Hardware Lottery is a foundational framework for AI infra engineers because it explains:
- **Why CUDA dominates**: NVIDIA's GPU+software stack won the hardware lottery for deep learning
- **TPU lock-in**: Google's domain-specific hardware optimizes for existing paradigms, creating a fast-lane for transformer-based research
- **The cost of deviation**: Alternative architectures (sparse models, neuromorphic, biologically-inspired approaches) face structural disadvantages on current hardware
- **Path dependency in infrastructure**: Infrastructure decisions today determine which research ideas have a chance tomorrow
