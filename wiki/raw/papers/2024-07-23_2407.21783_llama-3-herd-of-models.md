# The Llama 3 Herd of Models

> **Abstract:** Modern artificial intelligence (AI) systems are powered by foundation models. This paper presents a new set of foundation models, called Llama 3. It is a herd of language models that natively support multilinguality, coding, reasoning, and tool usage. Our largest model is a dense Transformer with 405B parameters and a context window of up to 128K tokens… We find that Llama 3 delivers comparable quality to leading language models such as GPT‑4 on a plethora of tasks.

## 1. Introduction & General Overview

Three key levers: **data**, **scale**, and **managing complexity**.

- **Data** – Pre‑training corpus of ~**15T multilingual tokens** (vs. 1.8T in Llama 2), improved curation and filtering.
- **Scale** – Flagship model pre‑trained with **3.8 × 10²⁵ FLOPs** (~50× Llama 2). 405B parameters on 15.6T tokens. Smaller models trained far beyond compute‑optimal for better inference‑time performance.
- **Complexity** – Standard **dense Transformer** (no MoE) for stability. Simple post‑training: **SFT, Rejection Sampling, DPO**.

### Development stages

1. **Language model pre‑training** → next‑token prediction on 15.6T tokens (8K → 128K context).
2. **Language model post‑training** → several rounds of SFT + DPO, alignment, tool‑use, coding, reasoning.
3. **Multimodal experiments** (not released):
   - Separate **image encoder** (ViT‑H/14, 630M→850M) and **speech encoder**.
   - **Vision adapter** (cross‑attention layers) trained on image‑text pairs.
   - **Video adapter** (temporal aggregator + extra cross‑attention) on video‑text pairs.
   - **Speech adapter** to integrate speech understanding.

## 2. Pre‑Training

### 2.1 Pre‑Training Data

**Corpus** – multiple sources up to end of 2023, cleaned and deduplicated.

#### Web data curation
- **PII & safety filtering** – domains with adult content, high PII, or rated harmful removed.
- **Text extraction** – custom HTML parser; markdown removed (harmful for models trained on web data).
- **De‑duplication** – URL‑level, document‑level (MinHash), line‑level (remove lines appearing >6 times per 30M documents).
- **Heuristic filtering** – duplicated n‑gram coverage ratio, "dirty word" count, token‑distribution KL divergence.
- **Model‑based quality filtering** – fasttext (Wikipedia‑referenced) + DistilRoberta classifiers trained on Llama 2 judgments.
- **Code & reasoning data** – dedicated pipelines with DistilRoberta classifiers trained on Llama 2 annotations to identify math/STEM/code pages.
- **Multilingual data** – fasttext language ID (176 languages), language‑specific dedup, fasttext quality classifiers, and a multilingual Llama 2‑based ranker.

#### Data mix determination
- **Knowledge classifier** to downsample over‑represented categories (e.g., arts & entertainment).
- **Scaling laws** for data mix: train small models on candidate mixes, extrapolate to large.
- Final mix: **~50% general knowledge, 25% math/reasoning, 17% code, 8% multilingual**.

#### Annealing data
- Annealing on small, high‑quality code and math data boosts benchmark scores.
- For Llama 3 8B: GSM8K +24%, MATH +6.4%; **negligible for 405B** (already strong in‑context learning).
- Used to evaluate small domain‑specific datasets quickly (train 8B from 50% checkpoint for 40B tokens with 30% new data weight).

### 2.2 Model Architecture

Standard **dense Transformer** with minor adaptations from Llama/Llama 2.

| Hyperparameter | 8B | 70B | 405B |
|----------------|----|-----|------|
| Layers | 32 | 80 | 126 |
| Model Dimension | 4,096 | 8,192 | 16,384 |
| FFN Dimension | 14,336 | 28,672 | 53,248 |
| Attention Heads | 32 | 64 | 128 |
| Key/Value Heads (GQA) | 8 | 8 | 8 |
| Peak LR | 3e‑4 | 1.5e‑4 | 8e‑5 |
| Activation | SwiGLU | SwiGLU | SwiGLU |
| Vocab Size | 128,000 | 128,000 | 128,000 |
| Positional Embeddings | RoPE θ=500,000 | ← | ← |

- **Vocabulary** – 100K tiktoken + 28K non‑English tokens → improves compression (3.94 characters/token vs. 3.17) and downstream performance.
- **GQA** – 8 key‑value heads for faster inference, smaller KV caches.
- **Document mask** – attention restricted to same document in long‑sequence pre‑training.
- **RoPE** base frequency 500,000 (effective up to 32K+ context).
- Compute‑optimal for 3.8 × 10²⁵ FLOPs → **402B parameters on 16.55T tokens**, chosen **405B** for robustness.

### 2.3 Scaling Laws

Two‑stage methodology to predict downstream benchmark performance from compute budget:

1. **Correlation between compute and performance**: Second‑order polynomial for 80‑model scaling‑law experiments with FLOPs from 6 × 10¹⁸ to 10²².
   - Next‑token prediction loss (validation), NLL on normalized negative log‑likelihood, and generation‑based task scores (e.g., MMLU, ARC‑Challenge) all follow the power‑law correlation.
   - Scaling‑law computations were done on 8K‑context‑length data.

2. **Data mix prediction**: Train small models on candidate mixes, extrapolate to large model; select mix minimizing validation loss on held‑out data. This allowed quantitative tradeoffs among general knowledge, math, code, and multilingual data.

### 2.4 Training Recipe

- **Hardware:** Up to 16K H100 GPUs on Meta's Grand Teton platform.
- **3D parallelism:** 4D for 405B (TP + CP on one node, PP + DP across nodes).
- **Reliability:** 405B pre‑training on 15.6T tokens had **466 job interruptions** over 54 days (1 interruption every 3 hours on average, ~78% unplanned due to hardware/network issues). Effective training time **38.5 days**.
- **Loss spikes:** Do not disrupt training if resolved quickly; only one manual intervention needed (restart from earlier checkpoint with different batch).

### 2.5 Post‑Training

Post‑training pipeline designed to elicit multiple capabilities in a single model:

1. **Reward model** trained on human preference data (helpfulness, safety, etc.) with safety pre‑training.
2. **SFT** on high‑quality human‑annotated and synthetic data across capabilities.
3. **Rejection Sampling (RS)** – generate K outputs per prompt, select best via RM + checks, train on best.
4. **DPO** – Direct Preference Optimization on preference pairs (chosen vs rejected) to further align.
5. **Final averaging** – average weights of RM‑selected checkpoints (different versions from RS and DPO stages).

Key decisions:
- Used only **DPO** (no on‑policy RL like PPO) for alignment — simpler, fewer moving parts.
- **Synthetic data generation** for tool‑use (BFCL, API calling), coding, reasoning, and multilingual tasks.
- Tool use: single‑turn tasks (BFCL), multi‑turn tasks (Nexus), zero‑shot function calling with Brave Search, Wolfram Alpha, and Python interpreter.
- Post‑training data: multiple rounds of SFT → DPO, with synthetic data generated by Llama 3 itself in some cases.

**Data quality and quantity** tradeoffs: SFT quality more important than quantity; DPO quantity more important than SFT quantity; code and reasoning benefit more from synthetic data than knowledge‑grounded tasks.

- Vision: Llama 3 + ViT‑H/14 trained on ~6B image‑text pairs → competitive with GPT‑4V on MMMU (val), VQAv2, AI2D, ChartQA, DocVQA.
- Video: Llama 3 + temporal aggregator + video‑text pairs → competitive with Gemini 1.0 Ultra, GPT‑4V on PerceptionTest, TVQA.

## 4. Safety

- **Llama Guard 3** – input/output safeguard LLM, released with the paper.
- **Llama Code Shield** – safety filter for LLM‑generated code.
- **Red teaming** with domain experts (CBRN, cyber, child safety) and automated testing.
- **Safety finetuning** integrated into post‑training pipeline (safety SFT examples + DPO preference pairs).
- **CyberSecEval 2** – cybersecurity risk evaluation suite.

## 5. Significance

- First open‑source model to **match or exceed GPT‑4** on multiple benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, ARC Challenge, MGSM).
- Demonstrated that a **dense Transformer** (no MoE) can achieve frontier performance at 405B scale.
- Established the **two‑stage development paradigm** (pre‑training → post‑training with SFT + RS + DPO) as the standard for LLM development.
- Validated **scaling laws for data mix** — quantitative optimization of general knowledge vs. math vs. code vs. multilingual tradeoffs.
- **Post‑training data quality > quantity** for SFT; **synthetic data** most effective for code and reasoning.
- **466 hardware interruptions over 54 days** — practical reliability data for frontier training.
- Multimodal (vision/video/speech) experiments while keeping the language model frozen, providing a blueprint for staged multimodal capability addition.
